'''John Roger Williams''' (born September 13, 1949) is an [[United States|American]] politician who has been the [[United States House of Representatives|U.S. Representative]] for [[Texas's 25th congressional district]] since 2013. Previously, he served under [[Governor of Texas|Governor]] [[Rick Perry]] as [[Secretary of State of Texas]] from November 2004 to July 1, 2007.

==Early life, education, and business career==
Williams was born in [[Evanston, Illinois]] in 1949<ref>{{cite web|last=Cooper |first=Kent |url=http://www.rollcall.com/new-members/roger-williams-house-r-texas-25.html |title=Roger Williams (Texas-25) |publisher=Roll Call |date=2014-08-11 |accessdate=2014-08-19}}</ref> and  raised in [[Fort Worth, Texas|Fort Worth]]. He was a star [[college baseball]] player for the [[TCU Horned Frogs baseball|Texas Christian University Horned Frogs]] (TCU) from 1968 to 1971 and was selected in the 25th round of the [[1971 MLB Draft]] by the [[Atlanta Braves]]. He played outfield and third base in the Braves' [[farm system]]. He reached as high as the [[Class A (baseball)|Class A]] [[Western Carolinas League]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Roger Williams  Minor Leagues Statistics & History|url=https://www.baseball-reference.com/register/player.fcgi?id=willia018joh|website=[[Baseball-Reference.com]]|publisher=[[Sports Reference LLC]]|accessdate=16 June 2017|language=en}}</ref> He returned to Texas to coach TCU's  baseball team before he embarked on a career in [[business]] and public affairs. Williams joined his father in a family [[automobile]] dealership founded in 1939.

==Early political activism (1994–2004)==
He served as Regional Finance Chairman for [[George W. Bush as Governor of Texas|Governor George W. Bush]] in his 1994 and 1998 elections. He became North Texas Chairman for the [[George W. Bush presidential campaign, 2000|Bush/Cheney 2000 campaign]], North Texas Finance Chairman in 2004, and National Grassroots Fundraising Chairman for the [[George W. Bush presidential campaign, 2004|2004 campaign]]. Williams was appointed by President [[George W. Bush]] in 2001 to be Chairman of the Republican National Finance Committee’s Eagles Program.

He has also served as State Finance Chair for [[John Cornyn]]'s [[United States Senate election in Texas, 2002|U.S. Senate campaign]] and as the National Director of the "Patriots" program for Cornyn.<ref>{{cite web|author=Joel |url=http://www.rogerforcongress.com/meet-roger |title=Roger Williams for Congress &#124; Meet Roger |publisher=Rogerforcongress.com |date=2005-02-08 |accessdate=2014-08-19}}</ref>

==Perry administration (2002–2007)==
Williams was appointed by Governor Perry to the Texas Motor Vehicle Board. He was nominated by [[Lieutenant Governor]] [[Bill Ratliff]] to the Special Committee on State Employee Compensation and Benefits.

Perry appointed Williams to be his fourth [[Secretary of State of Texas]]. As secretary of state, Williams was the Chief Elections Officer for Texas, assisted county election officials and ensured the uniform application and interpretation of election laws through the state. He oversaw the state’s efforts to comply with the federal [[Help America Vote Act]].{{Citation needed|date=April 2013}}

Williams worked to promote economic development, investment, and job creation in Texas.{{Citation needed|date=April 2013}} He was also Perry's chief liaison to [[Mexico]] and the Border Region of Texas. In this capacity, he worked with Mexican federal, state, and local officials on common issues.{{Citation needed|date=April 2013}}

As the overseer and director of Texas elections, Williams's office was in the spotlight during the [[Texas gubernatorial election, 2006|2006 Texas governor's race]]. Independent candidate [[Kinky Friedman|Richard Friedman]] (commonly known as "Kinky") successfully lobbied to have the name "Kinky" placed on the election ballot. Another Independent candidate, [[Carole K. Strayhorn]], sought to have the name "Grandma" placed on the ballot next to her name. (Strayhorn's campaign slogan was "one tough grandma".) Strayhorn's request was denied. Friedman and Strayhorn finished third and fourth, respectively, in the election, behind Democrat [[Chris Bell (politician)|Chris Bell]] and the winner, incumbent Republican Perry.

Williams announced on June 11, 2007, that he would leave the appointed position to "pursue other opportunities".

==State Victory chairman (2007–2008)==
On July 16, 2007, Williams was named chairman of the Texas [[Republican Party (United States)|Republican]] Victory 2008 Coordinated Campaign, which assists candidates up and down the [[general election]] ballot.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.star-telegram.com/state_news/story/171326.html |accessdate=July 23, 2007 |title=unknown title }}{{dead link|date=May 2016|bot=medic}}{{cbignore|bot=medic}}</ref>  Republicans again won all statewide office in 2008 and gained a seat in the [[United States House of Representatives]] but lost four seats in the [[Texas House of Representatives]] and county offices in populous [[Harris County, Texas|Harris County]].

Late in 2008, Williams formed an exploratory committee to consider a run for the [[United States Senate]] seat held by fellow Republican [[Kay Bailey Hutchison]] of [[Dallas, Texas|Dallas]], who had formed her own committee to consider a 2010 race for governor of Texas. Williams considered a run for Senate in 2012 but decided to run for the U.S. House of Representatives instead.<ref name="williamsswitches">{{cite web |url= http://trailblazersblog.dallasnews.com/archives/2011/06/roger-williams-switches-from-s.html|title= Roger Williams switches from Senate to House race |author= Wayne Slater|date= 28 June 2011|work= Trailblazers Blog|publisher= Dallas Morning News|accessdate=18 July 2011}}</ref>

==U.S. House of Representatives==

===2012 election===
{{See also|United States House of Representatives elections in Texas, 2012#District 25}}
In June 2011, Williams announced that he was dropping his Senate bid to instead run for [[Texas' 25th congressional district]] seat.<ref name="williamsswitches" /><ref>{{cite web|author=Alex |url=http://www.rogerforcongress.com |title=Roger Williams for Congress |publisher=Rogerforcongress.com |date= |accessdate=2014-08-19}}</ref>  Williams ranked first with 25 percent of the vote and qualified for [[runoff election]]. Wes Riddle ranked second with 15 percent and met Williams in the second round of balloting.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=739104 |title=Ourcampaigns.com |publisher=Ourcampaigns.com |date= |accessdate=2014-08-19}}</ref> In the July 31 election, Williams defeated Riddle 58 to 42 percent.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=767639 |title=Ourcampaigns.com |publisher=Ourcampaigns.com |date= |accessdate=2014-08-19}}</ref> In the November general election, Williams defeated Democratic nominee Elaine Henderson 58%–37%.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=759994 |title=Ourcampaigns.com |publisher=Ourcampaigns.com |date= |accessdate=2014-08-19}}</ref>

===2014 re-election===

In the November 4, 2014 general election, Williams defeated Democrat Marco Montoya and Libertarian John Betz, with Williams receiving 107,120 votes (60.22%) over Montoya's 64,463 votes (36.24%) and Betz's 6,300 votes (3.54%).<ref>{{cite web|title=2014 Statewide General Election Results|url=http://elections.sos.state.tx.us/elchist175_state.htm|website=Texas Secretary of State|publisher=Texas Secretary of State}}</ref> Williams unsuccessfully challenged [[National Republican Congressional Committee]] Chairman [[Greg Walden]] of [[Oregon]] in Walden's bid for a second term as chairman after the 2014 elections.<ref>{{cite web|last1=Center|first1=Shira|title=Roger Williams Makes Play for NRCC Chairman|url=http://atr.rollcall.com/nrcc-chairman-roger-williams-greg-walden/?dcz=emailalert|website=www.rollcall.com|publisher=Roll Call|accessdate=31 July 2014}}</ref>

===2016 re-election===

Williams won his third term in the House in the general election held on November 8, 2016, when [[Donald Trump]] carried the [[electoral vote]] majority over [[Hillary Rodham Clinton]]. Williams polled 180,988 votes (58.4 percent) to 117,073 (37.7 percent) received by the Democrat Kathi Thomas. [[Libertarian Party (United States)|Libertarian]] Loren Marc Schneiderman held the remaining 12,135 ballots (3.9 percent).<ref>{{cite web|url=http://elections.sos.state.tx.us/elchist319_state.htm|title=Election Results|date=November 8, 2016|publisher=Texas Secretary of State|accessdate=December 17, 2016}}</ref>

===Political stances===

====Immigration and refugees====
Williams supported President [[Donald Trump]]'s 2017 [[Executive Order 13769|executive order]] imposing a ban on entry to the U.S. to citizens of seven Muslim-majority countries, calling it a "commonsense" measure and saying that opponents "are lost in the [[political correctness]] of this."<ref name="Blake1">{{cite web|url=https://www.washingtonpost.com/news/the-fix/wp/2017/01/29/heres-where-republicans-stand-on-president-trumps-controversial-travel-ban/|title=Whip Count: Here’s where Republicans stand on Trump’s controversial travel ban|date=January 31, 2017|last1=Blake|first1=Aaron|work=Washington Post}}</ref>

==Personal life==
Williams and his wife, Patty, live in [[Weatherford, Texas|Weatherford]], the seat of [[Parker County, Texas|Parker County]] west of Fort Worth, and have two daughters who attended TCU. He owns an [[automobile]] dealership in Weatherford. He attends the University Christian Church in Fort Worth.

Williams is a trustee of TCU and the [[George Bush School of Government and Public Service]] at [[Texas A&M University]] in [[College Station, Texas|College Station]].

Williams is the coach of the Republican team for the [[Congressional Baseball Game]].<ref name="what">{{cite news|url=http://www.cnn.com/2017/06/14/politics/congressional-shooting-victims/index.html|title=Congressional baseball practice shooting: What we know about the victims|last=Hamedy|first=Saba|date=June 15, 2017|work=CNN|accessdate=15 June 2017}}</ref> On June 14, 2017, he was present during [[2017 Congressional baseball shooting|a shooting attack]] on a practice for the game. He was taken from the area on a stretcher due to an ankle injury he suffered while jumping into the dugout during the attack.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.foxnews.com/us/2017/06/14/virginia-gop-baseball-practice-shooting-multiple-people-shot.html|title=Steve Scalise shot in Virginia, aides also hit|date=2017-06-14|work=Fox News|access-date=2017-06-14|language=en-US}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.nbcnews.com/news/us-news/congressman-steve-scalise-shot-alexandria-virginia-park-n772111|title=Congressman Steve Scalise and others shot at Alexandria, Virginia, park|work=NBC News|access-date=2017-06-14|language=en}}</ref> A member of his staff was shot and taken to the hospital, where he was treated for a leg injury and released.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.statesman.com/news/state--regional-govt--politics/roger-williams-staffer-among-those-shot-gop-baseball-practice/bA2Dt84Eiy17hIhQWg5qHL/|title=Roger Williams’ staffer among those shot at GOP baseball practice|last=Tilove|first=Jonathan|date=June 14, 2017|work=Austin American-Statesman|accessdate=14 June 2017}}</ref><ref name = "what" />
