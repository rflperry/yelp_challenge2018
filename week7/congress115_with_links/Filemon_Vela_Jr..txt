'''Filemón Bartolomé Vela Jr.''' {{IPAc-en|ˈ|f|ɪ|l|ə|ˌ|m|ɒ|n|_|ˈ|v|ɛ|l|ə}} (born February 13, 1963)<ref name=NationalJournal>{{cite web|last=Hopkins|first=Christopher Snow|title=Texas, 34th House District|url=http://www.nationaljournal.com/congress-legacy/texas-34th-house-district-20121102|work=nationaljournal.com|accessdate=8 November 2012}}</ref> is an American lawyer and politician who has been the [[United States House of Representatives|U.S. Representative]] for {{ushr|TX|34}} since 2013. He is a member of the [[Democratic Party (United States)|Democratic Party]].

==Early life and education==
Vela was born in [[Harlingen, Texas]] and raised in [[Brownsville, Texas]]. His father, [[Filemon Bartolome Vela Sr.]], was a long-serving United States federal judge.  The Reynaldo G. Garza-Filemon B. Vela United States Courthouse in Brownsville is named in Judge Vela's honor. His mother, [[Blanca Vela|Blanca Sanchez Vela]], served as Brownsville's first female mayor from 1999 to 2003.<ref name="vela.house.gov">vela.house.gov</ref><ref name=monitor>{{cite news|first=Ty|last=Johnson|title=Former Brownsville mayor, feminist 'trailblazer' Blanca Vela dies at 78 |url=http://www.themonitor.com/news/local/article_93ce870a-99d3-11e3-8d61-001a4bcf6878.html |work=[[The Monitor (Texas)]] |publisher= |date=2014-02-18 |accessdate=2014-03-13}}</ref><ref name=kveo>{{cite news|first=|last=|title=Brownsville's former and only female mayor, Blanca Vela, passes away |url=http://www.kveo.com/news/brownsvilles-former-and-only-female-mayor-blanca-vela-passes-away |work=[[KVEO]] |publisher= |date=2014-02-19 |accessdate=2014-03-13}}</ref>

Filemon attended [[Saint Joseph Academy (Brownsville, Texas)|Saint Joseph Academy]] in Brownsville, Texas, and went on to receive his B.A. degree from [[Georgetown University]] in 1985. During his time at Georgetown, he served as an intern at the [[Federal Judicial Center]] – the research and education agency of the federal judicial system. He also served as an intern in [[Solomon P. Ortiz|Congressman Solomon P Ortiz's]] Washington, D.C. office. Vela earned his J.D. degree from [[University of Texas School of Law|University of Texas at Austin School of Law]] in 1987.<ref name="vela.house.gov"/>

==Law career==
For more than 20 years, Filemon practiced law in [[South Texas]], as a plaintiff attorney.  His legal practice focused on complex civil litigation. As a trial attorney, Filemon successfully represented clients who were the victims of racial discrimination, consumers who had been severely injured by defective products, and employees injured during work.{{citation needed|date=June 2016}}

Some notable cases include Earl Shinhoster v. [[Ford Motor Company]], in which Vela represented the family of deceased Director of the [[NAACP]] who was killed in a car rollover accident in what was the largest wrongful death settlements in Alabama history, and Robert Trevino v. [[Walmart]], in which Vela represented a group of American farm workers who were told they could not shop in a Mississippi store.{{citation needed|date=June 2016}}

Vela also represented several South Texas school districts. In ''[[Edinburg Consolidated Independent School District|Edinburg School District]] v. Landmark'', Vela represented [[Edinburg, Texas|Edinburg]] to fight for more funding. In Pharr-San Juan-Alamo Independent School District v. Landmark, he represented the district in fighting contractors accused of building a poorly constructed school facility.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://vela.house.gov/index.php/about-filemon/full-biography |title=Full Biography |publisher=Vela.house.gov |date= |accessdate=2014-08-19}}</ref>

==U.S. House of Representatives==

===2012 election===
{{Main|United States House of Representatives elections in Texas, 2012#District 34}}
Vela decided to run in the newly created [[Texas's 34th congressional district]] as a Democrat. In the May 29 primary, Vela ranked first in an 8-candidate field with 40% of the vote.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=760010 |title=Ourcampaigns.com |publisher=Ourcampaigns.com |date= |accessdate=2014-08-19}}</ref> In the July 31 run-off primary, Vela defeated Denise Saenz Blanchard 67% to 33%.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=767642 |title=Ourcampaigns.com |publisher=Ourcampaigns.com |date= |accessdate=2014-08-19}}</ref><ref>http://enr.sos.state.tx.us/enr/results/july31_163_state.htm</ref>

In the general election, Vela defeated Republican Jessica Bradshaw 62% to 36%, winning the election.<ref>{{cite web|title=TX-TopRaces-Glance-Sum|url=http://www.kxxv.com/story/20031305/tx-topraces-glance-sum?clienttype=printable|work=kxxv.com|accessdate=8 November 2012}}</ref>

===Tenure===
In July 2013, he decided to quit the [[Congressional Hispanic Caucus]] because of his opposition to the [[John Hoeven|Hoeven]]-[[Bob Corker|Corker]] Amendment that tied border security with a [[amnesty|pathway to citizenship]]. He said "erecting more border fence drives a wedge between border communities which are culturally united."<ref>{{cite web|last=Martin |first=Gary |url=http://blog.chron.com/txpotomac/2013/07/rep-filemon-vela-quits-congressional-hispanic-caucus-to-protest-lawmakers-acceptance-of-border-militarization/ |title=Rep. Filemon Vela quits Congressional Hispanic Caucus to protest lawmakers' acceptance of border 'militarization' - Texas on the Potomac |publisher=Blog.chron.com |date=2013-07-02 |accessdate=2014-08-19}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.politico.com/story/2013/07/filemon-vela-quits-hispanic-caucus-border-surge-93676.html?hp=l8 |title=Filemon Vela quits Hispanic caucus over border surge - Seung Min Kim |publisher=Politico.Com |date=2013-07-02 |accessdate=2014-08-19}}</ref>
