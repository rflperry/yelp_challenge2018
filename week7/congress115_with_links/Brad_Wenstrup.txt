'''Brad Robert Wenstrup''' (born June 17, 1958)<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ballotpedia.org/wiki/index.php/Brad_Wenstrup |title=Brad Wenstrup |publisher=Ballotpedia |date= |accessdate=March 8, 2012}}</ref> is an [[United States|American]] politician, [[U.S. Army Reserve]] officer,<ref name="wenstrup.house.gov">{{cite web |url=http://wenstrup.house.gov/about/|title=Biography - U.S. Representative Brad Wenstrup|website=wenstrup.house.gov|accessdate=June 14, 2017}}</ref> and Doctor of Podiatric Medicine, who has been the [[United States House of Representatives|U.S. Representative]] for {{ushr|OH|2}} since 2013. A [[Republican Party (United States)|Republican]], he defeated U.S. Representative [[Jean Schmidt]] in the 2012 Republican primary election and Democrat William R. Smith in the [[United States House of Representatives elections in Ohio, 2012|2012 general election]]. Wenstrup is a [[Colonel (United States)|colonel]] in the U.S. Army Reserve<ref>{{cite news|last1=Wentling|first1=Nikki|url=http://wenstrup.house.gov/about|title=About Brad – U.S. Representative Brad Wenstrup|work=house.gov|accessdate=April 22, 2015}}</ref> and an [[Iraq War]] veteran. After the [[2017 Congressional baseball shooting|shooting of Congressman Steve Scalise]] on the morning of June 14, 2017, Wenstrup attended to the wounded congressman until he was transported to MedStar Washington Hospital Center.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Wentling |first1=Nikki |url=https://www.stripes.com/like-i-was-back-in-iraq-congressman-combat-doc-tended-to-shot-scalise-1.473494|title='Like I was back in Iraq': Congressman, combat doc tended to shot Scalise |work=Stars and Strips|date=2017-06-14|accessdate=June 14, 2017}}</ref>

==Early life, education, and medical career==
Wenstrup was born and raised in [[Cincinnati, Ohio]], the son of Joan (née Carletti) and Frank John "Jack" Wenstrup. His father was of German, Irish, and English descent, while his mother was of Italian ancestry. He has one sister, Amy Castellini (nee Wenstrup).<ref>{{cite web|url=http://freepages.genealogy.rootsweb.ancestry.com/%7Ebattle/reps/wenstrup.htm|title=Ancestry of Brad Wenstrup|work=ancestry.com|accessdate=April 22, 2015}}</ref> In 1976, he graduated from [[St. Xavier High School (Cincinnati)|St. Xavier High School]] in Cincinnati.<ref name="Biographical Directory of the United States Congress">{{cite news|title=Brad Wenstrup |url=http://bioguide.congress.gov/scripts/biodisplay.pl?index=W000815|accessdate=October 21, 2013}}</ref> Wenstrup graduated  ''cum laude'' with a B.A. in Psychology from the [[University of Cincinnati]]. He then attended the [[Dr. William M. Scholl College of Podiatric Medicine|Scholl College of Podiatric Medicine]] of [[Rosalind Franklin University of Medicine and Science]] where he earned an B.S. in [[Biology]] and a [[Podiatrist|Doctor of Podiatric Medicine]] degree. He practiced podiatric medicine in Cincinnati for over 24 years.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://usabrad.com/bio |title=Wenstrup for Congress |publisher=Usabrad.com |accessdate=March 8, 2012 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120415070253/http://usabrad.com/bio |archive-date=April 15, 2012 |deadurl=yes |df=mdy-all }}</ref>

==Military service==
Wenstrup joined the [[U.S. Army Reserve]] in 1998, attaining the rank of [[Colonel (United States)|colonel]].<ref name="auto">{{cite web|url=http://www.dispatch.com/news/20170304/with-house-colleagues-watching-us-rep-wenstrup-receives-military-promotion|title=With House colleagues watching, U.S. Rep. Wenstrup receives military promotion|first=Jessica|last=Wehrman|publisher=|accessdate=June 14, 2017}}</ref> In 2005-06, he served a tour in Iraq as a combat surgeon.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://wenstrup.house.gov/about/|title=My Story {{!}} U.S. Representative Brad Wenstrup|website=wenstrup.house.gov|language=en|access-date=2018-05-15}}</ref> Wenstrup served with the 344th Combat Support Hospital in Iraq from 2005 to 2006, calling his deployment "the worst thing that ever happened to me and the best thing I ever got to do."<ref>{{cite web|url=http://thehill.com/capital-living/new-member-of-the-week/313955-iraq-war-veteran-wenstrup-takes-his-fight-to-capitol-hill|title=Iraq War vet takes his fight to Capitol Hill|first=Meghashyam|last=Mali|date=July 29, 2013|publisher=|accessdate=June 14, 2017}}</ref> Wenstrup was awarded the [[Bronze Star Medal]] and [[Combat Action Badge]].<ref name="auto"/>

During his tour of duty in Iraq, Wenstrup's sister asked what she could send him. He told her "I wear the same clothes everyday, we're fed, and most days I'm not leaving the base. But the people here have nothing. They were under an oppressed regime and have had nothing for so long." His sister helped organize donations of toys, school supplies, and hygiene supplies donated by local companies, and Wenstrup worked with the base chaplain to distribute the donations to the locals.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://magazine.uc.edu/issues/0509/hero.html|title=University of Cincinnati alum, doctor serves patients in Iraq|website=University of Cincinnati|accessdate=June 14, 2017}}</ref> For his actions during the 2017 shooting, he was awarded the [[Soldier's Medal]].<ref>{{cite news |last=Shane |first=Leo |date=27 April 2018 |title=Congressman awarded Soldier's Medal for heroism in last year's baseball team shooting |url=https://www.armytimes.com/news/pentagon-congress/2018/04/27/congressman-awarded-soldiers-medal-for-heroism-in-last-years-baseball-team-shooting/ |work=Army Times |access-date=27 April 2018 }}</ref>

==2009 Cincinnati mayoral election==
{{See also|Election Results, Mayor of Cincinnati, Ohio}}
Wenstrup ran for mayor of Cincinnati against incumbent [[Mark L. Mallory|Mark Mallory]] in the November 2009 election. Mallory defeated Wenstrup by a 54%-to-46% margin.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=556780 |title=Cincinnati Mayor Race – Nov 03, 2009 |publisher=Our Campaigns |date= |accessdate=March 8, 2012}}</ref>

==U.S. House of Representatives==

===Elections===

====2012====
{{See also|United States House of Representatives elections in Ohio, 2012#District 2}}
He ran for the [[U.S. House of Representatives]] in the newly redrawn [[Ohio's 2nd congressional district]], held by incumbent Republican U.S. Congresswoman [[Jean Schmidt]]. He was endorsed by the Anderson Tea Party and the Ohio Liberty Council, which is a coalition of Ohio Tea Party groups.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.wcpo.com/dpp/news/political/elections_local/Wenstrup-upsets-Schmidt-for-2nd-Congressional-District-nomination |title=Wenstrup upsets Schmidt for 2nd Congressional District nomination |publisher=Wcpo.com |date=March 7, 2012 |accessdate=March 8, 2012 |deadurl=yes |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20120308101832/http://www.wcpo.com/dpp/news/political/elections_local/Wenstrup-upsets-Schmidt-for-2nd-Congressional-District-nomination |archivedate=March 8, 2012 |df=mdy-all }}</ref> In a surprise, he defeated her in the March Republican primary by 49%-to-43%.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.politico.com/2012-election/map/#/House/2012/Primary/OH |title=2012 Ohio District 2 Primary |publisher=Politico |date= |accessdate=March 8, 2012}}</ref> She carried six counties (all located in eastern part of the CD), while Wenstrup won the two most populous counties (both located in the western part of CD): [[Hamilton County, Ohio|Hamilton County]] (59%) and [[Clermont County, Ohio|Clermont County]] (50%).<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=748435 |title=OH District 2 – R Primary Race – Mar 06, 2012 |publisher=Our Campaigns |date= |accessdate=March 8, 2012}}</ref>

Wenstrup defeated [[Democratic Party (United States)|Democrat]] William R. Smith, 59%–41%.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://elections.msnbc.msn.com/ns/politics/2012/ohio/house/2/#.UJsY4KWtwhx|title=Ohio Congressional District 2 election results|work=msnbc.com|accessdate=April 22, 2015}}</ref>
<ref>{{cite web|url=http://vote.ohio.gov/Results.aspx?race=U.S.%20House%20of%20Representatives|title=2014 Elections Results|work=ohio.gov|accessdate=April 22, 2015}}</ref>

====2014====
{{See also|United States House of Representatives elections in Ohio, 2014#District 2}}
Wenstrup won re-election to a second term by defeating [[Democratic Party (United States)|Democrat]] Marek Tyszkiewicz 66%–34%.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.politico.com/2014-election/results/map/house/ohio/|title=Ohio Election Results 2014: House Map by District, Live Midterm Voting Updates|work=POLITICO|accessdate=April 22, 2015}}</ref>

====2016====
Wenstrup won re-election to a third term, defeating Democrats William Smith and Janet Everhard (write in) 65%–32.82%–2.17%.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.sos.state.oh.us/SOS/elections/Research/electResultsMain/2016Results.aspx|title=2016 Official Elections Results|website=www.sos.state.oh.us|accessdate=June 14, 2017}}</ref>

===Tenure===
Wenstrup began his first term on January 3, 2013.  During his first year in office he held an open [[town hall meeting]] in each of the 8 counties in his congressional district, the last of which was in Adams County on July 29, 2013.

He has held a number of conference call town hall 'meetings' (at least one in 2017 and at least three in 2016). The day of a 'meeting' a message is left with the time to call in that evening, the phone number and the meeting code. Opportunities are given to ask questions (live) and answer polls.

Wenstrup's customer service survey received accolades from ''Roll Call'', with 75% of respondents saying they were "satisfied" or "very satisfied" with their experience with Congressman Wenstrup's office.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.rollcall.com/news/how_to_conduct_a_congressional_customer_service_survey_commentary-239795-1.html|title=How to Conduct a Congressional Customer Service Survey - Commentary|date=February 3, 2015|website=Roll Call|accessdate=June 14, 2017}}</ref>

Rep. Wenstrup was an original co-sponsor of H.R. 3949, VA Prescription Data Accountability Act 2017, which became law during the 115th Congress, 1st Session (2017). The bill helps protect veterans receiving prescription medicines and prevents misuse of such prescription medicines.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.congress.gov/bill/115th-congress/house-bill/1545/cosponsors|title=Cosponsors - H.R.1545 - 115th Congress (2017-2018): VA Prescription Data Accountability Act 2017|last=Ann|first=Kuster,|date=2017-11-21|website=www.congress.gov|language=en|access-date=2018-05-15}}</ref>

==Personal life==
Wenstrup is married to Monica Wenstrup (Klein).  She works as a financial consultant.<ref>{{cite news |accessdate=July 17, 2012 |url=http://cincinnati.com/blogs/politics/2012/03/07/wenstrup-has-to-plan-for-nuptials-and-november-campaign/ |title=Wenstrup has to plan for nuptials and November campaign |date=July 3, 2012 |first=Deirdre |last=Shesgreen |work=Politics Extra |publisher=[[Gannett Company]] |location=[[Cincinnati]] |deadurl=yes |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20120712165810/http://cincinnati.com/blogs/politics/2012/03/07/wenstrup-has-to-plan-for-nuptials-and-november-campaign/ |archivedate=July 12, 2012 |df=mdy-all }}</ref> They have one son, Brad, Jr.<ref name="wenstrup.house.gov"/> and he and his wife recently adopted a daughter.
