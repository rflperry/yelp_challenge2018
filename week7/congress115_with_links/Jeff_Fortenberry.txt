'''Jeffrey Lane Fortenberry''' (born December 27, 1960) is the [[United States House of Representatives|U.S. Representative]] for {{ushr|NE|1}}, a post he has filled since 2005. He is a member of the [[Republican Party (United States)|Republican Party]]. The district is based in [[Lincoln, Nebraska|Lincoln]] and includes most of the eastern third of the state outside the immediate [[Omaha]] area. He is the current dean of Nebraska's Congressional delegation.<ref>{{cite news|last1=Morton|first1=Joseph|title=Incumbents Jeff Fortenberry, Adrian Smith easily win re-election in Nebraska House races|url=http://www.omaha.com/news/politics/incumbents-jeff-fortenberry-adrian-smith-easily-win-re-election-in/article_af97917a-a61e-11e6-b52f-0b198e36e612.html|work=Omaha World Herald|date=November 8, 2016}}</ref>

==Early life, education, and early career==
He graduated from [[Catholic High School (Baton Rouge)|Catholic High]] in [[Baton Rouge, Louisiana|Baton Rouge]], [[Louisiana]]. He holds a master's degree in public policy from [[Georgetown University]], a master's degree in theology from [[Franciscan University of Steubenville]], and a bachelor's degree in [[economics]] from [[Louisiana State University]].

He has previously worked as an economist, in local economic development, and as a publishing executive for Sandhills Publishing. He was also a policy analyst for the Senate Subcommittee on Intergovernmental Relations.<ref>{{cite web|title=Jeff Fortenberry – Early Career – Analyst |url=http://www.fortenberry.house.gov/biography |deadurl=yes |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20141205225221/http://fortenberry.house.gov/biography/ |archivedate=2014-12-05 |df= }}</ref>

==Lincoln City Council (1997–2001)==
Fortenberry was an at-large member of the Lincoln City Council from 1997 to 2001. His main commitments in this role were community revitalization and increasing public safety, but doing both without raising taxation.  Among the economic development and community revitalization projects he worked on were the transition of a major public hospital and building a new baseball stadium.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.custercountyrepublicans.com/elected_fortenberry.htm |title=Archived copy |accessdate=2010-11-01 |deadurl=yes |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20110708213702/http://www.custercountyrepublicans.com/elected_fortenberry.htm |archivedate=2011-07-08 |df= }}</ref>

==U.S. House of Representatives (2005–present)==

===Elections===
;2004
Incumbent Republican U.S. Congressman [[Doug Bereuter]] of [[Nebraska's 1st congressional district]] decided to retire. Fortenberry decided to run and won the 7-candidate Republican primary with 39% of the vote. He defeated [[Curt Bromm]] (33%), the [[Speaker of the Nebraska House of Representatives|Speaker]] of the [[Nebraska Legislature]], and [[Club for Growth]]-endorsed businessman [[Greg Ruehle]] (21%).<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.sos.state.ne.us/elec/canvass/primary2004/HOUSE.htm#Republican1|title=Member of the U.S. House of Representatives|publisher=}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=4891|title=Our Campaigns - NE - District 01 - R Primary Race - May 11, 2004|publisher=}}</ref> In the general election, he defeated State Senator [[Matt Connealy]] 54%–43%. He won all but two counties: Thurston and Burt.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=4220|title=Our Campaigns - NE - District 01 Race - Nov 02, 2004|publisher=}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.sos.state.ne.us/elec/canvass/general2004/HOUSE.htm|title=Member of the U.S. House of Representatives|publisher=}}</ref>

;2006
Fortenberry won re-election to a second term, defeating former [[Lieutenant Governor of Nebraska|Lieutenant Governor]] [[Maxine Moul]], 58%–42%, winning all but Burt County.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=206986|title=Our Campaigns - NE - District 01 Race - Nov 07, 2006|publisher=}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.sos.state.ne.us/elec/ElectNight/general.htm |title=Archived copy |accessdate=2006-11-10 |deadurl=yes |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20061108225305/http://www.sos.state.ne.us/elec/ElectNight/general.htm#data |archivedate=2006-11-08 |df= }}</ref>

;2008
He won re-election to a third term, defeating Marine veteran [[Max Yashirin]] 70–30%.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=389424|title=Our Campaigns - NE - District 01 Race - Nov 04, 2008|publisher=}}</ref>

;2010
He was challenged in the Republican primary for the first time since 2004. He drew two opponents and won with 84% of the vote.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=631063|title=Our Campaigns - NE District 01- R Primary Race - May 11, 2010|publisher=}}</ref> He won re-election to a fourth term, defeating legislative staffer Ivy Harper, 71%–29%.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ourcampaigns.com/CandidateDetail.html?CandidateID=236357|title=Our Campaigns - Candidate - Ivy Harper|publisher=}}</ref>

;2012
He drew two opponents in the Republican primary again, but won with 86% of the vote.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=751588|title=Our Campaigns - NE District 1 - R Primary Race - May 15, 2012|publisher=}}</ref>

;2014
He won re-election to a sixth term, defeating attorney and Democrat Dennis Crawford.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.yankton.net/news/state_ap_ne/article_0cc3a671-a273-55ce-9138-539b2a2f9997.html |title=Archived copy |accessdate=2014-11-05 |deadurl=yes |archiveurl=https://archive.is/20141105203544/http://www.yankton.net/news/state_ap_ne/article_0cc3a671-a273-55ce-9138-539b2a2f9997.html |archivedate=2014-11-05 |df= }}</ref>

'''2016'''

He won re-election to a seventh term, defeating doctor and Democrat Dan Wik.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.omaha.com/news/politics/incumbents-jeff-fortenberry-adrian-smith-easily-win-re-election-in/article_af97917a-a61e-11e6-b52f-0b198e36e612.html|title=Incumbents Jeff Fortenberry, Adrian Smith easily win re-election in Nebraska House races|last=Bureau|first=Joseph Morton / World-Herald|newspaper=Omaha.com|access-date=2017-02-09|language=en}}</ref>

===Tenure===
;Agriculture, energy, and environment
Fortenberry introduced the Renewable Fuels for America’s Future Act of 2010. The act was described by the ''[[Lincoln Journal Star]]'' editorial board as "a smart and thoughtful way to reduce subsidies for the production of ethanol."<ref>{{cite web|url=http://journalstar.com/news/opinion/editorial/article_c1101a16-99e7-11df-a4d1-001cc4c03286.html|title=Editorial, 7/28: Jeff Fortenberry’s ethanol plan has merit|date=28 July 2010|work=JournalStar.com}}</ref> The act would result in taxpayer savings of  $5.67 billion, according to economists Ernie Goss of Creighton University and Bruce Babcock of [[Iowa State University]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://ethanolproducer.com/article.jsp?article_id=6865|title=Ethanol Producer Magazine – The Latest News and Data About Ethanol Production|publisher=}}</ref>

;Healthcare
Fortenberry voted against the [[Patient Protection and Affordable Care Act]], but said in 2010 that he supported "the right type of [[Health care reform in the United States|(health care) reform]]" incorporating measures to reduce costs, improve outcomes and protect vulnerable people.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://fremonttribune.com/news/local/article_46c71328-e2a7-11df-a7a9-001cc4c03286.html|title=Fortenberry faces newcomer Harper in 1st District|author=Don Walton/Lincoln Journal Star|date=28 October 2010|work=Fremont Tribune}}</ref> He introduced H.R. 321, the SCHIP Plus Act of 2009 to offer eligible families the choice of retaining coverage for their children in the State Children's Health Insurance Program or using SCHIP funds to help pay for a family insurance plan, saving both family and taxpayer dollars.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://fortenberry.house.gov/2009/01/fortenberryoffersplanforresponsibleexpansionofschip.shtml |title=Archived copy |accessdate=2010-11-02 |deadurl=yes |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20101103094240/http://fortenberry.house.gov/2009/01/fortenberryoffersplanforresponsibleexpansionofschip.shtml |archivedate=2010-11-03 |df= }}</ref>

;Foreign affairs
In an October 2010 endorsement, the ''Lincoln Journal Star'' described Fortenberry as "uncommonly well-informed on international issues".<ref>{{cite web|url=http://journalstar.com/news/opinion/editorial/article_7954615a-d7f4-11df-9d3c-001cc4c002e0.html|title=Editorial, 10/15: Lincoln Journal Star endorses Jeff Fortenberry|date=15 October 2010|work=JournalStar.com}}</ref>

;Pro-life
Fortenberry received a 100% pro-life score from the [[National Right to Life Committee]] in a ranking of members of the 111th Congress (2009-2011).<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.capwiz.com/nrlc/scorecard.xc?chamber=H&state=US&session=111&x=8&y=14|title=National Right to Life - NRLC Scorecard|publisher=}}</ref>  He speaks annually at the [[March for Life (Washington, D.C.)|March for Life]].{{citation needed|date=April 2017}}

===Caucus memberships for the 115th Congress===

In the 115th Congress, Fortenberry is co-chairman of the Nuclear Security Working Group, Congressional Caucus on Beef, Religious Minorities in the Middle East Caucus, and Friends of Switzerland Caucus. He is the vice chair of the Congressional Friends of Jordan Caucus. He is Chairman of the Congressional Catholic Staff Association. He is a member of several other caucuses.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://fortenberry.house.gov/about/caucus-membership|title=Caucus Membership|date=2014-12-09|newspaper=Congressman Jeff Fortenberry|access-date=2017-02-09|language=en}}</ref> Fortenberry is also co-chairman of the [[United States Congressional International Conservation Caucus]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Our Members|author=|url=https://royce.house.gov/internationalconservation/members.html|format=|publisher=U.S. House of Representatives International Conservation Caucus |date=|accessdate=1 August 2018}}</ref>
