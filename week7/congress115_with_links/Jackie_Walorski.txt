'''Jacqueline R. Walorski''' {{IPAc-en|w|ə|ˈ|l|ɔr|s|k|i}} (born August 13, 1963) is an [[United States|American]] politician who has been the [[United States House of Representatives|U.S. Representative]] for [[Indiana's 2nd congressional district]] since 2013. She is a member of the [[Republican Party (United States)|Republican Party]], and she was a member of the [[Indiana House of Representatives]], representing Indiana's 21st district, from 2005 to 2010. In 2010, Walorski won the Republican nomination for Indiana's 2nd congressional district, but narrowly lost in the [[United States House of Representatives elections in Indiana, 2010#District 2|general election]] to Democratic incumbent [[Joe Donnelly]]. In 2012, Walorski went on to win the open seat.

==Early life, education, and career==
Born in [[South Bend, Indiana]] on August 13, 1963,<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.washingtontimes.com/campaign-2012/candidates/jackie-walorski-60423/|archive-url=https://archive.is/20130209014008/http://www.washingtontimes.com/campaign-2012/candidates/jackie-walorski-60423/|dead-url=yes|archive-date=2013-02-09|title=Candidates  :Jackie Walorski|newspaper=[[The Washington Times]]|accessdate=2016-11-11}}</ref> Walorski grew up with her two older brothers in the city's Gilmer Park neighborhood. Her mother, Martha C. (née Martin), worked as a meat cutter at a local grocery store, and her father, Raymond B. Walorski, worked as a firefighter and owned an appliance store.<ref name="Walorski Roots"/><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.palmerfuneralhomes.com/obits/obituary.php?id=30767|title=Raymond B. Walorski Obituary – Palmer Funeral Homes|author=Consolidated Funeral Services|publisher=}}</ref> She has Polish and German ancestry.<ref name="National Journal">{{cite web|url=http://www.nationaljournal.com/congress-legacy/indiana-2nd-house-district-20121101|title=Indiana,  2nd House District|work=Nationaljournal.com|last=Izadi|first=Elahe|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140323080043/http://www.nationaljournal.com/congress-legacy/indiana-2nd-house-district-20121101|archive-date=March 23, 2014|via=Internet Archive|date=November 1, 2012|access-date=May 2, 2018}}</ref> As a child, she attended Hay Elementary School and graduated from [[Riley High School]] in 1981.<ref name="Walorski Roots">{{cite web|url=http://articles.southbendtribune.com/2012-09-16/news/33885763_1_indiana-s-2nd-congressional-district-candidates-stress-jackie-walorski|title=Candidates stress their roots: Jackie Walorski|author =James Brosher|publisher=southbendtribune.com|date=September 16, 2012}}</ref> She then attended [[Liberty Baptist College]] from 1981–83, and graduated from [[Taylor University]], receiving her [[Bachelor of Arts|B.A.]] in Communications and Public Administration in 1985.<ref name="votesmart.org">{{cite web|url=http://votesmart.org/candidate/biography/34205/jackie-walorski?flavour=mobile|title=Representative Jackie Walorski's Biography|publisher=votesmart.org|date=March 10, 2013}}</ref>

Walorski began her career as a television reporter for [[WSBT-TV]], a [[CBS]] affiliate in [[South Bend, Indiana|South Bend]], from 1985 to 1989, and was the executive director of the [[St. Joseph County, Indiana|St. Joseph County Humane Society]] from 1989-91.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://bakersfieldadvocacy.org/voter-resources/candidate/34205|title=Bakersfield Advocacy Greater Bakersfield Chamber of Commerce Representing the Interests of Business with Government|website=Bakersfield Advocacy|date=March 10, 2013|deadurl=yes|archiveurl=https://archive.is/20130414105351/http://bakersfieldadvocacy.org/voter-resources/candidate/34205|archivedate=April 14, 2013|df=}}</ref> Walorski was appointed as the director of institutional advancement at [[Ancilla College]] in 1991, a position she held until she was appointed as the director of membership at the [[St. Joseph County, Indiana|St. Joseph County Chamber of Commerce]] in 1996.<ref name="votesmart.org"/> She later worked as the director of annual giving at [[Indiana University South Bend]] from 1997-99.<ref name="National Journal" />

Walorski moved to Romania in 2000 and founded Impact International, a foundation to provide medical supplies and attention to impoverished children.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://projects.wsj.com/campaign2012/candidates/view/jackie-walorski--IN-H|title=Jackie Walorski (R)|website=[[Wall Street Journal]]|date=March 10, 2013|archive-date=June 15, 2015|access-date=May 2, 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150615015727/http://projects.wsj.com/campaign2012/candidates/view/jackie-walorski--IN-H|via=Internet Archive}}</ref> Walorski did Christian missionary work in Romania before returning to the U.S. in 2004.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.in.gov/library/files/HPR1229.pdf.pdf|title=HOWEY Political Report: GOP's Finest Hour? Walorski's world travels brought her to the precipice of change|author=Brian A. Howey|publisher=in.gov|date=March 16, 2006}}</ref>

==Indiana House of Representatives==

===Elections===
In 2004, Walorski ran for a seat in the [[Indiana House of Representatives]] after incumbent Republican State Representative Richard Mangus of decided to retire. She ran for Indiana's 21st District, a district which represented the suburban area between South Bend and Elkhart. Walorski defeated Democrat Carl H. Kaser 64%–36%.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=75912|title=Our Campaigns – IN State House 021 Race – November 2, 2004|website=Ourcampaigns.com|accessdate=2016-11-11}}</ref> In 2006, she won a second term with 53% of the vote.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=309862|title=Our Campaigns – IN State House 021 Race – November 7, 2006|website=Ourcampaigns.com|accessdate=2016-11-11}}</ref> In 2008, she won a third term unopposed.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=483495|title=Our Campaigns – IN State House 021 Race – November 4, 2008|website=Ourcampaigns.com|accessdate=2016-11-11}}</ref>

===Tenure===
During her tenure in the Indiana House, Walorski was a sponsor of Indiana's [[Voter ID laws in the United States|Voter ID law]], requiring  voters to present Government issued identification during in person voting.<ref name="National Journal"/> The Voter ID law led to many lawsuits and was brought before the [[Supreme Court of the United States|Supreme Court]], where the law was upheld in [[Crawford v. Marion County Election Board]], and is cited as helping the expansion of Voter ID laws in other states.<ref>{{cite news | url=http://usatoday30.usatoday.com/news/washington/2008-01-06-court_N.htm | title =Voter ID case could affect election laws | author =Joan Biskupic | publisher =usatoday.com | date =January 6, 2008}}</ref>

Walorski has been criticized for missing a committee vote and the opportunity for stopping the Daylight Saving Time bill from passing out of committee, even though that bill died on the House floor.<ref>South Bend Tribune, February 17, 2005 by Martin DeAgostino</ref><ref name="staffblogs.etruth.com">[https://archive.is/20120716073712/http://staffblogs.etruth.com/TimVandenack/archive/2012/05/17/dems-blast-walorskis-2005-non-vote-on-daylight-savings-time.aspx]</ref> After a different bill passed introducing DST, she authored and introduced a bill to rescind DST, a measure that ended up dying.<ref name="staffblogs.etruth.com"/>

Walorski authored legislation combating identity theft, including in 2006 when she sponsored a bill requiring companies to notify customers who are Indiana residents, of any security breaches that could cause identity theft, identity deception or fraud, and making it a Class C felony and imposing a $50,000 fine on anyone who has the identities of over 100 persons.<ref>{{cite web | url=http://articles.southbendtribune.com/2006-05-31/news/26921375_1_identity-theft-social-security-number-credit-card-numbers | title =New identity theft law to take effect July 1 in Indiana | author =James Wensits | publisher =southbendtribune.com | date =May 31, 2006}}</ref> With Walorski saying that "Identity theft is the most rapidly growing crime in the United States. We need to find a solution to this problem before it gets any bigger in Indiana."<ref>{{cite web | url =http://www.insideindianabusiness.com/newsitem.asp?id=16332 | title =Legislation Would Require Companies to Notify Customers of Security Breaches | publisher =insideindianabusiness.com | date =January 4, 2006 | deadurl =yes | archiveurl =https://web.archive.org/web/20131213005618/http://www.insideindianabusiness.com/newsitem.asp?id=16332 | archivedate =December 13, 2013 | df = }}</ref>

==U.S. House of Representatives==

===Elections===
;2010
{{See also|United States House of Representatives elections in Indiana, 2010#District 2}}
On January 31, 2009, Walorski formally announced her bid to challenge incumbent [[Democratic Party of the United States|Democratic]] U.S. Congressman [[Joe Donnelly]] in {{ushr|IN|2}}. Walorski won the [[Republican Party (United States)|Republican]] primary on May 4, 2010<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.fox59.com/news/sns-ap-in--congress-indiana-2nddistrict,0,1382460.story |accessdate=May 5, 2010 |deadurl=yes |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20110722191440/http://www.fox59.com/news/sns-ap-in--congress-indiana-2nddistrict%2C0%2C1382460.story |archivedate=July 22, 2011 |title=Election results |df= }}</ref> with 61% of the votes,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.wndu.com/localnews/headlines/92833604.html|title=State Rep. Jackie Walorski wins 2nd Congressional district GOP primary|publisher=Wndu.com|accessdate=2010-08-23|deadurl=yes|archiveurl=https://archive.is/20120918161941/http://www.wndu.com/localnews/headlines/92833604.html|archivedate=2012-09-18|df=}}</ref> defeating opponents Martin Dolan, Jack Jordan, and [[Tony Zirkle]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://nwitimes.com/app/blog/news/?p%3D4433 |title=Archived copy |accessdate=2010-05-05 |deadurl=yes |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20110206230348/http://nwitimes.com/app/blog/news/?p=4433 |archivedate=2011-02-06 |df= }}</ref> She was defeated, 48%–47% on November 2, 2010 by Donnelly.<ref>[http://www.cnn.com/ELECTION/2010/results/individual/#INH02 2010 Election Results], CNN.com; accessed November 9, 2016.</ref>

;2012
{{See also|United States House of Representatives elections in Indiana, 2012#District 2}}
On March 22, 2011, Walorski announced that she would run for Indiana's 2nd Congressional District again. Over the Indiana legislature's 2011-2013 legislative session, the predominantly Republican Indiana House and Senate redrew Indiana's congressional districts. After redistricting, the newly drawn 2nd district included all [[Elkhart County, Indiana|Elkhart County]], Walorski's home county, and the demographics of the new district included more registered Republican voters.
<ref>{{cite web|title=Elkhart County Fares Well in Redistricting Changes|url=http://www.etruth.com/Know/News/Story.aspx?ID=539494|publisher=The Elkhart Truth|accessdate=November 9, 2016|deadurl=yes|archiveurl=https://archive.is/20120730142442/http://www.etruth.com/Know/News/Story.aspx?ID=539494|archivedate=July 30, 2012|df=}}</ref>

Incumbent Democratic Congressman [[Joe Donnelly]] decided not to seek re-election, opting instead to run for the U.S. Senate.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://thecaucus.blogs.nytimes.com/2011/05/09/donnelly-to-run-for-senate-in-indiana|title=Donnelly to Run for Senate in Indiana|author=Michael D. Shear|date=May 9, 2011|work=New York Times|accessdate=August 3, 2011}}</ref> Walorski ran against [[Libertarian Party of the United States|Libertarian]] candidate Joe Ruiz of [[Mishawaka, Indiana|Mishawaka]]<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.joeruizforcongress.com|title=Joe Ruiz for Congress}}</ref> and [[Democratic Party of the United States|Democratic]] candidate Brendan Mullen of [[Granger, Indiana|Granger]], an [[Iraq War]] veteran.

On May 8, 2012, Walorski easily won the primary election with 73% of the vote, winning all 10 counties in the 2nd District.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://hosted.ap.org/dynamic/files/elections/2012/by_state/IN_Page_0508.html?SITE=CSPANELN&SECTION=POLITICS|title=News From The Associated Press|publisher=ap.org|accessdate=November 9, 2016}}</ref> Many articles have been written about Walorski's role as a woman running for Congress.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://howeypolitics.com/main.asp?SectionID=10&SubSectionID=21&ArticleID=7762|title=Brian Howey: Brooks and Walorski take aim at GOP glass ceiling|publisher=howeypolitics.com|accessdate=November 9, 2016}}</ref>

Walorski defeated Mullen 49%–48%.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.politico.com/2012-election/map/#/House/2012/IN|title=2012 election result report from Politico|work=POLITICO|accessdate=November 9, 2016}}</ref> She took office on January 3, 2013. At the same time, Donnelly was elected to the Senate.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2012/11/07/us/politics/indiana-senate-race.html?_r=0|title =Democrat Wins Race for Senate in Indiana|publisher =nytimes.com|date=November 6, 2012|accessdate=January 3, 2012|first=Matthew L.|last=Wald}}</ref>

Walorski has received endorsements from the National Federation of Small Business and the U.S. and Indiana Chambers of Commerce.

===Tenure===
Walorski voted to repeal the [[Affordable Care Act]], also known as Obamacare.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://articles.southbendtribune.com/2013-05-17/news/39343609_1_upton-vote-repeal-health-care|title=Walorski, Upton vote to repeal health care law|work=SouthBendTribune|date=May 17, 2013}}</ref>

In 2015, Walorski rejected the [[Pain Capable Unborn Child Protection Act]], a bill banning [[Late termination of pregnancy]], an abortion procedure  given beyond 20 weeks into a pregnancy.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.huffingtonpost.com/2015/01/16/republicans-20-week-abortion-ban_n_6488374.html|title=GOP Congresswomen Get Cold Feet On Anti-Abortion Bill|work=Huffington Post|date=February 22, 2015}}</ref> In 2013, Walorski had said she would support a ban on late-term abortions.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://articles.southbendtribune.com/2013-06-20/news/40104289_1_walorski-r-jimtown-abortions|title=Walorski supports ban on late-term abortions|work=Huffington Post|date=June 20, 2013}}</ref>

==Political positions==

Walorski has a 63 percent rating from [[Heritage Action for America]] based on her conservative voting record.<ref name="HAFA">{{cite web|title=Heritage Action Scorecard|url=https://www.heritageactionscorecard.com/members/member/W000813|website=Heritage Action for America|accessdate=27 December 2017}}</ref>

===Crime===

Walorski has a 69 percent rating from the [[National Association of Police Organizations]] for her voting record regarding legislature of importance to police.<ref name="OTICrime">{{cite web|title=Jackie Walorski on Crime|url=http://www.ontheissues.org/IN/Jackie_Walorski_Crime.htm|website=On The Issues|accessdate=27 December 2017}}</ref>

===Defense===
On May 25, 2018, Walorski introduced legislation to double the death gratuity paid to the families of service members killed on active duty. The legislation would increase the current death gratuity of $100,000 to $200,000. Under the bill, at least 60% of the benefit would be paid to the surviving spouse. Service members could choose how the remaining 40 percent would be disbursed. The bill would also cap death benefits for members of Congress at $74,000. This would result in a payment of about $100,000 less than what would be paid under the current system.<ref name="Ripon">{{cite news |author=<!--Staff writer(s); no by-line.--> |title=Walorski aims to create more generous military death benefits with new bill |url=https://riponadvance.com/stories/walorski-aims-create-generous-military-death-benefits-new-bill/ |work=Ripon Advance |location=United States |date=31 May 2018 |access-date=7 June 2018}}</ref>

===Energy & oil ===

Walorski supports expanded gas and oil exploration in the United States and offshore energy production.<ref name="OTIEnergy">{{cite web|title=Jackie Walorski on Energy & Oil|url=http://www.ontheissues.org/IN/Jackie_Walorski_Energy_+_Oil.htm|website=On The Issues|accessdate=27 December 2017}}</ref>

=== Guns ===

While serving in the Indiana House of Representatives, Walorski received a "A" rating from the [[National Rifle Association]] for her gun-related voting record. Walorski opposes restrictions on gun purchases and any restrictions related to the [[Second Amendment to the United States Constitution|Second Amendment]].<ref name="OTIGun">{{cite web|title=Jackie Walorski on Gun Control|url=http://www.ontheissues.org/IN/Jackie_Walorski_Gun_Control.htm|website=On The Issues|accessdate=27 December 2017}}</ref>

=== Health care===

Walorski favors repealing and replacing the [[Affordable Care Act]] ("Obamacare"). She supports market-based health insurance.<ref name="OTIHealth">{{cite web|title=Jackie Walorski on Health Care|url=http://www.ontheissues.org/IN/Jackie_Walorski_Health_Care.htm|website=On The Issues|accessdate=27 December 2017}}</ref>

in 2017, Walorski introduced a bill to suspend the medical device tax imposed under Obamacare for five years. Walorski stated that she hopes to eventually kill the tax altogether.<ref name="Device">{{cite web |url=https://walorski.house.gov/medical-device-tax-relief/ |title=Walorski Introduces Bill to Provide Relief from Job-Killing Medical Device Tax |author=<!--Not stated--> |date=12 December 2017 |website=Congresswoman Jackie Walorski |publisher=United States House of Representatives |access-date=13 June 2018}}</ref>

===Taxes and spending===
Walorski supports a [[balanced budget amendment]] to the US constitution.<ref name="OTIBudget">{{cite web|title=Jackie Walorski on Budget & Economy|url=http://www.ontheissues.org/IN/Jackie_Walorski_Budget_+_Economy.htm|website=On The Issues|accessdate=27 December 2017}}</ref> In addition to being a strong supporter of a balanced budget amendment, Walorski has advocated privatizing Social Security. In March 2010 she said, "I think the one thing we have to do is the thing that Bush actually tried to do a couple years ago, which is privatize Social Security and allow people to invest in their own retirement."<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.etruth.com/article/20120808/NEWS01/708089937|title=- Elkhart Truth|publisher=etruth.com|date=August 8, 2012|deadurl=yes|archiveurl=https://archive.is/20130122160022/http://www.etruth.com/article/20120808/NEWS01/708089937|archivedate=January 22, 2013|df=}}</ref>

Walorski supports a single-rate tax system, an end to the estate tax, and a radical simplification of the tax code.<ref name="taxcut">{{cite web |url=http://www.ontheissues.org/IN/Jackie_Walorski_Tax_Reform.htm |title=Jackie Walorski on Tax Reform |author=<!--Not stated--> |date=31 January 2017 |website=OnTheIssues |publisher=Jesse Gordon |access-date=13 June 2018}}</ref>

Walorski voted in favor of the [[Tax Cuts and Jobs Act of 2017]].<ref name="Almukhtar">{{cite web|last1=Almukhtar|first1=Sarah|title=How Each House Member Voted on the Tax Bill|url=https://www.nytimes.com/interactive/2017/12/19/us/politics/tax-bill-house-live-vote.html?_r=1|website=The New York Times|accessdate=27 December 2017|date=19 December 2017}}</ref>

===Trade===
In a statement released on May 31, 2018, Walorski announced her opposition to tariffs on goods imported from American allies. She said that such duties threaten American businesses and workers. Tariffs announced the same day by the Trump administration were the impetus for her statement. These include a 25-percent tariff on steel and a 10-percent tariff on aluminum. Walorski also asked that system for granting exclusions for certain kinds of products be accelerated. Walorski and 15 other members of House Committee on Ways and Means also addressed a letter to Commerce Secretary Wilbur Ross asking that companies that successfully apply for exclusion receive rebates on tariffs already paid while their applications were pending; President Trump signed an executive order to ensure such refunds take place.<ref name="Center16">{{cite news |author=<!--Staff writer(s); no by-line.--> |title=Walorski speaks out against steel, aluminum tariffs on allies |url=http://www.wndu.com/content/news/Walorski-speaks-out-against-steel-aluminum-tariffs-on-allies-484209561.html |work=WNDU |location=United States |date=31 May 2018 |access-date=7 June 2018}}</ref>

=== Abortion ===

Walorski opposes abortion. She opposes federal and state money from funding [[abortion]] and churches providing birth control. She supports efforts to notify parents or guardians when a minor has an abortion.<ref name="OTIAbortion">{{cite web|title=Jackie Walorski on Abortion|url=http://www.ontheissues.org/IN/Jackie_Walorski_Abortion.htm|website=On The Issues|accessdate=27 December 2017}}</ref>

In October 2017, Walorski asked the Indiana State Department of Health to deny an application to open an abortion clinic in South Bend. Walorski said the clinic would undermine efforts to reduce the number of abortions in the area.<ref name="Abort">{{cite news |author=<!--Staff writer(s); no by-line.--> |title=Walorski Asks State to Reject South Bend Abortion Clinic |url=https://www.usnews.com/news/best-states/indiana/articles/2017-10-25/walorski-asks-state-to-reject-south-bend-abortion-clinic |work=US News |location=United States |date=25 October 2017 |access-date=6 June 2018}}</ref>

=== Cannabis ===

Walorski has a "D" rating from [[NORML]] for her voting history regarding [[cannabis]]-related causes. Walorski opposes veterans having access to medical marijuana if recommended by their [[Veterans Health Administration]] doctor and if it is legal for medicinal purposes in their state of residence.<ref name="NORML">{{cite web|title=Indiana Scorecard|url=http://norml.org/congressional-scorecard/indiana|website=NORML|accessdate=27 December 2017|language=en-us}}</ref>

=== LGBT rights ===

Walorski opposes [[same-sex marriage]].<ref name="OTICivilRights">{{cite web|title=Jackie Walorski on Civil Rights|url=http://www.ontheissues.org/IN/Jackie_Walorski_Civil_Rights.htm|website=On The Issues|accessdate=27 December 2017}}</ref>

===Immigration===

Walorski supported President [[Donald Trump]]'s 2017 [[Executive Order 13769|executive order]] to impose a temporary ban on entry to the U.S. to citizens of seven Muslim-majority countries. She believes that it "will allow our national security officials to examine the vetting process and strengthen safeguards to prevent terrorists from entering our homeland."<ref name="Blake1">{{cite web|last1=Blake|first1=Aaron|title=Coffman, Gardner join Republicans against President Trump's travel ban; here's where the rest stand|url=http://www.denverpost.com/2017/01/29/republicans-on-trump-travel-ban/|website=Denver Post|accessdate=30 January 2017}}</ref>

==Personal life==

In 1995, Walorski married her husband, Dean Swihart, a schoolteacher in [[Mishawaka, Indiana|Mishawaka]].<ref name="standwithjackie.com"/> She resides in [[Jimtown, Indiana|Jimtown]], an unincorporated suburban community west of Elkhart, and is a member of South Gate Church, an [[Assemblies of God]] [[megachurch]] in South Bend.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.indianaag.org/northwest-area/74-south-bend-southgate-church/view-details.html |title=South Bend Southgate Church |website=Indianaag.org |date=2013-04-21 |accessdate=2016-11-11}}</ref>
