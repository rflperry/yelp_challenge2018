'''Allen Lucas Messer''' (born February 27, 1969) is an [[United States|American]] politician, lobbyist, and author who has represented [[Indiana's 6th congressional district]] in the [[United States House of Representatives]] since 2013. He is a member of the [[Republican Party (United States)|Republican Party]].

Born in [[Evansville, Indiana]], Messer is a graduate of [[Wabash College]] and [[Vanderbilt University Law School]]. After an unsuccessful run for the U.S. House in 2000, Messer served as the first executive director of the [[Indiana Republican Party]] from 2001 to 2005.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.newspapers.com/image/148661258/?terms=Luke%2BMesser%2Bexecutive%2Bdirector%2Bof%2BIndiana%2BRepublican%2BState%2BCommittee%2BIt%2Bis%2Ba%2Bnewly%2Bcreated%2Bposition%2C%2Brequiring%2Bthat%2BMesser |title=23 Aug 2001, Page 2 - The Republic at |publisher=Newspapers.com |date=2001-08-23 |accessdate=2018-04-17}}</ref> Messer was appointed to serve in the [[Indiana House of Representatives]] in 2003, after State Representative [[W. Roland Stine]] was killed in a car accident. He represented Indiana's 57th District from 2003 to 2006, when he opted not to run for reelection and instead joined Ice Miller LLP's lobbying division. From 2006 to 2012, Messer was a registered lobbyist. He ran for the U.S. House again in 2010, but was unsuccessful in his primary challenge to Republican [[Dan Burton]]. Burton retired in 2012, and Messer was elected to replace him, defeating [[Democratic Party (United States)|Democratic]] nominee Brad Bookout.

On July 26, 2017, Messer announced that he would run for the U.S. Senate in [[United States Senate election in Indiana, 2018|2018]].<ref>{{cite web|author=By $${element.Contributor} |url=http://www.rollcall.com/news/politics/indiana-rep-luke-messer-running-for-senate |title=Indiana Rep. Luke Messer Running for Senate |publisher=Rollcall.com |date=2017-07-26 |accessdate=2018-04-17}}</ref> He was unsuccessful in the May 8 primary election, losing to [[Mike Braun]].

==Early life, education, and law career==
Messer graduated from Greensburg Community High School in 1987.<ref name="in.gov">{{cite web |url=http://www.in.gov/legislative/house_republicans/newsroom/luke.html | title =Luke Messer Sworn in Today as State Representative for House District 57 |publisher =in.gov | date =May 28, 2003 | accessdate =January 17, 2013}}</ref> Messer attended [[Wabash College]] where he was a member of the [[Phi Delta Theta]] Fraternity and majored in speech. He graduated in 1991.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Torrez|first1=Jonathan|title=The Wabash College Bachelor |url=http://www.wabash.edu/bachelor/issues/2006f/fullpaper.pdf |website=wabash.edu |publisher=Wabash College Board of Publications|accessdate=December 9, 2014}}</ref> He received a law degree from [[Vanderbilt University Law School]] in 1994.<ref name="nationaljournal.com">{{cite web|url= http://www.nationaljournal.com/congress-legacy/indiana-6th-house-district-20121101
|title=Indiana’s 6th House District Luke Messer bio; The National Journal |publisher=nationaljournal.com |date= |accessdate=December 9, 2012}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=https://pqasb.pqarchiver.com/indystar/access/1913977181.html?FMT=CITE&FMTS=CITE:FT&type=current&date=Oct+14%2C+1999&author=&pub=Indianapolis+Star&desc=Ex-McIntosh+aide+is+seeking+GOP+nod+to+fill+seat&pqatl=google |title=Ex-McIntosh aide is seeking GOP nod to fill seat |work=The Indianapolis Star |date=October 14, 1999 |accessdate=September 15, 2012}} {{subscription required}}</ref> Shortly after graduating from Vanderbilt, he served as an Associate Counsel at [[Koch Industries]] from 1995 to 1996.<ref>{{cite web | url=http://www.solitical.com/official/details/33997 | title =LUKE MESSER INFORMATION | publisher =solitical.com | accessdate =January 17, 2013}}</ref>

==Early political career==
Messer started his political career in 1997 as the press secretary for Tennessee Representative [[Ed Bryant]]. He was the legal counsel on the [[United States House Committee on Oversight and Government Reform|House Subcommittee for Government Reform]] for Indiana Representatives [[David M. McIntosh|David McIntosh]] and [[Dan Burton]] from 1998 to 1999, and the legal counsel to U.S. Representative [[Jimmy Duncan (U.S. politician)|Jim Duncan]] later in 1999.<ref>{{cite web | url=http://votesmart.org/candidate/biography/33997/luke-messer#.UPjqSyfol5I | title =REPRESENTATIVE LUKE MESSER'S BIOGRAPHY  |publisher =votesmart.org | accessdate =January 17, 2013}}</ref> In 1998, he was the campaign manager for [[Virginia Murphy Blankenbaker]]'s unsuccessful congressional campaign.<ref>{{cite web | url=http://www.gwu.edu/~action/2004/bush/bushorgin.html | title =President George W. Bush-Campaign Organization, Indiana | publisher =georgewashingtonuniversity.edu | accessdate =January 17, 2013}}</ref> In 1999, Messer returned to Indiana and practiced law at the Barnes & Thornburg Law Firm in [[Indianapolis]].<ref>{{cite web | url=http://www.wabash.edu/alumni/news.cfm?news_ID=1142 | title =Wabash Alumnus Elected to Indiana General Assembly | publisher =wabash.edu | date =May 22, 2003 | accessdate =January 17, 2013}}</ref>

In 2000, Messer ran for the [[United States House of Representatives]] in [[Indiana's 2nd congressional district]], where [[incumbent]] [[David M. McIntosh]] was retiring to [[Indiana gubernatorial election, 2000|run for governor]]. Messer received the endorsement of ''[[The Indianapolis Star]]''.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://pqasb.pqarchiver.com/indystar/access/1912924381.html?FMT=ABS&FMTS=ABS:FT&type=current&date=Apr+17%2C+2000&author=&pub=Indianapolis+Star&desc=Candidate+Messer's+good+ideas+for+positive+change&pqatl=google |title=Candidate Messer's good ideas for positive change |publisher=Pqasb.pqarchiver.com |date=April 17, 2000 |accessdate=September 15, 2012}}</ref> He lost the election to [[Mike Pence]].

== Lobbying work ==
Messer was a registered lobbyist from 2006 to 2012.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://secure.in.gov/apps/ilrc/registration/browse/lobbyistInfo?lobbyistId=6268&searchText=messer&searchType=lobbyistName&type=&id=6268|title=Lobbyist Browsing|website=secure.in.gov|access-date=July 2, 2017}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.nwitimes.com/news/local/lawmaker-to-work-for-firm-linked-to-toll-road-lease/article_dbf6f052-27b7-5acb-a232-7c774b324101.html|title=Lawmaker to work for firm linked to Toll Road lease |last=GUINANE |first=PATRICK|work=nwitimes.com|access-date=July 2, 2017|language=en}}</ref>

In 2006, Messer joined Ice Miller LLP's lobbying division as a partner of their public affairs group.<ref>{{cite web|author=InsideIndianaBusiness.com Report |url=http://www.insideindianabusiness.com/newsitem.asp?ID=17596 |title=State Representative Luke Messer Joins Ice Miller – Newsroom – Inside INdiana Business with Gerry Dick |publisher=Insideindianabusiness.com |date= |accessdate=September 15, 2012}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.bondbuyer.com/news/indiana-lawmaker-lobbyist|title=INDIANA: Lawmaker-Lobbyist|work=Bond Buyer|access-date=June 21, 2017|language=en}}</ref><ref name="GUINANEpguinane@nwitimes.com317.637.9078">{{Cite news|url=http://www.nwitimes.com/news/local/lawmaker-to-work-for-firm-linked-to-toll-road-lease/article_dbf6f052-27b7-5acb-a232-7c774b324101.html|title=Lawmaker to work for firm linked to Toll Road lease |last=GUINANE |first=PATRICK |work=nwitimes.com|access-date=June 21, 2017|language=en}}</ref> His decision to join Ice Miller came a month after voting in favor of Indiana leasing the Indiana Toll Road to Cintra-Macquarie, an international consortium, for "75 years at a cost of $3.85 billion." Ice Miller, Indiana's largest law firm, represented Cintra-Macquarie in the deal.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.americanregistry.com/recognition/25-largest-indianapolis-area-law-firms/20210|title=25 Largest Indianapolis-Area Law Firms|last=|first=|date=March 2005|website=AmericanRegistry.com}}</ref> Messer said he "did not know they represented anyone in connection with the Toll Road."<ref name="GUINANEpguinane@nwitimes.com317.637.9078"/>

Messer served as the Indiana co-chair of [[John McCain]]'s [[John McCain presidential campaign, 2008|2008 presidential campaign]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://seattletimes.com/html/politics/2008199303_apflippingindiana.html|title=Politics &#124; Obama out to flip Indiana to Dems &#124; Seattle Times Newspaper|last=Davies|first=Tom|date=September 24, 2008|publisher=Seattletimes.com|accessdate=September 15, 2012}}</ref> In 2010, Messer ran for the House of Representatives in [[Indiana's 5th congressional district]]. He challenged [[Dan Burton]], the incumbent representative, in the [[United States House of Representatives elections in Indiana, 2010#District 5|Republican primary]]. Burton narrowly defeated Messer.<ref name="is2">{{cite web |url=http://www.indystar.com/apps/pbcs.dll/article?AID=2011107090316|title=Candidates line up for Pence's open seat in Congress|last=Schneider|first=Mary Beth |date=July 9, 2011|accessdate=July 11, 2011|work=[[The Indianapolis Star]]}}</ref> Messer then became president and CEO of School Choice Indiana, a lobbying group that supported Indiana's private [[school voucher]] law.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.sfgate.com/news/article/Messer-defeats-Bookout-wins-Pence-s-6th-seat-4014932.php|title=Messer defeats Bookout, wins Pence's 6th seat|date=November 6, 2012|agency=Associated Press|newspaper=San Francisco Chronicle |accessdate=November 7, 2012}}</ref>

Since being elected to Congress in 2012, Ice Miller LLP has been Messer's top source of campaign contributions, having given him $82,238.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.opensecrets.org/politicians/contrib.php?cycle=Career&cid=N00012546&type=I|title=Rep. ==Political views and legislation==

===Social issues===
Messer is [[pro-life]]. He has a 100% rating from Indiana Right to Life for his abortion-related voting record. He opposes the federal government funding organizations that offer abortions, unless the abortions are the result of rape or incest or the woman's life is threatened.<ref name="OTIAbortion">{{cite web|title=Luke Messer on Abortion|url=http://www.ontheissues.org/IN/Luke_Messer_Abortion.htm|website=On The Issues|accessdate=December 28, 2017}}</ref>

On January 4, 2013, Messer voted for the Title X Abortion Provider Prohibition Act, which prohibits distribution of federal [[family planning]] funds to organizations that offer abortions unless the abortion is the result of pregnancy from incest or rape or the woman's life is at risk.<ref name="OTIAbortion" />

Messer opposes [[same-sex marriage]].<ref name="OTICivil">{{cite web|title=Luke Messer on Civil Rights|url=http://www.ontheissues.org/IN/Luke_Messer_Civil_Rights.htm|website=On The Issues|accessdate=December 28, 2017}}</ref>

===Economic issues===
On May 9, 2013, Messer voted for the Full Faith and Credit Act, which prioritized spending if the debt limit is reached.<ref name="OTIBudget">{{cite web|title=Luke Messer on Budget & Economy|url=http://www.ontheissues.org/IN/Luke_Messer_Budget_+_Economy.htm|website=On The Issues|accessdate=December 28, 2017}}</ref>

Messer voted for the [[Tax Cuts and Jobs Act of 2017]] and has stated his support for reforming the tax code to simplify it and reduce tax rates.<ref>{{cite news |title=Rep. Luke Messer: GOP Tax plan will create more jobs and bigger paychecks|url=https://www.cnbc.com/video/2017/11/02/rep-luke-messer-gop-tax-plan-will-create-more-jobs-and-bigger-paycheck.html|accessdate=December 4, 2017|publisher=CNBC|date=November 2, 2017}}</ref>

In 2013, he signed a pledge sponsored by conservative advocacy group [[Americans for Prosperity]] promising to vote against any global warming legislation that would raise taxes.{{citation needed|date=December 2017}}

Messer supports a [[balanced budget amendment]]. He opposes federal stimulus spending and supports limiting federal spending growth to per-capita inflation rate.<ref name="OTIBudget"/>

===Education===
In July 2017, Messer authored legislation to "upend the way American students pay for college."<ref name="Forbes">{{cite news|title=A New Way To Pay For College Gets A Boost In Congress|url=https://www.forbes.com/sites/prestoncooper2/2017/07/06/a-new-way-to-pay-for-college-gets-a-boost-in-congress/#68356331203b|accessdate=July 25, 2017|publisher=Forbes|date=July 6, 2017}}</ref> His legislation lays the framework for income share agreements, which have several advantages over traditional student loans.<ref name="Forbes"/> Messer has introduced legislation to require annual debt letters to be sent to student loan borrowers, which is based on an Indiana University program that reduced borrowing at the institution by 10 percent.<ref>{{cite news|title=Bill would make IU student debt initiative a nationwide requirement |url=http://www.heraldtimesonline.com/news/local/bill-would-make-iu-student-debt-initiative-a-nationwide-requirement/article_fd88c489-ae11-5278-a897-ce5f0db2ec36.html|accessdate=July 25, 2017|publisher=The Bloomington Herald Times|date=March 18, 2017}}</ref> Messer worked with Sen. Patty Murray to restore Pell Grant eligibility to students who were attending ITT Tech when the institution closed, by convincing the Education Department to restore these benefits using an existing statute.<ref>{{cite news|title=Bipartisan Solution |url=https://www.insidehighered.com/news/2016/10/31/wide-praise-restoration-pell-grant-eligibility-students-closed-colleges|accessdate=July 25, 2017 |publisher=Inside Higher Ed|date=October 31, 2016}}</ref>

In August 2013, Messer worked to pass bipartisan legislation to prevent student loan interest rates from doubling in 2013 and to link student loan interest rates to market rates.<ref>{{cite news|title=An Unusual Feat in Congress: Student Loan Bill Breezes On|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2013/08/01/us/politics/an-unusual-feat-in-congress-student-loan-bill-breezes-on.html|accessdate=July 25, 2017|publisher=The New York Times|date=July 31, 2013}}</ref>

Messer supports the expansion of school voucher programs.<ref name="Messer1"/>

===Health care===
Messer is in favor of repealing the [[Affordable Care Act]] ("Obamacare") and replacing it with "something better".<ref name="Wiechmann5417"/>

In May 2017, Messer voted for the House bill [[American Health Care Act of 2017]], to partially repeal the [[Patient Protection and Affordable Care Act]].<ref name="Wiechmann5417">{{cite web|last1=Wiechmann|first1=Stephanie|title=Congressman Messer Votes For AHCA As Healthcare Plan Passes House|url=https://indianapublicradio.org/news/2017/05/congressman-messer-votes-for-ahca-as-healthcare-plan-passes-house/|website=Indiana Public Radio|accessdate=December 28, 2017|date=May 4, 2017}}</ref>

===Immigration===
In July 2017, Messer authored legislation that would prevent undocumented immigrants from claiming the child tax credit.<ref>{{cite news|title=GOP rep urges Trump to prevent illegal immigrants from claiming child tax credits,|url=http://thehill.com/policy/finance/319244-gop-rep-urges-trump-to-prevent-illegal-immigrants-from-claiming-tax-credits|accessdate=July 25, 2017|publisher=The Hill|date=February 13, 2017}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=http://money.cnn.com/2017/12/04/pf/taxes/undocumented-immigrants-tax-bill/index.html|title=How the GOP tax bills hurt undocumented immigrants|last=DePillis|first=Lydia|work=CNNMoney|access-date=December 4, 2017}}</ref> President Donald Trump included the same proposal in his 2018 budget request to Congress.<ref>{{cite news|title=The proposal was included in President Donald Trump's 2018 budget request to Congress. |url=http://eaglecountryonline.com/local-article/messer-applauds-trump-budgets-elimination-of-child-tax-credit-for-illegal-immigrants/|accessdate=July 25, 2017|publisher=WSCH|date=May 24, 2017}}</ref>

Messer commented on the work of a 2013 bipartisan House working group on immigration reform, saying that a pathway to citizenship and a deal on metrics to measure border security would be the biggest challenges to final passage of immigration reform.<ref name="msnbc.com">{{Cite news |url=http://www.nbcnews.com/id/51394871/t/house-working-group-unveil-immigration-plan/#.WVF03BMrLBI|title=House working group to unveil immigration plan |date=April 8, 2013|work=msnbc.com|access-date=June 26, 2017|language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Citation|title=Immigration deal in sight |url=http://www.nbcnews.com/video/daily-rundown/51394017#51394017|accessdate=July 13, 2017}}</ref> Messer told Indiana's Biz Voice Magazine, "Those who came here unlawfully will have to pay penalties and back fees." <ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.bizvoicemagazine.com/media/archives/13julaug/LukeMesser.pdf|title=Popular Newcomer Eyes Progress|last=Patrick|first=Rebecca|date=July 2013|website=|access-date=}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.voanews.com/a/obama-puts-spotlight-on-immigration-reform/1677797.html|title=Obama Puts Spotlight on Immigration Reform|work=VOA|access-date=June 26, 2017|language=en}}</ref><ref name="msnbc.com"/>

Messer supported President [[Donald Trump]]'s 2017 [[Executive Order 13769|executive order]] to impose a temporary ban on entry to the U.S. to citizens of seven Muslim-majority countries, describing it as a measure to "protect Americans from terrorist threats" and saying that "President Trump is right to prioritize American safety."<ref name="Blake1">{{cite web|last1=Blake|first1=Aaron|title=Coffman, Gardner join Republicans against President Trump's travel ban; here's where the rest stand|url=http://www.denverpost.com/2017/01/29/republicans-on-trump-travel-ban/|website=Denver Post|accessdate=January 30, 2017}}</ref>

===Veterans===
Messer supported a GI Bill reform package passed by the House on June 25, 2017<ref>{{cite news|title=The Latest: House approves big expansion of GI Bill benefits|url=http://abcnews.go.com/Politics/wireStory/latest-house-rejects-plan-address-vas-budget-gap-48825458|accessdate=July 25, 2017|publisher=ABC News |date=July 24, 2017}}</ref> and signed into law by President Trump,<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.legion.org/legislative/238884/gi-bill-officially-becomes-forever-benefit|title=GI Bill officially becomes a forever benefit|website=The American Legion|language=en|access-date=October 25, 2017}}</ref> which included a provision he authored that would retroactively restore education benefits to veterans attending schools that close mid-semester, like ITT Technical Institute.<ref>{{cite news|title=Expanded GI Bill passes House on unanimous vote |url=http://www.journalgazette.net/news/local/indiana/20170725/expanded-gi-bill-passes-house-on-unanimous-vote|accessdate=July 25, 2017|publisher=Fort Wayne Journal Gazette|date=July 25, 2017}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|title=Veterans await help after ITT Tech shutdown|url=http://wishtv.com/2017/07/14/veterans-await-help-after-itt-tech-shutdown/|accessdate=July 25, 2017|publisher=WISH-TV|date=July 14, 2017}}</ref>

===Crime===
In February 2013, Messer voted in favor of reauthorizing the [[Violence Against Women Act]].<ref name="OTICivil"/>

Messer has a 62% rating from the [[National Association of Police Organizations]] for his voting record on issues importance to police and crime.<ref name="OTIcrime">{{cite web|title=Luke Messer on Crime|url=http://www.ontheissues.org/IN/Luke_Messer_Crime.htm|website=On The Issues|accessdate=December 28, 2017}}</ref>

Messer has a "D" rating from [[NORML]] for his voting history regarding [[cannabis]]-related causes. Messer opposes veterans having access to medical marijuana if recommended by their [[Veterans Health Administration]] doctor and if it is legal for medicinal purposes in their state of residence.<ref name="NORML">{{cite web|title=Indiana Scorecard|url=http://norml.org/congressional-scorecard/indiana|website=NORML|accessdate=December 28, 2017|language=en-us}}</ref>

===Gun rights===

In 2012, the [[National Rifle Association]] gave Messer an "A" rating for his gun-related voting record. Messer opposes restrictions on gun purchases.<ref name="OTIGun">{{cite web|title=Luke Messer on Gun Control|url=http://www.ontheissues.org/IN/Luke_Messer_Gun_Control.htm|website=On The Issues|accessdate=December 28, 2017}}</ref>

==Personal life==
Messer and his wife Jennifer have two daughters and one son. Luke and Jennifer Messer are the authors of a children's book, ''Hoosier Heart''.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://projects.wsj.com/campaign2012/candidates/view/luke-messer--IN-H |title=Election 2012 Luke Messer bio; The Wallstreet Journal |publisher=projects.wsj.com |accessdate=December 9, 2012}}</ref>

Messer was cited for [[driving under the influence]] (DUI) in 1990 and 1996.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.indystar.com/story/news/politics/2018/04/19/luke-messer-indiana-primary-2018-senate-race-didnt-disclose-duis-when-lawmaker-drunk-driving/511788002/ |title=Luke Messer didn’t disclose DUIs when he replaced lawmaker killed by drunk driver |publisher=IndyStar.com |date=2018-04-19 |accessdate=2018-04-19}}</ref>

Following Messer's election to Congress, he sold his house in [[Shelbyville, Indiana]] and moved to [[McLean, Virginia]], a [[Washington, D.C.]] suburb.<ref name=goes/> He is now listed as a registered voter at his mother's address in [[Greensburg, Indiana]].<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.journalgazette.net/news/local/local-politics/20170726/messer-announces-senate-bid |title=Messer announces Senate bid|last=Francisco|first=Brian|date=|work=journalgazette.net|access-date=October 4, 2017|language=en-US}}</ref> Messer has clarified that he owns the home with his mother and lives there when he is in the state.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.wibc.com/blogs/tony-katz/tony-katz-today/exclusive-messer-sets-abc-news-associated-press-straight-wifes|title=EXCLUSIVE: Messer Sets ABC News, Associated Press Straight on Wife's Employment, Living Situation {{!}} 93.1 WIBC|work=93.1 WIBC|access-date=October 4, 2017|language=en}}</ref> He faced criticism from his opponents in the 2018 Republican primary election for the [[United States Senate]] for moving his family to the Washington, D.C. area.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.rollcall.com/news/home/indiana-reps-wrestle-residency|title=Indiana Reps Still Wrestling With Residency|date=September 15, 2015|website=Roll Call|access-date=July 15, 2017}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.rollcall.com/news/politics/indiana-senate-primary-messer-rokita|title=Indiana Headed for Another Member-on-Member Senate Primary|date=March 23, 2017|website=Roll Call|access-date=July 15, 2017}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.journalgazette.net/blog/political-notebook/20170521/republicans-with-eye-on-senate-heat-up-their-feud|title=Republicans with eye on Senate heat up their feud {{!}} Political notebook {{!}} Journal Gazette|website=www.journalgazette.net|access-date=July 15, 2017}}</ref>

[[Fishers, Indiana|Fishers]], an Indianapolis suburb, has paid Messer's wife, Jennifer Messer, $580,000 since 2015 in legal consulting she primarily does from the family's Washington, D.C. area home.<ref name="Indianapolis Star">{{Cite news|url=http://www.indystar.com/story/news/politics/2017/05/11/ap-fishers-pays-rep-luke-messers-wife-20-k-month/318555001/|title=AP: Fishers pays Rep. Luke Messer's wife $20K/month|work=Indianapolis Star|access-date=June 13, 2017 |language=en}}</ref> She is paid $20,000 a month as a part-time contract attorney for the city.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://cbs4indy.com/2017/06/21/ap-report-fishers-pays-rep-luke-messers-wife-240k-for-part-time-job/|title=AP report: Fishers pays Rep. Luke Messer’s wife $240K for part-time job|date=June 21, 2017 |website=CBS 4 - Indianapolis News, Weather, Traffic and Sports {{!}} WTTV|access-date=June 22, 2017}}</ref> Jennifer Messer began the work for the City of Fishers two years before her husband was elected to Congress.<ref name="Indianapolis Star"/> Fishers Mayor Scott Fadness said the arrangement helped usher in an era of "unprecedented" economic success in the growing suburb of about 85,000 people.<ref name="Indianapolis Star"/><ref name="Indianapolis Star"/> Messer has defended his wife's work, calling her "the brains of the Messer outfit", and Jennifer defended her work in an op-ed for ''[[The Indianapolis Star]]'', calling an [[Associated Press]] story about her "unfair, intellectually dishonest and straight-up sexist".<ref name="rollcall.com">{{cite web |url=http://www.rollcall.com/news/politics/messer-defends-wifes-contract-city|title=Messer Defends Wife’s $20K a Month Contract With City|date=May 12, 2017 |website=Roll Call|access-date=June 13, 2017}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.indystar.com/story/opinion/2017/05/15/jennifer-messer-i-work-diligently-fishers/323033001/|title=Jennifer Messer: 'I work diligently for Fishers'|work=Indianapolis Star|access-date=June 22, 2017|language=en}}</ref>
