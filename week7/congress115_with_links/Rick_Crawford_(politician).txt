'''Eric Alan "Rick" Crawford''' (born January 22, 1966)<ref>[http://crawford.house.gov/biography/ crawford.house.gov]</ref> is an [[United States|American]] [[politician]] who has been the [[United States House of Representatives|U.S. Representative]] for {{ushr|AR|1}} since 2011. He is a member of the [[Republican Party (United States)|Republican Party]]. Before he was elected to Congress, Crawford was a [[radio]] announcer, [[business]]man and a [[soldier]] in the [[United States Army]].

==Early life and education==

Crawford was born at [[Homestead Base, Florida|Homestead Air Force Base]] in [[Florida]], the son of Ruth Anne and Donny J. "Don" Crawford.<ref>[http://freepages.genealogy.rootsweb.ancestry.com/%7Ebattle/reps/crawford.htm freepages.genealogy.rootsweb.ancestry.com]</ref> Crawford grew up in a military family; his father served in the [[United States Air Force]]. He graduated from [[Alvirne High School]] in [[Hudson, New Hampshire|Hudson]], [[New Hampshire]]. He enlisted in the Army and served as an [[Explosive Ordnance Disposal|explosive ordnance disposal technician]] for four years,<ref>{{cite news | first = Andrew | last = Stiles | title = National Review: Turning Arkansas Red | date = 2010-10-27 |url=https://www.npr.org/templates/story/story.php?storyId=130855895 |work=[[National Public Radio]] | accessdate = 2010-10-31 }}</ref> and advanced to the rank of [[Sergeant]]. After his service, he attended [[Arkansas State University]] in [[Jonesboro, Arkansas|Jonesboro]], [[Arkansas]], and graduated in 1996 with a [[Bachelor of Arts|B.S.]] in Agriculture Business and Economics.

== Radio career ==
He has been a [[news anchor]] and agri-reporter on [[KAIT-TV]] in Jonesboro and farm director on [[KFIN|KFIN-FM]]. He owned and operated the AgWatch Network, a farm news network heard on 39 radio stations in Arkansas, [[Missouri]], [[Tennessee]], [[Mississippi]], and [[Kentucky]].<ref>[http://www.meetrickcrawford.com/about/ meetrickcrawford.com About]</ref>

== U.S. House of Representatives ==
[[File:Rick Crawford, Official Portrait, 112th Congress.jpg|thumb|Crawford's freshman portrait]]

===Elections===
;2010
{{Main|United States House of Representatives elections in Arkansas, 2010#District 1*}}
Crawford chose to run for [[Arkansas' 1st congressional district]] after [[Democratic Party (United States)|Democratic]] [[United States House of Representatives|U.S. Representative]] [[Robert Marion Berry|Marion Berry]] decided to retire. Crawford received the endorsements of [[governor of Minnesota|Governor]] [[Tim Pawlenty]] of [[Minnesota]], former Arkansas [[governor of Arkansas|Governor]] [[Mike Huckabee]], current Governor [[Asa Hutchinson]], and former U.S. Representative [[Ed Bethune]] of [[Arkansas' 2nd congressional district]].<ref>[http://www.meetrickcrawford.com/endorsements/ meetrickcrawford.com endorsements]</ref> He won the Republican [[Primary election|primary]], defeating [[Princella Smith]] 72 to 28 percent.<ref>[http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=572929 www.ourcampaigns.com AR District 01 – R Primary, 2010]</ref> He won the [[general election]], defeating Berry's chief of staff [[Chad Causey]] 52 to 43 percent.<ref>[http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=487762 ourcampaigns.com, AR – District 01, 2011]</ref>

;2012
{{Main|United States House of Representatives elections in Arkansas, 2012#District 1}}
Crawford won re-election to a second term by defeating Democratic prosecutor, Scott Ellington, 56 to 39 percent.<ref>[http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=705041 ourcampaigns.com, AR – District 01, 2012]</ref>

;2014
{{Main|United States House of Representatives elections in Arkansas, 2014#District 1}}
Crawford won re-election to a third term, defeating [[Heber Springs, Arkansas|Heber Springs]] [[Mayor]] Jackie McPherson 63 to 33 percent.<ref>http://results.enr.clarityelections.com/AR/53237/149792/Web01/en/summary.html</ref>

;2016
Crawford won re-election to a fourth term, defeating Libertarian candidate Mark West 76 to 24 percent. <ref>https://ballotpedia.org/Rick_Crawford_(Arkansas)</ref>

===Tenure===
On January 5, 2011, Crawford was sworn into office as a member of the [[112th United States Congress|112th Congress]]. He is the first Republican to represent his district in Washington since [[Reconstruction era of the United States|Reconstruction]]. The last Republican to represent the district was [[Asa Hodges]] who vacated the seat on March 3, 1875, during [[Reconstruction era in the United States|Reconstruction]].<ref>[http://bioguide.congress.gov/scripts/biodisplay.pl?index=H000671 bioguide.congress.gov Asa Hodges]</ref> Crawford is a member of the [[Republican Study Committee]].<ref>[http://www.mcallennow.com/voter-resources/official/283914/Rick/Crawford mcallennow.com voter resources, Rick Crawford]</ref>

Crawford voted to repeal [[U.S. President]] [[Barack H. Obama]]'s [[Patient Protection and Affordable Care Act]] and to return federal non-security spending to fiscal year 2008 levels.  He also voted to terminate taxpayer financing of presidential election campaigns and party conventions.<ref>[http://crawford.house.gov/voterecord/ crawford.house.gov vote record]</ref>

In 2010, Crawford signed a pledge sponsored by [[Americans for Prosperity]] to vote against any [[Politics of global warming|global warming]] legislation that would raise taxes.<ref>[http://americansforprosperity.org/noclimatetax//wp-content/uploads/2010/04/crawford-rick.pdf /americansforprosperity.org Americans for Prosperity Applauds  U.S. House  Candidate Rick Crawford]</ref>

Crawford supported President [[Donald Trump]]'s 2017 [[Executive Order 13769|executive order]] to impose a ban on travel to the U.S. by citizens of seven [[Muslim]]-majority countries, saying that the order was "designed to keep our nation safer" although "Green card holders and aides of the U.S. military should be allowed entry."<ref name="Blake1">{{cite web|last1=Blake|first1=Aaron|title=Whip Count: Here’s where Republicans stand on Trump's controversial travel ban|url=https://www.washingtonpost.com/news/the-fix/wp/2017/01/29/heres-where-republicans-stand-on-president-trumps-controversial-travel-ban|newspaper=Washington Post|date=January 31, 2017}}</ref>

Crawford voted in favor of the [[Tax Cuts and Jobs Act of 2017]], supporting [[tax reform]].<ref name="Almukhtar122117">{{cite web|last1=Almukhtar|first1=Sarah|title=How Each House Member Voted on the Tax Bill|url=https://www.nytimes.com/interactive/2017/12/19/us/politics/tax-bill-house-live-vote.html?_r=0|website=The New York Times|accessdate=21 December 2017|date=19 December 2017}}</ref> He believes that the bill will make it easier for people to file their taxes and that "the vast majority of middle-income families in my district will get to keep more of their money to use as they wish." He also believes that local businesses will hire more and provide pay raises to current employers in the wake of the bill's implementation.<ref name="NADG122017">{{cite web|title=Senate OKs tax bill; House revote set|url=http://www.nwaonline.com/news/2017/dec/20/senate-oks-tax-bill-house-revote-set-20/?news-national|website=Northwest Arkansas Democrat Gazette|accessdate=21 December 2017}}</ref>

===Legislation===
On January 18, 2013, Crawford introduced the [[Farmers Undertake Environmental Land Stewardship Act (H.R. 311; 113th Congress)]] into the House.<ref name=311sum>{{cite web|title=H.R. 311 – Summary|url=http://beta.congress.gov/bill/113th-congress/house-bill/311|publisher=United States Congress|accessdate=10 March 2014}}</ref> The bill would require the [[Environmental Protection Agency]] (EPA) to modify the Spill Prevention, Control, and Countermeasure (SPCC) rule, which regulates oil discharges into navigable waters and adjoining shorelines.<ref name=cbo311>{{cite web|title=H.R. 311 – CBO|url=http://www.cbo.gov/publication/44719|publisher=Congressional Budget Office|accessdate=10 March 2014}}</ref> The rule requires certain farmers to develop an [[oil spill]] prevention plan that is certified by a professional [[engineer]] and may require them to make [[infrastructure]] changes.<ref name="cbo311"/> According to supporters, this bill would "ease the burden placed on farmers and ranchers" by making it easier for smaller farms to self-certify and raising the level of storage capacity under which farms are exempted.<ref name=HighPlainsJ1>{{cite news|title=Committee passes legislation to ease burden of SPCC program|url=http://www.hpj.com/archives/2013/dec13/dec23/1104FUELSActsr.cfm|accessdate=11 March 2014|newspaper=High Plains Journal|date=23 December 2013|deadurl=yes|archiveurl=https://archive.is/20140311142021/http://www.hpj.com/archives/2013/dec13/dec23/1104FUELSActsr.cfm|archivedate=11 March 2014|df=}}</ref>

== Political positions ==

===LGBT rights===

Crawford opposed the landmark ''[[Obergefell v. Hodges]]'' ruling legalizing [[same-sex marriage]] and believes that it should have been decided state-by-state, not by the Supreme Court.<ref name="TBP62815">{{cite web|title=Arkansas politicians, activists divided on same sex marriage ruling - Talk Business & Politics|url=https://talkbusiness.net/2015/06/arkansas-politicians-activists-divided-on-same-sex-marriage-ruling/|website=Talk Business & Politics|accessdate=21 December 2017|date=28 June 2015}}</ref>

==Personal life==
Crawford and his wife, Stacy, live in Jonesboro with their children. He attends Nettleton Baptist Church, a [[Southern Baptist]] congregation in Jonesboro.<ref>{{cite news |author=Staff |title=Ten Southern Baptists sworn in as new reps. |url=http://www.bpnews.net/bpnews.asp?id=34378 |accessdate=25 December 2014 |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20141226010629/http://www.bpnews.net/34378 |archivedate=26 December 2014 |newspaper=Baptist Press |date=5 January 2011}}</ref>
