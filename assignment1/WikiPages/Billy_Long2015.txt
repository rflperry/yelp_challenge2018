'''William H. "Billy" Long II'''<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.legistorm.com/memberbio/2770/Rep_Billy_Long_MO.html |title=Representative William H. Long (Billy) (R-Missouri, 7th) - Biography from |publisher=LegiStorm |date= |accessdate=2011-07-25}}</ref> (born August 11, 1955)<ref>John Bicknell [http://news.yahoo.com/s/cq/20101103/pl_cq_politics/politics000003758822 112th Congress: Billy Long, R-Mo. (7th District))] ''[[CQ Politics]]'' November 3, 2010</ref> is an [[United States|American]] politician who has been the [[United States House of Representatives|U.S. Representative]] for {{ushr|Missouri|7}} since 2011. He is a member of the [[Republican Party (United States)|Republican Party]]. In the 2010 election, he won the open U.S. House seat vacated by incumbent [[Roy Blunt]], who decided to run for the [[United States Senate]].

== Early life and education ==
Long is a fourth-generation native of Missouri. He was born in [[Springfield, Missouri]], in 1955. He dropped out of the [[University of Missouri]] in [[Columbia, Missouri|Columbia]] to attend the Missouri Auction School in [[Kansas City, Missouri|Kansas City]]. He received his Certified Auctioneer Institute designation via the National Auctioneers Association Education Institute and Trustees.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.congress.org/election/candidate/id/153758 |title=Election |publisher=Congress.org |date= |accessdate=2011-07-25}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://billylongforcongress.com/Groups/1000059963/Billy_Long_for/About_Billy_Long/About_Billy_Long.aspx |title=About Billy Long |publisher=Billy Long for Congress |date=2010-11-02 |accessdate=2011-07-25}}</ref>

== Professional career ==
Long owned Billy Long Auctions, [[Limited liability company|LLC]]. He was also a talk radio show host on Springfield-based station [[KWTO (AM)|KWTO]]. He is a member of the [[National Association of Realtors]], [[National Auctioneers Association]], [[United States Chamber of Commerce|the Springfield Area Chamber of Commerce]], the [[National Rifle Association]], and the Greater Springfield Board of Realtors.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.billylongforcongress.com/Groups/1000059963/Billy_Long_for/About_Billy_Long/About_Billy_Long.aspx |title=About Billy Long |publisher=Billy Long for Congress |date=2010-11-02 |accessdate=2011-07-25}}</ref>

Long also participated in the [[World Poker Tour]] participating in professional sanctioned games including the Southern Poker Championship at the Beau Rivage and the Bellagio Cup.

==U.S. House of Representatives==

===2010 election===
{{See also|United States House of Representatives elections in Missouri, 2010#District 7}}
Long jumped into the race for the 7th District after [[Roy Blunt]] gave it up to make an ultimately successful run for the U. S. Senate.  In the crowded seven-way Republican primary—the real contest in this heavily Republican district—Long narrowly won with 36 percent of the vote.  He defeated Democratic challenger Scott Eckersley with 63 percent of the vote.

===2012 election===
{{See also|United States House of Representatives elections in Missouri, 2012#District 7}}
In the 2012 general election, Long won a bid for reelection by 63.9% of votes against Democratic challenger [[Jim Evans (politician)|Jim Evans]] (30.9%) and Libertarian candidate Kevin Craig (5.2%).<ref>[http://enr.sos.mo.gov/ENR/Views/TabularData.aspx?TabView=StateRaces^Federal%20/%20Statewide%20Races^011656688155 Nov 6, 2012 General Election]. Retrieved 27 February 2013.</ref>

===2014 election===
{{See also|United States House of Representatives elections in Missouri, 2014#District 7}}
In the August 5th, 2014 primary race, Long defeated sole Republican challenger Marshall Works 62.4% to 37.6%.<ref>{{cite web |title=2014 Missouri House Primaries Results |work=[[Politico]] |publisher= |date=August 6, 2014 |url=http://www.politico.com/2014-election/results/map/house/missouri/ |accessdate=August 8, 2014}}</ref>  He is set to re-face Democrat [[Jim Evans (politician)|Jim Evans]] and Libertarian Kevin Craig in the November general election.

===Committee assignments===

Long is a member of the House Energy and Commerce Committee.  This influential House committee has jurisdiction over our nation’s telecommunications, consumer protection, food and drug safety, public health research, environmental quality, energy policy, and interstate and foreign commerce. 

As of July 31, 2014, the House Energy and Commerce Committee has held 172 hearings  and marked up and debated 60 bills. Fifty-three bills have been approved by the House and 18 bills ultimately signed by the president, thus making the Energy and Commerce Committee the most active committee in the House of Representatives.
