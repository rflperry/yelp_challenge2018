'''Dennis "Denny" L. Heck''' (born July 29, 1952) is an [[United States|American]] politician who has been the [[United States House of Representatives|United States Representative]] for [[Washington (U.S. state)|Washington's]] [[Washington's 10th congressional district|10th congressional district]] since 2013. Heck was previously the [[Democratic Party (United States)|Democratic]] nominee for U.S. Representative for the 3rd district in 2010, but was defeated by [[Jaime Herrera]] (R). In 2012 Heck ran and won in the newly created 10th district, defeating Republican [[Dick Muri]].<ref name="Schrader">{{cite web|last=Schrader|first=Jordan|author2=Shannon, Brad|title=Democrats Derek Kilmer, Denny Heck win Congressional races|url=http://www.thenewstribune.com/2012/11/06/2358023/democrats-derek-kilmer-denny-heck.html|work=thenewstribune.com|publisher=Tacoma News, Inc.|accessdate=9 November 2012}}</ref>

Heck served as Chief of Staff to Washington Governor [[Booth Gardner]] from 1990 to 1993.  Previously, he was Majority Leader of the [[Washington State House of Representatives]] and Chief Clerk of the House.  He founded and served as CEO of [[TVW (Washington)|TVW]], the state equivalent of [[C-SPAN]].  From 1999 to 2012, he served on the board of directors of Intrepid Learning Solutions,<ref>[http://www.intrepidls.com Intrepid Leaning Solutions]</ref> which he co-founded with [[Christopher Hedrick]].

==Early life, education, and business career==
Heck was born in [[Vancouver, Washington]] in 1952, and raised in the [[Lake Shore, Washington|Lake Shore]] area of [[Clark County, Washington|Clark County]] where he graduated from [[Columbia River High School]]. He graduated from [[The Evergreen State College]] in [[Olympia, Washington|Olympia]] in 1973.

Heck was the co-founder and from 1999 to 2012 was board director of [[Intrepid Learning Solutions]],<ref>[http://www.intrepidls.com/company/directors/ Intrepid Learning Solutions]</ref> a company specializing in business oriented education and training programs. He helped found [[Digital Efficiency]]<ref>[http://www.digitalfnc.com/whoweare.html Digital Efficiency]</ref> which specializes in aiding businesses and medical facilities in transferring toward an all digital format. Heck helped found [[TVW (Washington)|TVW]],<ref>[http://www.tvw.org/about/history.cfm?CFID=619481&CFTOKEN=72224912&bhcp=1 TVW]</ref> the public affairs network for the state. TVW provides coverage of the [[Washington State Legislature]] and sessions of the [[Washington Supreme Court]].

Heck is the author of ''Challenges and Opportunities:  The Transformation of Washington's Schools'', published in 1987.

==Washington House of Representatives==
Starting in 1976, Heck was elected to five terms in the [[Washington House of Representatives]], representing the [[Washington's 17th legislative district|17th Legislative District]] in Clark, [[Skamania County, Washington|Skamania]], and [[Klickitat County, Washington|Klickitat]] Counties. During that time he was elected Majority Leader, the second-ranking position in the House. He also co-chaired the Education Committee and wrote the state’s historic Basic Education Act.

Heck served as [[Chief of staff#Government|Chief of Staff]] for Governor [[Booth Gardner]] during his second term (1989–93).

Heck announced his candidacy to replace the retiring [[Democratic Party (United States)|Democratic]] incumbent [[Brian Baird]].<ref>[http://www.columbian.com/news/2010/jan/07/denny-heck-enters-race-for-bairds-seat/ Denny Heck enters race for Baird’s seat]</ref> He won the primary with 31% of the vote and faced runner-up [[Republican Party (United States)|Republican]] [[Jaime Herrera]], who won 28% of the vote.<ref name=HeckWinsPrimary>{{cite news |url=http://blogs.cqpolitics.com/eyeon2010/2010/08/herrera-heck-poll.html |title=Herrera Up Big in WA-3 Poll |author=Kyle Trygstad |work=CQ Politics |date=2010-08-26 |accessdate=2010-09-01}}</ref>

==Personal life==
Heck and his wife Paula Fruci, married since 1976, have two sons.

Heck wrote and self-published a mystery novel entitled "The Enemy You Know." In 2008, he wrote and performed a one-man play, "Our Times" to several sold out audiences.{{Citation needed|reason=I mean, a play? Seriously? Is this even notable? Maybe, but get ya house in order and cite a newspaper or something|date=August 2010}} He and his wife, Paula, who directed the play, donated all proceeds to local charities.

Heck has supported numerous organizations within [[Washington (U.S. state)|Washington]], both actively and as a past contributor. He currently serves on the Board of Trustees for [[Evergreen State College]].<ref>[http://www.evergreen.edu/trustees/ Evergreen State College Trustees]</ref> He serves on the Board for the [[Washington State History Museum]]<ref>[http://research.washingtonhistory.org/Results.aspx?q=board%20members WA History Museum]</ref> He is a member of the Steering Committee for the [http://www.washingtonlearns.wa.gov/ Washington Learns] Commission; a long-term strategy to improve the education system of Washington.<ref>[http://www.washingtonlearns.wa.gov/steering.htm Washington Learn Steering Committee]</ref>
