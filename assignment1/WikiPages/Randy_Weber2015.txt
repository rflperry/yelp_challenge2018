'''Randy Weber''' (born July 2, 1953) is an [[United States|American]] [[Businesspeople|businessman]] and a [[politician]]. He is a member of the [[United States House of Representatives]]. He has represented [[Texas' 14th congressional district]] since 2013, succeeding [[Ron Paul]]. He defeated former Congressman [[Nick Lampson]] on November 6, 2012, by a 53% to 45% margin. He was a member of the [[Texas House of Representatives]], representing the 29th district. He is a member of the [[Republican Party (United States)|Republican Party]].<ref name="HousChron11072012" />

==Early life, education, and business career==
He graduated from [[Alvin Community College]] and in 1977 earned a BS in public affairs from the [[University of Houston–Clear Lake]]. In 1981, he founded Weber's Air & Heat Air-Conditioning Company.<ref name=autogenerated1>[http://www.randyweber.org/meet-randy/ Randy Weber - Meet Randy]</ref>

==Early political career==
He was a city councilman of [[Pearland, Texas]] for six years. In addition he served as [[Brazoria County, Texas|Brazoria County Grand Jury Commissioner]], Brazoria County Redistricting Committee, [[Texas Republican Party Convention]] delegate, president of Brazoria County Cities Association, member of the Brazoria County Republican Party, and chairman of the Pearland Area Republican Party Headquarters.<ref>[http://votesmart.org/candidate/biography/102026 Representative Randy Weber Biography - Project Vote Smart<!-- Bot generated title -->]</ref>

==Texas House of Representatives==

===Elections===
After State Representative Glenda Dawson died on September 12, 2006,<ref>[http://www.chron.com/news/houston-deaths/article/State-Rep-Glenda-Dawson-of-Pearland-dies-1903890.php State Rep. Glenda Dawson, of Pearland, dies - Houston Chronicle<!-- Bot generated title -->]</ref> a special election was held on December 19. Businessman Mike P. O'Day earned 48% of the vote, falling just short of the 50% threshold. Weber earned 28% of the vote, qualifying for the run-off.<ref>[http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=323849 Our Campaigns - TX State House 029 - Special Election Race - Dec 19, 2006<!-- Bot generated title -->]</ref> In the January run-off, O'Day defeated him 57%-43%.<ref>[http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=330366 Our Campaigns - TX State House 029 - Special Election Runoff Race - Jan 16, 2007<!-- Bot generated title -->]</ref>

O'Day decided to retire. Weber ran for the March 2008 primary and won with 59% of the vote.<ref>[http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=425862 Our Campaigns - TX State House 029 - R Primary Race - Mar 04, 2008<!-- Bot generated title -->]</ref> He won the general election with 60% of the vote.<ref>[http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=425854 Our Campaigns - TX State House 029 Race - Nov 04, 2008<!-- Bot generated title -->]</ref> He won re-election to a second term in 2010 with 85% of the vote.<ref>[http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=644869 Our Campaigns - TX State House 029 Race - Nov 02, 2010<!-- Bot generated title -->]</ref>

==U.S. House of Representatives==

===Elections===
;2012
{{See also|United States House of Representatives elections in Texas, 2012#District 14}}
After Republican U.S. Representative [[Ron Paul]] of [[Texas' 14th congressional district]] decided to retire, nine Republicans decided to run in the newly redrawn district. Weber received endorsements from Governor [[Rick Perry]] and Ron Paul. He ranked first with 28% of the vote, but failed to reach the 50% threshold. In the run-off, he defeated Pearland City Councilwoman Felicia Harris 62%-37%.<ref>[http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=767638 Our Campaigns - TX District 14 - R Runoff Race - Jul 31, 2012<!-- Bot generated title -->]</ref> He defeated former Congressman [[Nick Lampson]] on November 6, 2012, by a 53% to 45% margin.<ref name="HousChron11072012">Pinkerton, James. [http://www.chron.com/news/politics/article/GOP-s-Weber-beats-Lampson-in-race-to-succeed-Ron-4014605.php GOP's Weber beats Lampson in race to succeed Ron Paul], ''[[Houston Chronicle]]'', November 7, 2012.</ref>
Based upon Weber's vote total in 2012, [[Larry Sabato|Larry Sabato's]] Crystal Ball named Weber one of their "Underachievers" because he received only 53.5% of the vote in his Congressional district while [[Mitt Romney]] received 59.3%.<ref>Kondik, Kyle. [http://www.centerforpolitics.org/crystalball/articles/sanford-joins-the-underachievers/ Sanford Joins “the Underachievers”], Larry Sabato's Crystal Ball, May 9, 2013.</ref>
