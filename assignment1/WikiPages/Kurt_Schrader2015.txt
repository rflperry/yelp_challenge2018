'''Kurt Schrader''' (born October 19, 1951) is an [[United States|American]] politician who has been the [[United States House of Representatives|U.S. Representative]] for {{ushr|OR|5}} since 2009. He is a member of the [[Democratic Party (United States)|Democratic Party]] and previously served in both houses of the [[Oregon Legislative Assembly]].

==Early life, education, and early career==
Schrader was born in Connecticut and received a Bachelor of Arts degree from [[Cornell University]] in 1973. While at Cornell, Schrader met [[Martha Schrader|Martha Northam]] and the two were married in 1975.<ref name=kohler>{{cite news|title=Vet makes a house call|last=Kohler|first=Vince|date=May 1, 1997|work=[[The Oregonian]]}}</ref> Schrader earned his [[Doctor of Veterinary Medicine]] degree from the [[University of Illinois]] in 1977. A year later, the Schraders moved to Oregon, and Kurt opened the Clackamas County Veterinary Clinic in [[Oregon City, Oregon|Oregon City]], to begin his veterinary practice.<ref name=kohler/>

Schrader served for 16 years on the Canby Planning Commission.

==Oregon legislature==

===Elections===
After winning his first election in 1996, Schrader served four terms in the [[Oregon House of Representatives]]. Schrader ran for the [[Oregon House of Representatives]] in 1994, where he lost to Republican [[Jerry Grisham]] in the general election by 38 votes.<ref name=hunsberger>{{cite news|title=More absentees vote but alter few races|last=Hunsberger|first=Brent|work=[[The Oregonian]]|date=November 11, 1996}}</ref> In 1996, Schrader ran again; and this time defeated Paul Kraxburger.<ref name=kohler2>{{cite news|title=Grisham wins by 38 votes|last=Kohler|first=Vince|work=[[The Oregonian]]|date=November 15, 1994}}</ref>  He was subsequently reelected to the House in 1998 and 2000.

In 2002, Schrader ran for the [[Oregon State Senate]] seat vacated by the retiring [[Verne Duncan]], representing the 20th district in southwestern [[Clackamas County, Oregon|Clackamas County]], including the cities of [[Barlow, Oregon|Barlow]], [[Canby, Oregon|Canby]], [[Gladstone, Oregon|Gladstone]], [[Johnson City, Oregon|Johnson City]], [[Oregon City, Oregon|Oregon City]] and portions of [[Milwaukie, Oregon|Milwaukie]]. He defeated fellow Oregon House member Kathy Lowe in a contentious Democratic primary, and then faced no Republican opposition in the general election.<ref name=mayes>{{cite news|title=Schraders ahead in legislative races|work=[[The Oregonian]]|last=Mayes|first=Steve|date=May 22, 2002}}</ref> [[Martha Schrader]] was the Democratic nominee to succeed her husband, but lost in the general election to [[Wayne Scott]].<ref name=mayes/> She served as a Clackamas County commissioner until 2009, when she was appointed by the same commission (with Martha recusing herself from voting) to replace her husband in the State Senate.<ref name=schrader>{{cite news|url=http://www.oregonlive.com/clackamascounty/index.ssf/2009/01/martha_schrader_will_replace_h.html|publisher=[[The Oregonian]]|date=January 9, 2009|accessdate=January 10, 2009|title=Martha Schrader will replace husband in State Senate}}</ref>

===Committee assignments===
In the Oregon Senate, Schrader served as co-chair of the Joint Ways and Means Committee in the [[72nd Oregon Legislative Assembly|2003]]<ref name=osl>{{cite web|url=http://www.leg.state.or.us/schrader/bio.htm|archiveurl=http://web.archive.org/web/20041211124245/http://www.leg.state.or.us/schrader/bio.htm|archivedate=December 11, 2004|title=Senator Kurt Schrader|publisher=Oregon State Legislature|accessdate=April 14, 2007}}</ref> and [[73rd Oregon Legislative Assembly|2005]] sessions, as well as chair of the Interim Joint Legislative Audit Committee in the 2005 session.{{Citation needed|date=August 2009}} To prepare for his House seat, Schrader resigned effective December 17.<ref name=schrader/>

== U.S. House of Representatives ==

===Election===
;2008
{{See also|United States House of Representatives elections in Oregon, 2008#Fifth congressional district|United States House of Representatives elections in Oregon, 2010#Fifth congressional district}}
In May 2008, Schrader won the Democratic nomination for {{ushr|OR|5}} for the seat being vacated by [[Darlene Hooley]].<ref name=schrader>{{cite news|url=http://www.oregonlive.com/special/index.ssf/2008/05/fifth_d.html|title=Schrader wins 5th District Democratic nomination|accessdate=May 20, 2008|publisher=OregonLive.com|date=May 20, 2008}}</ref> In the general election, Schrader defeated Republican [[Mike Erickson]], winning election to the U.S. House.<ref name=5th>{{cite news|url=http://www.oregonlive.com/special/index.ssf/2008/11/fifth.html|title=Schrader wins 5th District|date=November 4, 2008|accessdate=November 4, 2008|publisher=OregonLive.com}}</ref>  Schrader won the election with 54 percent of the vote to Erickson's 38 percent.  Schrader won all seven of the counties in the 5th congressional district, though he posted a plurality win in Polk County.

;2010

Schrader was challenged by Republican nominee and [[Oregon House of Representatives|Oregon State Representative]] [[Scott Bruun]] and Pacific Green nominee Chris Lugo. Despite several polls showing Bruun ahead and expert tracker [[Nate Silver]] of [[FiveThirtyEight]] predicting Schrader would likely lose his bid for reelection, the final vote tally had Schrader winning by a fairly comfortable five-point margin, picking up 51% of the vote to Bruun's 46%. It was the closest House race in Oregon in 2010, a year in which Republicans picked up at least 63 House seats, but only [[United States House of Representatives elections in Washington, 2010#Third congressional district|one]] on the [[U.S. West Coast|West Coast]].

===Tenure===

Schrader voted for the [[Budget Control Act of 2011|Budget Control Act]]. He voted both in favor of the [[Patient Protection and Affordable Care Act]] and for funding [[State Children's Health Insurance Program|SCHIP]].<ref>http://www.thepoliticalguide.com/Profiles/House/Oregon/Kurt_Schrader/Views/</ref><ref>http://www.ontheissues.org/House/Kurt_Schrader.htm</ref>

Schrader is a political moderate, breaking with his party more frequently than 63% of the Democratic Caucus.<ref name=votewithparty>{{cite news|title=House Voting with Party Scores, 111th Congress|url=http://projects.washingtonpost.com/congress/111/house/party-voters/|work=Washington Post|accessdate=September 14, 2010}}</ref>

On December 17, 2009, Schrader announced that he would become a member of the [[Blue Dog Coalition]].<ref name=bluedog>{{cite news|title=Blue Dogs Welcome New Members|url=http://www.house.gov/melancon/BlueDogs/Press%20Releases/2009%20-%20BD%20New%20Member%20Announcement%20-%2012.16.09.pdf|publisher=Blue Dog Coalition|date=December 17, 2009|accessdate=December 17, 2009}}</ref>

;Abortion

Representative Schrader is pro-choice and has gotten a 100 rating from both Planned Parenthood and the NARAL (National Abortion and Reproductive Rights Action League).<ref name="votesmart.org">http://votesmart.org/candidate/evaluations/10813/kurt-schrader#.UKrmkOOe9yc</ref> In May 2012, Representative Schrader opposed and voted against the Prenatal Nondiscrimination Act of 2012, introduced by Republican Representative Trent Franks of Arizona.<ref>http://schrader.house.gov/schrader-in-the-news/schrader-says-he-tries-to-be-a-centrist-the-salem-statesman-journal-june-13-2012/</ref> The bill proposed imposing criminal penalties for giving abortions in special cases, notably when based on gender, race or color of the child or parent.<ref>http://www.govtrack.us/congress/bills/112/hr3541</ref>

;The Environment

Representative Schrader has received a score of 66% from the Environment America and an 83% from the Sierra Club for his position on Clean Water.<ref name="votesmart.org"/> The League of Conservation Voters gave him a score of 93. Representative Schrader is a strong supporter of alternative energy. He, along with Oregon Senators Ron Wyden and Jeff Merkley, helped award a grant in September 2012, from the Department of Energy to the Northwest National Marine Renewable Energy Center (NNMREC). The grant will be used to create a new ocean energy test facility to test wave energy. “The expansion of Oregon State University’s wave energy technology research furthers our tradition of blazing the trail to a secure energy future,” Schrader said of the grant.<ref>http://schrader.house.gov/press-releases/wyden-merkley-schrader-announce-wave-energy-grant/</ref>

;Health Care

Representative Schrader received a 100% rating from the Children’s Health Fund. In 2010 he received a 100% from American Public Health Association.<ref name="votesmart.org"/> He firmly supports the Affordable Care Act. Schrader is the Co-Chair (along with Pennsylvania Representative Allyson Schwartz) of the New Dem Health Care Task Force, which set forth an agenda of increasing the health care system efficiency to “more effectively implement health care policy in this country that improves payment and delivery systems” Representative Schrader said. The New Democrats introduced legislation to improve research for doctors and nurses to give them tools to make the best decisions for their patients. Under the leadership of Representative Schrader, this legislation was included in the Affordable Care Act.<ref>http://schrader.house.gov/health-care-resource-center/congressman-schrader-releases-new-dem-priorities-to-support-innovation-in-our-health-care-system1/</ref>

===Legislation===
Schrader coauthored the [[Veterinary Medicine Mobility Act of 2014 (H.R. 1528; 113th Congress)]], a bill that would amend the [[Controlled Substances Act]] to clarify that [[veterinarian]]s are not required to have separate registrations to dispense [[controlled substances]] outside of their principal place of business, such as when treating animals on a [[farm]].<ref name=1528sum>{{cite web|title=H.R. 1528 - Summary|url=https://beta.congress.gov/bill/113th-congress/house-bill/1528|publisher=United States Congress|accessdate=7 July 2014}}</ref><ref name=cbo1528>{{cite web|title=CBO - H.R. 1528|url=http://www.cbo.gov/publication/45287|publisher=Congressional Budget Office|accessdate=7 July 2014}}</ref><ref name=HSVMAsupport>{{cite web|last1=Kellogg|first1=Barry|title=Protect Mobile Veterinary Services and Public Health and Safety: Support the Veterinary Medicine Mobility Act|url=http://www.hsvma.org/veterinary_medicine_mobility_act_051513?utm_medium=hsvmaweb&utm_campaign=advocacy|publisher=Humane Society Veterinary Medicine Association|accessdate=8 July 2014|date=15 May 2013}}</ref>
