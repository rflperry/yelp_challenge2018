'''Keith James Rothfus''' (born April 25, 1962) is the [[United States House of Representatives|U.S. Representative]] for [[Pennsylvania's 12th congressional district]]. He succeeded [[Democratic Party (United States)|Democrat]] [[Mark Critz]], whom he defeated in the [[United States House of Representatives elections, 2012|2012 election]].<ref>{{cite web|last=Cernetich|first=Kelly|title=Critz Concedes Race to Rothfus|url=http://www.politicspa.com/critz-concedes-race-to-rothfus/44075/|publisher=PoliticsPA|accessdate=7 November 2012}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=Rothfus wins seat in Pa.'s redrawn 12th Congressional District|url=http://www.wpxi.com/news/news/rothfus-wins-seat-pas-redrawn-12th-congressional-d/nSy5k/|publisher=WPXI|accessdate=7 November 2012}}</ref> Prior to being elected to Congress, he worked as a [[lawyer]].

==Early life and education==
Keith Rothfus was born in 1962 in [[Endicott, New York]]. He graduated from [[West Seneca West Senior High School]] in 1980. He attended the [[State University of New York College at Buffalo]] for his bachelor's degree in [[Information Systems]]. He later earned his Juris Doctorate from the [[University of Notre Dame Law School]] in 1994.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://ballotpedia.org/wiki/index.php/Keith_Rothfus |title=Keith Rothfus |publisher=Ballotpedia |date= |accessdate=2012-11-18}}</ref>

==Law career==
Throughout most of his adult life he has been a corporate attorney, working for private companies big and small. He was employed by the [[United States Department of Homeland Security]] from 2006&mdash;2007. He has also been a member of the [[Edgeworth, PA]] [[Zoning Board]]. He also serves on the Board of Directors of the [[Veterans Leadership Program]] (VLP) of Western Pennsylvania.<ref name=bio/><ref>{{cite web|url=http://keithrothfus.com/who-is-keith-rothfus |title=The Keith You Know |publisher=keithrothfus.com |date= |accessdate=2012-11-18}}</ref>

==U.S. House of Representatives==

===Elections===
;2010
{{Main|United States House of Representatives elections in Pennsylvania, 2010#District 4}}
Rothfus decided to run for congress in [[Pennsylvania's 4th congressional district]]. In the Republican primary, he defeated U.S. Attorney [[Mary Beth Buchanan]] 65%-35%.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=494772 |title=PA - District 04 - R Primary Race - May 18, 2010 |publisher=Our Campaigns |date= |accessdate=2012-11-18}}</ref> Rothfus challenged Democrat U.S. Congressman [[Jason Altmire]] and lost 51%-49%.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=493126 |title=PA - District 04 Race - Nov 02, 2010 |publisher=Our Campaigns |date= |accessdate=2012-11-18}}</ref><ref name=bio1>{{cite news|last=Schmitz|first=Jon and McNulty, Timothy|title=Rothfus upsets Critz in bitter battle for U.S. House|url=http://www.post-gazette.com/stories/news/politics-state/rothfus-upsets-critz-in-bitter-battle-660971/|accessdate=8 November 2012|newspaper=Pittsburgh Post-Gazette|date=7 November 2012}}</ref>

;2012
{{Main|United States House of Representatives elections in Pennsylvania, 2012#District 12}}
Rothfus ran for U.S. House again in 2012. This time, he decided to run in the newly redrawn [[Pennsylvania's 12th congressional district]]. He won the Republican primary unopposed and faced incumbent Democrat [[Mark Critz]] in the general election, who had defeated fellow Democratic incumbent [[Jason Altmire]] in the Democratic primary. Rothfus led Critz in fundraising for the second half of 2012. It was a highly competitive election. Outside groups spent nearly $10 million.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.njherald.com/story/20022902/w-pa-house-race-seen-as-a-key-congressional-race|title=New Jersey Herald - GOP's Rothfus beats incumbent Critz in W. Pa. race|publisher=Njherald.com|accessdate=2012-11-18}}</ref>

Rothfus defeated Critz 52%-48%.<ref name=bio1/><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.electionreturns.state.pa.us/ElectionsInformation.aspx?FunctionID=13&ElectionID=53&OfficeID=11|title=Commonwealth of PA - Elections Information |publisher=Electionreturns.state.pa.us|date=2012-11-06 |accessdate=2012-11-18}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.politico.com/2012-election/map/#/House/2012/PA|title=2012 Election Results Map by State - Live Voting Updates|publisher=Politico.Com|accessdate=2012-11-18}}</ref> He took office when the [[113th United States Congress|113th Congress]] convened on January 3, 2013.

;2014
{{Main|United States House of Representatives elections in Pennsylvania, 2014#District 12}}
Rothfus successfully ran for re-election to a second full term in the U.S. House in 2014. He was re-nominated in the May 20 Republican primary and faced Democratic nominee Erin McClelland in the general election. He defeated McClelland 59%-41%, winning a second term.<ref>{{cite web|title=2014 Pennsylvania House Election Results|url=http://www.politico.com/2014-election/results/map/house/pennsylvania/#.VFoQZPnF9qU|publisher=Politico|accessdate=5 November 2014}}</ref>
