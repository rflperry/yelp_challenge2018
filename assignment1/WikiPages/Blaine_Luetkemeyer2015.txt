'''Blaine Luetkemeyer''' ({{IPAc-en|ˈ|l|uː|t|k|əm|aɪər}}; born May 7, 1952) is the [[United States House of Representatives|U.S. Representative]] for {{ushr|MO|3}}. He represented {{ushr|MO|9}} from 2009 to 2013, until that district was eliminated as a result of the [[2010 United States Census|2010 Census]]. The district contains most of Northeastern Missouri. Luetkemeyer is a member of the [[Republican Party (United States)|Republican Party]].

==Early life, education, and pre-political career==
Luetkemeyer was born in [[Jefferson City, Missouri]] on May 7, 1952.<ref name=washpost>{{cite web| title = Members of Congress: Blaine Luetkemeyer| publisher = The Washington Post| url = http://projects.washingtonpost.com/congress/members/l000569/| accessdate = 2 January 2011}}</ref> He attended [[Lincoln University of Missouri|Lincoln University]] in Jefferson City and graduated with a [[Bachelor of Arts]] degree in [[political science]] and with a minor in [[business administration]]. A lifelong farmer who is the fourth generation of his family to own their farm, Luetkemeyer has also owned several small businesses, as well as running a bank and serving as an insurance agent. He also served on the Board of Trustees for the village of [[St. Elizabeth, Missouri|St. Elizabeth]].

==Missouri state politics==
In 1998, Luetkemeyer was elected to the [[Missouri House of Representatives]] from the 115th district.<ref>http://sos.mo.gov/archives/history/historicallistings/molegl.asp</ref> During his time as a state representative, Luetkemeyer served as chair of the Financial Services Committee and as House Republican Caucus Chairman. During his time in the state legislature, Luetkemeyer co-sponsored the statewide constitutional amendment defining marriage as being between a man and a woman, which was overwhelmingly approved by Missouri voters statewide in 2004 by a margin of 71-29. He also worked on legislation which allowed Missourians to carry concealed firearms, banned partial-birth abortions, and reformed worker compensation laws.

In 2004, he did not seek reelection but instead was one of seven Republicans who ran for the office of [[Missouri State Treasurer|State Treasurer]]. He finished second in the Republican primary, losing to [[Sarah Steelman]] who went on to win the general election. In 2005, Luetkemeyer was appointed by former [[Governor of Missouri|Governor]] [[Matt Blunt]] to serve as Missouri Tourism Director, a post he held until running for the [[U.S. House of Representatives]] in 2008. One of his projects was working with Blunt and [[Lieutenant Governor of Missouri|Lt. Governor]] [[Peter Kinder]] to start the Tour of Missouri, a [[cycling]] event modeled on the [[Tour de France]]. {{cn|date=December 2013}}

===Legislation===
On October 23, 2013, Luetkemeyer introduced the bill [[To enhance the ability of community financial institutions to foster economic growth and serve their communities, boost small businesses, increase individual savings (H.R. 3329; 113th Congress)]] into the House.<ref name=3329sum>{{cite web|title=H.R. 3329 - Summary|url=http://beta.congress.gov/bill/113th-congress/house-bill/3329|publisher=United States Congress|accessdate=4 May 2014}}</ref> The bill would direct the [[Federal Reserve System|Federal Reserve]] to revise certain regulations related to small [[bank holding companies]] (BHCs).<ref name=cbo3329>{{cite web|title=CBO - H.R. 3329|url=http://cbo.gov/publication/45134|publisher=Congressional Budget Office|accessdate=4 May 2014}}</ref><ref name=TuesdayHouse6>{{cite news|title=Tuesday: House reforms Dodd-Frank, Senate debates energy bill|url=http://thehill.com/blogs/floor-action/senate/205264-tuesday-house-reforms-dodd-frank-senate-debates-energy-bill|accessdate=6 May 2014|newspaper=The Hill|date=6 May 2014|author=Cristina Marcos|author2=Ramsey Cox}}</ref> Current regulations allow BHCs with assets of less than $500 million that satisfy other tests to incur higher amounts of debt than larger institutions in order to acquire other banks.<ref name="cbo3329"/> H.R. 3329 would apply the less-stringent standard to more BHCs by raising the asset limit to $1 billion, and the bill also would allow savings and loan holding companies to qualify.<ref name="cbo3329"/>

On June 26 2014, Luetkemeyer introduced H.R.4986, that would end the controversial [[Operation Choke Point]]<ref name=HR4986>{{cite web|title=H.R.4986 - End Operation Choke Point Act of 2014|url=https://www.congress.gov/bill/113th-congress/house-bill/4986|website=113th Congress (2013-2014)|publisher=[[United States Congress]]|accessdate=24 November 2014}}</ref> which was designed to limit the activities of money launderers, but has come under criticism for alleged abuse.<ref name=Raasch-11-14>{{cite news|last1=Raasch|first1=Chuck|title=Luetkemeyer says feds to investigate 'Operation Choke Point'|url=http://www.stltoday.com/news/local/govt-and-politics/luetkemeyer-says-feds-to-investigate-operation-choke-point/article_92390dd9-3d35-5818-8ec1-71ea9f85b5c4.html|accessdate=22 November 2014|publisher=[[St. Louis Post-Dispatch]]|date=14 November 2014}}</ref>     Later, on November 20, 2014, in a further effort to end Operation Choke Point,  Luetkemeyer introduced additional legislation that would require federal banking agencies to put in writing any suggestion or order to terminate a customer’s banking account.<ref name=Leut-PR-11-24-14>{{cite web|title=Luetkemeyer Introduces Legislation to Protect Customers from Operation Choke Point|url=http://luetkemeyer.house.gov/news/documentsingle.aspx?DocumentID=398497|accessdate=24 November 2014}}</ref><ref name=hr5758>{{cite web|title=H.R.5758 - Financial Institution Customer Protection Act of 2014|url=https://www.congress.gov/bill/113th-congress/house-bill/5758/text?q=%7B%22search%22%3A%5B%22hr5758%22%5D%7D|accessdate=24 November 2014|website=113th Congress (2013-2014)|publisher=[[United States Congress]]}}</ref>

==Personal life==
Luetkemeyer has been married since 1976 to his wife Jackie. They have three children. He has one granddaughter, Riley, and two grandsons, Luke and Evan.  Luetkemeyer is a member of the [[Knights of Columbus]], the Eldon Chamber of Commerce, the Farm Bureau, the [[National Rifle Association]], and attends St. Lawrence Catholic Church. <ref name=official-bio>{{cite web|title=Biography - Congressman Blaine Luetkemeyer|url=http://luetkemeyer.house.gov/biography/|website=The Office of Blaine Leutkemeyer|publisher=[[United States House of Representatives]]|accessdate=24 November 2014}}</ref>
