'''Adam Daniel Kinzinger'''<ref>[http://www.legistorm.com/memberbio/2749/Rep_Adam_Kinzinger_IL.html]</ref> (born February 27, 1978) is the [[United States House of Representatives|U.S. Representative]] for {{ushr|IL|16}}. He is a member of the [[Republican Party (United States)|Republican Party]].

==Early life, education, and early political career==
Kinzinger was born in 1978 in [[Kankakee, Illinois|Kankakee]], but he was raised in [[Bloomington, Illinois]]. He graduated from [[Normal Community West High School]] in 1996 and earned a bachelor's degree from [[Illinois State University]] in 2000. He served{{when|date=May 2014}} as an intern for former U.S. Senator [[Peter Fitzgerald (senator)|Peter Fitzgerald]] as part of a program offered at [[Illinois State University|Illinois State]].{{fact|date=May 2014}}

In 2003, Kinzinger joined the [[United States Air Force]]. He was commissioned a [[2nd Lieutenant]] in November 2003 and later awarded his [[Aircrew Badge|pilot wings]]. Kinzinger was initially a [[Boeing KC-135 Stratotanker|KC-135 Stratotanker]] pilot and flew missions in [[South America]], [[Guam]], [[Iraq]] and [[Afghanistan]]. He later switched to flying the [[RC-26B Metroliner|RC-26]] [[surveillance aircraft]] and was stationed in Iraq twice.<ref>http://www.rrstar.com/insight/x2079019554/Chuck-Sweeny-Manzullo-challenger-Kinzinger-got-politics-bug-early</ref> Kinzinger has served in the [[Air Force Special Operations Command]], [[Air Combat Command]], [[Air Mobility Command]], and [[Air National Guard]] and was progressively promoted to his current rank of [[Major]].<ref>[http://electadam.com/inner.asp?z=1 Adam Kinzinger's military service profile]</ref>

Kinzinger served as a County Board member in [[McLean County, Illinois]] from 1998 to 2003. Kinzinger defeated an incumbent County Board member in 1998 while still a student at Illinois State. Kinzinger served in that role until 2003, when he decided to serve full-time in the [[U.S. Air Force]].<ref>[http://www.stoptheaclu.com/archives/2009/03/03/adam-kinzinger-for-illinois-11th-hero-patriot/ "STOP the ACLU" Kinzinger runs for Congress, March 3, 2009]</ref>

==U.S. House of Representatives==

===Elections===
[[File:Adam Kinzginer at event.jpg|thumb|left|200px|Kinzinger at a campaign event.]]
;2010
{{main|United States House of Representatives elections in Illinois, 2010#District 11*}}
In January 2009, Kinzinger met with Republican U.S. Congressmen [[Mike Pence]], [[Mark Kirk]], and [[Peter Roskam]] to discuss a possible run for Congress.<ref name=ak>[http://www.rollcall.com/issues/54_74/atr/31446-1.html "Roll Call" Illinois:First GOPer Lines Up to Take Out Halvorson, January 20, 2009]</ref> Kinzinger decided to run in [[Illinois' 11th congressional district]], held by [[Democratic Party (U.S.)|Democratic]] U.S. Congresswoman [[Debbie Halvorson]]. He started campaigning full-time in May 2009, when he returned home from his 3rd tour in Iraq. In the early autumn, Kinzinger held eight town halls across the 11th Congressional District that were attended by nearly 2,000 people. He was endorsed by former [[Alaska]] Governor [[Sarah Palin]]. On February 2, 2010, Kinzinger won the five-candidate Republican primary with 64% of the vote.<ref>http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=512637</ref>

In the general election, he was endorsed by the ''[[Chicago Tribune]]'' and the ''[[Chicago Sun-Times]]''. On November 2, 2010, Kinzinger defeated Halvorson 57%–43%.<ref>http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=489035</ref>

;2012
{{main|United States House of Representatives elections in Illinois, 2012#District 16}}
After redistricting, he decided to run in the newly redrawn [[Illinois' 16th congressional district]]. He faced fellow U.S. Congressman [[Don Manzullo]], a 67 year old politician first elected in 1992, in the March primary. Kinzinger currently represents 31% of the district, while Manzullo has represented at least 44% of the district in the past ten years. Kinzinger defeated the longtime incumbent 56%–44%.<ref>http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=734301</ref> In the general election, he defeated Democrat Wanda Rohl and won re-election to a second term, 62%–38%.<ref>http://www.politico.com/2012-election/map/#/House/2012/IL</ref>

===Tenure===
Kinzinger sponsored the [[Veteran Emergency Medical Technician Support Act of 2013]], H.R. 235 in the [[113th United States Congress|113th Congress]].<ref name=235overview>{{cite web|title=H.R. 235 - Congress.gov|url=http://beta.congress.gov/bill/113th-congress/house-bill/235|publisher=United States Congress|accessdate=April 1, 2013}}</ref> This legislation would make it easier for veterans with emergency medical technician training in the military to get civilian licenses to perform the same job outside of the military. The bill passed the House of Representatives by a [[voice vote]] on February 12, 2013 and was referred to the [[United States Senate]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Bill Summary & Status - H.R. 235 - All Congressional Actions|url=http://thomas.loc.gov/cgi-bin/bdquery/z?d113:H.R.235:@@@X|publisher=Library of Congress|accessdate=April 1, 2013}}</ref>

On June 5, 2014, Kinzinger introduced the bill [[To require the Secretary of Energy to prepare a report on the impact of thermal insulation on both energy and water use for potable hot water (H.R. 4801; 113th Congress)]], which would require the [[United States Secretary of Energy]] to prepare a report on the effects that [[thermal insulation]] has on both energy consumption and systems for providing [[potable water]] in [[:Category:Buildings of the United States government|federal buildings]].<ref name=cbo4801>{{cite web|title=CBO - H.R. 4801|url=http://cbo.gov/publication/45466|publisher=Congressional Budget Office|accessdate=23 June 2014}}</ref><ref name=ASEcommitteepass>{{cite web|last1=LaFreniere|first1=Kelsey|title=Alliance Vice-Chair Rep. Kinzinger Pushes For Energy Efficiency|url=https://www.ase.org/blog/alliance-vice-chair-rep-kinzinger-pushes-energy-efficiency|publisher=Alliance to Save Energy|accessdate=23 June 2014|date=11 June 2014}}</ref> Kinzinger argued that "with the federal government being the single largest consumer of energy in the country, doing our best to maximize the potential savings from improved insulation systems is a commonsense step I think everybody can agree on."<ref name="ASEcommitteepass"/>

In 2010 Kinzinger signed a pledge sponsored by [[Americans for Prosperity]] promising to vote against any Global Warming legislation that would raise taxes.<ref>http://americansforprosperity.org/noclimatetax//wp-content/uploads/2010/01/kinzinger.pdf</ref>

Kinzinger is a member of both the [[Republican Study Committee]] and [[Republican Main Street Partnership]].

==Personal life==
In 2006, the Wisconsin Red Cross named Kinzinger "Hero of the Year" for wrestling a knife-wielding man to the ground and disarming him. The man had cut the throat of a woman on a street in downtown [[Milwaukee, Wisconsin]].<ref>[http://www.pantagraph.com/articles/2006/08/18/news/doc44e4dec184663319987097.txt ''Bloomington Pantagraph'', "Former local board member stops knife attack", August 17, 2006]</ref> Recalling the event in an ABC Milwaukee interview, Kinzinger stated "The whole time it was, to me, kind of a done deal that I was going to get stabbed in the process, but I knew that this wasn't something I could wake up to ... everyday with that memory that I watched her die."<ref>{{cite web|author=&ldquo;&rdquo;|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=4TvCyQasadw|title=Adam Kinzinger saves woman's life/Milwaukee TV report|publisher=YouTube|date=2010-06-22|accessdate=2010-08-23}}</ref> The woman survived. For this act Kinzinger also received the United States Air Force [[Airman's Medal]] and the National Guard's Valley Forge Cross for Heroism.
