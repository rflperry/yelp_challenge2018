'''Thomas Evert Petri''' (born May 28, 1940) is the [[United States House of Representatives|U.S. Representative]] for {{ushr|WI|6}}, serving since 1979. He is a member of the [[Republican Party (United States)|Republican Party]]. The district includes most of the east-central part of the state. On April 11, 2014, Petri announced he would retire after his current term ends in January 2015.<ref>http://www.businessweek.com/ap/2014-04-11/longtime-wis-dot-gop-rep-dot-tom-petri-to-retire</ref>

==Early life, education, and early career==
Born in [[Marinette, Wisconsin]] of German and Norwegian ancestry,<ref>http://freepages.genealogy.rootsweb.ancestry.com/~battle/reps/petri.htm</ref> he graduated from [[Fond du Lac High School|Goodrich High School]] in [[Fond du Lac, Wisconsin]]. Petri then attended [[Harvard University]], where he received his bachelors of arts and law degrees. He served as a volunteer with the [[Peace Corps]].<ref>[http://www.wisconsinhistory.org/dictionary/index.asp?action=view&term_id=1933&keyword=petri Thomas Petri, Wisconsin Historical Society]</ref>

==Early political career==

===Wisconsin Senate===
Petri served in the [[Wisconsin State Senate]] from 1973 to 1979.<ref>'Wisconsin Blue Book 1979-1980, Biographical Sketch of Thomas Petri, pg. 15</ref>

===1974 U.S. Senate election===
{{See also|United States Senate election in Wisconsin, 1974}}
Petri ran for the [[United States Senate|U.S. Senate]] in 1974. He won the Republican primary with 85% of the vote.<ref>http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=336237</ref> In the general election, incumbent Democrat U.S. Senator [[Gaylord Nelson]] defeated Petri 62%-36%. Petri only won five counties in the state.<ref>http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=6359</ref><ref>'Wisconsin Blue Book 1975,' pg. 779, 815</ref>
In 1977-78, Petri made a run for governor for Wisconsin, but lost his party's convention nomination to Bob Kasten in 1978. Kasten lost the Republican primary to Lee Sherman Dreyfus, who won the general election over incumbent Marty Schreiber.

==U.S. House of Representatives==

===Elections===
In 1979, he won a special election to finish the term of the late U.S. Congressman [[William A. Steiger]], who had died shortly after being re-elected in 1978. He won the seat in his own right in 1980 and has been reelected 16 times. He has only won re-election with less than 64% once, in 1992. That year, he defeated State Representative [[Peg Lautenschlager]] 53% to 47%. He won ten of the district's thirteen counties. He lost [[Manitowoc County, Wisconsin|Manitowoc]], [[Brown County, Wisconsin|Brown]], and [[Outagamie County, Wisconsin|Outagamie]] counties.<ref>http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=28468</ref> He has run unopposed in 1990, 1994, 2002, and 2006. He faced no major-party opposition in 1986 and 1998. In April 2014, Petri announced he will not seek re-election in November 2014.<ref>'U.S. Rep. Tom Petri won't seek re-election,' '''Milwaukee Sentinel Journal,''' Jason Stein, Patrick Marley, Don Walker, April 11, 2014</ref>

===Tenure===
Petri is a member of The [[Republican Main Street Partnership]] and supports [[stem-cell research]], although he generally opposes [[abortion]]. Although he also has called for a moratorium on the [[capital punishment in the United States|death penalty]], he has voted against other restrictions on it.<ref name="Tom Petri on the Issues">{{cite web | url = http://ontheissues.org/House/Tom_Petri.htm | title = Tom Petri on the Issues | publisher = OnTheIssues.org | accessdate = 2008-07-05}}</ref> Petri is a member of the moderate <ref>{{cite web | url = http://www.nationalreview.com/article/351534/tuesday-group-still-lives-jonathan-strong | title = The Tuesday Group Still Lives | publisher = National Review Online | accessdate = 2014-03-10}}</ref> Republican conference, [[The Tuesday Group (of moderate Republican congressmen)|The Tuesday Group]], and received a $10,000 contribution from The Tuesday Group in 2008 <ref>[http://www.opensecrets.org/pacs/expenditures.php?cmte=C00433060&cycle=2008 Tuesday Group PAC Expenditures] OpenSecrets.org. Accessed January 20, 2012</ref> as well as a $5,000 contribution in the 2012 cycle.<ref name="OpenSecrets"/>

On transportation issues, Petri was the author and sponsor of three surface transportation laws. He supported the laws that eliminated the 55-miles-per-hour speed limit and got rid of the Interstate Commerce Commission.<ref>{{cite news|last=Martin|first=Aaron|title=Petri wonâ€™t seek re-election|url=http://riponadvance.com/news/petri-wont-seek-re-election/7317|accessdate=16 April 2014|newspaper=The Ripon Advance|date=15 April 2014}}</ref> 

Petri's three largest contributors in the 2012 campaign cycle were labor unions,<ref name="OpenSecrets">[http://www.opensecrets.org/politicians/contrib.php?cycle=2012&cid=N00004426&type=I&newmem=N Top 20 Contributors - Representative Tom Petri 2011 - 2012] OpenSecrets.org. Accessed January 17, 2012</ref> and Petri has often voted with labor unions on closely contested issues.{{cn|date=December 2014}} Petri has repeatedly voted for [[Project Labor Agreement]]s (PLAs),<ref>[http://clerk.house.gov/evs/2011/roll396.xml FINAL VOTE RESULTS FOR ROLL CALL 396] House Clerk. June 3, 2011. Accessed January 14, 2012</ref> and twice cast a deciding vote to allow the use of PLAs in government contracts.<ref>[http://clerk.house.gov/evs/2011/roll126.xml FINAL VOTE RESULTS FOR ROLL CALL 126] House Clerk. February 19, 2011. Accessed January 14, 2012</ref><ref>[http://clerk.house.gov/evs/2011/roll413.xml FINAL VOTE RESULTS FOR ROLL CALL 413] House Clerk. June 13, 2011. Accessed January 14, 2012</ref><ref>[http://culberson.house.gov/red-state-dozens-of-republicans-vote-for-handouts-to-big-labor/ Red State: Dozens of Republicans Vote for Handouts to Big Labor] June 14, 2011. Accessed January 14, 2012</ref>{{failed verification|date=December 2014}} Petri also voted to permit the use of taxpayer funds to comply with the [[Davis-Bacon Act]],<ref>[http://clerk.house.gov/evs/2011/roll395.xml FINAL VOTE RESULTS FOR ROLL CALL 395] House Clerk. June 2, 2011. Accessed January 17, 2012</ref> voted to use federal funds for collective bargaining by the Transportation Security Administration,<ref>[http://clerk.house.gov/evs/2011/roll403.xml FINAL VOTE RESULTS FOR ROLL CALL 403] House Clerk. June 2, 2011. Accessed January 17, 2012</ref> and voted in favor of $233 million in taxpayer funding for the [[National Labor Relations Board]].<ref>[http://clerk.house.gov/evs/2011/roll075.xml FINAL VOTE RESULTS FOR ROLL CALL 75] House Clerk. February 17, 2011. Accessed January 17, 2012</ref>

In 1994, Petri introduced H.R. 4469, titled "The Multicare Act of 1994", which would have greatly expanded the government's role in healthcare.{{cn|date=December 2014}} This bill would have established federally funded government-run health insurance programs, and would have authorized states to require an individual to purchase insurance from one of these government-run insurance plans.<ref>[http://www.govtrack.us/congress/bills/103/hr4469 H.R. 4469 (103rd): Multicare Act of 1994] GovTrack.us. Accessed June 24, 2012</ref> Petri continued to push for the passage of Multicare until 2004.<ref>http://web.archive.org/web/20040607185534/http://www.petripeople.org/issues.htm</ref>

In 2005, Petri voted to fund the "[[Gravina Island Bridge|bridge to nowhere]],"<ref>[http://clerk.house.gov/evs/2005/roll065.xml FINAL VOTE RESULTS FOR ROLL CALL 65] House Clerk. March 10, 2005. Accessed January 14, 2012</ref> and voted to prevent the [[Arctic Refuge drilling controversy|drilling for oil in ANWR]].<ref>[http://clerk.house.gov/evs/2005/roll122.xml FINAL VOTE RESULTS FOR ROLL CALL 122] House Clerk. April 20, 2005. Accessed January 14, 2012</ref>

On November 2, 2005, Petri voted against the Online Freedom of Speech Act.<ref>[http://clerk.house.gov/evs/2005/roll559.xml FINAL VOTE RESULTS FOR ROLL CALL 559] House Clerk. November 2, 2005. Accessed January 14, 2012</ref>

On January 18, 2007 Petri voted in favor of HR 6, which made it more difficult to obtain a lease to drill for oil domestically.<ref>[http://clerk.house.gov/evs/2007/roll040.xml FINAL VOTE RESULTS FOR ROLL CALL 40] House Clerk. January 18, 2007. Accessed June 27, 2012</ref>

On August 1, 2011, Petri voted for the [[Budget Control Act of 2011]] which raised the nation's debt limit and included the creation of the "[[United States Congress Joint Select Committee on Deficit Reduction|super committee]]" that was empowered to produce legislation that would be immune from amendments or filibuster.<ref>[http://clerk.house.gov/evs/2011/roll690.xml FINAL VOTE RESULTS FOR ROLL CALL 690] House Clerk. August 1, 2011. Accessed April 5, 2012</ref>

In 2011, Petri sponsored an amendment to allow Michigan based Badger Ferry to dump more than 500 tons of coal ash a year into Lake Michigan.<ref>[http://www.jsonline.com/news/wisconsin/groups-oppose-badger-ferrys-effort-for-pollution-exemption-ug2vln8-133503043.html Groups oppose Badger ferry's effort for pollution exemption] Bergquest, Lee. ''[[The Milwaukee Journal Sentinel]]'' November 8, 2011. Accessed January 20, 2012</ref> He received $14,751 in campaign donations from executives of Lake Michigan Carferry,<ref>[http://query.nictusa.com/cgi-bin/can_ind/H0WI06061/A-E/ Individuals Who Gave To: PETRI, THOMAS - Whose Last Name Begins With 'A-E'] FEC.gov. Accessed January 20, 2012</ref><ref>[http://query.nictusa.com/cgi-bin/can_ind/H0WI06061/K-O/ Individuals Who Gave To: PETRI, THOMAS - Whose Last Name Begins With 'K-O'] FEC.gov. Accessed January 20, 2012</ref> the owner of Badger Ferry.

In 2012, Petri inroduced H.R. 4148, titled "Fox-Wisconsin Heritage Parkway National Heritage Area Act of 2012", which would place 1,444 square miles of land in Wisconsin under the control of the federal government.<ref>[http://www.govtrack.us/congress/bills/112/hr4148/text H.R. 4148: Fox-Wisconsin Heritage Parkway National Heritage Area Act of 2012] GovTrack.us. Accessed June 24, 2012</ref>

Petri endorsed [[Mitt Romney]] in the 2008 GOP presidential primary.<ref>{{cite press release | url = http://www.newsbull.com/forum/topic.asp?TOPIC_ID=51040 | publisher = Mitt Romney Campaign | date = 2007-08-27 | title =  U.S. Representative Tom Petri Endorses Governor Mitt Romney | accessdate = 2008-07-05}}</ref> Petri subsequently endorsed [[John McCain]] in the [[United States presidential election, 2008|2008 presidential election]].

Petri was honored by [[U.S. English (organization)|U.S. English, Inc.]] in May 2008 for his votes and co-sponsorships of official English legislation in the 110th Congress.<ref>{{cite news | url = http://www.fdlreporter.com/apps/pbcs.dll/article?AID=/20080516/FON0101/805160430/-1/archive | title = Rep. Tom Petri recognized for dedication to English language | publisher = Fond Du Lac Reporter  | date = 2008-05-16 | accessdate = 2008-07-05}} {{Dead link|date=September 2010|bot=H3llBot}}</ref> Petri had previously sponsored legislation declaring English as an official language in 1999.<ref name="Tom Petri on the Issues"/>

In 2005, Petri introduced the Direct Loan Reward Act,<ref>{{cite news | url = http://peacecorpsonline.org/messages/messages/467/2028192.html | last = Mathews | first = Charlie | publisher =  Herald Times | title =  Tom Petri Touted his Direct Loan Reward Act | accessdate = 2008-07-05 | date = 2005-02-07}}</ref> and in 2006 introduced the Student Aid Reward (STAR) Act.<ref>{{cite web | url =  http://peacecorpsonline.org/messages/messages/467/2093057.html | title = Petri receives education honor | date = 2007-03-13}}</ref>

Petri has pushed for additional federal spending for [[Oshkosh Corporation]] while he bought stock in the company.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.defensenews.com/article/20140208/DEFREG02/302080023 |title=Lawmaker Holds Stock in Defense Contractor He Champions |last1=SLACK |first1=DONOVAN |date=8 February 2014 |website=www.defensenews.com |publisher=Gannett Government Media |accessdate=8 February 2014}} </ref> The third-most-senior Republican representative is under additional ethics violations scrutiny by the [[Office of Congressional Ethics]] in regard to another Wisconsin-based business, the Manitowoc Company, in which he owns a sizable financial interest.<ref>House, Billy (September 30, 2014). "Ethics Committee will Continue investigation of Tom Petri" National Journal.
