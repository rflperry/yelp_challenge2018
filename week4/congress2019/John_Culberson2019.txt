'''John Abney Culberson''' (born August 24, 1956) is an American attorney and politician who has served as the [[United States House of Representatives|U.S. Representative]] for {{ushr|TX|7}} since 2001. He is a member of the [[Republican Party (United States)|Republican Party]]. The district takes in large portions of western [[Houston]] and surrounding [[Harris County, Texas|Harris County]]. He is a member of the [[United States House Committee on Appropriations]].

==Early life, education, and career==
Culberson was born in Houston, the son of Eleanor (née Abney) and James Vincent Culberson.<ref name=frps1>{{cite web |url=http://freepages.genealogy.rootsweb.ancestry.com/~battle/reps/culberson.htm|title=culberson|work=ancestry.com}}</ref> His great-grandmother was Swedish.<ref name=frps1/> Culberson attended [[Lamar High School (Houston)|Lamar High School]].<ref>{{cite web|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20060329055851/http://www.culberson.house.gov/bio.aspx |archivedate=2006-03-29|url=http://www.culberson.house.gov/bio.aspx |title=Biography|publisher=U.S. Congressman John Culberson, 7th District of Texas}}</ref><ref name="HISDAlumni">"[http://www.houstonisd.org/HISDConnectDS/v/index.jsp?vgnextoid=c3783acb02efc010VgnVCM10000052147fa6RCRD Distinguished HISD Alumni] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120515061020/http://www.houstonisd.org/HISDConnectDS/v/index.jsp?vgnextoid=c3783acb02efc010VgnVCM10000052147fa6RCRD |date=May 15, 2012 }}," ''[[Houston Independent School District]]''</ref> He graduated from [[Southern Methodist University]] (SMU) in 1981 with a degree in [[history]]. He earned his [[Juris Doctor]] degree from [[South Texas College of Law]] in 1989.

==Texas House of Representatives==
During his time in law school, Culberson was elected to the [[Texas House of Representatives]], serving his first term beginning in 1987. He was a member of the Republican Whip team, becoming [[Minority Whip]] in 1999 during his last term. Culberson began working for the law firm of Lorance and Thompson as a civil defense attorney after he graduated from law school.<ref>{{cite news|title=U.S. Rep. John Culberson (R)|url=https://www.texastribune.org/bidness/explore/john-culberson/|accessdate=April 13, 2018|work=Ethics Explorer |publisher=Texas Tribune|date=January 31, 2014}}</ref>

===Elections===
;2000
Culberson won the Republican nomination for the 7th District in 2000 after 15-term incumbent [[William Reynolds Archer, Jr.|Bill Archer]] announced his retirement. He finished first in the Republican primary — traditionally the real contest in what has historically been a heavily Republican district – and defeated Peter Wareing in the runoff. He won easily in November, taking about 75% of the vote.

;2008
{{See also|United States House of Representatives elections in Texas, 2008#District 7}}
In 2008, Culberson defeated businessman [[Michael Peter Skelly]] with 56% of the vote.<ref name=results>{{cite news|title=Texas' 7th Congressional District|url=https://ballotpedia.org/Texas%27_7th_Congressional_District|accessdate=April 13, 2018 |publisher=Ballotpedia}}</ref>

;2010
{{See also|United States House of Representatives elections in Texas, 2010#District 7}}
Culberson ran unopposed.

;2012
{{See also|United States House of Representatives elections in Texas, 2012#District 7}}
Culberson was challenged by the Democratic nominee James Cargas, an energy lawyer for the City of Houston, Green party nominee Lance Findley, and Libertarian Drew Parks.

;2014
{{See also|United States House of Representatives elections in Texas, 2014#District 7}}
In the November 4, 2014 general election, Culberson again defeated Democrat James Cargas, who polled 4,092 votes (62.1 percent) in the March 4 primary election.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://team1.sos.state.tx.us/enr/results/mar04_169_state.htm?x=0&y=3852&id=407 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140307081703/https://team1.sos.state.tx.us/enr/results/mar04_169_state.htm?x=0&y=3852&id=407 |dead-url=yes |archive-date=March 7, 2014 |title=Democratic primary election returns |publisher=team1.sos.state.tx.us |accessdate=March 6, 2014 }}</ref> Culberson was unopposed in the Republican primary.

;2016
{{See also|United States House of Representatives elections in Texas, 2016#District 7}}
Culberson defeated James Lloyd and Maria Espinoza in the Republican primary election on March 1.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.harrisvotes.com/HISTORY/20160301/cumulative/Rep/cumulative.pdf|title=Republican Party Cumulative Report|date=March 1, 2016|accessdate=April 21, 2016}}</ref> Culberson polled 44,202 votes (57.3 percent) to James Lloyd's 19,182 (24.9 percent) and the third candidate, Maria Espinoza's 13,772 (17.8 percent).<ref>{{cite web |url=https://enrpages.sos.state.tx.us/public/mar01_273_state.htm|title=Republican primary returns|date=March 1, 2016|accessdate=March 3, 2016|deadurl=yes |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20160306124107/https://enrpages.sos.state.tx.us/public/mar01_273_state.htm |archivedate=March 6, 2016}}</ref>

He secured his eighth term in the general election held on November 8, when, with 143,542 votes (56.2 percent), he defeated the Democrat James Cargas (born 1966) of Houston, who garnered 111,991 ballots (43.8 percent).<ref>{{cite web |url=http://elections.sos.state.tx.us/elchist319_state.htm|title=Election Results|date=November 8, 2016|publisher=Texas Secretary of State|accessdate=December 18, 2016}}</ref>

After [[Hillary Clinton]] carried Culberson's 7th district in the [[U.S. presidential election, 2016|2016 presidential election]], Democrats began to regard the congressman as vulnerable in 2018.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bloomberg.com/view/articles/2017-05-21/the-one-thing-democrats-like-about-trump|title=The One Thing Democrats Like About Trump|date=May 21, 2017|publisher=|accessdate=December 28, 2017|via=www.bloomberg.com}}</ref>

;2018
{{See also|United States House of Representatives elections in Texas, 2018#District 7}}

Culberson defeated Edward Ziegler in the Republican primary with 76% of the vote. [[Lizzie Pannill Fletcher]] is the Democratic nominee.<ref name=results/>

==Political positions==
Culberson describes himself as a "Fiscally conservative 'Jeffersonian Republican'... committed to Thomas Jefferson's vision of limited government, individual liberty, and states' rights."<ref name=":2">{{Cite web|url=https://newrepublic.com/article/114924/meet-john-culberson-house-republican-who-said-lets-roll|title=Meet the House Republican Who Compared Himself to the Flight 93 Heroes|date=September 30, 2013|website=New Republic|access-date=February 26, 2017}}</ref>

As of April 2018, he had voted with his party in 97.6% of votes so far in the current session of Congress and voted in line with President Trump's position in 98.6% of the votes.<ref name=":0">{{Cite news |url=https://projects.propublica.org/represent/members/C001048-john-abney-culberson |title=Represent |work=ProPublica|access-date=February 26, 2017|language=en}}</ref><ref name=":1">{{Cite news|url=https://projects.fivethirtyeight.com/congress-trump-score/john-abney-culberson/|title=Tracking John Abney Culberson In The Age Of Trump|last=Bycoffe|first=Aaron|date=January 30, 2017 |work=FiveThirtyEight|access-date=February 26, 2017|language=en-US}}</ref>

===Vote Smart issue positions===
[[Project Vote Smart|Vote Smart]], a non-profit, non-partisan research organization that collects and distributes information on candidates for public office in the United States, "researched presidential and congressional candidates' public records to determine candidates' likely responses on certain key issues." According to Vote Smart's 2016 analysis, Culberson generally supports pro-life legislation, opposes an income tax increase, opposes reducing defense spending in order to balance the budget, opposes federal spending as a means of promoting economic growth, supports lowering corporate taxes as a means of promoting economic growth, opposes requiring states to adopt federal education standards, supports government funding for the development of renewable energy, opposes the federal regulation of greenhouse gas emissions, supports repealing the [[Affordable Care Act]], supports the construction of a wall along the Mexican border, and supports requiring immigrants who are unlawfully present to return to their country of origin before they are eligible for citizenship.<ref name="VoteSmart">{{cite web|title=John Culberson's Issue Positions (Political Courage Test)|url=https://votesmart.org/candidate/political-courage-test/5488/john-culberson/#.WtAU3sgh0jt|publisher=[[Vote Smart]] |accessdate=April 12, 2018}}</ref>

=== ACORN ===
Three years after the [[Association of Community Organizations for Reform Now]] (ACORN) had been dissolved, Culberson included language in an appropriations bill that said "None of the funds made available in this Act may be distributed to the Association of Community Organizations for Reform Now (ACORN) or its subsidiaries or successors."<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.huffingtonpost.com/2013/06/04/defund-acorn_n_3384060.html|title=House GOP Stomps Again On ACORN's Grave |last=Carter |first=Zach|date=June 4, 2013|website=The Huffington Post|access-date=February 26, 2017}}</ref>

===Presidential citizenship bill===
In 2009, Culberson co-sponsored [[United States presidential eligibility legislation|legislation]] which would require all future presidential candidates to provide proof of their citizenship when filing to run for office.<ref name=":2" /> The legislation, sponsored by [[Bill Posey]], was in response to [[Barack Obama citizenship conspiracy theories]] which questioned the legitimacy of [[Barack Obama]]'s birth certificate.<ref>{{cite news|last1=Sorin|first1=Hillary|title=Rep. Culberson co-sponsors "birther" bill on Capitol Hill|url=https://blog.chron.com/txpotomac/2009/07/rep-culberson-co-sponsors-birther-bill-on-capitol-hill/|accessdate=April 12, 2018|publisher=Houston Chronicle|date=July 23, 2009}}</ref>

===Guns===
In 2016, Culberson wrote a letter to Attorney General [[Loretta Lynch]] threatening to block President Obama's executive order on guns by defunding the [[United States Department of Justice]].<ref>{{cite news|last1=Devaney|first1=Tim|title=Republican eyes DOJ budget to block Obama gun orders|url=http://thehill.com/regulation/264714-house-republican-defund-doj-over-gun-executive-orders|accessdate=April 13, 2018|publisher=The Hill|date=January 4, 2016}}</ref>

=== Donald Trump ===
Culberson was the first person to endorse [[Ted Cruz]] in the [[Republican Party presidential primaries, 2016|2016 U.S. presidential primaries]].<ref>{{cite news|last1=McCabe|first1=Dave|last2=Feickert|first2=Lucy|title=Cruz nets first endorsement |url=http://thehill.com/blogs/ballot-box/presidential-races/239218-cruz-nets-first-endorsement|accessdate=April 13, 2018|publisher=The Hill|date=April 17, 2015}}</ref> In June 2016, Culberson said "I always have and always will support the Republican nominee. The party should unify behind the presumptive nominee, [[Donald Trump]], to defeat [[Hillary Clinton]]."<ref>{{cite news |last1=Livingston |first1=Abby|last2=Daniel|first2=Annie|title=Texas Republicans in Congress Not United Behind Trump |url=https://www.texastribune.org/2016/06/15/texas-congressional-delegation-endorsements/|accessdate=April 13, 2018|publisher=Texas Tribune|date=June 15, 2016}}</ref> Culberson did not comment after the release of the [[Donald Trump Access Hollywood tape]].<ref>{{cite news|last1=Svitek|first1=Patrick|title=Where Texas Republicans Stand on Donald Trump |url=https://www.texastribune.org/2016/10/09/where-texas-republicans-currently-stand-donald-tru/|accessdate=April 13, 2018 |publisher=Texas Tribune|date=October 9, 2016}}</ref>

In February 2017, he voted against a resolution that would have directed the House to request 10 years of Trump's tax returns, which would then have been reviewed by the [[United States House Committee on Ways and Means|House Ways and Means Committee]] in a closed session.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://indy100.com/article/republican-vote-donald-trump-tax-president-united-states-229-185-congress-7603851|title=These are all the Republicans who don't want you to see Donald Trump's tax returns|date=February 28, 2017|work=indy100|access-date=March 1, 2017|language=en-GB}}</ref>

Culberson supported President [[Donald Trump]]'s 2017 [[Executive Order 13769|executive order]] to suspend the refugee resettlement program and curtail immigration from seven Muslim-majority countries. He stated that "This is a necessary pause in the refugee program until our intelligence agencies can develop adequate background checks to ensure that the people coming into the country are coming in for the right reasons."<ref name="Blake1">{{cite web|url=http://www.denverpost.com/2017/01/29/republicans-on-trump-travel-ban/ |title=Coffman, Gardner join Republicans against President Trump's travel ban; here's where the rest stand|website=Denver Post |last1=Blake|first1=Aaron|accessdate=January 30, 2017}}</ref>

=== Environment ===
Culberson rejects the scientific consensus on climate change.<ref name=":4">{{Cite web|url=http://www.latimes.com/business/hiltzik/la-fi-mh-inside-gop-science-climate-change-20150304-column.html|title=Inside GOP science policy, Part 3: climate change |last=Hiltzik |first=Michael|website=latimes.com|access-date=February 26, 2017}}</ref><ref name=":5">{{Cite news |url=http://www.sciencemag.org/news/2015/01/money-chase-2016-new-head-key-house-science-spending-panel-likes-limited-government |title=The money chase, 2016: New head of key House science spending panel likes limited government, unlimited exploration|date=January 28, 2015|work=Science {{!}} AAAS|access-date=February 26, 2017}}</ref> He has alleged that scientists have falsified climate change data.<ref name=":4" /><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://culberson.house.gov/news/documentsingle.aspx?DocumentID=353161|title=Rep. Culberson Challenges Scientific Integrity of Climate Data|date=December 14, 2009|website=U.S. Congressman John Culberson|access-date=February 26, 2017}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=https://townhall.com/columnists/johnculberson/2009/12/08/hold-your-breath-texas-n1127558|title=John Culberson - Hold Your Breath Texas|last=Culberson|first=John|work=Townhall|access-date=February 26, 2017|language=en}}</ref> He has said that "the liberal obsession with climate change... is driven by their desire to raise more money for the government".<ref name=":5" /> He opposes cap-and-trade programs.<ref name=":4" />

Culberson has a lifetime score of 4% from the [[League of Conservation Voters]].<ref>{{Cite news |url=http://scorecard.lcv.org/moc/john-culberson|title=Check out Representative John Culberson's Environmental Voting Record |work=League of Conservation Voters Scorecard|access-date=February 26, 2017|language=en}}</ref>

On August 1, 2008, to protest the House going into summer recess without discussing a pending energy bill, Culberson and other House Republicans stayed to make speeches about the energy bill in question. The [[Democratic Party (United States)|Democratic]] leadership in the House, which controls services in the chamber, responded by cutting the microphones and cameras. Culberson used social media services [[Twitter]] and [[Qik]] to provide a live account of the proceedings. Culberson later compared this episode to the Iranian government's crackdown against dissidents who used Twitter to protest a restriction on foreign media in June 2009.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Zimmerman|first1=Eric|title=UPDATED: Culberson: House GOP just like Iran dissidents |url=https://web.archive.org/web/20090620190657/http://briefingroom.thehill.com:80/2009/06/17/culberson-house-gop-just-like-iran-dissidents/|accessdate=April 24, 2018|publisher=The Hill|date=June 17, 2009}}</ref>

=== Healthcare ===
Culberson opposes the [[Patient Protection and Affordable Care Act]] (Obamacare) and supports it repeal.<ref>{{cite news|last1=Svitek |first1=Patrick|title=At feisty town hall, Culberson stays course on Obamacare repeal |url=https://www.texastribune.org/2017/03/25/feisty-town-hall-culberson-doesnt-flinch-obamacare-repeal-push/|accessdate=April 13, 2018 |publisher=Texas Tribune|date=March 25, 2017}}</ref> On May 4, 2017, Culberson voted to repeal Obamacare and pass the [[American Health Care Act of 2017]].<ref name=":02">{{Cite web|url=https://www.washingtonpost.com/graphics/politics/ahca-house-vote/|title=How the House voted to pass the GOP health-care bill|website=Washington Post|access-date=May 4, 2017}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.cnn.com/2017/05/04/politics/house-health-care-vote/index.html|title=How every member voted on health care bill |last=Staff|first=C. N. N.|website=CNN|access-date=May 4, 2017}}</ref>

In 2013, Culberson said "like 9/11, 'let's roll!'" to describe a vote to make a delay of the [[Patient Protection and Affordable Care Act]] a condition for funding the government.<ref name=":2"/>

=== Science ===
Culberson marked up a 2016 spending bill to include a requirement that the [[National Science Foundation]] direct about 70% of its funding to biology, computing, engineering, and math and physical sciences. The earmarked funds would not cover geoscience and the social and behavioral sciences.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.sciencemag.org/news/2015/05/key-house-republican-says-70-nsf-s-research-dollars-should-go-core-science-not-geo-or|title=Key House Republican says 70% of NSF's research dollars should go to "core" science—not geo or social research|date=May 14, 2015|work=Science {{!}} AAAS|access-date=February 26, 2017}}</ref>

In 2008, he expressed concern about foreign-born students coming to the United States to obtain advanced academic degrees and then returning to their countries of origin.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://blog.chron.com/sciguy/2008/08/do-most-chinese-students-come-here-to-steal-secrets/|title=Do most Chinese students come here to steal secrets?|date=August 21, 2008|work=SciGuy|access-date=February 26, 2017 |language=en-US}}</ref>

===Disaster relief===
Culberson was the only Texas Republican to support the $50.7 billion relief effort after [[Hurricane Sandy]].<ref>{{cite news |last1=Dejean|first1=Ashley|title=Texas Lawmakers Who Voted Against Relief for Hurricane Sandy Now Ask For Help With Harvey |url=https://www.motherjones.com/politics/2017/08/texas-lawmakers-who-voted-against-relief-for-hurricane-sandy-now-ask-for-help-with-harvey/|accessdate=April 13, 2018|publisher=Mother Jones|date=August 28, 2017}}</ref> As a member of the U.S. House Appropriations Committee, Culberson has been active in seeking aid in the wake of [[Hurricane Harvey]].<ref>{{cite news |last1=Livingston |first1=Abby|title=Political fallout of Hurricane Harvey could begin this year in Houston |url=https://www.texastribune.org/2018/01/15/political-fallout-hurricane-harvey-could-begin-year-houston/|accessdate=April 13, 2018 |publisher=Texas Tribune|date=January 15, 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|last1=Lewis|first1=Brooke|title=Culberson, flood victims rally to show support for Hurricane Harvey bill|url=https://www.chron.com/news/houston-texas/houston/article/Culberson-flood-victims-rally-to-show-support-12529002.php|accessdate=April 13, 2018|publisher=Houston Chronicle|date=January 26, 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |last1=Schneider|first1=Andrew|title=How Rep. Culberson's Seat Went From GOP Stronghold To 'Toss Up' |url=https://www.houstonpublicmedia.org/articles/news/2018/02/19/268307/how-rep-culbersons-seat-went-from-gop-stronghold-to-toss-up/ |accessdate=April 13, 2018|publisher=Houston Public Media|date=February 19, 2018}}</ref>
