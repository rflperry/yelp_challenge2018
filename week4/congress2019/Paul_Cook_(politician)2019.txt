'''Paul Joseph Cook''' (born March 3, 1943) is an [[United States|American]] politician who has been the [[U.S. House of Representatives|U.S. Representative]] for [[California's 8th congressional district]] since 2013. Previously, he served as a member of [[California State Assembly]] from 2006 to 2012 and on the [[Yucca Valley]] city council from 1998 until 2006. He is a [[United States Republican Party|Republican]].

==Early life, education, and military career==
Cook was born in [[Meriden, Connecticut]] in 1943. He was raised in Meriden and did not permanently move to California until the end of his military career. In 1966, he graduated from [[Southern Connecticut State University]], earning a B.S. in teaching. Later that year, he joined the [[United States Marine Corps]]. As an infantry officer, Cook served in the [[Vietnam War]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://cook.house.gov/about/full-biography|title=Biography of Col. Paul Cook (Ret.)|publisher=Cook.house.gov|accessdate=October 14, 2013}}</ref> His actions in combat earned him many honors, including the [[Bronze Star]] and two [[Purple Heart]]s. He served in the Marine Corps for 26 years.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.rollcall.com/new-members/paul-cook-r-calif-8.html|title=Paul Cook, R (Calif.-8th)|publisher=Roll Call|date=October 10, 2013|accessdate=October 14, 2013}}</ref>

After he retired from the Marine Corps in 1992 as a colonel, he earned an [[Masters of Public Administration|MPA]] from [[California State University, San Bernardino]] in 1996 and a master's in political science from [[University of California Riverside]] in 2000.

==Academic career==
From 1993 to 1994, he was Director of Yucca Valley Chamber of Commerce. From 1998 to 2002, he was a professor at [[Copper Mountain College]]. Cook taught courses on political violence and terrorism at [[University of California Riverside]] since 2002.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.nationaljournal.com/congress-legacy/california-8th-house-district-20121101 |title=California, 8th House District |publisher=NationalJournal.com |date= |accessdate=2013-10-14}}</ref>

==California Assembly==

===Elections===
In 2006, Cook ran for California's 65th Assembly District. Cook won a five candidate Republican primary field with 29% of the vote.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=217111 |title=CA State Assembly 65 – R Primary Race – Jun 06, 2006 |publisher=Our Campaigns |date= |accessdate=2013-10-14}}</ref> In the general election, Cook defeated Democrat Rita Ramirez-Dean 60%–37%.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=219294 |title=CA State Assembly 65 Race – Nov 07, 2006 |publisher=Our Campaigns |date= |accessdate=2013-10-14}}</ref> In 2008, he won re-election to a second term, defeating Democrat Carl Wood 53%–47%.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=334722 |title=CA State Assembly 65 Race – Nov 04, 2008 |publisher=Our Campaigns |date= |accessdate=2013-10-14}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|last=York|first=Anthony|url=http://www.capitolweekly.net/article.php?_adctlid=vjq2q43wvsl855oxjrv6rjkdct1yk&issueId=xjptu9p7nq5i7a&xid=xjpuz5r83m9kbp|title=Legislative results include warning signs for Assembly Republicans|publisher=Capitol Weekly|accessdate=2013-10-14|deadurl=yes|archiveurl=https://archive.is/20131015073915/http://www.capitolweekly.net/article.php?_adctlid=vjq2q43wvsl855oxjrv6rjkdct1yk&issueId=xjptu9p7nq5i7a&xid=xjpuz5r83m9kbp|archivedate=2013-10-15|df=}}</ref> In 2010, he won re-election to a third term, defeating Wood again 58%–42%.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=656273 |title=CA State Assembly 65 Race – Nov 02, 2010 |publisher=Our Campaigns |date= |accessdate=2013-10-14}}</ref>

===Tenure===
The 65th district included the cities of [[Banning, California|Banning]], [[Beaumont, California|Beaumont]], [[Big Bear Lake, California|Big Bear Lake]], [[Calimesa]], [[Cherry Valley, California|Cherry Valley]], [[Hemet]], [[Moreno Valley]], [[Perris]], [[San Jacinto, California|San Jacinto]], [[Sun City, Menifee, California|Sun City]], [[Twentynine Palms]], [[Yucaipa]], [[Yucca Valley]] and other smaller communities and unincorporated areas in [[Riverside County]] and [[San Bernardino County]].

The California Chamber of Commerce and the California Taxpayers Association gave Cook a perfect 100% rating, 2007–2011.<ref name="Joinpaulcook">{{cite web|title=Biography|url=http://www.joinpaulcook.com/biography.asp|work=joinpaulcook.com|accessdate=3 January 2013}}</ref> In 2010, Democratic [[Speaker of the California State Assembly|Speaker of the Assembly]] [[John Pérez]] appointed Cook to chair the Veterans Affairs Committee, the first time a Democratic speaker had appointed a Republican to chair a committee since 2002.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://republican.assembly.ca.gov/inc/article.aspx?id=223004 |title=Assemblyman Cook Named Chair Of Assembly Veterans Affairs Committee |publisher=Republican.assembly.ca.gov |date=2010-03-19 |accessdate=2013-10-14}}</ref>

==U.S. House of Representatives==

===2012 campaign===
{{See also|United States House of Representatives elections in California, 2012#District 8}}
In January 2012, 34-year incumbent [[Jerry Lewis (California politician)|Jerry Lewis]] announced he would not seek re-election in November. Cook entered the primary for the district, which had been renumbered from the 41st to the 8th in redistricting. He finished second in the 13-candidate all-party open primary. He earned 15% of the vote. Fellow Republican and conservative activist Gregg Imus ranked first with 16% of the vote.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=740812 |title=CA – District 08 – Open Primary Race – Jun 05, 2012 |publisher=Our Campaigns |date= |accessdate=2013-10-14}}</ref> Cook was endorsed by the California Off-Road Vehicle Association (CORVA) past presidents, the [[San Bernardino Sun]], National Vietnam and Gulf War Veterans Coalition, the County Farm Bureau, state Assemblyman [[Steve Knight (politician)|Steve Knight]], state Senator [[Sharon Runner]], and U.S. Congressman [[Ed Royce]].<ref name="Paul Cook">{{cite web|title=Join Paul Cook Blog {{!}} Thoughts from Colonel Paul Cook|url=http://www.joinpaulcook.com/blog/|publisher=Paul Cook|accessdate=1 March 2013}}</ref> In the November election, Cook defeated Imus 58%–42%.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=768683 |title=CA – District 08 Race – Nov 06, 2012 |publisher=Our Campaigns |date= |accessdate=2013-10-14}}</ref>

=== Tenure ===

In 2013, Cook co-signed a letter to then president [[Barack Obama]], urging him to finalize the [[Keystone XL pipeline]], stating that it was about "jobs, jobs jobs." He also expressed fear that [[China]] "is ready to take advantage of America's missteps with the Keystone pipeline."<ref name="HDD2013">{{cite web|title=Congressman Paul Cook Urges President Obama to Allow Keystone Pipeline – High Desert Daily|url=http://highdesertdaily.com/2013/02/congressman-paul-cook-urges-president-obama-keystone-pipeline/|website=High Desert Daily|accessdate=22 December 2017}}</ref>

Early in 2017, Cook voted in favor of [[Efforts to repeal the Patient Protection and Affordable Care Act|repealing the Affordable Care Act]]. His reason for voting for the repeal was to ensure that "every American has access to quality care to fit their budget."<ref name="Moore11317">{{cite news |last1=Moore|first1=Stacy|title=Obamacare repeal gets Paul Cook's vote|url=http://www.hidesertstar.com/news/article_1935eab8-d9f6-11e6-b5d0-7b0328822249.html|newspaper=Hi-Desert Star|accessdate=22 December 2017}}</ref> In August 2017, he voted in favor of outlawing [[Late termination of pregnancy|late term abortions]], unless the woman was a victim of rape or incest or that her life was threatened.<ref name="Celentano10617">{{cite web|last1=Celentano|first1=Catherine|title=Here’s how Inland-area members of Congress voted on abortion limits, federal budgets|url=https://www.pe.com/2017/10/06/heres-how-inland-area-members-of-congress-voted-on-abortion-limits-federal-budgets/|website=Press Enterprise|accessdate=22 December 2017|date=6 October 2017}}</ref>

Cook voted in favor of the [[Tax Cuts and Jobs Act of 2017]].<ref name="Almukhtar122117">{{cite web|last1=Almukhtar|first1=Sarah|title=How Each House Member Voted on the Tax Bill|url=https://www.nytimes.com/interactive/2017/12/19/us/politics/tax-bill-house-live-vote.html?_r=0|website=The New York Times|accessdate=22 December 2017|date=19 December 2017}}</ref> By voting for the bill, Cook says that the bill will "deliver crucial tax relief for middle-class and low-income Americans." He voted for this bill because more than 90 percent of taxpaying constituents will receive a tax break. He also supports it because it simplifies the tax code.<ref name="HCN122017">{{cite web|title=Rep. Cook Votes for Final Version of Tax Reform Bill|url=http://www.highlandnews.net/news/political/rep-cook-votes-for-final-version-of-tax-reform-bill/article_0697af0e-e599-11e7-a8e5-afea28788f55.html|website=Highland Community News|accessdate=22 December 2017|language=en}}</ref>

== Political positions ==
In the first session of the [[115th United States Congress]], Cook was ranked the 33rd most bipartisan member of the [[United States House of Representatives|House]] by the Bipartisan Index, a metric published by [[Richard Lugar#The Lugar Center|The Lugar Center]] and [[Georgetown University|Georgetown's]] [[McCourt School of Public Policy]] to assess [[United States Congress|congressional]] bipartisanship.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.thelugarcenter.org/assets/htmldocuments/Senate%20Scores%20115th%20Congress%20First%20Session.pdf|publisher=The Lugar Center|title=The Lugar Center - McCourt School Bipartisan Index|date=April 24, 2018|accessdate=July 9, 2018|location=Washington, D.C.}}</ref>

=== Abortion ===

Cook is [[pro-life]].<ref name="OTI"/>

=== Education ===

Cook opposes [[Common Core State Standards]].<ref name="OTI">{{cite web|title=Paul Cook on the Issues|url=http://www.ontheissues.org/CA/Paul_Cook.htm|website=On The Issues|accessdate=22 December 2017}}</ref>

=== Health care ===

Cook supports repealing the [[Affordable Care Act]] ("Obamacare"). He supports legislation that "decreases premiums, makes it easier for employers to offer affordable healthcare options for their employees, and allows greater freedom for people to purchase a plan of their choice."<ref name="Moore11317" />

=== Immigration ===

Cook believes that the [[Deferred Action for Childhood Arrivals]] (DACA) is unconstitutional.

==Personal life==
Cook resides in [[Yucca Valley]] with his wife, Jeanne.<ref name="Joinpaulcook" />
