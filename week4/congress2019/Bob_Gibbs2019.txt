'''Robert Brian Gibbs'''<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.washingtontimes.com/campaign-2012/candidates/robert-bob-brian-gibbs-57239/ |title=Archived copy |accessdate=2012-08-31 |deadurl=yes |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20121028232400/http://www.washingtontimes.com/campaign-2012/candidates/robert-bob-brian-gibbs-57239/ |archivedate=2012-10-28}}</ref> (born June 14, 1954) is the [[United States House of Representatives|U.S. Representative]] for {{ushr|OH|7}}. He is a member of the [[Republican Party (United States)|Republican Party]].

==Early life, education, and agricultural career==
Gibbs was born on June 14, 1954 in [[Peru, Indiana]]. His family moved to [[Cleveland]] in the 1960s, and Gibbs graduated from Bay Village Senior High School. In 1974, he graduated from the [[Ohio State University Agricultural Technical Institute]]<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://ati.osu.edu/news/u-s-rep-bob-gibbs-74-speak-40th-commencement|title=U. S. Rep. Bob Gibbs '74 to speak at 40th Commencement|website=ati.osu.edu|language=en|access-date=2018-05-24}}</ref> and moved to [[Lakeville, Ohio]] where he co-founded Hidden Hollow Farms, Ltd. Formerly a producer of swine, Hidden Hollow Farms currently produces corn and soybeans.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://thehill.com/homenews/campaign/126335-new-members-2010-ohio#18|title=New Members 2010 |publisher=The Hill|date=|accessdate=2011-03-09}}</ref>

Gibbs served as president of the [[Ohio Farm Bureau Federation]],<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.farmanddairy.com/news/mcclure-unseats-ofb-president-in-state-leadership-shake-up/195.html|title=McClure unseats OFB president in state leadership shake-up - Farm and Dairy|last=Crowell|first=Susan|date=2000-12-07|work=Farm and Dairy|access-date=2018-05-24|language=en-US}}</ref> Ohio's largest agriculture organization which has over 210,000 members. Gibbs first joined the Ohio Farm Bureau board of trustees in 1985. Gibbs also served as a board member of the Farm Bureau Bank, the Ohio Livestock Coalition, the Ohio Cooperative Council, and the Ohio Farm Bureau Alliance. Gibbs was also president of the Loundonville Farmers Equity in Loudonville, Ohio, where he served on the board for 12 years. Gibbs has also served as president of the [[Holmes County, Ohio|Holmes County]] (Ohio) extension advisory committee, the Holmes County Farm Bureau, and as a supervisor for the Holmes County Soil & Water Conservation Service.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://gibbs.house.gov/about-me/full-biography|title=Full Biography|publisher=House.gov|date=|accessdate=2011-03-09|deadurl=yes|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20110303085140/https://gibbs.house.gov/about-me/full-biography|archivedate=2011-03-03|df=}}</ref>

==Ohio House of Representatives==
[[Image:Map.GIF|thumb|left|200px]]

===Elections===
Gibbs was first elected to the Ohio General Assembly in 2002, defeating [[Democratic Party (US)|Democrat]] Tom Mason of Ashland for a newly drawn district in the Ohio House.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.sos.state.oh.us/elections/election-results-and-data/2002-elections-results/state-representative/|title=State Representative - Ohio Secretary of State|website=www.sos.state.oh.us|language=en-US|access-date=2018-05-25}}</ref> He was re-elected in 2004 in a rematch against Mason.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.sos.state.oh.us/elections/election-results-and-data/2004-elections-results/ohio-house-of-representatives-november-2-2004/#gref|title=Ohio House of Representatives: November 2, 2004 - Ohio Secretary of State|website=www.sos.state.oh.us|language=en-US|access-date=2018-05-25}}</ref> During the 2006 statewide election, Gibbs was re-elected, receiving 60% over [[Democratic Party (US)|Democratic]] challenger James P. Riley,<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.sos.state.oh.us/elections/election-results-and-data/2006-elections-results/ohio-house-of-representatives-november-7-2006/#gref|title=Ohio House of Representatives: November 7, 2006 - Ohio Secretary of State|website=www.sos.state.oh.us|language=en-US|access-date=2018-05-25}}</ref> a former township trustee from Sullivan, Ohio. Gibbs began his third term in the Ohio House of Representatives on January 2, 2007 and decided to run for Ohio Senate in 2009 to fill the seat vacated by state senator Ron Amstutz, due to term limits.

===Tenure===
In 2006 Gibbs was appointed to serve as a member of the special task force to study eminent domain and its use and application in Ohio. The committee spent most of the year studying the issue and issued its final report in August 2006 with recommendations to the General Assembly.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.farmanddairy.com/news/legislature-weighs-eminent-domain/831.html|title=Legislature weighs eminent domain |publisher=Farm and Dairy|date=2007-06-14|accessdate=2011-03-09}}</ref>

===Committee assignments===
During his last term Gibbs was chairman of the House ways and means committee. He was also a member of the agriculture & natural resources committee, financial institutions, real estate and securities committee, health care access and affordability committee, and the insurance committee. {{citation needed|date=August 2015}}

==Ohio Senate==

===Elections===
Gibbs won election to the [[Ohio Senate]] in 2008, and began his first term in 2009. On August 16, 2007, Gibbs formally announced that he would be a candidate for the 22nd district senate seat being vacated by the term limited incumbent senator, [[Ron Amstutz]], (R- Wooster). Gibbs originally expected to face a primary challenge from state representative [[Jim Carmichael]] (R-Wayne County), but Carmichael dropped out of the race on October 21, 2007 in order to run for Wayne County commissioner. Gibbs faced Democrat James E. Riley, a job/security representative for the [[United Automobile Workers|U.A.W.]] international union. Gibbs won with 59% of the vote.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.sos.state.oh.us/elections/election-results-and-data/2008-election-results/state-senator-november-4-2008/#gref|title=State Senator: November 4, 2008 - Ohio Secretary of State|website=www.sos.state.oh.us|language=en-US|access-date=2018-05-25}}</ref>

However, due to winning election to Congress in 2010, Gibbs resigned from the senate only serving half of one term.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.gongwer-oh.com/programming/news_articledisplay.cfm?article_ID=792340204&newsedition_id=7923402&locid=2|title=Gongwer News Service - Ohio|website=www.gongwer-oh.com|language=en|access-date=2018-05-15}}</ref>

==U.S. House of Representatives==

===Elections===

====2010====
{{See also|United States House of Representatives elections in Ohio, 2010#District 18*}}
Gibbs faced Democratic incumbent [[Zack Space]] and Constitution Party nominee Lindsey Sutton in the general election. He won the Republican primary in an 8-way field. Following close results and a recount, Gibbs was certified the winner on June 4, 2010, a month after the primary election.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.sos.state.oh.us/elections/election-results-and-data/2010-elections-results/representative-to-congress---republican-may-4-2010/|title=Representative to Congress - Republican: May 4, 2010 - Ohio Secretary of State|website=www.sos.state.oh.us|language=en-US|access-date=2018-05-25}}</ref>

On November 2, 2010, Gibbs defeated Space in the [[general election]] by nearly 14%. Gibbs won 14 of the 16 counties in the 18th congressional district.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.sos.state.oh.us/elections/election-results-and-data/2010-elections-results/representative-to-congress-november-2-2010/|title=Representative to Congress: November 2, 2010 - Ohio Secretary of State|website=www.sos.state.oh.us|language=en-US|access-date=2018-05-25}}</ref>

====2012====
{{See also|United States House of Representatives elections in Ohio, 2012#District 7}}
After redistricting, Gibbs decided to run in the newly redrawn [[Ohio's 7th congressional district]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=752274|title=Our Campaigns - OH District 07 - R Primary Race - Mar 06, 2012|website=www.ourcampaigns.com|access-date=2018-05-15}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.timesreporter.com/newsnow/x117255925/Gibbs-facing-challengers-to-represent-redrawn-7th |title=Archived copy |accessdate=2012-02-14 |deadurl=yes |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20130727014218/http://www.timesreporter.com/newsnow/x117255925/Gibbs-facing-challengers-to-represent-redrawn-7th |archivedate=2013-07-27 |df= }}</ref>  He faced Joyce Healy-Abrams, the Democratic nominee.<ref>{{cite web|title=Ohio Secretary of State|url=http://www.sos.state.oh.us/sos/upload/elections/2012/gen/UScongress.pdf}}</ref> Gibbs went on to defeat Healy-Abrams in the November general election.<ref>{{cite news|publisher=Medina Gazette|work=[[medinagazette.northcoastnow.com]]|accessdate=17 November 2012|date=7 November 2012|title=U.S. Rep. Bob Gibbs wins re-election in 7th District|last=Genson|first=Loren|url=http://medinagazette.northcoastnow.com/2012/11/07/u-s-rep-bob-gibbs-wins-re-election-in-7th-district/}}</ref>

====2014====
{{See also|United States House of Representatives elections in Ohio, 2014#District 7}}
Gibbs won re-election to a third term unopposed.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.cnn.com/election/2014/results/state/OH/house/07|title=Ohio House results -- 2014 Election Center -- Elections and Politics from CNN.com|work=CNN|access-date=2018-05-15|language=en-US}}</ref>

===Tenure===
On March 4, 2013, Gibbs introduced the [[Reducing Regulatory Burdens Act of 2013 (H.R. 935; 113th Congress)]], a bill that would prohibit the [[Environmental Protection Agency]] (EPA) and states authorized to issue a permit under the [[National Pollutant Discharge Elimination System]] (NPDES) from requiring a permit for some discharges of pesticides authorized for use under the [[Federal Insecticide, Fungicide, and Rodenticide Act]] (FIFRA).<ref name=cbo935>{{cite web|title=CBO – H.R. 935|url=http://www.cbo.gov/publication/45192|publisher=Congressional Budget Office|accessdate=27 July 2014}}</ref><ref name=935sum>{{cite web|title=H.R. 935 – Summary|url=https://beta.congress.gov/bill/113th-congress/house-bill/935|publisher=United States Congress|accessdate=27 July 2014}}</ref>  In 2018, Gibbs was supported by the [[Great America Committee]], a [[political action committee]] registered by [[Vice President of the United States|Vice President]] [[Mike Pence]].<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.politico.com/story/2018/02/07/mike-pence-pac-donations-395985|title=Pence's PAC gives to 30 House members in second round of donations|work=POLITICO|access-date=2018-07-25|language=en}}</ref>

==Personal life==
He is married to the former Jody Cox of Wooster, Ohio. They have three grown children and are members of the Nashville United Methodist Church in [[Nashville, Ohio]]. {{citation needed | date=August 2015}}
