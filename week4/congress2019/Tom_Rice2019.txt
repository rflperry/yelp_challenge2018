'''Hugh Thompson Rice Jr.''' (born August 4, 1957) is the [[United States House of Representatives|U.S. Representative]] for {{ushr|SC|7}}. Elected to the [[113th Congress]] in November 2012, he is a conservative Republican, supports the [[United States pro-life movement|right to life movement]] and thinks the U.S. government has strayed from tenets of the Constitution.<ref name="Tom Rice: Inexperience could be a good thing.">{{cite news|last=Jones|first=Steve|title=Tom Rice: Inexperience could be a good thing.|url=http://www.myrtlebeachonline.com/2012/11/03/3151793/tom-rice-inexperience-could-be.html|accessdate=November 7, 2012|newspaper=The Sun News|date=November 3, 2012}}</ref> He was a member of the freshman class chosen to sit at the House Republican leadership table. Rice was chair of the [[Horry County, South Carolina|Horry County]] Council until he resigned from the position on December 31.<ref name="Horry County Chair seat empty Dec. 31, filing opens mid-January">{{cite web|last=Hinnant |first=Lauren |title=Horry County Chair seat empty Dec. 31, filing opens mid-January |url=http://www2.wbtw.com/news/grand-strand-wbtw/2012/dec/19/horry-county-chair-seat-empty-dec-31-filing-opens-ar-5197919/ |archive-url=https://archive.is/20130209123829/http://www2.wbtw.com/news/grand-strand-wbtw/2012/dec/19/horry-county-chair-seat-empty-dec-31-filing-opens-ar-5197919/ |dead-url=yes |archive-date=February 9, 2013 |publisher=WBTW News 13 |accessdate=December 19, 2012 }}</ref>

Rice won re-election in 2014. He defeated Democratic challenger [[Gloria Bromell Tinubu]] on November 4, 2014.<ref name="Rep. Tom Rice declared victor in bid for 7th District">{{cite web|last1=Jones|first1=Steve|title=Rep. ==Early life, education and pre-congressional career==
Rice was born in [[Charleston, South Carolina|Charleston]] on August 4, 1957. He was four years old when his parents divorced, and his teacher mother took him and his brother Clay to [[Myrtle Beach, South Carolina|Myrtle Beach]]. Rice’s first job was a busboy when he was 12, and he was variously a night shift fry cook, a grocery store bag boy and [[miniature golf]] course manager while still in high school. Rice was 16 when his father died.

Rice had a scholarship to [[Duke University]] but enrolled at the [[University of South Carolina]] in [[Columbia, South Carolina|Columbia]], where he earned a master's degree in accounting in 1979 and a law degree in 1982.

After college, he worked at the accounting/consulting firm of [[Deloitte|Deloitte & Touche]] in [[Charlotte, North Carolina|Charlotte]], where he earned his [[Certified Public Accountant|CPA certificate.]] In 1985 he returned to Myrtle Beach to practice tax law with the Van Osdell law firm, then established his own practice, Rice & MacDonald, in 1997. He was elected chair of Horry County Council in 2010.

===Elections===
Rice won election in the U.S. Congress elections, 2012 for the [[U.S. House elections, 2012|U.S. House]], representing [[United States House of Representatives elections in South Carolina, 2012|South Carolina's]] South Carolina's 7th congressional district. Rice defeated Jay Jordan, Randal Wallace, [[Dick Withington]], James Mader, Chad Prosser, Katherine Jenerette, and Renee Culler in the [[Republican Party (United States)|Republican]] primary on June 12, 2012 to advance to a runoff. He defeated major rival [[Andre Bauer]] in the run-off election on June 26, 2012 Rice defeated [[Gloria Bromell Tinubu]] (D) in the general election on November 6, 2012.<ref>[http://hosted.ap.org/dynamic/files/elections/2012/by_state/SC_Page_0612.html?SITE=WYFFTVELN&SECTION=POLITICS WYFF News-2012 Primary Results]</ref><ref>[https://archive.is/20130104144137/http://www2.scnow.com/news/grand-strand/2012/jun/26/tinubu-wins-democratic-runoff-brittain-concedes-ar-4043005/ ''SC Now'' "Tinubu wins Democratic runoff; Rice beats Bauer for GOP spot," June 26, 2012]</ref>

Rice won re-election in 2014 defeating Democratic challenger Gloria Bromell Tinubu with 60.15 percent of the votes over Rommel-Tinubu's 39.85 percent.<ref name="Rep. Tom Rice declared victor in bid for 7th District" />

On January 8, 2013, Congressman [[Sam Graves]] (R-MO) appointed Rice chairman of the Subcommittee on Economic Growth, Tax and Capital Access. Graves said that Rice would be a "great fit" because of his knowledge about the challenges facing small business owners. Rice said he would "work to limit government overreach, enact serious tax reform, and lower the cost of doing business in America, so our small business owners—America’s economic engine—can compete in our economy and the global marketplace."<ref name="Rep. Rice Named Small Business Subcommittee Chairman">{{cite web|title=Rep. Rice Named Small Business Subcommittee Chairman|url=http://rice.house.gov/media-center/press-releases/rep-rice-named-small-business-subcommittee-chairman|publisher=US House of Representatives|date=January 8, 2013 |accessdate=January 13, 2013}}</ref>

On January 22, 2013, Rice was appointed to the following  subcommittees: [[United States House Transportation Subcommittee on Highways and Transit|Highways and Transit]], [[United States House Transportation Subcommittee on Water Resources and Environment|Water Resources and Environment]], and [[United States House Transportation Subcommittee on Coast Guard and Maritime Transportation|Coast Guard and Maritime Transportation]]. Rice said the appointments would allow him to work for the funding and construction of [[Interstate 73]] as well as the dredging of the Georgetown Port.<ref>{{cite web|title=Rep. Rice Appointed Transportation Subcommittees|url=http://rice.house.gov/media-center/press-releases/rep-rice-appointed-transportation-subcommittees|publisher=US House of Representatives/Rep. Tom Rice news release|accessdate=January 27, 2013}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|last=Jones|first=Steve|title=Rice appointed to key subcommittees for 7th District |url=http://www.myrtlebeachonline.com/2013/01/25/3292838/rice-appointed-to-key-subcommittees.html#storylink=misearch|accessdate=January 27, 2013|newspaper=[[The Sun News]]|date=January 25, 2013}}</ref>

On November 11, 2013, Rice was appointed to the water resources conference committee which is to help resolve differences between the U.S. House and Senate versions of the Water Resources Reform and Development Act of 2013. The version that has recently passed the House would allow for the dredging of the Georgetown port which is a $33 million project and would bring an estimated $30 million annual economic boost to the local economy. In a press release from his office Congressman Rice stated, "My district sent me to Washington to work on our infrastructure needs and the needs of the state," and, "Since joining the Transportation and Infrastructure Committee, I have made it my goal to do whatever it takes to champion South Carolina's ports."<ref>{{cite news|last=Jones|first=Steve|title=Rice named to Water Resources conference committee.|url=http://www.myrtlebeachonline.com/2013/11/15/3839343/rice-named-to-water-resources.html|accessdate=15 November 2013|newspaper=[[The Sun News]]|date=15 November 2013|deadurl=yes|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20131118033123/http://www.myrtlebeachonline.com/2013/11/15/3839343/rice-named-to-water-resources.html|archivedate=18 November 2013|df=}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=Rice named to Water Resources conference committee|url=http://rice.house.gov/media-center/in-the-news/rice-named-to-water-resources-conference-committee|publisher=Congressman Tom Rice|accessdate=16 November 2013}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=Speaker Boehner Appoints Negotiators to Water Resources Reform Conference Committee|url=http://www.speaker.gov/press-release/speaker-boehner-appoints-negotiators-water-resources-reform-conference-committee#sthash.AFqhTq1E.dpuf|publisher=Speaker of the House John Boehner|accessdate=16 November 2013}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|title=WRRDA Moves Forward with House Conferee Appointments|url=http://transportation.house.gov/news/documentsingle.aspx?DocumentID=361471|accessdate=15 November 2013|newspaper=transportation.house.gov|date=15 November 2013}}</ref>

==Personal life==
Rice and his family reside in Myrtle Beach.{{citation needed|date=April 2017}} In his free time he enjoys fishing, hunting, golfing and spending time with his family and friends.{{citation needed|date=April 2017}} He married his wife, Wrenzie, in 1982 and they have three adult sons; H.T., Jacob, and Lucas.{{citation needed|date=April 2017}}
