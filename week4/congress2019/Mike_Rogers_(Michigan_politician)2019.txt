'''Michael J. Rogers''' (born June 2, 1963) is a former [[U.S. House of Representatives|U.S. Representative]] for {{ushr|MI|8}}. A member of the [[Republican Party (United States)|Republican Party]], Rogers served from 2001 to 2015. From 2011 to 2015, he was Chairman of the [[United States House Permanent Select Committee on Intelligence|Permanent Select Committee on Intelligence]].

Rogers has a contract to speak on national-security topics on [[CNN]].{{citation needed|date=July 2017}} In June 2016, he presented a six-part series on CNN, titled ''[[Declassified (CNN series)|Declassified: Untold Stories of American Spies]]''.

==Early life, education, and law enforcement career==
Rogers was born in Livingston County, Michigan, the son of Joyce A. and John C. Rogers.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://thomas.loc.gov/cgi-bin/query/z?r111:H20JY0-0131:|title=Congressional Record - 111th Congress (2009-2010) - THOMAS (Library of Congress)|publisher=}}</ref> He graduated from [[Adrian College]], [[Adrian, Michigan]] in 1985, from which he earned a bachelor's degree in Criminal Justice and Sociology, and served in the [[United States Army]] from 1985 to 1989. He worked as a [[Special Agent]] with the [[Federal Bureau of Investigation]] in its Chicago office, specializing in organized crime and public corruption, 1989–1994. He is a member of the [[Society of Former Special Agents of the Federal Bureau of Investigation]]. In 2017, Mike Rogers was interviewed to be the new director of the Federal Bureau of Investigation, after [[James Comey]] was dismissed.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.detroitnews.com/story/news/politics/2017/05/15/ex-rep-mike-rogers-interviewed-fbi-director/101707834/|title=Ex-Rep. Mike Rogers interviewed for FBI director|work=Detroit News|access-date=2017-05-22|language=en}}</ref>

==Michigan State Senate==

===Elections===
He was first elected in 1994. In 1998, he won a second term with 68% of the vote.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=365682|title=Our Campaigns - MI State Senate 26 Race - Nov 03, 1998|publisher=}}</ref>

===Tenure===
He represented three counties: [[Clinton County, Michigan|Clinton]], [[Livingston County, Michigan|Livingston]], and [[Shiawassee County, Michigan|Shiawassee]]. He served as Majority Leader from 1999 to 2000.

Rogers wrote legislation creating the [[Michigan Education Savings Plan]], which allows Michigan families to set aside tax-free funds for educating their children when they are ready for college or vocational training.<ref>http://www.legislature.mi.gov/documents/1999-2000/publicact/pdf/2000-PA-0161.pdf</ref>

He was elected as a [[Republican Party (United States)|Republican]] from the 8th District of [[Michigan]] to the [[United States House of Representatives]] in one of the nation's closest congressional races of 2000. He defeated Democratic State Senator [[Dianne Byrum]] by 111 votes to win the District 8 seat left open by [[Debbie Stabenow]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://miboecfr.nicusa.com/election/results/00gen/06008000.html |title=2000 Official Michigan General Election Results – 8th District Representative in Congress 2 Year Term (1) Position |publisher=Miboecfr.nicusa.com |date= |accessdate=2010-08-22}}</ref>  However, he was reelected six times, in a newly reconfigured - and more Republican-leaning - district with almost no difficulty. Said district was created as a result of the 2000 decennial census.  The Michigan Legislature removed portions of Genesee and Washtenaw Counties and replaced them with the most Republican areas of Oakland County.<ref>[[Michigan's 8th congressional district]]</ref>{{Better source|reason=per WP:CIRCULAR|date=November 2017}}  Congressman Rogers usually ran up large margins in the areas of the district outside heavily Democratic [[Lansing, Michigan|Lansing]], the district's largest city.

===Tenure===
[[File:Former Congressman Mike Rogers at Hudson Institute talked Clear and Present Danger ~ Confronting the Cyber Threat from China and Russia.jpg|thumb|Mike Rogers at [[Hudson Institute]] talked about Clear and Present Danger: Confronting the Cyber Threat from China and Russia]]
[[File:Secretary of Defense Ash Carter and former Congressman Mike Rogers during the Center for the Study of the Presidency and Congress Eisenhower award dinner.jpg|thumb|Mike Rogers and Secretary of Defense [[Ash Carter]] talked during the [[Center for the Study of the Presidency and Congress]] Eisenhower award dinner]]
Rogers' measure to make education savings plans free of federal taxes was adopted in 2003 (see [[Economic Growth and Tax Relief Reconciliation Act of 2001]]). His [[health savings account]] program for low-income families who are covered by Medicaid was signed into law on February 8, 2008.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://frwebgate.access.gpo.gov/cgi-bin/getdoc.cgi?dbname=109_cong_bills&docid=f:s1932enr.txt.pdf |title=MICROCOMP output file |format=PDF |date= |accessdate=2010-08-22}}</ref>

In 2006, he cosponsored H.R. 4411, the [[Goodlatte]]-Leach Internet Gambling Prohibition Act<ref>{{cite web|url=http://thomas.loc.gov/cgi-bin/bdquery/z?d109:h.r.04411:|title=Bill Summary & Status  -  109th Congress (2005 - 2006)  - H.R.4411 - THOMAS (Library of Congress)|publisher=}}</ref> and H.R. 4777, the Internet Gambling Prohibition Act.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://thomas.loc.gov/cgi-bin/bdquery/z?d109:h.r.04777:|title=Bill Summary & Status  -  109th Congress (2005 - 2006)  - H.R.4777 - THOMAS (Library of Congress)|publisher=}}</ref> He has also introduced pain care management legislation pertaining to Americans who are restricted by severe, chronic pain.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://frwebgate.access.gpo.gov/cgi-bin/getdoc.cgi?dbname=110_cong_bills&docid=f:h2994ih.txt.pdf|title=FDsys - Browse Congressional Bills|publisher=}}</ref>

Rogers was the primary sponsor of the [[Respect for America's Fallen Heroes Act]], H.R. bill 5037, which was signed into law by President [[George W. Bush]] on May 29, 2006. This bill is designed to ban protests on Federal Lands from occurring near the funerals of soldiers that were killed in action.

The CBO has said that Rogers's H.R. 1206 to make it easier for states to obtain waivers from some Medical Loss Ratio requirements would add $1.1 billion to the deficit between 2013 and 2022.<ref>Viebeck, Elise. [http://thehill.com/blogs/healthwatch/other/266827-cbo-gop-bill-revising-health-law-ratio-will-add-to-deficit "CBO: GOP bill revising health law ratio will add to deficit."] ''The Hill'', 8 November 2012.</ref>

In November 30, 2011 Congressman Rogers introduced the [[Cyber Intelligence Sharing and Protection Act]] (CISPA).<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.govtrack.us/congress/bills/112/hr3523|title=Cyber Intelligence Sharing and Protection Act (2012; 112th Congress H.R. 3523) - GovTrack.us|work=GovTrack.us}}</ref> "The bill would allow the government to share all of its classified cyber-security knowledge with private companies, forming knowledge-sharing agreements that would hopefully keep China (and other countries and hackers) out of American computer networks. The catch is that the information shared is a two-lane street—companies would also be allowed to share private data with the federal government, provided there is a reasonable 'cyber threat.'"<ref>{{cite web|last=Koebler|first=Jason|title=CISPA Author Rogers: China's Cyber 'Predators' Must Be Stopped|url=https://www.usnews.com/news/articles/2012/04/17/cispa-author-rogers-chinas-cyber-predators-must-be-stopped_print.html|accessdate=2012-04-17}}</ref>  "In the current version, most personal information would be stripped from data shared with the government, and the bill no longer defines intellectual property theft as something relating to national security "We think we're making huge progress with the privacy groups, so they understand what we're trying to accomplish, which isn't anything nefarious," Rogers said"<ref>{{cite web|last=Koebler|first=Jason|title=CISPA Author Rogers: China's Cyber 'Predators' Must Be Stopped|url=https://www.usnews.com/news/articles/2012/04/17/cispa-author-rogers-chinas-cyber-predators-must-be-stopped_print.html|publisher=US News|accessdate=2012-04-17}}</ref>

Rogers has reaffirmed his support for the NSA's programs, stating on October 30, 2013, "You can't have your privacy violated if you don't know your privacy is violated."<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.techdirt.com/articles/20131029/18020225059/mike-rogers-you-cant-have-your-privacy-violated-if-you-dont-know-about-it.shtml|title=Mike Rogers: You Can't Have Your Privacy Violated If You Don't Know About It|work=Techdirt.}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.msnbc.com/msnbc/top-house-republican-says|title=GOPer: Trust us on that spying thing|author=Adam Serwer|date=October 31, 2013|work=MSNBC}}</ref>

Rogers introduced and supported the [[Intelligence Authorization Act for Fiscal Years 2014 and 2015 (H.R. 4681; 113th Congress)]], a bill that would [[authorization bill|authorize]] a variety of intelligence agencies and their [[appropriations bill (United States)|appropriations]] for fiscal years 2014 and 2015.<ref name=MarcosIntelPrograms>{{cite news|last1=Marcos|first1=Cristina|title=House authorizes intel programs through 2015|url=http://thehill.com/blogs/floor-action/house/207730-house-passes-intelligence-authorization|accessdate=3 June 2014|publisher=The Hill|date=30 May 2014}}</ref><ref name=4681sum>{{cite web|title=H.R. 4681 - Summary|url=http://beta.congress.gov/bill/113th-congress/house-bill/4681|publisher=United States Congress|accessdate=3 June 2014}}</ref> The total spending authorized by the bill is classified, but estimates based on [[Global surveillance disclosures (2013–present)|intelligence leaks]] made by [[Edward Snowden]] indicate that the budget could be approximately $50 billion.<ref name=TCwilhelm30>{{cite news|last1=Wilhelm|first1=Alex|title=House Votes To Fund Intelligence Programs Through 2015|url=https://techcrunch.com/2014/05/30/house-votes-to-fund-intelligence-programs-through-2015/|accessdate=3 June 2014|publisher=TechCrunch|date=30 May 2014}}</ref><ref name=MasnickTD>{{cite news|last1=Masnick|first1=Mike|title=Latest Snowden Leaks Detail The 'Black Budget' And How Much The Gov't Wastes On Useless Surveillance|url=http://www.techdirt.com/articles/20130829/10405424350/latest-snowden-leaks-detail-black-budget-how-much-govt-wastes-useless-surveillance.shtml|accessdate=3 June 2014|publisher=TechDirt|date=29 August 2013}}</ref> Rogers said that members of Congress "have somehow decided over the last year that our intelligence services are the problem... they are part of the solution."<ref name="MarcosIntelPrograms"/>

In March 2014, Rogers announced he would not seek an 8th term in Congress.<ref>http://www.detroitnews.com/article/20140328/POLITICS02/303280068/</ref>  He later launched "Something to Think About", a daily radio segment.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.westwoodone.com/LEARN-MORE/Press/Something-to-Think-About-with-Mike-Rogers-Launches-in-January-on-Radio-Stations-Nationwide-Through-Nations-Largest-Talk-Platform|title="Something to Think About" with Mike Rogers Launches in January on Radio Stations Nationwide Through Nation's Largest Talk Platform|website=www.westwoodone.com|language=en-US|access-date=2017-05-14}}</ref>  Former Michigan State Senator Mike Bishop won the Republican primary and defeated Democratic challenger Eric Schertzing.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.detroitnews.com/story/news/politics/elections/2014/11/04/michigan-congress-eighth-district-election-results/18491127/|title=Former State Senate Majority Leader Mike Bishop wins 8th Congressional District|date=November 4, 2014|work=Detroit News}}</ref>

==Personal life==
Rogers is the youngest of five sons. His father was a public school teacher-administrator-football coach and his mother was the director of a local Chamber of Commerce. Rogers' older brother Bill was a state representative in Michigan. He resides in [[Howell, Michigan]]. His wife, Kristi Rogers, previously served as the CEO and as a managing director of Aspen Healthcare Services, and now is a managing partner for Principal to Principal.
