'''Douglas L. LaMalfa''' (born July 2, 1960) is an [[United States|American]] [[politician]]. A [[California Republican Party|Republican]], he has been the [[United States House of Representatives|U.S. Representative]] for [[California's 1st congressional district]], located in Northern California, since 2013. LaMalfa previously served in the [[California State Assembly]], representing the [[California's 2nd State Assembly district|2nd district]], from 2002 to 2008, and he served in the [[California State Senate]], representing the [[California's 4th State Senate district|4th district]], from 2010 to 2012.

==Early life, education and career==
LaMalfa is a fourth-generation rice farmer and a lifelong Northern California resident.  He graduated from [[California Polytechnic State University|Cal Poly San Luis Obispo]] with a [[Bachelor's degree]] in Ag/Business.   He and his wife, Jill, along with their four children, make their home on the family rice farm in [[Richvale, California|Richvale]], an unincorporated suburban community south of [[Chico, California|Chico]], that was founded by his great-grandfather in 1931.

LaMalfa is an owner and manager of the Dsl Lamalfa Family Partnership, which owns and operates the family farm.<ref name="Johnson12817">{{cite web|last1=Johnson|first1=Rifa|title=Rep. LaMalfa highest earner of farm subsidies in Congress, report shows|url=http://www.chicoer.com/article/NA/20171208/NEWS/171209733|website=ChicoER News|accessdate=December 25, 2017}}</ref>

==California Assembly==
===Elections===
In 2002, LaMalfa ran for the [[California Assembly]] in the 2nd District. He won the Republican primary with 59% of the vote,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=286835|title=CA State Assembly 02- R Primary Race|date=March 5, 2002|publisher=Our Campaigns|accessdate=April 13, 2014}}</ref> and the general election with 67%.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=5606|title=CA State Assembly 02 Race|date=November 5, 2002|publisher=Our Campaigns|accessdate=April 13, 2014}}</ref> He won re-election in 2004 (68%)<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=71853|title=CA State Assembly 02 Race|date=November 2, 2004|publisher=Our Campaigns |accessdate=April 13, 2014}}</ref> and 2006 (68%).<ref>{{cite web|url=http://vote.ss.ca.gov/Returns/stasm/0259.htm|title=Archived copy|accessdate=July 15, 2007|deadurl=yes|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20070611170134/http://vote.ss.ca.gov/Returns/stasm/0259.htm|archivedate=June 11, 2007}}</ref>

===Tenure===
LaMalfa worked with [[Bernie Richter]] as an early supporter of [[California Proposition 209 (1996)|Proposition 209]], which ended [[affirmative action]] in California. He worked for passage of the Protection of Marriage Act, [[California Proposition 22 (2000)|Proposition 22]], which banned [[Same-sex marriage]] in California, and after that initiative was overturned by the courts, he was an early supporter and active in the [[California Proposition 8 (2008)|Proposition 8]] campaign.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.douglamalfa.com/about|title=Doug LaMalfa for U.S. Representative|publisher=Douglamalfa.com|date=November 21, 2013|accessdate=April 13, 2014}}</ref>

LaMalfa opposed [[Mike Feuer]]'s [[microstamping]] bill, AB 1471, which was signed into law by Governor [[Arnold Schwarzenegger]] on October 13, 2007.<ref>{{cite web |author=Feuer |url=http://www.leginfo.ca.gov/pub/07-08/bill/asm/ab_1451-1500/ab_1471_bill_20071013_chaptered.html|title=AB 1471 Assembly Bill – CHAPTERED|publisher=Leginfo.ca.gov|accessdate=April 13, 2014}}</ref>

LaMalfa was a co-author of ACA 20, which, similar to Arizona's new law, would empower law enforcement to act as Immigration, Customs Enforcement Agents and would have cracked down on illegal immigration.<ref>{{cite web|author=Haynes|url=http://www.leginfo.ca.gov/pub/05-06/bill/asm/ab_0001-0050/aca_20_bill_20050628_amended_asm.html|title=ACA 20 Assembly Constitutional Amendment – AMENDED|publisher=Leginfo.ca.gov|accessdate=April 13, 2014}}</ref> ACA 20 failed to pass the first hearing by a 3-to-6 vote.{{citation needed|date=July 2017}}

In 2007, he successfully passed AB 1645, a law that would prevent seizures of firearms in the event of an emergency or natural disaster. This was the first pro-gun legislation passed and signed into law in a decade. When LaMalfa was named the California Rifle and Pistol Association's "Legislator of the Year" for 2007, he said "Receiving this award today from the California Rifle and Pistol Association is a truly humbling honor."<ref>{{cite news|title=LaMalfa, Legislator of the Year|url=http://www.appeal-democrat.com/corning_observer/news/lamalfa-legislator-of-the-year/article_62721e97-bc2f-5444-a2c2-ff5e36f45258.html|accessdate=December 5, 2015|publisher=Corning Observer|date=March 26, 2008}}</ref>

==California Senate==
===2010 election===
In 2010, he ran for the [[California State Senate]] in the 4th District. In the Republican primary, he defeated State Representative [[Rick Keene]] 58%–42%.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=653299|title=CA State Senate 04-R Primary Race|date=June 8, 2010|publisher=Our Campaigns|accessdate=April 13, 2014}}</ref> In the general election, he defeated Lathe Gill 68%–32%.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=653297|title=CA State Senate 04 Race|date=November 2, 2010 |publisher=Our Campaigns|accessdate=April 13, 2014}}</ref>

===Tenure===
In November 2011, LaMalfa opposed a proposed [[high-speed rail|bullet train]]. He did so, saying "In light of the High Speed Rail plan that was submitted and that the numbers still do not work, California in this dire fiscal crisis that we're in, we're going to introduce legislation to repeal the HSR Authority and the funding for that the state was going to put forward".<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.kmjnow.com/pages/landing?Move-Afoot-to-Derail-CAs-Bullet-Train-Pr=1&blockID=562442&feedID=806|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130908200247/http://www.kmjnow.com/pages/landing?Move-Afoot-to-Derail-CAs-Bullet-Train-Pr=1&blockID=562442&feedID=806 |dead-url=yes|archive-date=September 8, 2013|title=Move Afoot to Derail CA's Bullet Train Project|accessdate=April 3, 2017}}</ref>

LaMalfa gave [[Democratic Party (United States)|Democratic]] [[Governor of California|Governor]] [[Jerry Brown]] a "C" for his fiscal policies in 2011.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://newstalk1290.wordpress.com/2011/05/17/gov-brown-releases-may-revise-to-close-state-deficit|title=Gov. Brown Releases May Revise to Close State Deficit |publisher=Newstalk1290.wordpress.com|date=May 17, 2011|accessdate=April 13, 2014}}</ref>

LaMalfa opposed [[FAIR Education Act|a bill]] that would require history teachers in all [[Education in California|California public schools]] to teach [[History of homosexuality|history of homosexuality and gay civil rights]]. LaMalfa stated the Governor Brown was "out of touch with what I think are still mainstream American values. That's not the kind of stuff I want my kids learning about in public school. They've really crossed a line into a new frontier."<ref>{{cite web|last=Siders|first=David|url=http://www.sacbee.com/2011/07/15/3771352/gov-brown-signs-law-requiring.html |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110718092111/http://www.sacbee.com/2011/07/15/3771352/gov-brown-signs-law-requiring.html|dead-url=yes|archive-date=July 18, 2011 |title=Gov. Brown signs law requiring teaching of gay history|date=July 18, 2011 |accessdate=April 3, 2017}}</ref>

LaMalfa strongly opposed the [[National Popular Vote Interstate Compact]], which would eliminate the [[Electoral College (United States)|Electoral College]]. He argued "I think this is dangerous. It flies in the face of 220 years of election law. We have an electoral college; it was put there for a reason."<ref>{{cite web|author=Don Thompson|url=http://www.utsandiego.com/news/2011/jul/14/calif-lawmakers-approve-change-to-electoral-votes|title=Calif lawmakers approve change to electoral votes|publisher=UTSanDiego.com|date=July 14, 2011|accessdate=April 13, 2014}}</ref>

==U.S. House of Representatives==
===Elections===
====2012====
{{Main|United States House of Representatives elections in California, 2012#District 1}}
In January 2012, 2nd district Congressman [[Wally Herger]] announced that he was retiring after thirteen terms. Hours after Herger announced his retirement, Republican consultant Dave Gilliard told Flash Report that Herger had endorsed LaMalfa as his successor in the district, which was renumbered as the 1st District in the 2010 round of redistricting.<ref>{{cite web|last=Sabalow|first=Ryan|url=http://www.redding.com/news/2012/jan/09/herger-announce-retirement-lamalfa-run-his-seat|title=UPDATED: Herger announces retirement; backs LaMalfa to run for his seat|publisher=Redding Record-Searchlight|accessdate=April 13, 2014}}</ref> LaMalfa finished first in the June 2012 primary election with 38% of the vote in an eight-person race, winning 10 out of the district's 11 counties.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.sos.ca.gov/elections/sov/2012-primary/pdf/13-sov-summary.pdf|title=Archived copy|accessdate=February 4, 2013|deadurl=yes|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20121018061728/http://www.sos.ca.gov/elections/sov/2012-primary/pdf/13-sov-summary.pdf|archivedate=October 18, 2012}}</ref>

On November 6, 2012, LaMalfa defeated [[Democratic Party (United States)|Democratic Party]] candidate Jim Reed 57%–43%.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.sos.ca.gov/elections/sov/2012-general/sov-complete.pdf|title=Archived copy|accessdate=June 3, 2013|deadurl=yes|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20121224021529/http://www.sos.ca.gov/elections/sov/2012-general/sov-complete.pdf|archivedate=December 24, 2012}}</ref>

==Political positions==

===Farming===
From 1995 to 2016, LaMalfa was the recipient of the largest amount of money from [[agricultural subsidies]]: over $1.7 million. As a member of the House Agricultural Committee, he oversees farm subsidies. In 2017, his spokesman, Parker Williams, stated that LaMalfa "voted to end direct farm subsidy payments in the very first farm bill he worked on" and that a new farm bill proposed does not provide subsidies for [[rice]] grown in California, a crop that LaMalfa farms.<ref name="Johnson12817"/>

===Immigration ===
LaMalfa has an A+ rating from [[NumbersUSA]], an organization that opposes illegal immigration.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.numbersusa.com/content/my/congress/9494/gradescoresheet|title=Representative Doug LaMalfa, Report Card &#124; NumbersUSA - For Lower Immigration Levels|website=NumbersUSA.com|accessdate=April 3, 2017}}</ref>

===LGBT rights===
In 2014, LaMalfa was named one of the most "anti-[[LGBT]]" politicians in congress by the [[Human Rights Campaign]].<ref name="Peters10714">{{cite web|last1=Peters|first1=Stephen|title=19 Members of Congress Inducted into HRC’s Hall of Shame {{!}} Human Rights Campaign|url=https://www.hrc.org/blog/hall-of-shame|website=Human Rights Campaign|accessdate=December 22, 2017}}</ref> He does not believe in [[same-sex marriage]] and feared that legalizing it would "open the floodgates" for [[polygamy]] to be legalized. He believes marriage is "an institution created by God and supposed to be held up and respected by men. And women."<ref name="Beauchamp3314">{{cite web|last1=Beauchamp|first1=Marc|title=Doug LaMalfa on gay marriage|url=http://www.redding.com/story/opinion/contributors/local-voices/2014/03/03/doug_lamalfa_on/96792428|website=Redding Record Searchlight|accessdate=December 22, 2017}}</ref> He endorsed the [[First Amendment Defense Act]].<ref name="Laslo81915">{{cite web|last1=Laslo|first1=Matt|title=In Wake Of Gay Marriage Ruling, Sacramento-Area Representatives Ponder Next Steps|url=http://www.capradio.org/articles/2015/08/19/in-wake-of-gay-marriage-ruling-sacramento-area-representatives-ponder-next-steps|website=Capital Public Radio|accessdate=December 22, 2017}}</ref>

===Voter fraud===
He stated that "California is just a sieve on its voter security" but does not think his own district had "illegal votes" because it does not "have the demographics that would be a really big push of that."<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.ldnews.com/story/news/local/2017/02/21/live-chat-doug-lamalfa-upbeat-trump/98222416|title=In live chat, Doug LaMalfa upbeat about Trump|work=Lebanon Daily News|access-date=March 27, 2017}}</ref>

=== Gun policy ===
LaMalfa's website states that he is "proud to have earned an A rating and the endorsements of the NRA and Gun Owners of America."<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.douglamalfa.com/issues-and-solutions/|title=Issues and Solutions {{!}} Doug LaMalfa for U.S. Representative|website=www.douglamalfa.com|language=en-US|access-date=2018-03-03}}</ref> He has received $13,500 from the NRA.<ref>{{cite web|title=Open Secrets: Gun Rights|url=https://www.opensecrets.org/industries/summary.php?ind=Q13&recipdetail=H&sortorder=S&cycle=All}}</ref>

===Taxes===
LaMalfa voted in favor of the [[Tax Cuts and Jobs Act of 2017]].<ref name="Almukhtar122117">{{cite web|last1=Almukhtar|first1=Sarah|title=How Each House Member Voted on the Tax Bill|url=https://www.nytimes.com/interactive/2017/12/19/us/politics/tax-bill-house-live-vote.html?_r=0|website=The New York Times|accessdate=December 22, 2017|date=December 19, 2017}}</ref> According to LaMalfa, the bill will enable his constituents to save more money and provide them [[tax relief]]. He says that the tax bill will give a "[[booster shot]] to the U.S. economy". He believes that the bill enable businesses to hire more workers and that there will be more products [[made in the USA]] as a result, especially in Redding.<ref name="Sandhu122017">{{cite web|last1=Sandhu|first1=Amber|title=How the Republican tax bill may affect the North State|url=http://www.redding.com/story/news/local/2017/12/20/how-tax-bill-may-affect-north-state/971836001/|website=Redding Record Searchlight|accessdate=December 22, 2017}}</ref>
