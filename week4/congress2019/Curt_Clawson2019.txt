'''Curtis Jay Clawson''' (born September 28, 1959) is an American politician who was the [[United States House of Representatives|United States Representative]] for [[Florida's 19th congressional district]] from 2014 to 2017. He is the former chief executive of Hayes Lemmerz, a Michigan-based automobile wheel and brakes supplier.<ref name="naples">{{cite news | url=http://www.naplesnews.com/business/exclusive-curt-clawson-business-record-congress-19 | title=NDN exclusive: Curt Clawson touts business acumen, but record is marred | work=Naples Daily News | date=March 12, 2014 | accessdate=July 27, 2014}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |last= |first= |date=April 22, 2014 |title=Ex-C.E.O. Wins Florida Primary for House Seat |url=https://www.nytimes.com/2014/04/23/us/politics/ex-ceo-wins-florida-primary-for-house-seat.html |newspaper=New York Times |location=New York City |publisher=New York Times Company |accessdate=April 23, 2014}}</ref>

==Education and career==

Clawson attended [[Batesville High School (Indiana)|Batesville High School]] in [[Batesville, Indiana]].  A high school basketball star, he was recruited by [[Gene Keady]].<ref name="CJC"/> After attending the [[University of Utah]] for one year and serving a mission for [[The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints]], Clawson transferred to [[Purdue University]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.news-press.com/story/news/politics/2014/03/29/curt-clawson-businessmans-life-has-been-series-of-turnarounds/7071977/ |title=Curt Clawson: Businessman's life has been series of turnarounds |publisher=News-press.com |date=March 29, 2014 |accessdate=April 10, 2018}}</ref>  At Purdue, he was a 2x All-Academic Big Ten selection (1982–83 and 1983–84).  He was a team captain for the 1983–84 Big Ten Champions, was a member of 2x NCAA teams (1982–83 and 1983–84) and an NIT Finalist team (1981–82).<ref name="TampaTimesLeary04222014"/>  He graduated in 1984 with a BA in Spanish and a BS from the [[Krannert School of Management]].<ref name="CJC"/>  He was named a "Purdue Old Master" in 2010 and received the Distinguished Alumni Award in 2014<ref name="CJC">[http://www.cla.purdue.edu/alumni/awards/distinguishedalumni/Recipients/Clawson.html Curtis J. Clawson : College of Liberal Arts : Purdue University<!-- Bot generated title -->] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140425081616/http://www.cla.purdue.edu/alumni/awards/distinguishedalumni/Recipients/Clawson.html |date=April 25, 2014 }}</ref>

In 1990, he earned an MBA from [[Harvard University]].<ref name="CJC"/>

Before his election to Congress, Clawson spent a quarter-century as an executive with several industrial firms, working at [[AlliedSignal]], [[American National Can]] and Hayes Lemmerz.

==U.S. House of Representatives==

===Elections===

====2014====

=====Special=====
{{main|Florida's 19th congressional district special election, 2014}}
Clawson was the [[Republican Party (United States)|Republican Party]] nominee in a [[Florida's 19th congressional district special election, 2014|special election]] to fill the seat being vacated by [[Trey Radel]].<ref name="WashPostSullivan04232014">Sullivan, Sean. [https://www.washingtonpost.com/blogs/post-politics/wp/2014/04/22/curt-clawson-wins-republican-nomination-in-florida-special-election/ Curt Clawson wins Republican nomination in Florida special election], ''Washington Post'', April 23, 2014.</ref> and won the election on June 24, 2014. In the April 22, 2014 Republican primary—the real contest in this heavily Republican district—Clawson defeated State Senate Majority Leader [[Lizbeth Benacquisto]] and former State Representative [[Paige Kreegel]] with 38% of the vote to Benacquisto's 26% and Kreegel's 25%.<ref name="WashPostSullivan04232014"/>  Clawson was endorsed in the primary by the [[Tea Party Express]].<ref name="TampaTimesLeary04222014">{{cite news |last=Leary |first=Alex |date=April 22, 2014 |title=Tea party candidate Curt Clawson wins Republican primary to replace former Rep. Trey Radel |url=http://www.tampabay.com/news/politics/national/tea-party-candidate-curt-clawson-wins-republican-primary-to-replace-former/2176397 |newspaper=Tampa Bay Times |location=Tampa |publisher=Times Publishing Company |accessdate=April 23, 2014 }}</ref>  He spent $2 million on advertising and in one of his ads he challenged U.S. President [[Barack Obama]] to a game of one on one basketball.<ref name="TampaTimesLeary04222014"/>

=====General=====
{{main|United States House of Representatives elections in Florida, 2014#District 19}}
Clawson won a full term in November 2014 with 64 percent of the vote.

===Tenure===
Clawson delivered the [[Tea Party movement|Tea Party]] response to President Obama's [[2015 State of the Union Address|State of the Union Address]] in 2015.<ref>Topaz, Jonathan (January 20, 2015) – [http://www.politico.com/story/2015/01/state-of-the-union-2015-tea-party-response-114427.html?ml=m_ms "Tea Party Response to Obama Hits Soft Tones"]. ''POLITICO''. Retrieved January 21, 2015.</ref>

In May 2016, Clawson announced that he would not seek re-election that year, citing the death of his mother in the summer of 2015 and how it "affected him and his father more deeply than he had thought it would."  "With the passing of my mom, it’s a good time to show support for my dad and be close to (him),” Clawson said.<ref>{{cite web|last=King|first=Ledyard|title=U.S. Rep. Curt Clawson won't seek re-election|url=http://www.news-press.com/story/news/2016/05/19/us-rep-curt-clawson-wont-seek-re-election/84625998/|work=[[The News-Press]]|date=May 20, 2016|accessdate=June 17, 2016}}</ref>

Clawson is a member of the [[Congressional Constitution Caucus]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Members|author=|url=https://congressionalconstitutioncaucus-garrett.house.gov/about-us/membership|format=|publisher=Congressional Constitution Caucus|date=|accessdate=May 8, 2018}}</ref>
