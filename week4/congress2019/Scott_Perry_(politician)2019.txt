'''Scott Gordon Perry''' (born May 27, 1962)<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.washingtontimes.com/campaign-2012/candidates/scott-gordon-perry-61618 |title=Scott Gordon Perry |publisher=Washington Times |agency=Associated Press |access-date=December 15, 2015 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140722195648/http://www.washingtontimes.com/campaign-2012/candidates/scott-gordon-perry-61618/ |archive-date=July 22, 2014 |dead-url=yes }}</ref> is the [[United States House of Representatives|U.S Representative]] for {{ushr|PA|4}}, serving since 2013. A member of the [[Republican Party (United States)|Republican Party]], he previously served as a member of the [[Pennsylvania House of Representatives]] for the [[Pennsylvania House of Representatives, District 92|92nd legislative district]] (2007–2013). Perry is also a [[Brigadier general (United States)|Brigadier General]] in the [[Pennsylvania Army National Guard]].

==Early life and education==
Perry was born in San Diego, and his family moved to Pennsylvania when he was seven years old.<ref>{{cite news |last=Neff |first=Blake |date=February 3, 2014 |title=Perry's hard road to Capitol Hill |url=http://thehill.com/capital-living/new-member-of-the-week/197212-perrys-hard-road-to-capitol-hill |work=The Hill |location=Washington, DC |ref={{sfnRef|"Perry's hard road to Capitol Hill"}}}}</ref> His mother and stepfather often struggled to find work, and their house had no running water nor electricity.{{sfn|"Perry's hard road to Capitol Hill"}}  Perry and his brother began working at an early age to help supplement the family income, and from age 13 until he was in his 20s his jobs included fruit picker, draftsman, dockworker, and insurance agent.{{sfn|"Perry's hard road to Capitol Hill"}}  In 1980, he graduated from [[Northern High School (Dillsburg, Pennsylvania)|Northern High School]] and the [[Northern York County School District|Cumberland-Perry Vo-Tech School]].<ref name=bio>{{cite web|title=Rep. Scott Perry bio|publisher=Pennsylvania House Republican Caucus |year=2008|url=http://www.repperry.com/?sectionid=2&sectiontree=2|access-date=January 11, 2009}}</ref> In 1991, he graduated from [[Pennsylvania State University]], where he earned a Bachelor of Science degree in Business Administration Management. In July 2012, he earned a master's degree in strategic planning from the [[United States Army War College]].<ref name=votesmart>{{cite web |title=Representative Scott Perry profile|work=Project Vote Smart|publisher=Project Vote Smart|year=2008|url=http://www.votesmart.org/bio.php?can_id=59980|access-date=January 9, 2009}}</ref>

==Military service==
===Army National Guard===
Perry began his military career in 1980 when he enlisted in the [[Pennsylvania Army National Guard]].<ref name=dvids>{{cite web |url=https://www.dvidshub.net/news/181997/pa-army-national-guard-names-new-general |title=Pa. Army National Guard names new general |publisher=[[DVIDS|Defense Video Imagery Distribution System]] |date=November 15, 2015 |access-date=June 22, 2017 |first=Neil |last=Gussman }}</ref> He attended basic training at [[Fort Dix, New Jersey]],<ref name=dvids /> and graduated from [[Advanced Individual Training]]<ref>{{cite web |url=https://votesmart.org/candidate/biography/59980/scott-perry |title=Scott Perry's Biography |access-date=June 22, 2017 |publisher=Vote Smart }}</ref> at [[Fort Belvoir, Virginia]] as a technical drafting specialist.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://gotyour6.org/served-our-country-in-the-military-and-now-in-office-congressman-elect-scott-perry/ |title=Served Our Country in the Military and Now in Office – Congressman-Elect Scott Perry |access-date=June 22, 2017 |date=December 13, 2012 |website=gotyour6.org }}</ref> He graduated from Pennsylvania's [[Officer Candidate School]] and was commissioned a [[second lieutenant]] in the Field Artillery.<ref name=dvids />

After receiving his commission, Perry qualified as a helicopter pilot in the Aviation branch.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.nationalguard.mil/Leadership/NGB-GOMO/bio-show/2933/ |title=Biography, Brigadier General Scott Perry |date=2015 |website=National Guard General Officer Management Office |publisher=National Guard Bureau |location=Arlington, VA |access-date=June 2, 2018 |ref={{sfnRef|"Biography, Brigadier General Scott Perry"}}}}</ref>  He served in a variety of staff and command assignments as he advanced through the ranks, including executive officer of 1st Battalion, 104th Cavalry Regiment during deployment to [[Bosnia and Herzegovina]] in 2002–2003, and commander of 2nd Battalion (General Support), 104th Aviation Regiment beginning in 2008.{{sfn|"Biography, Brigadier General Scott Perry"}}

===War in Iraq===
In 2009–2010, Perry commanded 2nd Battalion, 104th Aviation Regiment during its pre-deployment training and service in Iraq for [[Operation Iraqi Freedom]].{{sfn|"Biography, Brigadier General Scott Perry"}} As Task Force Diablo, 2-104th Aviation was credited with flying 1,400 missions, accruing over 13,000 combat flight hours, and transporting over 3 million pounds of cargo and 43,000 soldiers and civilians.{{sfn|"Biography, Brigadier General Scott Perry"}}  Perry was credited with flying 44 missions and accruing nearly 200 combat flight hours.{{sfn|"Biography, Brigadier General Scott Perry"}}

===Post-Iraq===
After returning from Iraq, Perry was promoted to colonel and assigned to command the Pennsylvania National Guard's 166th Regiment (Regional Training Institute) (2010–2012).{{sfn|"Biography, Brigadier General Scott Perry"}}  From 2012 to 2014, Perry commanded the garrison at the [[Fort Indiantown Gap]] National Training Center.{{sfn|"Biography, Brigadier General Scott Perry"}}  In May 2014, Perry was assigned as one of the assistant division commanders of the [[28th Infantry Division (United States)|28th Infantry Division]], and he was promoted to [[Brigadier general (United States)|brigadier general]] in November 2015.<ref name=dvids />  In May 2016, Perry was assigned as assistant adjutant general for Army at the Pennsylvania National Guard's Joint Force Headquarters.{{sfn|"Biography, Brigadier General Scott Perry"}}

==Hydrotech==
In 1993, Perry founded Hydrotech Mechanical Services, Inc., a mechanical contracting firm in [[Dillsburg, Pennsylvania|Dillsburg]]. The firm provides contract construction and maintenance services to municipal and investor-owned utilities from [[North Carolina]] to [[New York (state)|New York]] specializing in large meter calibration. In 2002, the [[Pennsylvania Department of Environmental Protection]] accused the company of altering sewage monitoring reports while doing work for the Memphord Estates Sewage Treatment Company. Perry faced charges of conspiring to falsify state-mandated sewage records. Upon review of the situation and circumstances, he was allowed to complete a diversion program and avoid any charges, which allowed him to keep his U.S security clearance.<ref>{{cite news|last1=Trimmer|first1=Eric|title=Candidate emerges as Smith's successor|url=http://www.eveningsun.com/localnews/ci_3365139|access-date=8 October 2014|publisher=The Hanover Evening Sun|date=January 2, 2006}}</ref> Perry maintains his innocence.<ref>{{cite news|last1=Scolforo|first1=Mark|title=Arrest records of state lawmakers raise questions of standards|url=http://www.post-gazette.com/news/politics-state/2010/11/14/Arrest-records-of-state-lawmakers-raise-questions-of-standards/stories/201011140255|access-date=October 8, 2014|publisher=Pittsburgh Post-Gazette|date=November 14, 2010}}</ref>

==Pennsylvania House of Representatives==

===Elections===
In 2006, State Representative [[Bruce Smith (Pennsylvania politician)|Bruce Smith]] of Pennsylvania's 92nd House District decided to retire. Perry won the Republican primary with 41% of the vote.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=277819 |title=PA State House 092 – R Primary Race – May 16, 2006 |publisher=Our Campaigns |date= |access-date=December 15, 2015}}</ref> He won the general election with 71% of the vote.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=291742 |title=PA State House 092 Race – Nov 07, 2006 |publisher=Our Campaigns |date= |access-date=December 15, 2015}}</ref> He took office on January 2, 2007.<ref>{{cite web|title=SESSION OF 2007 191ST OF THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY No. 1|work=LEGISLATIVE JOURNAL |publisher =Pennsylvania House of Representatives|date=January 2, 2007 |url=http://www.legis.state.pa.us/WU01/LI/HJ/2007/0/20070102.PDF|access-date=January 9, 2009}}</ref> In 2008, he won re-election to a second term unopposed.<ref name="ourcampaigns.com">{{cite web |url=http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=440799 |title=PA State House 092 Race – Nov 04, 2008 |publisher=Our Campaigns |date= |access-date=December 15, 2015}}</ref> In 2010, he won re-election to a third term unopposed.<ref name="ourcampaigns.com"/>

==U.S. House of Representatives==
Perry is a member of the [[Freedom Caucus]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://blogs.rollcall.com/218/house-freedom-caucus-forms-fight-club/|title=House Freedom Caucus Forms 'Fight Club' in House|work=218|date=July 22, 2015|access-date=February 7, 2018}}</ref>

In January 2018, Perry suggested that ISIS may have committed the [[2017 Las Vegas shooting]], in contrast to local and federal law enforcement officials' assertions that it was committed by Stephen Paddock who had no ISIS affiliation.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://thehill.com/homenews/house/369672-gop-lawmaker-somethings-not-adding-up-on-las-vegas-shooting|title=GOP lawmaker: 'Something's not adding up' on Las Vegas shooting|last=Samuels|first=Brett|date=2018-01-18|work=TheHill|access-date=2018-01-19}}</ref>

===Elections===
;2012
{{Main|United States House of Representatives elections in Pennsylvania, 2012#District 4}}
In 2012, Perry gave up his state house seat to run for the 4th congressional district. The district had previously been the 19th District, represented by six-term incumbent Republican [[Todd Platts]], who was giving up the seat to honor a self-imposed term limit. In 2010, when Platts wanted to become the [[Comptroller General of the United States|U.S. Comptroller General]], he spoke to Perry about his running for the seat.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ydr.com/politics/ci_19779324 |title=USA TODAY: Latest World and US News – USATODAY.com |publisher=Ydr.com |date= |access-date=December 15, 2015}}</ref>

Perry won a seven-way primary with over 50% of the vote.  Although being outspent nearly 2 to 1 throughout the campaign he was able to beat his closest competitor on election day with nearly 3 times as many votes.

On November 6, 2012, Perry defeated [[Democratic Party (US)|Democrat]] Harry Perkinson 60%–34%.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://elections.nytimes.com/2012/results/house |title=House Map – Election 2012 – NYTimes.com |publisher=Elections.nytimes.com |date= |access-date=December 15, 2015}}</ref>

;2014
{{Main|United States House of Representatives elections in Pennsylvania, 2014#District 4}}
In 2014, Perry was unchallenged in the Republican primary. His Democratic party challenger in the general election was former mayor of [[Harrisburg, Pennsylvania|Harrisburg]], [[Linda D. Thompson]].  Perry won the general election 75%–25%.<ref name=PAgeneral>{{cite web |url=http://www.electionreturns.state.pa.us/Default.aspx?EID=41&ESTID=2&CID=0&OID=11&CDID=0&PID=0&DISTID=0&IsSpecial=0&PageRefID=1 |title=Pennsylvania 2014 General Election – November 4, 2014 Official Results |publisher=Pennsylvania Secretary of State |date=November 4, 2014 |access-date=January 16, 2015 |deadurl=yes |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20150215091446/http://www.electionreturns.state.pa.us/Default.aspx?EID=41&ESTID=2&CID=0&OID=11&CDID=0&PID=0&DISTID=0&IsSpecial=0&PageRefID=1 |archivedate=February 15, 2015 |df= }}</ref>

;2016
{{Main|United States House of Representatives elections in Pennsylvania, 2016#District 4}}
Perry was unchallenged in the 2016 Republican primary. His Democratic Party challenger in the general election was Joshua Burkholder of Harrisburg.<ref name=statusquo>{{cite web |url=http://www.pennlive.com/news/2016/02/pas_congressional_races_like_s.html|title=Pa's Congressional race lineup: Like status quo? Voters will get chance to keep it.|work=[[The Patriot-News]] |last=Thompson|first=Charles|date=February 17, 2016|access-date=February 18, 2016}}</ref> Perry won the election 66%–34%.<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.cnn.com/election/results/states/pennsylvania/house/04|title=Full 2016 election results: Pennsylvania House 04|website=www.cnn.com|access-date=November 12, 2016}}</ref>
