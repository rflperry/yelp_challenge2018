'''Ludmya Bourdeau "Mia" Love''' (born December 6, 1975) is an Haitian-American politician and the [[United States House of Representatives|U.S. Representative]]-elect for [[Utah's 4th congressional district]]. Previously the Mayor of [[Saratoga Springs, Utah]] from 2010 to 2014, Love will be the first [[Haitian American]] and the first black female Republican in Congress,<ref name="Will">{{cite news |url= http://www.newsmax.com/GeorgeWill/Utah-Love-Black-Caucus/2012/09/24/id/457357/ |title= Utah's Mia Love Battles Stereotypes |first= George |last= Will |date= September 24, 2012 |work= Newsmax.com |publisher= [[Newsmax Media]] |archive-url= http://web.archive.org/web/20141104235235/http://www.newsmax.com/GeorgeWill/Utah-Love-Black-Caucus/2012/09/24/id/457357/ |archive-date= 2014-11-04}}</ref><ref name="Fox 2012-08-28">{{cite news |url= http://www.foxnews.com/politics/2012/08/28/republican-convention-to-feature-rising-star-mia-love/ |title= Rising GOP star Mia Love glides into the spotlight at convention |publisher= [[Fox News Channel|Fox News]] |date= August 28, 2012 |archive-url= http://web.archive.org/web/20141105161139/http://www.foxnews.com/politics/2012/08/28/republican-convention-to-feature-rising-star-mia-love/ |archive-date= 2014-11-05}}</ref> as well as the first black woman from Utah in Congress.<ref>{{citation |url= http://www.cawp.rutgers.edu/press_room/news/documents/PressRelease_11-05-14-electionresults.pdf |title= 2014: Not a Landmark Year for Women, Despite Some Notable Firsts |type= Press Release |work= CAWP Election Watch |publisher= The Center for American Women and Politics, [[Eagleton Institute of Politics]], [[Rutgers University]] |date= November 21, 2014 |archive-url= http://web.archive.org/web/20141124221853/http://www.cawp.rutgers.edu/press_room/news/documents/PressRelease_11-05-14-electionresults.pdf |archive-date= 2014-11-24}}</ref>

Love was the Republican nominee for the 4th congressional district in 2012, losing narrowly to incumbent Democratic Representative [[Jim Matheson]]. She was also a speaker at the [[2012 Republican National Convention]]. On May 18, 2013, Love said that she would run again in 2014.<ref>{{cite news |last= Roche |first= Lisa Riley |title= Mia Love announces she's officially running against Matheson — again |newspaper= Deseret News |date= May 20, 2013 |url= http://www.deseretnews.com/article/865580306/Mia-Love-announces-shes-officially-running-against-Matheson-2-again.html?pg=all |archive-url= http://web.archive.org/web/20141105161457/http://www.deseretnews.com/article/865580306/Mia-Love-announces-shes-officially-running-against-Matheson-2-again.html?pg=all |archive-date= 2014-11-05}}</ref> She won the Republican nomination at the April 26, 2014 Utah Republican convention and was elected to the House of Representatives on November 4, 2014, defeating Democratic opponent [[Doug Owens]].<ref name="Will"/><ref name="Fox 2012-08-28"/>

==Early political career==
Love served as the community spokesperson in an effort to get the developer of her neighborhood in Saratoga Springs to spray against flies.<ref name="From Broadway to Capitol Hill"/>

===Saratoga Springs City Council===
Love won a seat on the [[Saratoga Springs, Utah|Saratoga Springs City Council]] (Utah County) in 2003, becoming the first female Haitian-American elected official in [[Utah County, Utah]]; she took office in January 2004.<ref name="Haddock 2004-09-27"/> After six years on the Council she was elected Mayor,<ref name="Levinson 2012-01-14">{{cite news |last= Levinson |first= Alexis |url= http://dailycaller.com/2012/01/14/aspiring-first-black-gop-congresswoman-dont-put-me-in-a-box|title=Aspiring first black GOP congresswoman: Don't put me in a box |newspaper= [[The Daily Caller]] |date= January 14, 2012 |archive-url= http://web.archive.org/web/20141105071330/http://dailycaller.com/2012/01/14/aspiring-first-black-gop-congresswoman-dont-put-me-in-a-box |archive-date= 2014-11-05 |deadurl= no }}</ref> winning with 861 votes to 594 for her opponent Jeff Francom.<ref>{{cite news |url= http://www.sltrib.com/news/ci_13741120 |first= Donald W. |last= Myers |title= Mia Love: Race not a factor for Utah's first black female mayor-elect |newspaper= The Salt Lake Tribune |date= November 9, 2009 |archive-url= http://web.archive.org/web/20141104233332/http://www.sltrib.com/news/ci_13741120|archive-date=2014-11-04 |deadurl= no }}</ref>

===Mayor of Saratoga Springs===
Love served as Mayor of Saratoga Springs from January 2010 to January 2014.<ref name="Mayor Mia Love"/> She was part of the city council that approved a transition from the agriculture tax to municipal tax. She worked with other city council members to cut expenses, reducing the city's shortfall during the economic downturn from $3.5 million to $779,000. Saratoga Springs now has the highest possible bond rating for a city of its size.<ref>{{cite news |last= Rolly |first= Paul |url= http://www.sltrib.com/52932194-82/republican-tax-love-congressional.html |title= Mia Love causing a GOP stir nationally |newspaper= The Salt Lake Tribune |date= November 19, 2011 |archive-url= http://web.archive.org/web/20141104233419/http://www.sltrib.com/52932194-82/republican-tax-love-congressional.html |archive-date= 2014-11-04 |deadurl= no }}</ref>

During her term as mayor, Love led the city through natural disasters including a wild fire which was followed shortly afterward by a severe mudslide.<ref>{{cite news |url= http://www.heraldextra.com/news/local/north/saratoga-springs/mia-love-thankful-for-chance-to-serve-saratoga-springs/article_cf8c7b2a-a59d-5cb6-8160-f7cc83ce5d8f.html |title =Mia Love thankful for chance to serve Saratoga Springs |newspaper= Provo Daily Herald |first= Cathy |last= Allred |date= December 26, 2013 |archive-url= http://web.archive.org/web/20131229000800/http://www.heraldextra.com/news/local/north/saratoga-springs/mia-love-thankful-for-chance-to-serve-saratoga-springs/article_cf8c7b2a-a59d-5cb6-8160-f7cc83ce5d8f.html |archive-date= 2013-12-29 |dead-url= no }}</ref>

===2012 congressional election===
{{See also|United States House of Representatives elections in Utah, 2012#District 4}}
Love ran in [[Utah's 4th congressional district]], which was created after the 2010 Census.<ref>{{cite news |last= Gehrke |first= Robert |title= Saratoga Springs Mayor Mia Love to kick off campaign |url= http://www.sltrib.com/53233798-90/love-mayor-district-congressional.html |newspaper= The Salt Lake Tribune |date= January 4, 2012 |archive-url= http://web.archive.org/web/20141104233638/http://www.sltrib.com/53233798-90/love-mayor-district-congressional.html |archive-date= 2014-11-04 |deadurl= no }}</ref><ref>{{cite news |last= Saulny |first= Susan |title= Black Mormons and the Politics of Identity |url =http://www.nytimes.com/2012/05/23/us/for-black-mormons-a-political-choice-like-no-other.html |newspaper= [[The New York Times]] |date= May 22, 2012 |archive-url= http://web.archive.org/web/20141105064855/http://www.nytimes.com/2012/05/23/us/for-black-mormons-a-political-choice-like-no-other.html|archive-date= 2014-11-05 |deadurl= no }}</ref> She competed for the Republican nomination against attorney Jay Cobb and State Reps. [[Stephen Sandstrom]] of [[Orem, Utah|Orem]] and [[Carl Wimmer]] of [[Herriman, Utah|Herriman]]; she won the nomination on April 21, 2012 at the 2012 Utah Republican Party Convention with over 70 percent of the vote and faced six-term Democratic incumbent [[Jim Matheson]] in the general election.

Nationally, Love received campaign support from [[Mitt Romney presidential campaign, 2012|2012 Republican Presidential nominee]] [[Mitt Romney]] and his wife [[Ann Romney]], [[House Majority Leader]] [[Eric Cantor]], [[House Budget Committee]] Chairman and [[Paul Ryan#2012 Vice Presidential campaign|2012 Republican Vice Presidential nominee]] [[Paul Ryan]] and Speaker of the House [[John Boehner]].<ref>{{cite news |last= Canham |first= Matt |url= http://www.sltrib.com/53424815-90/chaffetz-love-campaign-wimmer.html |title= Chaffetz helps Love with Washington introductions, but says he won't endorse |newspaper= The Salt Lake Tribune |date= February 1, 2012 |archive-url= http://web.archive.org/web/20141104233736/http://www.sltrib.com/53424815-90/chaffetz-love-campaign-wimmer.html |archive-date= 2014-11-04 |deadurl= no }}</ref><ref>{{cite news |url= http://www.sltrib.com/54609778-90/boehner-campaign-center-district.html |title= Love getting boost from national GOP stars |first= Robert |last= Gehrke |newspaper= [[The Salt Lake Tribune]] |date= 2012-08-01 |archive-url= http://web.archive.org/web/20141104233843/http://www.sltrib.com/54609778-90/boehner-campaign-center-district.html |archive-date= 2014-11-04 |deadurl= no }}</ref>  

In August 2012, ''[[National Journal]]'' named Love one of "Ten Republicans to follow on Twitter".<ref>{{cite web |last= Mazmanian |first= Adam |url= http://www.nationaljournal.com/daily/ten-republicans-to-follow-on-twitter-20120826 |title= Ten Republicans to follow on Twitter |work= NationalJournal.com |publisher= [[National Journal]] |date= August 27, 2012 |archive-url= http://web.archive.org/web/20141105175457/http://www.nationaljournal.com/daily/ten-republicans-to-follow-on-twitter-20120826 |archive-date= 2014-11-05 |deadurl= no }}</ref> She was a speaker at the [[2012 Republican National Convention]] on August 28, 2012. She spoke of the lessons she learned from her parents.<ref>{{cite news |url= http://www.deseretnews.com/article/765600023/Utah-congressional-candidate-gives-speech-at-RNC.html |title= 'Revive' America, Utah congressional candidate Mia Love tells RNC, draws rousing cheers |newspaper= Deseret News |agency= [[Associated Press|AP]] |date= August 28, 2012 |archive-url= http://web.archive.org/web/20141105180124/http://www.deseretnews.com/article/765600023/Utah-congressional-candidate-gives-speech-at-RNC.html |archive-date= 2014-11-05 |deadurl= no }}</ref>  In her speech she also stated, "Mr. President, I am here to tell you we are not buying what you are selling in 2012."<ref name="NatlJMiaLoveNov2014">{{citation |url= http://www.nationaljournal.com/almanac/2014-new-members/utah-4-mia-love-r-20141105 |contribution= Utah-4: Mia Love (R) |title= Almanac: 2014 New members of Congress |publisher= [[National Journal]] |archive-url= http://web.archive.org/web/20141124220003/http://www.nationaljournal.com/almanac/2014-new-members/utah-4-mia-love-r-20141105 |archive-date= 2014-11-24 |deadurl= no }}</ref>

In September 2012, ''[[Forbes]]'' investigated a claim in a September 2012 article by ''[[Mother Jones (magazine)|Mother Jones]]'' that no law then existed which would have allowed Love's parents to become citizens of the United States. After ''[[Forbes]]'' investigated the ''Mother Jones'' claim and identified the law in question, ''Mother Jones'' was forced to issue a correction.<ref name="MJ"/><ref name="Anderson 2012-09-28"/><ref>{{cite news |last= Gehrke |first= Robert |title =Love's story of immigrant parents called into question |url= http://www.sltrib.com/54960191-78/campaign-citizenship-family-haiti.html |newspaper= The Salt Lake Tribune |date= September 24, 2012 |archive-url= http://web.archive.org/web/20141104233958/http://www.sltrib.com/54960191-78/campaign-citizenship-family-haiti.html |archive-date= 2014-11-04 |dead-url= no }}</ref> In an October interview, her father stated that Mia's birth as a US citizen was key to gaining permanent legal status and citizenship.<ref name="From Broadway to Capitol Hill"/>

Love lost the election to [[Jim Matheson]] by 768 votes out of 245,277 votes cast,<ref>{{citation |url= http://elections.utah.gov/Media/Default/2012%20Canvass/2012%20General%20Canvass%20Report.xlsx |title= Utah Lieutenant Governor's Office, General Canvass Report, 2012 |work= elections.utah.gov |format= [[XLSX]] formated [[spreadsheet]] }}</ref> a difference of 0.31%. She was regarded to have run a weak campaign, switching campaign managers three times, trying to "nationalize" the race rather than focus on local issues and missing interviews and appointments because of rifts in her campaign staff.<ref name=tangles>{{cite news |url= http://www.sltrib.com/54522813-82/campaign-love-republican-congressional |title= Mia Love's campaign is all in tangles |newspaper= The Salt Lake Tribune |first= Paul |last= Rolly |date= July 21, 2012  |archive-url= http://web.archive.org/web/20141212210722/http://www.sltrib.com/54522813-82/campaign-love-republican-congressional |archive-date= 2014-12-12 |deadurl= no }}</ref>

On December 17, 2013, Matheson announced that he would not run for re-election, leading Love to be the favored candidate to win in the upcoming election. In early October 2014, the [[National Journal]] listed Utah's 4th district as the number one most likely district to change hands in November.<ref>{{cite news |url= http://www.nationaljournal.com/hotline/latest-edition/the-hotline-s-house-race-rankings-the-30-districts-most-likely-to-change-hands-in-november-20141008 |title= The Hotline's House Race Rankings: The 30 Districts Most Likely to Change Hands in November |first= Jack |last= Fitzpatrick |date= October 8, 2014 |work= National Journal Hotline |publisher=[[National Journal]] |archive-url= http://web.archive.org/web/20141105201933/http://www.nationaljournal.com/hotline/latest-edition/the-hotline-s-house-race-rankings-the-30-districts-most-likely-to-change-hands-in-november-20141008 |archive-date= 2014-11-05 |deadurl= no }}</ref>

In early 2014 Love was made a member of the Republican National Committee's National Advisory Council on African-American outreach.<ref>{{cite news |url= http://flcourier.com/2014/03/06/rncs-advisory-councils-to-focus-on-minority-communities |date= March 6, 2014 |newspaper= Florida Courier |title= RNC's advisory councils to focus on minority communities |archive-url= http://web.archive.org/web/20141105202243/http://flcourier.com/2014/03/06/rncs-advisory-councils-to-focus-on-minority-communities |archive-date= 2014-11-05 |deadurl= no }}</ref>

On April 26, 2014 Love won the Republican nomination for the 4th congressional district at the Utah Republican Convention, with 78% of the vote at the convention.<ref name="NatlJMiaLoveNov2014"/><ref>{{cite news |url= http://www.breitbart.com/Big-Government/2014/04/26/Mia-Love-Wins-GOP-Nomination-for-Congress |title= Mia Love Wins GOP Nomination for Congress |first= Warner Todd |last= Huston |date= April 27, 2014 |newspaper= [[Breitbart (website)|Breitbart]] |archive-url= http://web.archive.org/web/20141105093252/http://www.breitbart.com/Big-Government/2014/04/26/Mia-Love-Wins-GOP-Nomination-for-Congress |archive-date= 2014-11-05 |deadurl= no }}</ref> On election night, Owens led Love until late on, when she pulled ahead and ultimately won by just over 4,000 votes.<ref name="UtahLtGovElectRes11052014">{{citation |title= Utah Election Results US Congressional District #4 |url= http://electionresults.utah.gov/elections/uscongress/4|work=electionresults.utah.gov |archive-url= http://web.archive.org/web/20141105202824/http://electionresults.utah.gov/elections/uscongress/4 |archive-date= 2014-11-05 |deadurl= no }}</ref><ref name="NatlJMiaLoveNov2014"/> Love, who outspent Owens by more than five-to-one, was considered to have underperformed in the district. Owens has said that he may run against Love in 2016.<ref>{{cite news |url= http://www.sltrib.com/1913878-155/a-love-owens-rematch-democrat-says-it |title= A Love-Owens rematch? Democrat says it could happen |newspaper= The Salt Lake Tribune |first= Matt |last= Canham |date= December 5, 2014 |archive-url= http://web.archive.org/web/20141212211054/http://www.sltrib.com/1913878-155/a-love-owens-rematch-democrat-says-it |archive-date= 2014-12-12 |dead-url= no }}</ref>

==Political positions==
A [[Tea Party movement|Tea Party]] conservative,<ref name=tangles/> Love says she favors "fiscal discipline, limited government, and personal responsibility."<ref name="Levinson 2012-01-14"/> She has also said that she asks herself three questions whenever she approaches an issue: "Is it affordable? Is it sustainable? Is it my job?"<ref>{{cite news |url= http://www.latimes.com/news/politics/la-pn-mia-love-breaks-the-gop-mold-but-can-she-win-20120525,0,6232231,full.story |title= Mia Love breaks the GOP mold, but can she win? |newspaper= LA Times |first= Mark Z. |last= Barabak |date= May 29, 2012 |archive-url= http://web.archive.org/web/20120819094443/http://www.latimes.com/news/politics/la-pn-mia-love-breaks-the-gop-mold-but-can-she-win-20120525,0,6232231,full.story |archive-date= 2012-08-19 |deadurl= yes}}</ref> At her college orientation, Love's father told her: "Mia, your mother and I never took a handout. You will not be a burden to society. You will give back." She underscored this philosophy on the campaign trail to sum up her conservative views.<ref>{{cite news |first= Harry |last= Bruinius |date= November 5, 2014 |title= Mia Love, first black Republican woman in Congress, is 'solid gold' for GOP |url= http://www.csmonitor.com/USA/Elections/House/2014/1105/Mia-Love-first-black-Republican-woman-in-Congress-is-solid-gold-for-GOP-video |newspaper= [[The Christian Science Monitor]] |archive-url= http://web.archive.org/web/20141202220003/http://www.csmonitor.com/USA/Elections/House/2014/1105/Mia-Love-first-black-Republican-woman-in-Congress-is-solid-gold-for-GOP-video |archivedate= 2014-12-02 |deadurl= no }}</ref>

===Spending and taxes===
Love proposes deep cuts to [[United States federal budget|federal spending]], particularly in the area of [[entitlement]] spending ([[Social Security (United States)|Social Security]], [[Medicare (United States)|Medicare]] and [[Medicaid]]).<ref name=tangles/> She also supports cutting taxes.<ref name=tangles/>

===Other issues===
She supports domestic energy exploration, local control of education, [[Second Amendment to the United States Constitution|Second Amendment]] rights, and state control of public lands.<ref>{{citation |url= http://www.love4utah.com/issues1 |contribution= Issues |title= Mia Love for Congress |publisher= Friends of Mia Love |archive-url= http://web.archive.org/web/20120823025707/http://www.love4utah.com/issues1|archive-date=2012-08-23 |deadurl= yes}}</ref>

===Congressional Black Caucus===
She has said that if elected to Congress, she would “join the [[Congressional Black Caucus]] and try to take that thing apart from the inside out.” She has described the mainly Democratic Caucus as characterized by “...demagoguery. They sit there and ignite emotions and ignite [[racism]] when there isn’t. They use their positions to instill fear. Hope and change is turned into fear and blame. Fear that everybody is going to lose everything and blaming Congress for everything instead of taking responsibility."<ref>{{cite news |last= Romboy |first= Dennis |url= http://www.deseretnews.com/article/705396842/Love-would-take-apart-Congressional-Black-Caucus-if-elected-in-Utahs-4th-District.html |title= Love would 'take apart' Congressional Black Caucus if elected in Utah's 4th District |newspaper= Deseret News |date= January 5, 2012 |archive-url= http://web.archive.org/web/20141105210314/http://www.deseretnews.com/article/705396842/Love-would-take-apart-Congressional-Black-Caucus-if-elected-in-Utahs-4th-District.html |archive-date= 2014-11-05 |dead-url= no }}</ref>

==Personal life==
Raised a Roman Catholic, Love joined [[The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints]] after graduating college in 1998.<ref>{{cite news |url= http://thegrio.com/2013/12/18/mia-love-poised-to-make-a-political-comeback |title= Mia Love poised to make a political comeback |first= David A. |last= Love |date= December 18, 2013 |publisher= [[The Grio]] |archive-url= http://web.archive.org/web/20141105171805/http://thegrio.com/2013/12/18/mia-love-poised-to-make-a-political-comeback |archive-date= 2014-11-05 |deadurl= no }}</ref> While a flight attendant she moved to Utah as part of her work, but also to be closer to the temple and to learn more about her faith.<ref name="From Broadway to Capitol Hill"/> There she got to know Jason Love, whom she had met briefly when he was an [[Missionary (LDS Church)|LDS missionary]] in [[Connecticut]]. The two were married in December 1998. Love turned down an offer to appear in the Broadway show "[[Smokey Joe's Café]]" that started two days before her marriage.<ref name="From Broadway to Capitol Hill"/><ref>{{cite news |url= http://www.nationalreview.com/corner/296682/daughter-haitian-immigrants-gop-congressional-nominee-utah-john-fund |first= John |last= Fund |author-link= John Fund |title= Daughter of Haitian Immigrants is GOP Congressional Nominee in Utah |journal= National Review Online |publisher= [[National Review]] |date= April 22, 2012 |archive-url= http://web.archive.org/web/20141105172202/http://www.nationalreview.com/corner/296682/daughter-haitian-immigrants-gop-congressional-nominee-utah-john-fund |archive-date= 2014-11-05 |deadurl= no }}</ref> They have three children.

'''Ludmya Bourdeau "Mia" Love''' (born December 6, 1975) is an Haitian-American politician and the [[United States House of Representatives|U.S. Representative]]-elect for [[Utah's 4th congressional district]]. Previously the Mayor of [[Saratoga Springs, Utah]] from 2010 to 2014, Love will be the first [[Haitian American]] and the first black female Republican in Congress,<ref name="Will">{{cite news |url= http://www.newsmax.com/GeorgeWill/Utah-Love-Black-Caucus/2012/09/24/id/457357/ |title= Utah's Mia Love Battles Stereotypes |first= George |last= Will |date= September 24, 2012 |work= Newsmax.com |publisher= [[Newsmax Media]] |archive-url= http://web.archive.org/web/20141104235235/http://www.newsmax.com/GeorgeWill/Utah-Love-Black-Caucus/2012/09/24/id/457357/ |archive-date= 2014-11-04}}</ref><ref name="Fox 2012-08-28">{{cite news |url= http://www.foxnews.com/politics/2012/08/28/republican-convention-to-feature-rising-star-mia-love/ |title= Rising GOP star Mia Love glides into the spotlight at convention |publisher= [[Fox News Channel|Fox News]] |date= August 28, 2012 |archive-url= http://web.archive.org/web/20141105161139/http://www.foxnews.com/politics/2012/08/28/republican-convention-to-feature-rising-star-mia-love/ |archive-date= 2014-11-05}}</ref> as well as the first black woman from Utah in Congress.<ref>{{citation |url= http://www.cawp.rutgers.edu/press_room/news/documents/PressRelease_11-05-14-electionresults.pdf |title= 2014: Not a Landmark Year for Women, Despite Some Notable Firsts |type= Press Release |work= CAWP Election Watch |publisher= The Center for American Women and Politics, [[Eagleton Institute of Politics]], [[Rutgers University]] |date= November 21, 2014 |archive-url= http://web.archive.org/web/20141124221853/http://www.cawp.rutgers.edu/press_room/news/documents/PressRelease_11-05-14-electionresults.pdf |archive-date= 2014-11-24}}</ref>

Love was the Republican nominee for the 4th congressional district in 2012, losing narrowly to incumbent Democratic Representative [[Jim Matheson]]. She was also a speaker at the [[2012 Republican National Convention]]. On May 18, 2013, Love said that she would run again in 2014.<ref>{{cite news |last= Roche |first= Lisa Riley |title= Mia Love announces she's officially running against Matheson — again |newspaper= Deseret News |date= May 20, 2013 |url= http://www.deseretnews.com/article/865580306/Mia-Love-announces-shes-officially-running-against-Matheson-2-again.html?pg=all |archive-url= http://web.archive.org/web/20141105161457/http://www.deseretnews.com/article/865580306/Mia-Love-announces-shes-officially-running-against-Matheson-2-again.html?pg=all |archive-date= 2014-11-05}}</ref> She won the Republican nomination at the April 26, 2014 Utah Republican convention and was elected to the House of Representatives on November 4, 2014, defeating Democratic opponent [[Doug Owens]].<ref name="Will"/><ref name="Fox 2012-08-28"/>

While at the University of Hartford she was part of the Hartt School's Music Theatre program.<ref name="From Broadway to Capitol Hill"/> She worked at Sento Corporation and the [[Ecopass|Ecopass Corporation]].<ref name="Mayor Mia Love">{{citation |url= http://www.saratogaspringscity.com/government/mayorcouncil/mlove.php |contribution= Mayor & City Council: Mayor Mia Love |title= Saratoga Springs official website (SaratogaSpringsCity.com) |publisher= City of [[Saratoga Springs, Utah]] |archive-url= http://web.archive.org/web/20120911160828/http://www.saratogaspringscity.com/government/mayorcouncil/mlove.php |archive-date= 2012-09-11 |deadurl= yes }}</ref> She was also a flight attendant with [[Continental Airlines]].<ref name="Deseret"/><ref name="Haddock 2004-09-27">{{cite news |first= Sharon |last= Haddock |url= http://www.deseretnews.com/article/595094224/Political-novice-lovin-it.html |title= Political Novice Lovin' it |newspaper= Deseret News |date= September 27, 2004 |archive-url= http://web.archive.org/web/20141105172855/http://www.deseretnews.com/article/595094224/Political-novice-lovin-it.html |archive-date= 2014-11-05 |dead-url= no }}</ref>

==Early political career==
Love served as the community spokesperson in an effort to get the developer of her neighborhood in Saratoga Springs to spray against flies.<ref name="From Broadway to Capitol Hill"/>

===Saratoga Springs City Council===
Love won a seat on the [[Saratoga Springs, Utah|Saratoga Springs City Council]] (Utah County) in 2003, becoming the first female Haitian-American elected official in [[Utah County, Utah]]; she took office in January 2004.<ref name="Haddock 2004-09-27"/> After six years on the Council she was elected Mayor,<ref name="Levinson 2012-01-14">{{cite news |last= Levinson |first= Alexis |url= http://dailycaller.com/2012/01/14/aspiring-first-black-gop-congresswoman-dont-put-me-in-a-box|title=Aspiring first black GOP congresswoman: Don't put me in a box |newspaper= [[The Daily Caller]] |date= January 14, 2012 |archive-url= http://web.archive.org/web/20141105071330/http://dailycaller.com/2012/01/14/aspiring-first-black-gop-congresswoman-dont-put-me-in-a-box |archive-date= 2014-11-05 |deadurl= no }}</ref> winning with 861 votes to 594 for her opponent Jeff Francom.<ref>{{cite news |url= http://www.sltrib.com/news/ci_13741120 |first= Donald W. |last= Myers |title= Mia Love: Race not a factor for Utah's first black female mayor-elect |newspaper= The Salt Lake Tribune |date= November 9, 2009 |archive-url= http://web.archive.org/web/20141104233332/http://www.sltrib.com/news/ci_13741120|archive-date=2014-11-04 |deadurl= no }}</ref>

===Mayor of Saratoga Springs===
Love served as Mayor of Saratoga Springs from January 2010 to January 2014.<ref name="Mayor Mia Love"/> She was part of the city council that approved a transition from the agriculture tax to municipal tax. She worked with other city council members to cut expenses, reducing the city's shortfall during the economic downturn from $3.5 million to $779,000. Saratoga Springs now has the highest possible bond rating for a city of its size.<ref>{{cite news |last= Rolly |first= Paul |url= http://www.sltrib.com/52932194-82/republican-tax-love-congressional.html |title= Mia Love causing a GOP stir nationally |newspaper= The Salt Lake Tribune |date= November 19, 2011 |archive-url= http://web.archive.org/web/20141104233419/http://www.sltrib.com/52932194-82/republican-tax-love-congressional.html |archive-date= 2014-11-04 |deadurl= no }}</ref>

During her term as mayor, Love led the city through natural disasters including a wild fire which was followed shortly afterward by a severe mudslide.<ref>{{cite news |url= http://www.heraldextra.com/news/local/north/saratoga-springs/mia-love-thankful-for-chance-to-serve-saratoga-springs/article_cf8c7b2a-a59d-5cb6-8160-f7cc83ce5d8f.html |title =Mia Love thankful for chance to serve Saratoga Springs |newspaper= Provo Daily Herald |first= Cathy |last= Allred |date= December 26, 2013 |archive-url= http://web.archive.org/web/20131229000800/http://www.heraldextra.com/news/local/north/saratoga-springs/mia-love-thankful-for-chance-to-serve-saratoga-springs/article_cf8c7b2a-a59d-5cb6-8160-f7cc83ce5d8f.html |archive-date= 2013-12-29 |dead-url= no }}</ref>

===2012 congressional election===
{{See also|United States House of Representatives elections in Utah, 2012#District 4}}
Love ran in [[Utah's 4th congressional district]], which was created after the 2010 Census.<ref>{{cite news |last= Gehrke |first= Robert |title= Saratoga Springs Mayor Mia Love to kick off campaign |url= http://www.sltrib.com/53233798-90/love-mayor-district-congressional.html |newspaper= The Salt Lake Tribune |date= January 4, 2012 |archive-url= http://web.archive.org/web/20141104233638/http://www.sltrib.com/53233798-90/love-mayor-district-congressional.html |archive-date= 2014-11-04 |deadurl= no }}</ref><ref>{{cite news |last= Saulny |first= Susan |title= Black Mormons and the Politics of Identity |url =http://www.nytimes.com/2012/05/23/us/for-black-mormons-a-political-choice-like-no-other.html |newspaper= [[The New York Times]] |date= May 22, 2012 |archive-url= http://web.archive.org/web/20141105064855/http://www.nytimes.com/2012/05/23/us/for-black-mormons-a-political-choice-like-no-other.html|archive-date= 2014-11-05 |deadurl= no }}</ref> She competed for the Republican nomination against attorney Jay Cobb and State Reps. [[Stephen Sandstrom]] of [[Orem, Utah|Orem]] and [[Carl Wimmer]] of [[Herriman, Utah|Herriman]]; she won the nomination on April 21, 2012 at the 2012 Utah Republican Party Convention with over 70 percent of the vote and faced six-term Democratic incumbent [[Jim Matheson]] in the general election.

Nationally, Love received campaign support from [[Mitt Romney presidential campaign, 2012|2012 Republican Presidential nominee]] [[Mitt Romney]] and his wife [[Ann Romney]], [[House Majority Leader]] [[Eric Cantor]], [[House Budget Committee]] Chairman and [[Paul Ryan#2012 Vice Presidential campaign|2012 Republican Vice Presidential nominee]] [[Paul Ryan]] and Speaker of the House [[John Boehner]].<ref>{{cite news |last= Canham |first= Matt |url= http://www.sltrib.com/53424815-90/chaffetz-love-campaign-wimmer.html |title= Chaffetz helps Love with Washington introductions, but says he won't endorse |newspaper= The Salt Lake Tribune |date= February 1, 2012 |archive-url= http://web.archive.org/web/20141104233736/http://www.sltrib.com/53424815-90/chaffetz-love-campaign-wimmer.html |archive-date= 2014-11-04 |deadurl= no }}</ref><ref>{{cite news |url= http://www.sltrib.com/54609778-90/boehner-campaign-center-district.html |title= Love getting boost from national GOP stars |first= Robert |last= Gehrke |newspaper= [[The Salt Lake Tribune]] |date= 2012-08-01 |archive-url= http://web.archive.org/web/20141104233843/http://www.sltrib.com/54609778-90/boehner-campaign-center-district.html |archive-date= 2014-11-04 |deadurl= no }}</ref>  

In August 2012, ''[[National Journal]]'' named Love one of "Ten Republicans to follow on Twitter".<ref>{{cite web |last= Mazmanian |first= Adam |url= http://www.nationaljournal.com/daily/ten-republicans-to-follow-on-twitter-20120826 |title= Ten Republicans to follow on Twitter |work= NationalJournal.com |publisher= [[National Journal]] |date= August 27, 2012 |archive-url= http://web.archive.org/web/20141105175457/http://www.nationaljournal.com/daily/ten-republicans-to-follow-on-twitter-20120826 |archive-date= 2014-11-05 |deadurl= no }}</ref> She was a speaker at the [[2012 Republican National Convention]] on August 28, 2012. She spoke of the lessons she learned from her parents.<ref>{{cite news |url= http://www.deseretnews.com/article/765600023/Utah-congressional-candidate-gives-speech-at-RNC.html |title= 'Revive' America, Utah congressional candidate Mia Love tells RNC, draws rousing cheers |newspaper= Deseret News |agency= [[Associated Press|AP]] |date= August 28, 2012 |archive-url= http://web.archive.org/web/20141105180124/http://www.deseretnews.com/article/765600023/Utah-congressional-candidate-gives-speech-at-RNC.html |archive-date= 2014-11-05 |deadurl= no }}</ref>  In her speech she also stated, "Mr. President, I am here to tell you we are not buying what you are selling in 2012."<ref name="NatlJMiaLoveNov2014">{{citation |url= http://www.nationaljournal.com/almanac/2014-new-members/utah-4-mia-love-r-20141105 |contribution= Utah-4: Mia Love (R) |title= Almanac: 2014 New members of Congress |publisher= [[National Journal]] |archive-url= http://web.archive.org/web/20141124220003/http://www.nationaljournal.com/almanac/2014-new-members/utah-4-mia-love-r-20141105 |archive-date= 2014-11-24 |deadurl= no }}</ref>

In September 2012, ''[[Forbes]]'' investigated a claim in a September 2012 article by ''[[Mother Jones (magazine)|Mother Jones]]'' that no law then existed which would have allowed Love's parents to become citizens of the United States. After ''[[Forbes]]'' investigated the ''Mother Jones'' claim and identified the law in question, ''Mother Jones'' was forced to issue a correction.<ref name="MJ"/><ref name="Anderson 2012-09-28"/><ref>{{cite news |last= Gehrke |first= Robert |title =Love's story of immigrant parents called into question |url= http://www.sltrib.com/54960191-78/campaign-citizenship-family-haiti.html |newspaper= The Salt Lake Tribune |date= September 24, 2012 |archive-url= http://web.archive.org/web/20141104233958/http://www.sltrib.com/54960191-78/campaign-citizenship-family-haiti.html |archive-date= 2014-11-04 |dead-url= no }}</ref> In an October interview, her father stated that Mia's birth as a US citizen was key to gaining permanent legal status and citizenship.<ref name="From Broadway to Capitol Hill"/>

Love lost the election to [[Jim Matheson]] by 768 votes out of 245,277 votes cast,<ref>{{citation |url= http://elections.utah.gov/Media/Default/2012%20Canvass/2012%20General%20Canvass%20Report.xlsx |title= Utah Lieutenant Governor's Office, General Canvass Report, 2012 |work= elections.utah.gov |format= [[XLSX]] formated [[spreadsheet]] }}</ref> a difference of 0.31%. She was regarded to have run a weak campaign, switching campaign managers three times, trying to "nationalize" the race rather than focus on local issues and missing interviews and appointments because of rifts in her campaign staff.<ref name=tangles>{{cite news |url= http://www.sltrib.com/54522813-82/campaign-love-republican-congressional |title= Mia Love's campaign is all in tangles |newspaper= The Salt Lake Tribune |first= Paul |last= Rolly |date= July 21, 2012  |archive-url= http://web.archive.org/web/20141212210722/http://www.sltrib.com/54522813-82/campaign-love-republican-congressional |archive-date= 2014-12-12 |deadurl= no }}</ref>

==U.S. House of Representatives==
===2014 election===

In March 2013, Love stated that she was seriously considering another run against Matheson.<ref>{{cite news |url= http://www.washingtontimes.com/news/2013/mar/17/gop-mayor-mia-love-thinking-about-another-run-cong |title= GOP Mayor Mia Love thinking about another run for Congress |first= Jessica |last= Chasmar |date= March 17, 2013 |newspaper= The Washington Times |archive-url= http://web.archive.org/web/20141105195505/http://www.washingtontimes.com/news/2013/mar/17/gop-mayor-mia-love-thinking-about-another-run-cong |archive-date= 2014-11-05 |deadurl= no }}</ref> 

In May 2013 she announced she would run in 2014. {{As of|July 2013}}, Love had raised over $475,000 for her campaign.<ref>{{cite news |last= Joseph |first= Cameron |title= Mia Love raises $475K for House rematch |date= July 15, 2013 |newspaper= [[The Hill (newspaper)|The Hill]] |url= http://thehill.com/blogs/ballot-box/house-races/311047-mia-love-raises-475k-for-house-rematch |archive-url= http://web.archive.org/web/20141105200046/http://thehill.com/blogs/ballot-box/house-races/311047-mia-love-raises-475k-for-house-rematch |archive-date= 2014-11-05 |deadurl= no }}</ref>

Love was an opening speaker at the 2013 [[Western Conservative Summit]]. She spoke on the need for increased grassroots organization in the GOP, and the need to be independent from the government.<ref>{{cite news |last= Patane |first= Matthew |title= Wisconsin Gov. Scott Walker opens Western Conservative Summit |url= http://www.denverpost.com/ci_23742030/wisconsin-gov-scott-walker-opens-western-conservative-summit |newspaper= The Denver Post |date= July 27, 2013 |archive-url= http://web.archive.org/web/20141105200343/http://www.denverpost.com/ci_23742030/wisconsin-gov-scott-walker-opens-western-conservative-summit|archive-date=2014-11-05 |deadurl= no }}</ref>

In August 2013, Love was chosen by [[Newsmax]] as an "Up and Comer" in their list of top "25 Influential Women of the GOP"<ref>{{cite journal |last= Patten |first= David A. |title= 25 Influential Women of the GOP |url= http://www.newsmax.com/top25Women |newspaper= [[Newsmax (magazine)|Newsmax]] |date= August 2013 |archive-url= http://web.archive.org/web/20141105200711/http://www.newsmax.com/top25Women |archive-date= 2014-11-05 |deadurl= no }}</ref> 

In November 2013, Love acknowledged the growing consensus that the Tea Party needed to shift away from being the "party of no"<ref>{{cite news |url= http://www.deseretnews.com/article/865590533/Mia-Love-rejects-tea-party-label-disagrees-with-tactics-that-led-to-shutdown.html?pg=all |first= Lisa Riley |last= Roche |title= Mia Love says I Don't Believe in Labels |newspaper= Deseret News |date= November 13, 2013 |archive-url= http://web.archive.org/web/20141105201111/http://www.deseretnews.com/article/865590533/Mia-Love-rejects-tea-party-label-disagrees-with-tactics-that-led-to-shutdown.html?pg=all |archive-date= 2014-11-05 |deadurl= no }}</ref> while still supporting the philosophy of the Tea Party and many of its leaders, including fellow Utah Sen. [[Mike Lee (U.S. politician)|Mike Lee]].<ref>{{cite news |last= Hallowell |first= Billy A. |title= Conservative Star Mia Love Denies Report That She Rejects the 'Tea Party' Label — and Clarifies Her Stance |url= http://www.theblaze.com/stories/2013/11/27/conservative-star-mia-love-denies-report-that-she-rejects-the-tea-party-label-and-clarifies-her-stance |publisher= [[TheBlaze]] |date= November 27, 2013 |archive-url= http://web.archive.org/web/20141105100941/http://www.theblaze.com/stories/2013/11/27/conservative-star-mia-love-denies-report-that-she-rejects-the-tea-party-label-and-clarifies-her-stance |archive-date= 2014-11-05 |deadurl= no }}</ref>

On December 17, 2013, Matheson announced that he would not run for re-election, leading Love to be the favored candidate to win in the upcoming election. In early October 2014, the [[National Journal]] listed Utah's 4th district as the number one most likely district to change hands in November.<ref>{{cite news |url= http://www.nationaljournal.com/hotline/latest-edition/the-hotline-s-house-race-rankings-the-30-districts-most-likely-to-change-hands-in-november-20141008 |title= The Hotline's House Race Rankings: The 30 Districts Most Likely to Change Hands in November |first= Jack |last= Fitzpatrick |date= October 8, 2014 |work= National Journal Hotline |publisher=[[National Journal]] |archive-url= http://web.archive.org/web/20141105201933/http://www.nationaljournal.com/hotline/latest-edition/the-hotline-s-house-race-rankings-the-30-districts-most-likely-to-change-hands-in-november-20141008 |archive-date= 2014-11-05 |deadurl= no }}</ref>

In early 2014 Love was made a member of the Republican National Committee's National Advisory Council on African-American outreach.<ref>{{cite news |url= http://flcourier.com/2014/03/06/rncs-advisory-councils-to-focus-on-minority-communities |date= March 6, 2014 |newspaper= Florida Courier |title= RNC's advisory councils to focus on minority communities |archive-url= http://web.archive.org/web/20141105202243/http://flcourier.com/2014/03/06/rncs-advisory-councils-to-focus-on-minority-communities |archive-date= 2014-11-05 |deadurl= no }}</ref>

On April 26, 2014 Love won the Republican nomination for the 4th congressional district at the Utah Republican Convention, with 78% of the vote at the convention.<ref name="NatlJMiaLoveNov2014"/><ref>{{cite news |url= http://www.breitbart.com/Big-Government/2014/04/26/Mia-Love-Wins-GOP-Nomination-for-Congress |title= Mia Love Wins GOP Nomination for Congress |first= Warner Todd |last= Huston |date= April 27, 2014 |newspaper= [[Breitbart (website)|Breitbart]] |archive-url= http://web.archive.org/web/20141105093252/http://www.breitbart.com/Big-Government/2014/04/26/Mia-Love-Wins-GOP-Nomination-for-Congress |archive-date= 2014-11-05 |deadurl= no }}</ref> On election night, Owens led Love until late on, when she pulled ahead and ultimately won by just over 4,000 votes.<ref name="UtahLtGovElectRes11052014">{{citation |title= Utah Election Results US Congressional District #4 |url= http://electionresults.utah.gov/elections/uscongress/4|work=electionresults.utah.gov |archive-url= http://web.archive.org/web/20141105202824/http://electionresults.utah.gov/elections/uscongress/4 |archive-date= 2014-11-05 |deadurl= no }}</ref><ref name="NatlJMiaLoveNov2014"/> Love, who outspent Owens by more than five-to-one, was considered to have underperformed in the district. Owens has said that he may run against Love in 2016.<ref>{{cite news |url= http://www.sltrib.com/1913878-155/a-love-owens-rematch-democrat-says-it |title= A Love-Owens rematch? Democrat says it could happen |newspaper= The Salt Lake Tribune |first= Matt |last= Canham |date= December 5, 2014 |archive-url= http://web.archive.org/web/20141212211054/http://www.sltrib.com/1913878-155/a-love-owens-rematch-democrat-says-it |archive-date= 2014-12-12 |dead-url= no }}</ref>

==Political positions==
A [[Tea Party movement|Tea Party]] conservative,<ref name=tangles/> Love says she favors "fiscal discipline, limited government, and personal responsibility."<ref name="Levinson 2012-01-14"/> She has also said that she asks herself three questions whenever she approaches an issue: "Is it affordable? Is it sustainable? Is it my job?"<ref>{{cite news |url= http://www.latimes.com/news/politics/la-pn-mia-love-breaks-the-gop-mold-but-can-she-win-20120525,0,6232231,full.story |title= Mia Love breaks the GOP mold, but can she win? |newspaper= LA Times |first= Mark Z. |last= Barabak |date= May 29, 2012 |archive-url= http://web.archive.org/web/20120819094443/http://www.latimes.com/news/politics/la-pn-mia-love-breaks-the-gop-mold-but-can-she-win-20120525,0,6232231,full.story |archive-date= 2012-08-19 |deadurl= yes}}</ref> At her college orientation, Love's father told her: "Mia, your mother and I never took a handout. You will not be a burden to society. You will give back." She underscored this philosophy on the campaign trail to sum up her conservative views.<ref>{{cite news |first= Harry |last= Bruinius |date= November 5, 2014 |title= Mia Love, first black Republican woman in Congress, is 'solid gold' for GOP |url= http://www.csmonitor.com/USA/Elections/House/2014/1105/Mia-Love-first-black-Republican-woman-in-Congress-is-solid-gold-for-GOP-video |newspaper= [[The Christian Science Monitor]] |archive-url= http://web.archive.org/web/20141202220003/http://www.csmonitor.com/USA/Elections/House/2014/1105/Mia-Love-first-black-Republican-woman-in-Congress-is-solid-gold-for-GOP-video |archivedate= 2014-12-02 |deadurl= no }}</ref>

===Spending and taxes===
Love proposes deep cuts to [[United States federal budget|federal spending]], particularly in the area of [[entitlement]] spending ([[Social Security (United States)|Social Security]], [[Medicare (United States)|Medicare]] and [[Medicaid]]).<ref name=tangles/> She also supports cutting taxes.<ref name=tangles/>

===Other issues===
She supports domestic energy exploration, local control of education, [[Second Amendment to the United States Constitution|Second Amendment]] rights, and state control of public lands.<ref>{{citation |url= http://www.love4utah.com/issues1 |contribution= Issues |title= Mia Love for Congress |publisher= Friends of Mia Love |archive-url= http://web.archive.org/web/20120823025707/http://www.love4utah.com/issues1|archive-date=2012-08-23 |deadurl= yes}}</ref>

===Congressional Black Caucus===
She has said that if elected to Congress, she would “join the [[Congressional Black Caucus]] and try to take that thing apart from the inside out.” She has described the mainly Democratic Caucus as characterized by “...demagoguery. They sit there and ignite emotions and ignite [[racism]] when there isn’t. They use their positions to instill fear. Hope and change is turned into fear and blame. Fear that everybody is going to lose everything and blaming Congress for everything instead of taking responsibility."<ref>{{cite news |last= Romboy |first= Dennis |url= http://www.deseretnews.com/article/705396842/Love-would-take-apart-Congressional-Black-Caucus-if-elected-in-Utahs-4th-District.html |title= Love would 'take apart' Congressional Black Caucus if elected in Utah's 4th District |newspaper= Deseret News |date= January 5, 2012 |archive-url= http://web.archive.org/web/20141105210314/http://www.deseretnews.com/article/705396842/Love-would-take-apart-Congressional-Black-Caucus-if-elected-in-Utahs-4th-District.html |archive-date= 2014-11-05 |dead-url= no }}</ref>

==Personal life==
Raised a Roman Catholic, Love joined [[The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints]] after graduating college in 1998.<ref>{{cite news |url= http://thegrio.com/2013/12/18/mia-love-poised-to-make-a-political-comeback |title= Mia Love poised to make a political comeback |first= David A. |last= Love |date= December 18, 2013 |publisher= [[The Grio]] |archive-url= http://web.archive.org/web/20141105171805/http://thegrio.com/2013/12/18/mia-love-poised-to-make-a-political-comeback |archive-date= 2014-11-05 |deadurl= no }}</ref> While a flight attendant she moved to Utah as part of her work, but also to be closer to the temple and to learn more about her faith.<ref name="From Broadway to Capitol Hill"/> There she got to know Jason Love, whom she had met briefly when he was an [[Missionary (LDS Church)|LDS missionary]] in [[Connecticut]]. The two were married in December 1998. Love turned down an offer to appear in the Broadway show "[[Smokey Joe's Café]]" that started two days before her marriage.<ref name="From Broadway to Capitol Hill"/><ref>{{cite news |url= http://www.nationalreview.com/corner/296682/daughter-haitian-immigrants-gop-congressional-nominee-utah-john-fund |first= John |last= Fund |author-link= John Fund |title= Daughter of Haitian Immigrants is GOP Congressional Nominee in Utah |journal= National Review Online |publisher= [[National Review]] |date= April 22, 2012 |archive-url= http://web.archive.org/web/20141105172202/http://www.nationalreview.com/corner/296682/daughter-haitian-immigrants-gop-congressional-nominee-utah-john-fund |archive-date= 2014-11-05 |deadurl= no }}</ref> They have three children.
