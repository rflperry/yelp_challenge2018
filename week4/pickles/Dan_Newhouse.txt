'''Daniel "Dan" Newhouse''' (born July 10, 1955) is an American politician from [[Washington (state)|Washington]], where he served as Director of the [[Washington (state)|Washington Department of Agriculture]] and is a former member of the [[Washington House of Representatives]]. He is a member-elect to the [[United States House of Representatives]] for {{ushr|wa|4}} and will be sworn in on January 3, 2015.

==Career==
Newhouse graduated from [[Washington State University]] (WSU) with a degree in [[agricultural economics]] in 1977.<ref name=wsu>{{cite web|url=http://wsm.wsu.edu/s/index.php?id=961#.UT4xbVf77hM|title=Dan Newhouse ’77—Farm to director's office|author=Washington State Magazine|accessdate=November 5, 2014}}</ref> He served in the [[Washington House of Representatives]] from 2003 through 2009.

In 2009, [[Christine Gregoire]], former Democratic [[Governor of Washington]], appointed Newhouse to head the state's agriculture department.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://producenews.com/9-news-section/story-cat/3524-3321|title=Dan Newhouse tapped to head Washington state Agriculture Department|work=The Produce News - Covering fresh produce around the globe since 1897.|accessdate=November 5, 2014}}</ref> When Democrat [[Jay Inslee]] was sworn in as the state's new governor in 2013, he opted not to retain Newhouse.<ref name=yakima/><ref>[http://www.yakimaherald.com/home/929310-8/newhouse-wont-be-reappointed-as-state-ag-chief "Newhouse won't be appointed as state Agriculture Chief"], yakimaherald.com; accessed November 30, 2014.</ref>

Newhouse successfully contested the seat representing {{ushr|WA|4}}, in the [[United States House of Representatives elections in Washington, 2014|2014 elections]].<ref name=yakima>{{cite web|url=http://www.yakimaherald.com/community/lowervalley/sunnysidecontent/1944182-8/newhouse-declares-for-hastings-seat-in-congress|title=Former state ag director Newhouse officially joins 4th District race|publisher=yakimaherald.com|accessdate=February 22, 2014}}</ref> He and fellow Republican [[Clint Didier]] advanced to the November election after finishing in the top two in the August [[primary election]],<ref>{{cite web|url=http://blog.seattlepi.com/seattlepolitics/2014/08/05/clint-didier-dan-newhouse-all-republican-contest-for-congress|title=Clint Didier, Dan Newhouse: All-Republican contest for Congress|work=Strange Bedfellows -- Politics News|accessdate=November 5, 2014}}</ref> marking the first time that two Republican candidates would square off in a general election since the state adopted the "top two" primary system. On November 4, 2014, Newhouse defeated Didier, by a margin of 51%-49%.<ref>[http://www.khq.com/story/27330702/newhouse-beats-fellow-goper-didier-in-4th Newhouse beats Didier in 4th congressional district], khq.com; accessed November 30, 2014.</ref><ref>[http://blog.seattlepi.com/seattlepolitics/2014/11/07/tea-party-turned-back-newhouse-likely-states-new-congressman "Tea Party turned back"], blog.seattlepi.com/seattlepolitics; accessed November 30, 2014.</ref>

==Personal life==
Newhouse was raised on a farm in [[Sunnyside, Washington]], by his parents, Ruth and Irv Newhouse, with four sisters and a brother. Both of his parents and all of his siblings graduated from WSU. Newhouse's father served in the Washington State House and Senate for a total of 34 years, retiring in 1998.<ref name=wsu/> Dan Newhouse owns a {{convert|600|acre|adj=on}} farm in Sunnyside.<ref name=yakima/> Dan Newhouse has two adult children, Devon and Jensena.
