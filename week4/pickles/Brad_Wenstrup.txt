'''Brad Robert Wenstrup'''<ref>http://beta.congress.gov/member/brad-wenstrup/2152/</ref> (born June 17, 1958)<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ballotpedia.org/wiki/index.php/Brad_Wenstrup |title=Brad Wenstrup |publisher=Ballotpedia |date= |accessdate=March 8, 2012}}</ref> is an [[United States|American]] politician who has been the [[United States House of Representatives|U.S. Representative]] for {{ushr|OH|2}} since 2013. A [[Republican Party (United States)|Republican]], he defeated U.S. Representative [[Jean Schmidt]] in the 2012 Republican primary election and Democrat William R. Smith in the [[United States House of Representatives elections in Ohio, 2012|2012 general election]]. Wenstrup is a [[Lieutenant colonel (United States)|Lieutenant Colonel]] and an [[Iraq War|Iraq war veteran]]. He ran unsuccessfully for Cincinnati mayor in 2009.

==Early life, education, and medical career==
Wenstrup was born and raised in [[Cincinnati, Ohio]], the son of Joan (n√©e Carletti) and Frank John "Jack" Wenstrup. His father was of German, Irish, and English descent, while his mother was of Italian ancestry.<ref>http://freepages.genealogy.rootsweb.ancestry.com/%7Ebattle/reps/wenstrup.htm</ref> In 1976, he graduated from St. Xavier High School in Cincinnati.<ref name="Biographical Directory of the United States Congress">{{cite news|title=Brad Wenstrup|url=http://bioguide.congress.gov/scripts/biodisplay.pl?index=W000815|accessdate=21 October 2013}}</ref>  Wenstrup graduated from the [[University of Cincinnati]] where he graduated ''cum laude'' with a BA in Psychology. For graduate school, he attended the [[Scholl College of Podiatric Medicine|William M. Scholl College of Podiatric Medicine]] and earned a M.S. in [[Biology]] and a [[Podiatrist|Doctor of Podiatric Medicine]] degree. He has practiced podiatric medicine in Cincinnati for over 25 years.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://usabrad.com/bio |title=Wenstrup for Congress |publisher=Usabrad.com |date= |accessdate=March 8, 2012}}</ref>

==2009 Cincinnati mayoral election==
{{See also|Election Results, Mayor of Cincinnati, Ohio}}
Wenstrup decided to challenge incumbent [[Mayor of Cincinnati|Cincinnati Mayor]] [[Mark L. Mallory|Mark Mallory]] in the November 2009 election. Mallory defeated him by a 54%-to-46% margin.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=556780 |title=Cincinnati Mayor Race - Nov 03, 2009 |publisher=Our Campaigns |date= |accessdate=March 8, 2012}}</ref>

==U.S. House of Representatives==

===Elections===
;2012
{{See also|United States House of Representatives elections in Ohio, 2012#District 2}}
He ran for the [[U.S. House of Representatives]] in the newly redrawn [[Ohio's 2nd congressional district]], held by incumbent Republican U.S. Congresswoman [[Jean Schmidt]]. He was endorsed by the Anderson Tea Party and the Ohio Liberty Council, which is a coalition of Ohio Tea Party groups.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.wcpo.com/dpp/news/political/elections_local/Wenstrup-upsets-Schmidt-for-2nd-Congressional-District-nomination |title=Wenstrup upsets Schmidt for 2nd Congressional District nomination |publisher=Wcpo.com |date=March 7, 2012 |accessdate=March 8, 2012}}</ref> In a surprise, he defeated her in the March Republican primary by 49%-to-43%.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.politico.com/2012-election/map/#/House/2012/Primary/OH |title=2012 Ohio District 2 Primary |publisher=Politico |date= |accessdate=March 8, 2012}}</ref> She carried six counties (all located in eastern part of the CD), while Wenstrup won the two most populous counties (both located in the western part of CD): [[Hamilton County, Ohio|Hamilton County]] (59%) and [[Clermont County, Ohio|Clermont County]] (50%).<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=748435 |title=OH District 2 - R Primary Race - Mar 06, 2012 |publisher=Our Campaigns |date= |accessdate=March 8, 2012}}</ref>

Wenstrup defeated [[Democratic Party (United States)|Democrat]] William R. Smith, 59%-41%<ref>http://elections.msnbc.msn.com/ns/politics/2012/ohio/house/2/#.UJsY4KWtwhx</ref>
<ref>http://vote.ohio.gov/Results.aspx?race=U.S.%20House%20of%20Representatives</ref>

;2014
{{See also|United States House of Representatives elections in Ohio, 2014#District 2}}
Wenstrup won re-election to a second term by defeating [[Democratic Party (United States)|Democrat]] Marek Tyszkiewicz 66%-34%.<ref>http://www.politico.com/2014-election/results/map/house/ohio/</ref>
