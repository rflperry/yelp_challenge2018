'''Jason Thomas Smith''' (born June 16, 1980)  is an [[United States|American]] [[politician]]. He is currently a member of the [[United States House of Representatives]] for [[Missouri's 8th congressional district]] after winning a [[Missouri's 8th congressional district special election, 2013|special election]] on June 4, 2013. Smith formerly represented the 150th District in the [[Missouri House of Representatives]], which encompasses parts of [[Crawford County, Missouri|Crawford]], [[Dent County, Missouri|Dent]], [[Phelps County, Missouri|Phelps]] and [[Reynolds County, Missouri|Reynolds]] counties. Smith also served as the [[Whip (politics)|Majority Whip]] during the 96th [[Missouri General Assembly]].<ref>[http://www.house.mo.gov/member.aspx?district=150&year=2011 Representative Jason Smith<!-- Bot generated title -->]</ref> He was a [[List of members of the American Legislative Exchange Council|member of]] the [[American Legislative Exchange Council]] (ALEC), serving as Missouri state leader.<ref>[http://www.alec.org/about-alec/state-chairmen/ State Chairs - ALEC - American Legislative Exchange Council<!-- Bot generated title -->]</ref>

== Early life, education, and business career ==
Jason Smith was born in [[St. Louis, Missouri|St. Louis]], Missouri, and grew up near [[Salem, Missouri|Salem]], Missouri, as a fourth-generation family farmer. After graduating from Salem High School in 1998, Smith attended the [[University of Missouri]] where he earned Bachelor of Science degrees in both Agricultural Economics and Business Administration.<ref name=autogenerated1>[http://www.house.mo.gov/bio.aspx?year=2011&district=150 Missouri House of Representatives<!-- Bot generated title -->]</ref> He continued his education at the [[Oklahoma City University School of Law]], earning his [[Juris doctorate|J.D.]] in 2004.<ref>[http://www.votesmart.org/bio.php?can_id=59318 Jason Smith's Biography - Project Vote Smart<!-- Bot generated title -->]</ref> When not involved in his legislative duties Representative Smith works as an attorney, real estate agent, and small business owner.

== Missouri House of Representatives ==

=== Elections ===
Following the resignation of the incumbent State Representative [[Frank Barnitz]], Smith ran for Missouri's 150th house district, based in [[Dent County, Missouri|Dent]], [[Phelps County, Missouri|Phelps]], [[Crawford County, Missouri|Crawford]], and [[Reynolds County, Missouri|Reynolds]] counties. In November 2005, he defeated Democrat Bobby Simpson 54%-44%.<ref>[http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=245691 Ourcampaigns.com<!-- Bot generated title -->]</ref><ref>[http://www.sos.mo.gov/elections/special/110805-rep150.asp SOS, Missouri - Elections: Special Election - November 8, 2005 - District 150, Missouri House of Representatives<!-- Bot generated title -->]</ref> The next year, in 2006, he defeated Democrat Jim O'Donnell 64%-32%.<ref>[http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=309750 Ourcampaigns.com<!-- Bot generated title -->]</ref><ref>[http://vote-mo.org/Election.aspx?State=MO&Election=MO20061107GA November 2006 General Election<!-- Bot generated title -->]</ref> In 2008, Smith defeated Democratic challenger James D. Ellis 70%-30%.<ref>[http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=446406 Ourcampaigns.com<!-- Bot generated title -->]</ref><ref>http://www.sos.mo.gov/enrweb/allresults.asp?eid=256</ref> He ran unopposed in 2010.<ref>http://www.sos.mo.gov/enrweb/raceresults.asp?eid=300&oid=67699&arc=</ref><ref>[http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=642224 Ourcampaigns.com<!-- Bot generated title -->]</ref>

==U.S. House of Representatives==

=== 2013 congressional election ===
{{Main|Missouri's 8th congressional district special election, 2013}}
Smith ran for the vacant [[Missouri's 8th congressional district|8th congressional district of Missouri]] after Republican incumbent [[Jo Ann Emerson]] resigned. Emerson left her elected position in order to accept the presidency of the [[National Rural Electric Cooperative Association]]. On February 9, 2013, per Missouri statute, Smith was selected by the 8th District Republican Central Committee to be the Republican candidate in the June special election. He was declared the winner of the special election held on June 4, 2013. He took 67.1% of the vote to Hodges' (D) 27.4%. The election of Smith marked the 47th consecutive Missouri U.S. House race in which Democrats failed to pick up a Republican-held seat dating back to 1994 - the second biggest Democratic pick-up drought in the nation.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://blog.lib.umn.edu/cspg/smartpolitics/2013/06/missouri_democratic_us_house_p.php |title=Missouri Democratic US House Pick-Up Drought Extends to 47 |work=Smart Politics |first=Eric |last=Ostermeier |date=June 5, 2013}}</ref>

== Personal life ==
He currently lives in [[Salem, Missouri]]. Smith is a member in the following organizations: [[National Rifle Association]], Salem, Steelville and Cuba Chambers of Commerce, Missouri Bar, Farm Bureau, Missouri Community Betterment Association, past President and current board member of the Salem FFA Alumni Association, and CASA (Court Appointed Special Advocate). He attends Grace Community Church of Salem, an [[Assemblies of God]] church.<ref name=autogenerated1 />
