'''Thomas MacArthur''' (born October 16, 1960) is an American businessman and politician. He served as the [[mayor]] of [[Randolph, New Jersey]], and is the member-elect of the [[United States House of Representatives]] for {{ushr|NJ|3}}. He is a member of the [[Republican Party (United States)|Republican Party]].

==Early life==
MacArthur grew up in [[Hebron, Connecticut]]. He received his [[bachelor's degree]] from [[Hofstra University]].<ref name=app>{{cite web|url=http://www.app.com/story/news/politics/new-jersey/2014/05/03/a-look-at-congressional-candidate-tom-macarthur/2130838/|title=A look at congressional candidate Tom MacArthur|author=Associated Press, @ap|date=May 3, 2014|publisher=|accessdate=November 5, 2014}}</ref> MacArthur worked in the insurance industry. He was chairman and [[chief executive officer]] of York Risk Services Group for 11 years. He served on the [[Randolph, New Jersey]] [[City council|Township Council]] from 2011 through 2013, including a tenure as mayor in 2013.<ref name=app/>

==2014 Congressional Campaign==
When [[Jon Runyan]], a [[Republican Party (United States)|Republican]] who represented {{ushr|NJ|3}} in the [[United States House of Representatives]], announced that he would not run for reelection in 2014, MacArthur chose to run for the Republican Party nomination. Mac Arthur resigned from the Randolph council to move into the congressional district.<ref>Knapp, Claire. [http://newjerseyhills.com/randolph_reporter/news/former-fire-chief-is-new-randolph-councilman-forstenhausler-will-fill/article_7528d66c-9284-11e3-8d8c-0019bb2963f4.html "Former fire chief is new Randolph Councilman; Forstenhausler will fill MacArthur’s term"], ''Randolph Reporter'', February 10, 2014. Accessed July 6, 2014. "Mark Forstenhausler, 54, was sworn in as a member of the Township Council on Thursday, Feb. 6, to complete the term vacated by Tom MacArthur."</ref> He ran against [[Steve Lonegan]] in the Republican Party's [[primary election]], and defeated him.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://articles.philly.com/2014-06-05/news/50333132_1_steve-lonegan-republican-party-third-district|title=MacArthur, Belgard to compete for N.J.'s Third District seat|work=Philly.com|accessdate=November 5, 2014}}</ref> He faced [[Aimee Belgard]] of the [[Democratic Party (United States)|Democratic Party]] in the general election.

MacArthur defeated Belgard by an 11-point margin, winning the popular vote in both Ocean and Burlington Counties.<ref>http://ivn.us/2014/11/05/republican-tom-macarthur-wins-njs-competitive-congressional-race-double-digits/</ref><ref>http://www.nj.gov/state/elections/2014-results/2014-unofficial-general-results-us-house.pdf</ref>

==Personal life==
MacArthur lives in [[Randolph, New Jersey]], and also owns homes in Toms River and [[Barnegat Light, New Jersey]]. He is married, and has a son and daughter. Another daughter died in 1996 at the age of 11.<ref name=app/><ref>{{cite web|url=http://newjerseyhills.com/randolph_reporter/news/macarthur-leaving-randolph-set-to-pursue-seat-in-congress/article_ef8658da-6412-11e3-870d-0019bb2963f4.html|title=MacArthur leaving Randolph; Set to pursue seat in Congress|work=New Jersey Hills Media Group|accessdate=November 5, 2014}}</ref>
