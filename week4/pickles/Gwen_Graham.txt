'''Gwen Graham''' (born January 31, 1963) is an American lawyer and politician. In 2014, she became one of only two [[Democratic Party (United States)|Democrats]] to unseat [[Republican Party (United States)|Republican]] incumbents in [[United States House of Representatives elections, 2014|elections]] to the [[United States House of Representatives]] vying for {{ushr|FL|2}}. She is the daughter of [[Bob Graham]], a former [[United States Senate|United States Senator]] and former [[Governor of Florida]].

==Early life==
Graham was born in [[Miami Lakes, Florida]].<ref name=npr/> Her father, [[Bob Graham]], is a former [[United States Senate|United States Senator]] and [[Governor of Florida]].<ref name=reuters>{{cite web|url=http://www.reuters.com/article/2014/08/25/us-usa-florida-congress-idUSKBN0GP0W820140825|title=With Graham name, Democrats see rare chance for Florida win|work=Reuters|accessdate=November 5, 2014}}</ref> She moved to [[Tallahassee, Florida|Tallahassee]] in 1978, when her father became Governor.<ref name=npr/> Graham graduated from [[Leon High School]] in 1980.<ref name=ml/>

==Career==
Graham received her bachelor's degree from the [[University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill]] in 1984 and her law degree from [[American University]]'s [[Washington College of Law]] in 1988.<ref name=ml/> She worked for [[Andrews & Kurth]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.politifact.com/florida/statements/2014/sep/23/steve-southerland/steve-southerland-says-gwen-graham-was-washington-/|title=Steve Southerland says Gwen Graham 'was a Washington lobbyist'|work=@politifact|accessdate=November 5, 2014}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.tallahassee.com/story/news/2014/09/14/florida-district-race-heats-steve-southerlan-gwen-graham/15580807/|title=Florida District 2 race heats up between Steve Southerland, Gwen Graham|author=Ledyard King|date=September 14, 2014|work=PolitiFact|accessdate=November 5, 2014}}</ref> She joined her father's [[Bob Graham presidential campaign, 2004|2004 presidential campaign]]. When he dropped out of the race following a heart attack, Graham joined [[Howard Dean]]'s [[Howard Dean presidential campaign, 2004|presidential campaign]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://articles.orlandosentinel.com/2003-12-07/news/0312060195_1_bob-graham-howard-dean-logan|title=Graham's Daughter Steps Into Politics|work=Orlando Sentinel|accessdate=November 5, 2014}}</ref> After [[John Kerry]] secured the Democratic Party's nomination, she went to work for [[John Kerry presidential campaign, 2004|his campaign]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.sptimes.com/2004/06/14/State/New_Graham_rising_on_.shtml|title=State: New Graham rising on political horizon|work=St. Petersburg Times|accessdate=November 5, 2014}}</ref>

Graham works as a [[Leon County, Florida|Leon County]] school administrator.<ref name=npr>{{cite web|url=http://www.npr.org/blogs/itsallpolitics/2013/04/02/176048386/daughter-of-former-fla-sen-bob-graham-running-for-congress|title=Daughter Of Former Fla. Sen. Bob Graham Running For Congress|work=[[NPR]]|date=April 2, 2013|accessdate=November 5, 2014}}</ref>

In 2013, Graham announced her candidacy against incumbent Republican Congressman [[Steve Southerland (Florida politician)|Steve Southerland]] in 2014.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.tallahassee.com/article/20130506/POLITICSPOLICY/305060015/Southerland-faces-tough-2014-re-election-bid|title=Southerland faces tough 2014 re-election bid|date=May 5, 2013|accessdate=May 24, 2013|work=[[Tallahassee Democrat]]|first=Ledyard|last=King}}</ref> The [[Democratic Congressional Campaign Committee]] announced that they will target the race and provide support to her.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.politico.com/story/2013/05/dccc-unveils-plan-to-boost-top-prospects-in-2014-91102.html|title=DCCC unveils plan to boost top prospects in 2014|date=May 9, 2013|accessdate=May 24, 2013|work=[[Politico]]|first=Alex|last=Isenstadt}}</ref> Graham defeated Southerland in the November election.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.tallahassee.com/story/news/local/state/2014/11/04/thirty-percent-of-congressional-district-voters-have-cast-ballots/18452597/|title=Gwen Graham defeats Steve Southerland|author=Karl Etters, Tallahassee Democrat|date=November 4, 2014|work=Tallahassee Democrat|accessdate=November 5, 2014}}</ref>

==Congressional Career==
Prior to her swearing in, Graham said that she would oppose [[Nancy Pelosi]] for the top Democratic leadership position.<ref>http://www.politico.com/story/2014/10/gwen-graham-nancy-pelosi-florida-111916.html</ref> During the voice vote, the ''[[Washington Post]]'' reported, "there were 'no nays audible in room." However, a spokesman for Graham said she did vote against Pelosi, and "it was just drowned out by the other people voting."<ref>http://www.tampabay.com/blogs/the-buzz-florida-politics/gwen-graham-says-she-doesnt-support-pelosi-despite-democrats-vote/2206945</ref><ref>http://www.washingtonpost.com/blogs/post-politics/wp/2014/11/18/house-democrats-reelect-nancy-pelosi-their-leader/</ref>

==Personal life==
Graham lives in Tallahassee.<ref name=reuters/> She married Mark Logan in 1985,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.saintpetersblog.com/archives/85631|title=Democrats recruiting Gwen Graham, daughter of former governor Bob Graham, to challenge Steve Southerland|work=SaintPetersBlog|accessdate=November 5, 2014}}</ref> and they had three children together.<ref name=reuters/> Graham and Logan divorced, and Graham is now married to Stephen Hurm.<ref name=ml>{{cite web|url=http://miamilaker.com/Community/TabId/58/ArtMID/472/ArticleID/206/Sen-Bob-Grahams-daughter-Gwen-holds-fundraiser.aspx|title=Sen. Bob Graham's daughter, Gwen, holds fundraiser|work=Miamilaker.com|accessdate=November 5, 2014}}</ref>
