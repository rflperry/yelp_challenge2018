'''Earl Leroy "Buddy" Carter'''  (born September 6, 1957) is a [[Georgia State Senate|Senate]] [[Republican Party (United States)|Republican]] in the [[152nd Georgia General Assembly]] from [[Pooler, Georgia]].<ref name="sgagov"><span class="plainlinks">[http://www.senate.ga.gov/senators/en-US/member.aspx?Member=778&Session=23 Senator Buddy Carter]</span>. Senate.ga.gov. Retrieved June 28, 2013.</ref> Carter was first elected Senator in the 2009 [[general election]] and serves Georgia's 1st district—a constituency which includes [[Bryan County, Georgia|Bryan County]] and portions of [[Chatham County, Georgia|Chatham]] and [[Liberty County, Georgia|Liberty]] counties.<ref><span class="plainlinks">[http://www.senate.ga.gov/senators/en-US/District.aspx?District=1&Session=23 District 1 Senator Buddy Carter (R)]</span>. Senate.ga.gov. Retrieved June 28, 2013.</ref>

Carter is giving up his state senate seat to run for the Republican nomination in {{ushr|GA|1}} in 2014. Recently, a controversy has emerged regarding S.B. 408, a bill authored by Buddy Carter that increases reimbursement rates for pharmacies in Georgia. As he is the owner of three pharmacies which would see increased profits as a result of the proposed action, this is considered to be in violation of the ethical guidelines of the State Senate.<ref>http://zpolitics.com/i-team-investigates-pharmacy-bill-carried-sen-buddy-carter/</ref>

==Early life and education==
Buddy Carter graduated from Robert W. Groves High School in 1975. He earned an associate degree from [[Young Harris College]] in 1977 and a [[Bachelor of Science]] in [[pharmacy]] from the [[University of Georgia]] in 1980.

==Political career==
Buddy Carter served on the planning and zoning commission for the city of [[Pooler, Georgia|Pooler]] from 1989–1993 and on Pooler's city council from 1994–1995. Carter served as Pooler's mayor from 1996–2004 as well. Senator Carter was first elected to the state legislature in 2005—serveing two terms in the [[Georgia House of Representatives|House of Representatives]] from 2006–2010. Senator Carter was elected to the [[Georgia Senate]] in 2009 and sworn into the [[upper house]] in 2010.<ref name="sgagov"/> He sits on the Senate [[Appropriations Committee (Georgia Senate)|Appropriations]], [[Health and Human Services Committee (Georgia Senate)|Health and Human Services]], [[Higher Education Committee (Georgia Senate)|Higher Education]], and [[Public Safety Committee (Georgia Senate)|Public Safety]] committees.<ref name="sgagov"/>
