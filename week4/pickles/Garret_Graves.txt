'''Garret Neal Graves''' (born January 31, 1972)<ref>{{cite web|url=http://info.cqrollcall.com/rs/cqrc/images/CQNews_NewMemberGuide2014.pdf|title=Guide to the New Congress|publisher=[[Roll Call]]|accessdate=December 6, 2014}}</ref> is the incoming [[United States Representative]] from [[Louisiana's 6th congressional district]]. In a [[runoff election]] on December 6, 2014, Graves, a [[Republican Party (United States)|Republican]], handily defeated the [[Democratic Party (United States)|Democratic]] candidate, former [[governor of Louisiana|Governor]] [[Edwin Edwards]]. Graves received 139,194 votes (62.4 percent) to Edwards's 83,773 (37.6 percent).<ref>{{cite web|url=http://staticresults.sos.la.gov/12062014/12062014_MultiParish.html|title=Runoff election returns, December 6, 2014|publisher=[[Louisiana Secretary of State]]|accessdate=December 6, 2014}}</ref>

==Career==
Graves served as an aide for nine years to former U.S. Representative [[Billy Tauzin]] of [[Louisiana's 3rd congressional district]]. He was also a legislative aide to the [[U.S. House Committee on Energy and Commerce]], of which Tauzin served as chairmam.<ref name=conflict/> In 2005, he became an aide for the [[United States Senate Committee on Commerce, Science and Transportation]], serving Republican [[United States Senate|U.S. Senator]] [[David Vitter]]. He was the staff director for the [[United States Senate Subcommittee on Climate Change and Impacts]]. He also worked for Democratic former U.S. Senator [[John Breaux]], a protege of Edwin Edwards.<ref name=nov4/> He served as a chief legislative aide to the [[U.S. Senate Committee on Environment and Public Works]].<ref name=resign/>

In 2008, Governor [[Bobby Jindal]] appointed Graves to manage the [[Louisiana Coastal Protection and Restoration Authority]]. In the position, he negotiated on behalf of the state with [[British Petroleum]] over the [[Deepwater Horizon oil spill]].<ref name=nov4>{{cite web|url=http://www.nola.com/politics/index.ssf/2014/11/edwin_edwards_garret_graves_he.html|title=Edwin Edwards, Garret Graves headed for runoff in 6th Congressional District|work=NOLA.com|accessdate=November 24, 2014}}</ref> He resigned the position effective February 17, 2014.<ref name=resign>{{cite web|url=http://www.nola.com/environment/index.ssf/2014/02/jindal_coastal_adviser_garret.html|title=Jindal coastal adviser Garret Graves resigns, effective Feb. 17|work=NOLA.com|accessdate=November 24, 2014}}</ref>

In March 2014, Graves announced his intention to run in the [[United States House of Representatives elections in Louisiana, 2014#District 6|2014 election]] to the [[United States House of Representatives]] for {{ushr|LA|6}}; [[incumbent]] Republican [[Bill Cassidy]] ran successfully instead against the incumbent Democrat [[Mary Landrieu]] for the U.S. Senate.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://theadvocate.com/news/politics/8485305-123/former-jindal-adviser-running-for|title=Former Jindal adviser running for Congress|work=theadvocate.com|accessdate=November 24, 2014}}</ref> In the 2014 [[jungle primary]], Edwin Edwards finished in first place with 30 percent of the vote; Graves was the runner-up with 27 percent. Graves and Edwards advanced to the December 6 runoff election.<ref name=nov4/>

==Personal life==
Graves is from [[Baton Rouge, Louisiana]],<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.houmatoday.com/article/20080104/NEWS/801040311|title=New coastal director has strong local knowledge|work=HoumaToday.com|accessdate=November 24, 2014}}</ref> and he still resides there.<ref name=resign/> His father, John, owns an engineering firm that contracts with the [[U.S. Army Corps of Engineers]].<ref name=conflict>{{cite web|url=http://www.houmatoday.com/article/20080203/news/659908125|title=Questions remain about potential conflict|work=HoumaToday.com|accessdate=November 24, 2014}}</ref>
