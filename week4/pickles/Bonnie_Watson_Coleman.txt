'''Bonnie Watson Coleman''' (born February 6, 1945) is an American [[Democratic Party (United States)|Democratic Party]] politician, who has served in the [[New Jersey]] [[New Jersey General Assembly|General Assembly]] since 1998, where she represents the [[15th Legislative District (New Jersey)|15th Legislative District]].<ref name=NJLEG>[http://www.njleg.state.nj.us/members/BIO.asp?Leg=180 Assemblywoman Watson Colemans's legislative web page], [[New Jersey Legislature]]. Accessed June 5, 2011.</ref> In 2015 she will become the first black woman in Congress from New Jersey.<ref>http://www.cawp.rutgers.edu/press_room/news/documents/PressRelease_11-05-14-electionresults.pdf</ref>

Watson Coleman served as the Majority Leader of the Assembly since 2006 to 2010. She serves in the Assembly on the Legislative Services commission.<ref name=NJLEG/> She had previously served on the Appropriations Committee (as Chairwoman from 2002–2006),<ref name=NJLEG/> the Budget Committee, and the Joint Budget Oversight Committee.

Watson Coleman served as New Jersey Democratic State Chairwoman from 2002–2006. She served on the Governing Boards Association of State Colleges from 1987–1998 and as its chair from 1991-1993. Watson Coleman was a member of the [[Ewing Township, New Jersey|Ewing Township]] Planning Board from 1996-1997. She was a member of the [[Richard Stockton College of New Jersey]] Board of Trustees from 1981–1998 and was its chair from 1990-1991.<ref name=NJLEG/>

In 1974, she established the first Office of Civil Rights, Contract Compliance and Affirmative Action, in the [[New Jersey Department of Transportation]] and remained the Director of that office for six years. In 1980, Watson Coleman joined the Department of Community Affairs, where she held a number of positions including, Assistant Commissioner, responsible for Aging, Community Resources, Public Guardian and Women Divisions.

Watson Coleman became the first [[African American]] woman to lead the State party when she was elected Chair of the [[New Jersey Democratic State Committee]], on February 4, 2002.

Watson Coleman received a [[Bachelor of Arts|B.A.]] from [[Thomas Edison State College]] in 1985, and attended [[Rutgers University]].<ref name=NJLEG/> Born in [[Camden, New Jersey|Camden]], she currently resides in [[Ewing Township, New Jersey|Ewing Township]].<ref>[http://votesmart.org/bio.php?can_id=24799 Assembly Member Bonnie Watson Coleman], [[Project Vote Smart]]. Accessed August 10, 2007.</ref> She is a member of [[Alpha Kappa Alpha]] sorority.<ref>{{cite book|last=Schaller|first=Thomas F.|last2=King-Meadows|first2=Tyson|title=Devolution and Black state legislators : challenges and choices in the twenty-first century|year=2006|publisher=State Univ. of New York Press|location=Albany|isbn=978-0-7914-6729-9|page=39}}</ref>

==2014 Congressional Campaign==

Following the announcement that Congressman [[Rush D. Holt, Jr.|Rush Holt]] would not be seeking another term in office, Bonnie Watson Coleman announced her intention to run for [[New Jersey's 12th congressional district]].<ref>http://www.nj.com/mercer/index.ssf/2014/02/assemblywoman_bonnie_watson_colemans_congressional_campaign_endorsed_by_top_mercer_county_officials.html</ref> Assemblywoman Watson Coleman will be the first woman of color elected to represent a New Jersey district in the United States House of Representatives and the only female member of New Jersey's congressional delegation.

On June 3, 2014, Watson Coleman won the Democratic primary for the [[New Jersey's 12th congressional district|12th congressional district]].<ref>{{cite web|last1=Davis|first1=Mike|title=Watson Coleman wins Democratic primary for 12th congressional district|url=http://www.nj.com/mercer/index.ssf/2014/06/watson_coleman_wins_democratic_primary_for_12th_congressional_district.html|website=NJ.com|accessdate=4 June 2014}}</ref>

Watson Coleman won the general election on November 4, 2014, defeating Republican candidate Alieta Eck.<ref>http://www.nj.com/mercer/index.ssf/2014/11/next_step_mercer_and_hunterdon_democrats_to_find_assembly_replacement_for_watson_coleman_after_congr.html</ref>
