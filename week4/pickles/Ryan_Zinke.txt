'''Ryan Zinke''' (born November 1, 1961) is an American politician and member of the [[Republican Party (United States)|Republican Party]] who served as a member of the [[Montana State Senate]], representing Senate District 2 from 2009 to 2011.<ref>{{cite web|last=Montana Legislature|title=RYAN ZINKE (R)|url=http://leg.mt.gov/css/Sessions/62nd/leg_info.asp?HouseID=0&SessionID=105&LAWSID=6491|accessdate=3 April 2011}}{{dead link|date=January 2014}}</ref> He is the Republican congressman-elect for [[Montana's at-large congressional district]] having won in the [[United States House of Representatives election in Montana, 2014|2014 election]].

==Military Career==
Zinke served as a [[United States Navy SEALs|U.S. Navy SEAL]] from 1985 to 2008, retiring at the rank of [[Commander (United States)#Naval rank|Commander]].  During his career as a SEAL, he served as a member of the [[United States Naval Special Warfare Development Group]] from 1990-1993 and again from 1996-1999.<ref>New York Times (2011). [http://www.nytimes.com/2011/05/05/world/middleeast/05seals.html In Bin Laden's Compound, Seals' All-Star Team]. Retrieved May 4, 2011.</ref> He was awarded two [[Bronze Stars]] for combat and four [[Meritorious Service Medal (United States)|Meritorious Service Medals]].<ref name=spotlight>{{cite web|last=GOPAC|title=Candidate Spotlight:Ryan Zinke|url=http://www.gopac.org/chairmanscorner/spotlight27.aspx|accessdate=3 April 2011}}{{dead link|date=January 2014}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|last=Johnson |first=Charles S. |url=http://mtstandard.com/news/local/zinke-releases-some-navy-records-on-seal-career-dems-seek/article_5661fcaa-204a-11e4-943d-0019bb2963f4.html |title=Zinke releases some Navy records on SEAL career; Dems seek more |publisher=Mtstandard.com |date=2008-02-01 |accessdate=2014-08-11}}</ref>

==Education==
He received a BS from the [[University of Oregon]], an MBA from [[National University (California)|National University]], and an MS from the [[University of San Diego]].<ref>{{cite web|last=Congress.org|title=Sen. Ryan Zinke (R-MT 2nd District)|url=http://congress.org/congressorg/bio/id/22748|accessdate=3 April 2011}}{{dead link|date=January 2014}}</ref>

==Political Career==
Zinke served in the Montana Senate from 2009 to 2011, representing [[Whitefish, Montana]].

He was the running mate of Montana gubernatorial candidate [[Neil Livingstone]] in the [[Montana gubernatorial election, 2012|2012 election]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://billingsgazette.com/news/state-and-regional/montana/article_02507b80-0c12-5767-a262-b21a8f35ee6e.html|title=Livingstone taps Zinke as running mate|date=July 10, 2011|accessdate=March 11, 2012|work=[[Billings Gazette]]|first=Charles S.|last=Johnson}}</ref> The Livingstone/Zinke ticket finished fifth out of seven in the Republican primary with 12,038 votes (8.8% of the vote).

In July 2012, Zinke created a super PAC to oppose President [[Barack Obama]]'s re-election. Zinke stated that the PAC would be a conduit to channel [[special operations forces]] frustration with the president, as special operations forces operators are limited in terms of their ability to criticize their civilian commanders.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.huffingtonpost.com/2012/07/19/ryan-zinke-navy-seal-super-pac_n_1686086.html|title=Navy Seal Pac}}</ref> Zinke's involvement with the Super PAC he created before it became a leading supporter of his campaign was criticized by Paul S. Ryan of the [[Campaign Legal Center]], who said that kind of action could lead to "the campaign-contribution limits being completely eviscerated." <ref>{{cite web|url=http://helenair.com/news/local/us-house-candidate-zinke-reaps-benefits-of-super-pac-he/article_ece9df48-9798-11e3-a279-0019bb2963f4.html|title=US House candidate Zinke reaps benefits of super PAC he founded}}</ref>

In spring of 2014, Zinke announced that he was running for [[Montana's at-large congressional district]], held by Republican [[Steve Daines]], who was [[United States Senate election in Montana, 2014|running for the U.S. Senate]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ryanzinke.com/ryan-zinke-announces-statewide-bus-tour/|title=Ryan Zinke Announces Statewide Bus Tour|date=30 April 2014|accessdate=18 May 2014}}</ref> who announced an intention to run for a [[United States Senate]] seat. Zinke won the Republican primary with 43,766 votes (33.25%) and faced Democrat[[John_Lewis_(Montana_politician)| John Lewis]], a former State Director for U.S. Senator [[Max Baucus]], in the general election.

During a campaign stop Zinke said the United States should focus on the "real enemy," referring to [[Hillary Clinton]] as the "[[Antichrist]]."<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.nytimes.com/2014/09/29/us/politics/midterm-elections-house-republicans.html|title=House Hopefuls in G.O.P. Seek Rightward Shift|newspaper=[[The New York Times]]|date=September 28, 2014|first=Jonathan|last=Weisman}}</ref> He later said his comment was "perhaps a little harsh," but "I had two friends in Benghazi and the truth does matter."<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.dailyinterlake.com/news/local_montana/article_c49ecbde-8d34-11e3-987f-0019bb2963f4.html|title=Candidate's comment draws national attention|newspaper=Daily Inter Lake|date=February 3, 2014|first=Jim|last=Mann}}</ref>

Zinke was elected to congress on November 4, 2014 with 56% of the vote and over 200,000 total votes.

Zinke is married to Lolita (n√©e Hand).<ref name=spotlight/> They have three children; Jennifer, Wolfgang, and Konrad.
