'''Elise M. Stefanik''' (born July 2, 1984) is an American politician from the state of [[New York]]. She is a member of the [[Republican Party (United States)|Republican Party]] and the member-elect to the [[United States House of Representatives]] in {{ushr|NY|21}}.<ref>{{cite news |last=Jaffe |first=Alexandra |date=September 23, 2014 |title=GOP chances of flipping Rep. Owens' seat grow |url=http://thehill.com/blogs/ballot-box/house-races/218610-gop-chances-of-flipping-rep-owens-seat-grow |newspaper=The Hill |location=Washington, D.C. |accessdate=October 5, 2014}}</ref> She is the youngest woman ever elected to Congress, elected in 2014 at age 30.<ref>{{cite web|title=The Insiders' Outsider|url=http://www.weeklystandard.com/articles/insiders-outsider_794964.html|publisher=The Weekly Standard|accessdate=30 September 2014}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|last1=Lachman|first1=Samantha|title=Elise Stefanik May Become Congress' Youngest Woman Ever|url=http://www.huffingtonpost.com/2014/06/24/elise-stefanik-congress-_n_5527928.html|publisher=Huffington Post|accessdate=30 September 2014}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|last1=LoGiurato |first1=Brett |url=http://www.businessinsider.com/elise-stefanik-wins-youngest-member-of-congress-2014-11|title=Elise Stefanik Wins, Now Youngest Member Of Congress |date=November 5, 2014|work=Business Insider|accessdate=November 6, 2014}}</ref>

==Early life and career==
Stefanik was born in [[Albany, New York]] in 1984. Her parents, Melanie and Ken Stefanik, own a plywood business in the Albany area.<ref>{{cite news |last=LoTemplio |first=Joe |date=October 6, 2014 |title=Stefanik delivers national radio address |url=http://www.pressrepublican.com/news/local_news/article_ad6353c3-5f57-51cd-8540-a8ff01cb1638.html |newspaper=[[Press-Republican]] |location=[[Plattsburgh (city), New York|Plattsburgh, New York]] |accessdate=October 9, 2014 }}</ref> She attended the [[Albany Academy for Girls]] and [[Harvard University]], graduating in 2006.<ref name=caller/><ref name=pressrepublican>{{cite web|url=http://www.pressrepublican.com/sunday/x335453894/Congressional-challengers-line-up |title=Congressional challengers line up » Sunday |publisher=Press-Republican |date=August 18, 2013 |accessdate=June 25, 2014}}</ref>  While at Harvard she was awarded the Women's Leadership Award.<ref>{{cite news |last=Toczauer |first=Nicole |date=October 4, 2014 |title=Meet Elise Stefanik, the Candidate Who Could Become the Youngest Woman in Congress |url=http://abcnews.go.com/Politics/meet-elise-stefanik-candidate-youngest-woman-congress/story?id=25963324 |newspaper=ABC News |location=New York |accessdate=October 4, 2014}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |last= |first= |date= |title=Harvard Gazette Archives |url=http://news.harvard.edu/gazette/2006/04.20/15-womensleadership.html |newspaper=Harvard University Gazette |location=Cambridge, Massachusetts |accessdate=October 5, 2014}}</ref> After graduating from Harvard at 21 years old,<ref name="PostHagerty05042014">{{cite news |last=Hagerty |first=Meg |date=May 4, 2014 |title=Stefanik's campaign ramping up |url=http://poststar.com/news/local/stefanik-s-campaign-ramping-up/article_d2154512-d3b7-11e3-9a69-0019bb2963f4.html |newspaper=[[The Post-Star|Glens Falls Post-Star]] |location=[[Glens Falls, New York]] |accessdate=October 5, 2014}}</ref> she joined the [[Presidency of George W. Bush|administration]] of [[President of the United States|President]] [[George W. Bush]],<ref name="PostHagerty05042014"/><ref name=cuse/> working on the [[Domestic Policy Council]].<ref name="PostHagerty05042014"/>

She also worked in the office of the [[White House Chief of Staff]] for second deputy chief of staff, [[Joshua Bolten]].<ref name=caller/><ref name="PostHagerty05042014"/> She has worked as communications director for the [[Foreign Policy Initiative]] and as the policy director for [[Tim Pawlenty]] during his [[Tim Pawlenty presidential campaign, 2012|2012 presidential campaign]].<ref name=caller>{{cite web|author=Jamie Weinstein |url=http://dailycaller.com/2013/08/07/elise-stefanik-wants-to-go-to-congress/ |title=Elise Stefanik wants to go to Congress |publisher=The Daily Caller |date= |accessdate=June 25, 2014}}</ref> 

Stefanik managed [[Paul Ryan]]’s debate preparation as he ran for [[Vice President of the United States]] in the [[United States presidential election, 2012|2012 general election]].<ref name="CenterRollCall11122014"/><ref name=caller/><ref name=cuse>{{cite web|url=http://www.syracuse.com/news/index.ssf/2014/06/elise_stefanik_declared_winner_of_gop_primary_in_ny-21.html |title=Elise Stefanik declared winner of GOP primary in NY-21 |publisher=syracuse.com |date= |accessdate=June 25, 2014}}</ref> Following the Romney-Ryan loss in the 2012 presidential election, she returned to [[upstate New York]] to join her parents' [[wholesale]] [[plywood]] business, located in [[Guilderland Center, New York]].<ref name=caller/><ref name=pressrepublican/>

In 2013, Stefanik declared her candidacy in the [[United States House of Representatives elections in New York, 2014|2014 election]] for the [[United States House of Representatives]] in {{ushr|ny|21}}. She defeated Matthew Doheny in the [[Republican Party (United States)|Republican]] [[primary election]] 61 to 39 percent,<ref>[http://hosted.ap.org/dynamic/files/elections/2014/by_state/NY_US_House_0624.html?SITE=AP&SECTION=POLITICS New York - Summary Vote Results], Associated Press, June 25, 2014</ref> She faced Aaron Woolf, the [[Democratic Party (United States)|Democratic Party]] nominee, in the general election on November 4.<ref name=cuse/> Stefanik defeated Woolf 55.2% to 33.5%, becoming the youngest woman ever elected to Congress.<ref name="CenterRollCall11122014">Center, Shira T. [http://www3.atr.rollcall.com/how-elise-stefanik-became-the-youngest-woman-ever-elected-to-congress/?dcz= How Elise Stefanik Became the Youngest Woman Ever Elected to Congress], ''[[Roll Call]]'', November 12, 2014.</ref><ref name=youngest>{{cite web|url=http://abcnews.go.com/Politics/elise-stefanik-youngest-woman-elected-congress/story?id=26694806|title=Elise Stefanik, the Youngest Woman Ever Elected to Congress - ABC News|author=ABC News|work=ABC News|accessdate=November 6, 2014}}</ref>{{#tag:ref|Stefanik won the election at age 30. Previously the youngest woman to win an election to Congress was [[Elizabeth Holtzman]], who was 31 when first elected in 1973.<ref name=youngest/>|group=lower-alpha}}
