'''Bradley Mark Walker''' (born May 20, 1969) is an businessman and pastor from the state of [[North Carolina]]. He was the [[Republican Party (United States)|Republican Party]] nominee for the [[United States House of Representatives]] in {{ushr|NC|6}} and became representative-elect after winning the 2014 general election.  Walker has not held elected office previously.

==Biography==

Walker was born in 1969<ref>http://www.nationaljournal.com/article/533128</ref> in [[Dothan, Alabama]] and grew up in panhandle of [[Florida]].  The son of a [[Independent Baptist]] minister, Walker eventually attended [[Trinity Baptist College]] for a time before moving with his family to [[Houston, Texas]]

From there, Walker moved to [[Piedmont Triad]] over 20 years ago. He married Kelly Sears, a trauma nurse, and worked for Flow Automotive. Several years later, Walker returned to college to pursue the ministry.  He attended Piedmont Baptist College, now [[Piedmont International University]], and graduated with a B.A. in Biblical Studies. Walker was ordained in the [[Southern Baptist]] Denomination.  He first worked at Calvary Baptist Church in [[Winston-Salem, North Carolina|Winston-Salem]]. Walker has spent most of ministry career as a worship pastor.  He has also served as an executive pastor and lead pastor.  

In 2008, Walker started with Lawndale Baptist Church in [[Greensboro, North Carolina|Greensboro]] as its Pastor of Arts and Worship.<ref>[http://www.greensboro-nc.gov/modules/showdocument.aspx?documentid=18056 Biography and Resume at the City of Greensboro, North Carolina website]</ref>

The Walkers have three children and live in [[Guilford County, North Carolina|Guilford County]].

==Congressional campaign==

Following the [[United States House of Representatives elections in North Carolina, 2014#District 6|primary election]], Walker and [[Phil Berger, Jr.]] advanced to a [[runoff election]]. Despite finishing second in the May primary, Walker unexpectedly won the runoff election by a significant margin.<ref>[http://www.washingtonpost.com/blogs/post-politics/wp/2014/07/15/baptist-minister-mark-walker-wins-republican-runoff-in-north-carolinas-6th-district/ "Baptist minister Mark Walker wins Republican runoff in North Carolina’s 6th District" Washington Post, July 15, 2014]</ref>

==Immigration and border security==
Asked at an event in [[Rockingham County, North Carolina|Rockingham County]], if military force was appropriate along the US-Mexican border, Walker stated that the national guard may be necessary to secure the border.<ref name=Record>{{cite news|title=War With Mexico Of Course Not|url=http://www.news-record.com/news/walker-war-with-mexico-of-course-not/article_eeb349fa-3f9e-11e4-8089-001a4bcf6878.html|accessdate=19 September 2014|publisher=News-Record|date=2014-09-19}}</ref> At this same event he suggested "we got to go laser or blitz somebody with a couple of fighter jets for a little while to make our point" to which the moderator asked if he had any qualms about starting a war with Mexico. Walker responded with "Well, we did it before, if we need to do it again, I don't have a qualm about it."<ref>http://www.newsobserver.com/2014/09/18/4162234/republican-mark-walker-no-qualms.html</ref>
