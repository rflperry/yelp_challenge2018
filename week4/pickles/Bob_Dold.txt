'''Robert James "Bob" Dold, Jr.'''<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.legistorm.com/memberbio/2748/Rep_Robert_Dold_IL.html |title=Representative Robert James Dold (Robert) (R-Illinois, 10th) – Biography from |publisher=LegiStorm |accessdate=2012-03-07}}</ref> (born June 23, 1969) is the former [[United States House of Representatives|U.S. Representative]] for {{ushr|IL|10}}, having served from 2011 to 2013. He is a member of the [[Republican Party (United States)|Republican Party]].<ref>{{cite news | title=Seals concedes congressional race in 10th District | publisher = [[ABC News]] | date=3 November 2010 | accessdate=2010-11-03 | url=http://abclocal.go.com/wls/story?section=news/local&id=7762723}}</ref> Prior to his election, Dold ran his family-owned business, Rose Pest Solutions.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.rosepestcontrol.com/ |title=Rose Pest Solutions |publisher=Rosepestcontrol.com |accessdate=2012-03-07}}</ref> <!-- Not to be confused with a similar company with the same name:<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.rosepestsolutions.com/|title=Rose Pest Solutions|publisher=|accessdate=25 November 2014}}</ref> --> In 2010, Dold defeated Democratic Party nominee [[Dan Seals (Illinois politician)|Dan Seals]] to replace Republican incumbent [[Mark Kirk]] in the U.S. House of Representatives. Dold was narrowly defeated by Democrat [[Brad Schneider]] in 2012, but regained the seat in 2014, defeating Schneider in a rematch. 

==Early life, education, and career==

Dold was born in [[Evanston, Illinois]], the son of Judith Gail (née Kelley) and Robert James Dold. His ancestry includes German, Swedish, Irish, Scottish, and English.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://freepages.genealogy.rootsweb.ancestry.com/%7Ebattle/reps/dold.htm |title=Bob Dold ancestry |publisher=Freepages.genealogy.rootsweb.ancestry.com |accessdate=2012-03-07}}</ref> He graduated from New Trier High School where he was quarterback of the football team and captain of the wrestling and lacrosse teams.<ref>{{cite web|title=Meet Bob|url=http://doldforcongress.com/about/|website=Dold for Congress|accessdate=9 September 2014}}</ref> He earned a BA from [[Denison University]] where he served as President of the Campus Governance Association, a law degree from [[Indiana University]] where he was selected by his classmates to give the commencement address, and an MBA from [[Northwestern University]]’s [[Kellogg School of Management]].<ref>[http://www.doldforcongress.com/pages/bio/18.php Bio] Bob Dold for Congress</ref>

Dold was an intern in the administration of Vice President [[Dan Quayle]].<ref>{{cite news|last1=Stiefel|first1=Lynne|title=Former veep weighs in|url=http://blogs.pioneerlocal.com/northshore/2010/01/quayle-on-obama.html|accessdate=9 September 2014|publisher=Pioneer Local|date=20 January 2010}}</ref> He also clerked for a New York State Judge and served as an investigative counsel for the Republican-led [[House Government Reform Committee|House Government Reform and Oversight Committee]].<ref>[http://www.pioneerlocal.com/pioneerpress/1968736,north-shore-dold-010710-s1.article Small businessman's roots run deep in district], Lynn Stiefel, ''[[Pioneer Press]]'', January 7, 2010</ref>

====Political positions====
Dold supports abortion rights.<ref>{{cite news|last1=Ford|first1=Quinn|title=In 10th district, first-term Republican Bob Dold tries to hold off challenger Brad Schneider|url=http://www.wbez.org/news/10th-district-first-term-republican-bob-dold-tries-hold-challenger-brad-schneider-103640|accessdate=12 September 2014|publisher=WBEZ|date=11-6-2012}}</ref> In 2012, Dold introduced H.R. 5650, the Protecting Women’s Access to Health Care Act, which prevents lawmakers from blocking funds to [[Planned Parenthood]].<ref>{{cite news|last1=Bassett|first1=Laura|title=Bob Dold Introduces Bill To Protect Planned Parenthood Funding|url=http://www.huffingtonpost.com/2012/05/09/bob-dold-planned-parenthood_n_1503283.html|accessdate=12 September 2014|publisher=Huffington Post|date=5-9-2012}}</ref> He has voiced support for stem cell research.<ref>{{cite news|last1=Lissau|first1=Russell|title=10th Dist. candidates back stem cell research, oppose injunction|url=http://prev.dailyherald.com/story/?id=403285|accessdate=12 September 2014|publisher=Daily Herald|date=2010-08-25}}</ref>

Dold voted for the fiscal cliff compromise bill, which permanently extended most of the Bush middle class tax cuts.<ref>{{cite news|last1=McClelland|first1=Edward|title=How Illinois Legislators Voted on Fiscal Cliff Bill|url=http://www.nbcchicago.com/blogs/ward-room/How-Illinois-Legislators-Voted-on-Fiscal-Cliff-Bill-185408262.html|accessdate=9 September 2014|publisher=NBC Chicago|date=2 January 2013}}</ref>

He has stated his support for gay marriage and immigration reform.<ref name=eyes>{{cite news|last1=Joseph|first1=Camerson|title=Illinois Republican eyes return to Congress|url=http://thehill.com/blogs/ballot-box/house-races/189761-illinois-republican-eyes-return-to-congress|accessdate=9 September 2014|publisher=The Hill|date=10 November 2013}}</ref> Regarding the [[Affordable Care Act]], he has stated his desire to improve the law rather than fight to repeal or defund it.<ref name=eyes/> Dold has said the Affordable Care Act was right to require insurance coverage for pre-existing conditions and to allow students to stay on a parent's insurance to age 26.<ref>{{cite news|last1=Pearson|first1=Rick|title=Dold, Schneider clash on abortion, gay marriage, health care|url=http://articles.chicagotribune.com/2012-08-09/news/ct-met-illinois-10th-congressional-district-0810-20120810_1_dold-health-care-gay-marriage|accessdate=12 September 2014|publisher=Chicago Tribune|date=8-9-2012}}</ref>

Dold supports gun control measures.<ref>{{cite news|last1=Condon|first1=Stephanie|title=Bloomberg super PAC supports gun control, gay rights|url=http://www.cbsnews.com/news/bloomberg-super-pac-supports-gun-control-gay-rights/|accessdate=12 September 2014|publisher=CBS News|date=2012-10-17}}</ref> He accepts the scientific consensus on climate change and believes steps should be taken to address the issue.<ref>{{cite news|last1=James|first1=Frank|title=Green GOP Group Caught Between 'Rock And A Hard Place'|url=http://www.npr.org/blogs/itsallpolitics/2014/04/22/305957364/green-gop-group-caught-between-rock-and-a-hard-place|accessdate=12 September 2014|publisher=NPR|date=2014-04-22}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|last1=Kiely|first1=Eugene|title=Democrats Distort Vote on Climate Change|url=http://www.factcheck.org/2013/04/democrats-distort-vote-on-climate-change/|accessdate=12 September 2014|publisher=FactCheck.org|date=2013-04-26}}</ref>

'''Robert James "Bob" Dold, Jr.'''<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.legistorm.com/memberbio/2748/Rep_Robert_Dold_IL.html |title=Representative Robert James Dold (Robert) (R-Illinois, 10th) – Biography from |publisher=LegiStorm |accessdate=2012-03-07}}</ref> (born June 23, 1969) is the former [[United States House of Representatives|U.S. Representative]] for {{ushr|IL|10}}, having served from 2011 to 2013. ==Early life, education, and career==

Dold was born in [[Evanston, Illinois]], the son of Judith Gail (née Kelley) and Robert James Dold. His ancestry includes German, Swedish, Irish, Scottish, and English.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://freepages.genealogy.rootsweb.ancestry.com/%7Ebattle/reps/dold.htm |title=Bob Dold ancestry |publisher=Freepages.genealogy.rootsweb.ancestry.com |accessdate=2012-03-07}}</ref> He graduated from New Trier High School where he was quarterback of the football team and captain of the wrestling and lacrosse teams.<ref>{{cite web|title=Meet Bob|url=http://doldforcongress.com/about/|website=Dold for Congress|accessdate=9 September 2014}}</ref> He earned a BA from [[Denison University]] where he served as President of the Campus Governance Association, a law degree from [[Indiana University]] where he was selected by his classmates to give the commencement address, and an MBA from [[Northwestern University]]’s [[Kellogg School of Management]].<ref>[http://www.doldforcongress.com/pages/bio/18.php Bio] Bob Dold for Congress</ref>

Dold was an intern in the administration of Vice President [[Dan Quayle]].<ref>{{cite news|last1=Stiefel|first1=Lynne|title=Former veep weighs in|url=http://blogs.pioneerlocal.com/northshore/2010/01/quayle-on-obama.html|accessdate=9 September 2014|publisher=Pioneer Local|date=20 January 2010}}</ref> He also clerked for a New York State Judge and served as an investigative counsel for the Republican-led [[House Government Reform Committee|House Government Reform and Oversight Committee]].<ref>[http://www.pioneerlocal.com/pioneerpress/1968736,north-shore-dold-010710-s1.article Small businessman's roots run deep in district], Lynn Stiefel, ''[[Pioneer Press]]'', January 7, 2010</ref>

==U.S. House of Representatives==

===Elections===
;2010
{{main|United States House of Representatives elections in Illinois, 2010#District 10*}}
In his first radio ad of the general election campaign, Dold described himself as a small business owner, fiscal conservative and social moderate.<ref>[http://www.chicagogop.com/home/dold-launches-first-radio-ad.html Dold Launches First Radio Ad], Dold for Congress Press Release, Chicago GOP, July 21, 2010. Retrieved August 18, 2010.</ref> Democratic opponent [[Dan Seals (Illinois politician)|Dan Seals]]' campaign disputed Dold's credentials as a "social moderate" because of presumed endorsements from conservative groups that the ''Daily Herald'' pointed out Dold hadn’t actually received.<ref>[http://www.dailyherald.com/story/?id=398552 Dold starts radio ad campaign in 10th Dist. race], Russell Lissau, ''[[Daily Herald (Arlington Heights)|Daily Herald]]'', August 4, 2010</ref> Dold was endorsed by the [[Chicago Tribune]],<ref>{{cite news|title=For the US House|date=7 October 2010|accessdate=2010-10-08|work=Chicago Tribune|url=http://www.chicagotribune.com/news/opinion/editorials/ct-edit-congress-20101007,0,587673.story?page=1}}</ref> the [[United States Chamber of Commerce|US Chamber of Commerce]]<ref name="pioneer">[http://www.pioneerlocal.com/glenview/news/2704246,glenview-10thbriefs-091610-s1.article], ''[[Pioneer Press]]'', Sept. 13, 2010 {{dead link|date=March 2012}}</ref> and the Electrical Contractors' Association.<ref name="newssunjuly">[http://webcache.googleusercontent.com/search?q=cache:21t4W4BC_KUJ:poll.suburbanchicagonews.com/newssun/news/2512508,5_1_WA20_POLBRFS_S1-100720.article Political Briefs], ''[[Lake County News-Sun]]'', July 20, 2010</ref> Former New York City mayor [[Rudy Giuliani]]<ref name="pioneer"/> and president of [[Americans for Tax Reform]] [[Grover Norquist]]<ref>[http://thecapitolfaxblog.com/2010/09/20/new-ads-by-foster-nrcc-norquist-endorses-dold-nazi-smear/ New ads by Foster, NRCC; Norquist endorses Dold; Nazi smear], Rich Miller, ''The Capitol Fax Blog'', September 20, 2010</ref> campaigned for Dold. After reporting nearly equal fund-raising for the second quarter,<ref>[http://blogs.suntimes.com/sweet/2010/07/dold_seals_tied_in_second_quar.html Dold, Seals tied in second quarter fund-raising in Illinois 10th House district], Lynn Sweet, ''[[Chicago Sun-Times]]'', July 15, 2010</ref> Dold's fund-raising outpaced Seals' in the third quarter. Dold began the final quarter with more cash on hand than Seals.<ref>{{cite web | author = Lynne Stiefel | title = 10th Congress District: Show me the money |work=[[Pioneer Press]] | date = 18 October 2010 | accessdate = 2010-10-18 | url = http://www.pioneerlocal.com/wilmette/news/2812556,glenview-10thfin-102110-s1.article}}</ref> At the request of the [[Federal Election Commission]] the Dold campaign amended their second quarter, 2010, filing in September, 2010 to reflect debts and expenditures that had been incurred in the second quarter but had not yet been billed when the filing period ended.<ref>[http://www.wgntv.com/news/wgntv-bob-dold-fed-investigation-sept30,0,4572973.story Candidate's campaign finance called into question FEC looking into Republican Bob Dold's campaign funds], Randi Belisomo, [[WGN-TV|WGN News]], September 30, 2010 {{dead link|date=September 2014}}</ref>

On October 7, 2010, the ''Chicago Tribune'' endorsed Dold because “he will be in the moderate, pro-choice, independent mold of Kirk.”<ref>Chicago Tribune, October 7, 2010: http://articles.chicagotribune.com/2010-10-07/news/ct-edit-congress-20101007_1_halvorson-adam-kinzinger-trillion-deficit/2</ref>

;2012
{{main|United States House of Representatives elections in Illinois, 2012#District 10*}}
The [[National Journal]]'s [[Cook Political Report]] named Dold one of the top 10 Republicans most vulnerable to redistricting in 2012<ref>{{cite web |url= http://www.nationaljournal.com/top-10-republicans-most-vulnerable-to-redistricting-20110415|title= Top 10 Republicans Most Vulnerable to Redistricting  |author= David Wasserman and Julia Edwards|date= April 15, 2011|work= Cook Political Report|publisher= National Journal|accessdate=April 25, 2011}}</ref> with the 10th becoming more Democratic following redistricting. The endorsement of Mark Kirk, who is popular in the district, was expected to help Dold.<ref name="nbcchicago1">{{cite web|last=McClelland |first=Edward |url=http://www.nbcchicago.com/blogs/ward-room/Ward-Room-Handicaps-The-Congressional-Races-143978246.html |title=Handicapping The Congressional Races |publisher=NBC Chicago |date=March 23, 2012 |accessdate=March 31, 2012}}</ref> Dold had a strong cash-on-hand advantage over his opponent [[Brad Schneider]].<ref>{{cite news|last=Lissau|first=Russell|title=Sheyman, Schneider lead Democratic candidates in 10th District cash race|url=http://www.dailyherald.com/article/20120202/news/702029882/|newspaper=Daily Herald|date=February 2, 2012}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|title=Sheyman Brings a 'Truly Progressive Voice'|url=http://buffalogrove.patch.com/articles/sheyman-brings-a-truly-progressive-voice|newspaper=Buffalo Grove Patch|date=March 7, 2012}}</ref> Dold earned the endorsement of the [[Chicago Tribune]] and [[Daily Herald (Arlington Heights)|Daily Herald]].<ref>{{cite news| url=http://www.chicagotribune.com/news/opinion/editorials/ct-edit-intro-1007-20121007,0,4787522.story | work=Chicago Tribune | title=Searching for solutions | date=October 7, 2012}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.dailyherald.com/article/20121009/discuss/710099946/|title=Endorsement: Dold over Schneider in 10th Congressional District|work=Daily Herald|accessdate=25 November 2014}}</ref> Schneider defeated Dold 51%-49%, a difference of just 3,000 votes.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.politico.com/2012-election/map/#/House/2012/IL|title=2014 Election Results Senate: Map by State, Live Midterm Voting Updates|work=POLITICO|accessdate=25 November 2014}}</ref>

On May 8, 2013, Dold announced in an e-mail to supporters that he would run for his old seat in 2014.<ref>{{cite news| url=http://articles.chicagotribune.com/2013-05-09/news/ct-met-bob-dold-rematch-0509-20130509_1_brad-schneider-outside-spending-10th-district | work=Chicago Tribune | title=Dold out to reclaim north suburban congressional seat | date=May 9, 2013}}</ref> He was actively recruited to run again.<ref>{{cite news|last1=Lester|first1=Kerry|title=Dold being recruited by top GOP to run again in 10th|url=http://www.dailyherald.com/article/20130415/news/704159797/|accessdate=9 September 2014|publisher=Daily Herald|date=15 April 2013}}</ref> The race has been ranked the #7 most likely flip for 2014 House rematches.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.rollcall.com/news/ranking_potential_flips_for_2014_house_rematches-224563-1.html?pg=2|title=Ranking Potential Flips for 2014 House Rematches|work=Roll Call|accessdate=25 November 2014}}</ref> Dold won the rematch and will take office at the start of the 114th Congress.

====Political positions====
Dold supports abortion rights.<ref>{{cite news|last1=Ford|first1=Quinn|title=In 10th district, first-term Republican Bob Dold tries to hold off challenger Brad Schneider|url=http://www.wbez.org/news/10th-district-first-term-republican-bob-dold-tries-hold-challenger-brad-schneider-103640|accessdate=12 September 2014|publisher=WBEZ|date=11-6-2012}}</ref> In 2012, Dold introduced H.R. 5650, the Protecting Women’s Access to Health Care Act, which prevents lawmakers from blocking funds to [[Planned Parenthood]].<ref>{{cite news|last1=Bassett|first1=Laura|title=Bob Dold Introduces Bill To Protect Planned Parenthood Funding|url=http://www.huffingtonpost.com/2012/05/09/bob-dold-planned-parenthood_n_1503283.html|accessdate=12 September 2014|publisher=Huffington Post|date=5-9-2012}}</ref> He has voiced support for stem cell research.<ref>{{cite news|last1=Lissau|first1=Russell|title=10th Dist. candidates back stem cell research, oppose injunction|url=http://prev.dailyherald.com/story/?id=403285|accessdate=12 September 2014|publisher=Daily Herald|date=2010-08-25}}</ref>

Dold voted for the fiscal cliff compromise bill, which permanently extended most of the Bush middle class tax cuts.<ref>{{cite news|last1=McClelland|first1=Edward|title=How Illinois Legislators Voted on Fiscal Cliff Bill|url=http://www.nbcchicago.com/blogs/ward-room/How-Illinois-Legislators-Voted-on-Fiscal-Cliff-Bill-185408262.html|accessdate=9 September 2014|publisher=NBC Chicago|date=2 January 2013}}</ref>

He has stated his support for gay marriage and immigration reform.<ref name=eyes>{{cite news|last1=Joseph|first1=Camerson|title=Illinois Republican eyes return to Congress|url=http://thehill.com/blogs/ballot-box/house-races/189761-illinois-republican-eyes-return-to-congress|accessdate=9 September 2014|publisher=The Hill|date=10 November 2013}}</ref> Regarding the [[Affordable Care Act]], he has stated his desire to improve the law rather than fight to repeal or defund it.<ref name=eyes/> Dold has said the Affordable Care Act was right to require insurance coverage for pre-existing conditions and to allow students to stay on a parent's insurance to age 26.<ref>{{cite news|last1=Pearson|first1=Rick|title=Dold, Schneider clash on abortion, gay marriage, health care|url=http://articles.chicagotribune.com/2012-08-09/news/ct-met-illinois-10th-congressional-district-0810-20120810_1_dold-health-care-gay-marriage|accessdate=12 September 2014|publisher=Chicago Tribune|date=8-9-2012}}</ref>

Dold supports gun control measures.<ref>{{cite news|last1=Condon|first1=Stephanie|title=Bloomberg super PAC supports gun control, gay rights|url=http://www.cbsnews.com/news/bloomberg-super-pac-supports-gun-control-gay-rights/|accessdate=12 September 2014|publisher=CBS News|date=2012-10-17}}</ref> He accepts the scientific consensus on climate change and believes steps should be taken to address the issue.<ref>{{cite news|last1=James|first1=Frank|title=Green GOP Group Caught Between 'Rock And A Hard Place'|url=http://www.npr.org/blogs/itsallpolitics/2014/04/22/305957364/green-gop-group-caught-between-rock-and-a-hard-place|accessdate=12 September 2014|publisher=NPR|date=2014-04-22}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|last1=Kiely|first1=Eugene|title=Democrats Distort Vote on Climate Change|url=http://www.factcheck.org/2013/04/democrats-distort-vote-on-climate-change/|accessdate=12 September 2014|publisher=FactCheck.org|date=2013-04-26}}</ref>
