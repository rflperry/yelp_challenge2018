'''John R. Moolenaar''' (born May 8, 1961) is a [[Republican Party (United States)|Republican]] politician from [[Michigan]] currently serving in the [[Michigan Senate]] after serving three terms in the [[Michigan House of Representatives]].<ref>[http://legislature.mi.gov/(S(oihnpzmtwrwfcs45ex1nab45))/documents/2011-2012/michiganmanual/2011-MM-P0164-p0164.pdf 2011-2012 Michigan Manual: State Senator John Moolenaar]</ref>

Moolenaar is a chemist and worked at Dow Chemical Company before entering politics.<ref>http://www.moolenaarforsenate.com/about-john.html</ref>  He was elected to the State House in 2002, having lost a race for the State Senate in 1990.<ref>http://blogs.rollcall.com/rothenblog/freshman-class-filled-with-losers/?dcz=</ref> Prior to his election to the Legislature, Moolenaar served on the [[Midland, Michigan|Midland]] City Council.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.senatorjohnmoolenaar.com/meet-senator-moolenaar/|title=Meet Senator Moolenaar - Senator John Moolenaar|work=Senator John Moolenaar|accessdate=29 October 2014}}</ref> In 2014, Moolenaar ran for the [[United States House of Representatives]] seat representing {{ushr|MI|4}}. He won the Republican [[primary election]],<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.mlive.com/news/saginaw/index.ssf/2014/08/4th_district_in_congress_race.html|title=Sen. John Moolenaar defeats Paul Mitchell in 4th District congressional Republican primary race|work=MLive.com|accessdate=29 October 2014}}</ref> and was subsequently elected to Congress after winning the general election.
