'''James Frederick "Jim" Bridenstine''' (born June 15, 1975) is an American politician who has been the [[United States House of Representatives|United States Representative]] for [[Oklahoma's 1st congressional district]], based in [[Tulsa]],<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.tulsaworld.com/news/article.aspx?subjectid=11&articleid=20110915_16_A10_CUTLIN858908&allcom=1 |title=Republican Jim Bridenstine to challenge incumbent John Sullivan |publisher=Tulsa World |date=2011-09-15 |accessdate=2012-07-05}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.krmg.com/news/news/local/bridenstine-announces-bid-congress/nDndw/ |title=Bridenstine announces bid for Congress |publisher=www.krmg.com |date=2011-09-15 |accessdate=2012-07-05}}</ref> since 2013. He is a member of the [[Republican Party (US)|Republican Party]].

== Early life, education, and military service==
Bridenstine was born in [[Ann Arbor, Michigan]] and is a [[Jenks High School]] graduate,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.gtrnews.com/greater-tulsa-reporter/10894/jim-bridenstine-a-challenger-for-dist-1 |title=GTR Newspapers &#124; Find Local Tulsa, Bixby, Broken Arrow, Jenks, Union, and Owasso News, Sports, and Entertainment:Jim Bridenstine a Challenger for Dist. 1 |publisher=Gtrnews.com |date=2012-04-24 |accessdate=2012-07-05}}</ref> a graduate of [[Rice University]] with three majors, and has an [[MBA]] from [[Cornell University]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://restoringamericaproject.org/2012/04/24/jim-bridenstine/ |title=Jim Bridenstine |publisher=Restoring America Project |date=2012-04-24 |accessdate=2012-07-05}}</ref> He is a former executive director of the [[Tulsa Air and Space Museum]] & Planetarium and is a [[Naval Aviator]] in the [[U.S. Navy Reserve]] where he flies the [[E-2C]] Hawkeye in Central and South America in support of the [[War on Drugs]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.jimbridenstine.com/html/about.php |title=About Jim Bridenstine the Republican Patriot For U.S. Congress |publisher=Jimbridenstine.com |date= |accessdate=2012-07-05}}</ref> Bridenstine is an [[Eagle Scout (Boy Scouts of America)|Eagle Scout]] and received several military awards, including the [[Air Medal]].  Bridenstine is also a current State of Oklahoma record holder in swimming.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.teamunify.com/czokslsc/UserFiles/File/2013_02_05_lcm_lsc.pdf |title=Oklahoma Swimming Records |publisher=teamunify.com |date= |accessdate=2013-01-01}}</ref>

==U.S. House of Representatives==

===2012 election===
{{see also|United States House of Representatives elections in Oklahoma, 2012#District 1}}
On June 26, 2012, Bridenstine pulled a major upset against five-term incumbent U.S. Congressman [[John Sullivan (Oklahoma)|John Sullivan]] in the Republican primary election.  Although he identified with the [[tea party movement|tea party]] and was perceived as running to Sullivan's right Bridenstine's actual policy statements differed little from those of Sullivan.  He defeated Sullivan 54%-46%.  He ran<ref>{{cite news |title=Tea Party favorite Bridenstine defeats incumbent Sullivan in Oklahoma House race |first= |last= |url=http://www.foxnews.com/politics/2012/06/27/tea-party-favorite-bridenstine-defeats-incumbent-sullivan-in-oklahoma-house/ |work=[[Fox News]] |date=June 27, 2012 }}</ref>
<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.kjrh.com/dpp/news/local_news/bridenstine-says-people-want-change |title=Bridenstine beats Sullivan in US Congressional District 1 race |publisher=Kjrh.com |date= |accessdate=2012-07-05}}</ref>
<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.newson6.com/story/18889004/five-term-oklahoma-congressman-john-sullivan-suffers-primary-defeat |title=Five-Term Oklahoma Congressman Sullivan Suffers Primary Defeat - NewsOn6.com - Tulsa, OK - News, Weather, Video and Sports - KOTV.com &#124; |publisher=NewsOn6.com |date= |accessdate=2012-07-05}}</ref>
<ref>{{cite web|author=posted by: Dan Phillips - email |url=http://www.ktul.com/story/18889029/bridenstine-defeats-incumbent-sullivan-in-primary |title=Tulsa's Channel 8 - News, Weather & Sports - Bridenstine Defeats Incumbent Sullivan In Primary |publisher=KTUL.com |date=2012-06-27 |accessdate=2012-07-05}}</ref>
<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.tulsaworld.com/news/article.aspx?subjectid=688&articleid=20120627_16_A1_CUTLIN551830 |title=Bridenstine claims upset over Sullivan |publisher=Tulsa World |date= |accessdate=2012-07-05}}</ref>
<ref>{{cite web|author=SEAN MURPHY, Associated Press |url=http://www.sfgate.com/news/article/Navy-pilot-upsets-5-term-Oklahoma-congressman-3665490.php |title=Navy pilot upsets 5-term Oklahoma congressman |publisher=SFGate |date=2012-06-27 |accessdate=2012-07-05}}</ref>{{update after|2012|12|15}} In November, he defeated Democratic nominee John Olson 63%-32% in [[Oklahoma's 1st congressional district]]. He won all of the five counties in the district.<ref>http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=705054</ref>  However, he had effectively clinched a seat in Congress by ousting Sullivan in the primary.  The 1st is a heavily Republican district with a [[Cook Partisan Voting Index]] of R+18.  [[John McCain]] won the district by 26 points in 2008, and [[Mitt Romney]] carried it almost as easily in 2012.

In the wake of the latest outbreak in May that cost 48 lives, it is painfully clear that we must do more."<ref name="GOPpredict"/> Bridenstine claimed 30 times more money was being spent on climate change research than on weather forecasting and warning, a claim that is not true.<ref>{{cite web|title=Rep. Jim Bridenstine says U.S. spends 30 times as much on climate change research as on weather forecasting|url=http://www.politifact.com/truth-o-meter/statements/2013/jun/14/jim-bridenstine/rep-jim-bridenstine-says-us-spends-30-times-much-c/|publisher=Politifact.com|accessdate=20 July 2014}}</ref>

==U.S. House of Representatives==

===2012 election===
{{see also|United States House of Representatives elections in Oklahoma, 2012#District 1}}
On June 26, 2012, Bridenstine pulled a major upset against five-term incumbent U.S. Congressman [[John Sullivan (Oklahoma)|John Sullivan]] in the Republican primary election.  Although he identified with the [[tea party movement|tea party]] and was perceived as running to Sullivan's right Bridenstine's actual policy statements differed little from those of Sullivan.  He defeated Sullivan 54%-46%. '''James Frederick "Jim" Bridenstine''' (born June 15, 1975) is an American politician who has been the [[United States House of Representatives|United States Representative]] for [[Oklahoma's 1st congressional district]], based in [[Tulsa]],<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.tulsaworld.com/news/article.aspx?subjectid=11&articleid=20110915_16_A10_CUTLIN858908&allcom=1 |title=Republican Jim Bridenstine to challenge incumbent John Sullivan |publisher=Tulsa World |date=2011-09-15 |accessdate=2012-07-05}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.krmg.com/news/news/local/bridenstine-announces-bid-congress/nDndw/ |title=Bridenstine announces bid for Congress |publisher=www.krmg.com |date=2011-09-15 |accessdate=2012-07-05}}</ref> since 2013. He is a member of the [[Republican Party (US)|Republican Party]].
