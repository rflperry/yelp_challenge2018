'''Mark Takai''' (born July 1, 1967) is an American politician from the state of [[Hawaii]]. He is a congressman-elect of the [[U.S. House of Representatives]], and will be representing the 1st district, as a [[Democratic Party (United States)|Democrat]]. He previously served in the Hawaii state legislature, from 1994 until 2014.

Takai is from [[Aiea, Hawaii]]. He serves in the [[Hawaii Army National Guard]] as a [[Lieutenant Colonel]] and took part in [[Operation Iraqi Freedom]] in 2009.<ref name=ltcol>{{cite web|url=http://www.hawaiireporter.com/state-rep-k-mark-takai-promoted-to-lieutenant-colonel/123 |title=State Rep. K. Mark Takai Promoted to Lieutenant Colonel |publisher=Hawaii Reporter |date=June 18, 2013 |accessdate=December 30, 2013}}</ref>

Takai became the Democratic nominee for the [[United States House of Representatives]] for {{ushr|HI|1}} in the 2014 [[United States House of Representatives elections in Hawaii, 2014|2014 elections]], following [[incumbent]] [[Colleen Hanabusa]]'s decision to run for the [[United States Senate]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.hawaiinewsnow.com/story/23077766/state-rep-mark-takai-launches-congressional-bid |title=State Rep. Mark Takai launches congressional bid - Hawaii News Now - KGMB and KHNL |publisher=Hawaii News Now |date=August 7, 2013 |accessdate=December 30, 2013}}</ref> He won that election with 51.9% of the vote, defeating Republican former Congressman [[Charles Djou]].

== Early life and education ==
Takai received his diploma from [[Pearl City High School (Hawaii)|Pearl City High School]] in 1985.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.hawaiireporter.com/hsta-and-nea-give-takai-huge-end-of-year-boost-with-endorsement/123 |title=HSTA and NEA Give Takai Huge End-of-Year Boost with Endorsement |publisher=Hawaii Reporter |date= |accessdate=2014-08-10}}</ref> He is a four-time High School Swimming Champion and a High School All-American Swimmer.<ref name=midweek>{{cite web|url=http://archives.midweek.com/content/columns/keepingscore_article/a_swim_star_turns_uh_watchdog/ |title=A Swim Star Turns UH Watchdog &#124; Keeping Score &#124; Midweek.com |publisher=Archives.midweek.com |date=2007-06-13 |accessdate=2014-08-10}}</ref> Takai received his [[Bachelor of Arts]] degree in Political Science and his [[Master of Public Health]] degree from the [[University of Hawaii at Manoa]].<ref>K. Mark Takai, Honolulu Star-Bulletin, [http://archives.starbulletin.com/2007/03/25/editorial/commentary3.html Editorial: University View; Let’s Give UH a Second Century of Promise], March 25, 2007</ref> While at the University, Takai was a [[Western Athletic Conference]] Champion swimmer, the President of the Student Senate, and the Editor-in-Chief of the campus newspaper.<ref name=midweek/>

==Hawaii House of Representatives (1994–2014)==
Takai hails from the 33rd House District representing the communities of [[Aiea, Hawaii]], on the shores of [[Pearl Harbor]]. He was first elected in 1994 and has won re-election nine more times (1994 through 2012).<ref name=ltcol/>

Takai is the Chairman of the House Committee on Veterans, Military, the Committee on International Affairs, and the Committee on Culture and the Arts. He is a member of the Committee on Education and the Committee on Higher Education. During the 2005 and 2006 sessions, Takai served as Vice Speaker of the House. He served as Chairman of the House Committee on Higher Education (2003 to 2004 sessions) and as Vice Chairman (1995 to 2002 sessions). Additionally, he was Chairman of the House Committee on Culture and the Arts (1997 to 2000 sessions).{{citation needed|date=August 2014}}

==Military Service==

Mark Takai is a member of the Hawaii Army National Guard. Takai was commissioned a First Lieutenant in the Hawaii Army National Guard (HIARNG) on July 19, 1999 and worked as the Preventive Medical Officer. Currently, he is the Division Chief for Soldiers Services and a School Liaison for the HIARNG.

Additionally, Lt. Col Takai serves as the President of the Hawaii National Guard Association and the President of the National Guard Association-Hawaii Insurance, Inc.

Takai served in [[Operation Iraqi Freedom]] as the Base Operations Officer (Camp Mayor) at Camp Patriot, Kuwait from February 2009 to September 2009. He served as the Company Commander of Charlie Company (Medical), 29th Brigade Support Battalion from November 2006 to May 2008. Mark was also called to active duty for six months (May to November 2005) and served as the Hawaii Army National Guard Deputy State Surgeon.

Among his numerous awards and decorations, Lt. Col Takai received the Meritorious Service Medal from the United States Army in 2009, the Distinguished Service Medal from the National Guard Association of the United States in 2011, and the Hawaii Distinguished Service Order in 2012.

Takai's official date of rank as Lieutenant Colonel was May 14, 2013.
