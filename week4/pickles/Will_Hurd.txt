'''William Ballard "Will" Hurd''' (born August 19, 1977) is an American politician who was elected to serve [[Texas's 23rd congressional district]] in the [[United States House of Representatives elections, 2014]]. He will take office on January 3, 2015. Hurd is the first black Republican elected to Congress from Texas.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.npr.org/blogs/codeswitch/2014/11/08/362547523/as-gop-swept-congress-black-republicans-took-home-historic-wins|title=As GOP Swept Congress, Black Republicans Took Home Historic Wins|author=Hansi Lo Wang|date=November 8, 2014|publisher=NPR|accessdate=November 17, 2014}}</ref>

==Early years==
Hurd is a graduate of [[John Marshall High School (Leon Valley, Texas)|John Marshall High School]] in the [[San Antonio, Texas|San Antonio]] suburb of [[Leon Valley, Texas]].<ref name="sa100303a"/>  After high school Hurd attended [[Texas A&M]] and served as the Student Body President in 1999 at the time of [[Aggie Bonfire]] collapse.<ref name="NatlJWillHurdNov2014"/> He graduated from A&M in 2000 with a degree in computer science, a minor in international relations.<ref name="NatlJWillHurdNov2014"/>

Hurd worked for the [[Central Intelligence Agency]] for nine years, stationed in [[Washington, D.C.]], including a tour of duty as an operations officer in [[Afghanistan]], [[Pakistan]], and [[India]].<ref name="sa100303a"/><ref name="NatlJWillHurdNov2014"/>

He returned to Texas after his CIA service and worked for Crumpton Group, strategic advisory firm, as a partner and a senior adviser with the cybersecurity firm FusionX.<ref name="NatlJWillHurdNov2014"/>

==2010 campaign==
Hurd announced his candidacy on November 19, 2009 for the Republican nomination in Texas's 23rd congressional district.<ref name="texmo"/><ref name="roll"/> His electronically-filed campaign finance records show him to have $70,000 on hand to fund his attempt.<ref name="Tribune"/>

On February 15, 2010, Hurd received the endorsement of the ''[[San Antonio Express-News]]''.<ref name="saend"/> In the [[primary election]] on March 2, 2010, he the received the greatest number of votes but failed to received a majority of the votes cast, resulting in a run-off election on April 13, 2010.<ref name="sa100303"/><ref name="primary"/> He faced second-place finisher [[Quico Canseco|Francisco "Quico" Canseco]], a San Antonio banker making his third attempt at a Congressional seat.<ref name="sa100303"/> Hurd lost to Canseco in the runoff 53%-47%. Canseco ultimately won the general election but lost in 2012.

==2014 campaign==
Hurd once again ran for the 23rd district in the [[United States House of Representatives elections, 2014]] and defeated incumbent [[Pete Gallego]] by 2,500 votes.<ref name="NatlJWillHurdNov2014"/>  He was endorsed by the ''[[San Antonio Express-News]]''.<ref name="NatlJWillHurdNov2014"/>

He did a post-election swing through some parts of his district that had heavily favored the incumbent Gallego in the voting.<ref> [http://www.fortstocktonpioneer.com/community/article_a9bc110a-80a4-11e4-9c21-d7ec24b280dd.html U.S. rep-elect comes through town] ''The Fort Stockton Pioneer'' December 11, 2015</ref>

==Political positions==
Hurd is pro-life.<ref>[http://www.lifenews.com/2014/11/05/black-pro-life-candidates-tim-scott-mia-love-and-will-hurd-win-huge-victories/ Life Site News, Nov. 5, 2014]</ref>

He supports stronger border security.<ref name="NatlJWillHurdNov2014"/>

He opposes granting citizenship to "illegal aliens".<ref name="NatlJWillHurdNov2014"/>
