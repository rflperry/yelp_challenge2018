'''Ralph Lee Abraham''' (born September 16, 1954)<ref>{{cite web|url=http://info.cqrollcall.com/rs/cqrc/images/CQNews_NewMemberGuide2014.pdf|title=Guide to the New Congress|publisher=[[Roll Call]]|accessdate=December 6, 2014}}</ref> is an American physician and former veterinarian from [[Mangham, Louisiana|Mangham]], [[Louisiana]], who won election on [[United States House of Representatives elections in Louisiana, 2014#District 5|December 6, 2014]], as a [[Republican Party (United States)|Republican]] to represent {{ushr|LA|5}} in the [[United States House of Representatives]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.myarklamiss.com/story/d/story/nela-doctor-running-for-congress/91351/n2Wcnu8DrkG-bKuzagMnNg|title=NELA Doctor Running for Congress|date=May 30, 2014|accessdate=June 2, 2014|work=myarklamiss.com}}</ref>  He defeated his [[Democratic Party (United States)|Democratic]] opponent, [[Mayor]] [[Jamie Mayo]] of [[Monroe, Louisiana|Monroe]], by a margin of 134,612 votes (64.2 percent) to 75,004 (35.8 percent).<ref>{{cite web|url=http://staticresults.sos.la.gov/12062014/12062014_23.html|title=Runoff election returns, December 6, 2014|publisher=[[Louisiana Secretary of State]]|accessdate=December 6, 2014}}</ref>

Abraham has served in the [[United States Coast Guard Auxiliary]] and the [[Mississippi National Guard]]. Abraham and his wife, Dianne, have three children. He is a graduate of [[Louisiana State University]] in [[Baton Rouge, Louisiana|Baton Rouge]] and the [[Louisiana State University School of Medicine]].<ref name=newsstar>{{cite web|url=http://www.thenewsstar.com/story/news/local/2014/11/22/abraham-remained-consistent-ersistent/19430723/|title=Abraham: ‘We remained consistent and persistent’|author=Greg Hilburn|date=November 22, 2014|work=''[[The News-Star|The Monroe News-Star]]''|accessdate=December 7, 2014}}</ref>

In his debate with opponent Mayo, Abraham recalled that he developed his medical practice "from a little trailer. ... You should come to my practice in Mangham on a daily basis and I can show you the debacle of the [[Patient Protection and Affordable Care Act|Obamacare]] law ... As a practicing physician, right now, it's not working and it's even dangerous. ... When there is a wrong, we stand up and correct it. When we don't, we become just another [[Socialism|socialist]] country. We've got to get back to fiscal responsibility."<ref name=debate>{{cite web|url=http://www.thenewsstar.com/story/news/politics/2014/11/20/mayo-abraham-face-th-district-debate/70035864/|title=Mayo, Abraham face off in 5th District debate|author=Kaleb Causey|date=November 20, 2014|publisher=''The Monroe News-Star''|accessdate=December 13, 2014}}</ref>Mayo dismissed Abraham as a "multimillionaire" and endorsed the expansion of the [[Medicaid]] program to incorporate more of the working poor. Abraham replied, "You would think if I've made millions of dollars from [Medicaid], then I would be for it. To expand Medicaid would take in 500,000-plus people. What the Obama administration won't tell you is that 260,000 of those would be moved from private insurance groups."<ref name=debate/>

Regarding [[illegal immigration]], Abraham said that he stands for "No [[amnesty]] (and) seal the borders. For those illegals, 5 million-plus now, ... we don't know who they are, what they are, or who they represent. No amnesty, seal the border."<ref name=debate/>

After his election, Abraham announced that Luke J. Letlow, his campaign manager, will  become his chief of staff in January.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.thenewsstar.com/story/news/local/2014/12/10/letlow-named-abrahams-chief-staff/20201499/|title=Letlow named Abraham’s chief of staff|author=Greg Hilburn|date=December 10, 2014|publisher=''[[The News-Star|Monroe News-Star]]''|accessdate=December 11, 2014}}</ref>
