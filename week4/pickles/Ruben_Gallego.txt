'''Ruben Gallego''' (born November 20, 1979) is a former state representative and United States representative-elect from [[Arizona]]. He served as assistant minority leader from 2012 until his resignation to run for Congress, due to Arizona's [[Resign-to-run laws#Arizona|resign-to-run law]].

==Personal life and education==
Gallego was born in Chicago<ref>{{cite web|url=http://info.cqrollcall.com/rs/cqrc/images/CQNews_NewMemberGuide2014.pdf|title=Guide to the New Congress|publisher=[[Roll Call]]|accessdate=December 6, 2014}}</ref> and is a first-generation American. Along with his three sisters, he was raised by a single mother.<ref name=Phoenix>{{cite news|last=Lopatin|first=Shari|title=Marine Turned Politician|url=http://www.phoenixmag.com/lifestyle/valley-news/201109/marine-turned-politician/|accessdate=22 August 2013|newspaper=Phoenix Magazine|date=September 2011}}</ref> On August 7, 2008, Ruben Marinelarena changed his name to Ruben Marinelarena Gallego to honor his single mother.<ref>http://www.washingtonpost.com/blogs/the-fix/wp/2014/06/12/this-arizona-candidate-changed-his-name-his-opponent-wasnt-happy-about-it/</ref> Gallego is married to Kate Gallego, a councilwoman on the Phoenix City Council.<ref>{{cite web|title=Contact Councilwoman Kate Gallego|url=http://phoenix.gov/district8/emdist8/|publisher=City of Phoenix|accessdate=27 February 2014}}</ref>  He graduated from [[Harvard University]] in 2004.<ref name=AZCentral>{{cite news|title=Candidate Q and A: Ruben Gallego|url=http://www.azcentral.com/news/election/questionnaire2010/azrep.php?action=ViewAns&canname=rgall237|accessdate=22 August 2013|newspaper=AZCentral.com}}</ref>

Gallego currently sits on the boards of Valley Citizens League, the Children’s Museum, and President’s Community Advisory Board for [[South Mountain Community College]].<ref name=Leg>{{cite web|title=Representative Ruben Gallego|url=http://www.azleg.gov/MembersPage.asp?Member_ID=105&Legislature=50&Session_ID=102|publisher=Arizona State Legislature|accessdate=22 August 2013}}</ref>

==Career==
Gallego served in the [[United States Marine Corps|Marines]], deploying to Iraq. He lost his best friend in combat in Iraq.<ref name="Phoenix"/> His desire to help fellow veterans motivated the formerly apolitical Gallego to get involved with politics.<ref name=SMDN>{{cite news|title=Ambition, Life Experience Driving State Representative|url=http://somonews.com/ambition-life-experience-driving-state-representative/|accessdate=22 August 2013|newspaper=South Mountain District News|date=31 May 2011}}</ref>

In 2011, he was named as a distinguished freshman lawmaker by The Arizona Republic.<ref name=Distinguished>{{cite news|last=Pitzl|first=Mary Jo|title=Arizona House and Senate distinguished freshmen|url=http://www.azcentral.com/news/election/azelections/articles/20110521arizona-legislators-freshmen.html|accessdate=22 August 2013|newspaper=The Arizona Republic|date=21 May 2011}}</ref> Gallego's first successful bill granted in-state tuition status to veterans who residing in Arizona.<ref name="Phoenix"/> Gallego supports the repeal of [[Arizona SB 1070]]. He considers education to be the most important long-term priority for Arizona.<ref name="AZCentral"/> In 2012, Gallego was elected assistant minority leader.<ref name=Memphis>{{cite news|title=Democrats select leaders in Arizona House, Senate|url=http://www.myfoxmemphis.com/story/20048601/2012/11/08/democrats-select-leaders-in-arizona-house-senate|accessdate=22 August 2013|newspaper=My Fox Memphis|date=13 November 2012}}</ref> 

He founded the group Citizens for Professional Law Enforcement, with the goal of recalling [[Maricopa County]] Sheriff [[Joe Arpaio]]. Gallego cited Arpaio's immigration policies and his use of taxpayer money to investigate [[Barack Obama]]'s [[Barack Obama citizenship conspiracy theories|citizenship]] as reasons for recalling Arpaio.<ref name=Huff>{{cite news|last=Celock|first=John|title=Joe Arpaio Opponents Form Super PAC To Unseat Arizona Sheriff|url=http://www.huffingtonpost.com/2012/09/25/joe-arpaio-super-pac-arizona_n_1912636.html|accessdate=22 August 2013|newspaper=Huffington Post|date=25 September 2012}}</ref>

Gallego works for Strategies 360 as Director of Latino and New Media operations. He also worked for Riester, one of the largest public relations firms in Arizona, and for Phoenix Councilman Michael Nowakowski.<ref name=Strategies>{{cite web|title=Ruben Gallego|url=http://www.strategies360.com/strategies-360-expands-arizona-office-with-phoenix-leader-ruben-gallego.html|publisher=Strategies 360|accessdate=22 August 2013}}</ref>

On February 27, 2014, Gallego announced his candidacy for U.S. Congress in [[Arizona's 7th congressional district]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Ruben Gallego, Arizona State Rep., Announces Bid For Congress|url=http://www.huffingtonpost.com/2014/02/27/ruben-gallego-ed-pastor_n_4869759.html|publisher=Huffington Post|accessdate=1 March 2014}}</ref> Gallego resigned from the Arizona House in March 2014.<ref>http://www.myfoxphoenix.com/story/24981824/rep-gallego-resigns-from-arizona-house</ref> [[Mayday PAC]], a [[super PAC]] seeking to reduce the role of money in politics, announced its endorsement of Gallego because of his impressive evolution on the issue of campaign finance reform.<ref>{{cite news|last1=Sullivan|first1=Sean|title=A leading ‘anti-super PAC’ just backed three more candidates for Congress|url=http://www.washingtonpost.com/blogs/post-politics/wp/2014/08/11/a-leading-anti-super-pac-just-backed-three-more-candidates-for-congress/|accessdate=11 August 2014|publisher=Washington Post|date=11 August 2014}}</ref> He was elected United States Representative for Arizona's 7th District on November 4, 2014.  On February 28, 2013 Gallego voted against an amendment that sought to raise campaign finance limits for federal candidates and abolish all limits for state candidates, HB 2523.<ref>http://votesmart.org/bill/16626/43942/123732/amends-campaign-finance-limits#.U_wW1EuA88M</ref> He has since been a vocal supporter of the Government By the People Act.<ref>http://gallegoforarizona.com/tag/ruben-gallego/</ref>
