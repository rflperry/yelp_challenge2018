'''Patrick Alan Nunnelee''' (born October 9, 1958) is an [[United States|American]] politician who has been the [[United States House of Representatives|U.S. Representative]] for {{ushr|MS|1}} since 2011. Previously he served in the [[Mississippi State Senate]], representing the 6th district, from 1995 to 2011. He is a member of the [[Republican Party (United States)|Republican Party]].

==Early life, education, and business career==
Nunnelee was born in [[Tupelo, Mississippi|Tupelo]], in [[Lee County, Mississippi|Lee County]], [[Mississippi]], on October 9, 1958.<ref name=votesmart>{{cite web|url=http://www.votesmart.org/bio.php?can_id=8326|title=VoteSmart.org|work=Project Vote Smart|accessdate=19 October 2014}}</ref> Nunnelee graduated from [[Clinton High School (Clinton, Mississippi)|Clinton High School]] in [[Clinton, Mississippi]] in 1976 and then attended [[Mississippi State University]], graduating with a bachelor’s degree in 1980. While a student at MSU he lost much of his eyesight to a degenerative eye disease. Although legally blind he continued his studies. His sight was restored after receiving [[Corneal transplantation|cornea transplants]].

Nunnelee was employed by American Funeral Assurance Co., eventually becoming vice president of sales and marketing. His father was also employed by the company, rising to president and CEO. In 1996, Nunnelee and his father founded Allied Funeral Associates, Inc. and Allied Funeral Associates Insurance Company and he has served as Vice-President and Director of both entities.<ref name=votesmart/>

Before beginning his political career he was a popular speaker, crediting God, organ donors, and organizations such as the [[Lions Clubs International|Lions Club]] for having his eyesight restored.

==Mississippi Senate==

===Elections===
In 1995, incumbent Republican State Senator [[Roger Wicker]] of the 6th district resigned in order to take up a seat in the [[U.S. House of Representatives]]. Nunnelee ran and won. In 1999, he won re-election to a second term unopposed.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=371288|title=Our Campaigns - MS State Senate 06 Race - Nov 02, 1999|publisher=|accessdate=19 October 2014}}</ref> In 2003, he won re-election to a third term with 69% of the vote.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=210764|title=Our Campaigns - MS State Senate 06 Race - Nov 04, 2003|publisher=|accessdate=19 October 2014}}</ref> In 2007, he won re-election to a fourth term with 66% of the vote.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=382152|title=Our Campaigns - MS State Senate 06 Race - Nov 06, 2007|publisher=|accessdate=19 October 2014}}</ref>

;2012

In March 2012, he won the primary (57%) defeating Ross (29%) and Estes (14%).<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.politico.com/2012-election/map/#/House/2012/Primary/MS|title=2014 Election Results Senate: Live Map by State, Midterm Races  Races|work=POLITICO|accessdate=19 October 2014}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=751578|title=Our Campaigns - MS District 01 - R Primary Race - Mar 13, 2012|publisher=|accessdate=19 October 2014}}</ref>

==Personal life==
Nunnelee married Tori Bedells of Clinton, MS, a graduate of [[University of Southern Mississippi]] Nursing School.<ref>{{Cite news | last=Long|first=Robert Lee|title=Alan Nunnelee wants to serve First District|newspaper=Desoto Times Tribune|date=May 26, 2010|url=http://www.desototimes.com/articles/2010/05/26/news/local/doc4bfdb0f82cd99896383477.txt}}</ref> They have three children: Reed Nunnelee (Flowood, MS), Emily Nunnelee (Mississippi State) and Nathan Nunnelee (Mississippi State).

Nunnelee underwent brain surgery at [[University of Texas MD Anderson Cancer Center]] after the discovery of a mass on his brain in May of 2014. He was then brought to [[TIRR Memorial Hermann Hospital]] and later [[Johns Hopkins Hospital]] to undergo [[chemotherapy]] and [[Radiation therapy|radiation]] and receive therapy to restore his speech and mobility on the left side of his body.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.hattiesburgamerican.com/story/news/local/2014/07/01/nunnelee-chemo/11927805/ |title=Nunnelee to undergo chemotherapy |publisher=''[[Hattiesburg American]]'' |date=July 1, 2014 |accessdate=July 31, 2014}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.clarionledger.com/story/news/2014/07/30/rep-nunnelee-plans-return-dc-following-brain-surgery/13392785/ |title=Rep. '''Patrick Alan Nunnelee''' (born October 9, 1958) is an [[United States|American]] politician who has been the [[United States House of Representatives|U.S. Representative]] for {{ushr|MS|1}} since 2011. Previously he served in the [[Mississippi State Senate]], representing the 6th district, from 1995 to 2011. He is a member of the [[Republican Party (United States)|Republican Party]].

==Early life, education, and business career==
Nunnelee was born in [[Tupelo, Mississippi|Tupelo]], in [[Lee County, Mississippi|Lee County]], [[Mississippi]], on October 9, 1958.<ref name=votesmart>{{cite web|url=http://www.votesmart.org/bio.php?can_id=8326|title=VoteSmart.org|work=Project Vote Smart|accessdate=19 October 2014}}</ref> Nunnelee graduated from [[Clinton High School (Clinton, Mississippi)|Clinton High School]] in [[Clinton, Mississippi]] in 1976 and then attended [[Mississippi State University]], graduating with a bachelor’s degree in 1980. While a student at MSU he lost much of his eyesight to a degenerative eye disease. Although legally blind he continued his studies. His sight was restored after receiving [[Corneal transplantation|cornea transplants]].

Nunnelee was employed by American Funeral Assurance Co., eventually becoming vice president of sales and marketing. His father was also employed by the company, rising to president and CEO. In 1996, Nunnelee and his father founded Allied Funeral Associates, Inc. and Allied Funeral Associates Insurance Company and he has served as Vice-President and Director of both entities.<ref name=votesmart/>

Before beginning his political career he was a popular speaker, crediting God, organ donors, and organizations such as the [[Lions Clubs International|Lions Club]] for having his eyesight restored.

==Mississippi Senate==

===Elections===
In 1995, incumbent Republican State Senator [[Roger Wicker]] of the 6th district resigned in order to take up a seat in the [[U.S. House of Representatives]]. Nunnelee ran and won. In 1999, he won re-election to a second term unopposed.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=371288|title=Our Campaigns - MS State Senate 06 Race - Nov 02, 1999|publisher=|accessdate=19 October 2014}}</ref> In 2003, he won re-election to a third term with 69% of the vote.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=210764|title=Our Campaigns - MS State Senate 06 Race - Nov 04, 2003|publisher=|accessdate=19 October 2014}}</ref> In 2007, he won re-election to a fourth term with 66% of the vote.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=382152|title=Our Campaigns - MS State Senate 06 Race - Nov 06, 2007|publisher=|accessdate=19 October 2014}}</ref>
