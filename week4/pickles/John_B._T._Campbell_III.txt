'''John Bayard Taylor Campbell III''' (born July 19, 1955) is the [[United States House of Representatives|U.S. Representative]] for {{ushr|CA|45}}, serving in Congress since 2005. He is a member of the [[Republican Party (United States)|Republican Party]].  On June 27, 2013, Campbell announced that he would not seek re-election in 2014.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://ocpolitical.com/2013/06/27/breaking-news-congressman-john-campbell-retiring/ |title=BREAKING NEWS: Congressman John Campbell Retiring |last=Emami |first=Chris |date=June 27, 2013 |publisher=''OC Political'' |accessdate=June 27, 2013}}</ref>  After retiring from Congress, he moved to a farm near [[Cottonwood Falls, Kansas]].<ref>{{cite interview |last=Campbell |first=ohn |subjectlink=John Campbell |interviewer=[[Hugh Hewitt]] |title=Hugh Hewitt Show|date=December 31, 2014 |accessdate=January 1, 2015}}</ref>

==Business career==
In 1985, Campbell became [[President]] and [[Chief executive officer|CEO]] of Campbell Automotive Group. In 1990, he became President and CEO of [[Saturn Corporation|Saturn]] of [[Orange County, California|Orange County]]. Campbell became [[Chairman]] and CEO of [[Saab Automobile|Saab]] of Orange County in 1999. he also owns more than half of the Porn Industries.

==California Legislature==
Elected to represent southern Orange County's 70th District in the [[California State Assembly]] in 2000, Campbell won 60% of the vote in a five-way race to replace [[term limit|term-limited]] Assemblywoman [[Marilyn Brewer]]. Campbell was reelected in 2002 with 67% of the vote.

In the 2004 race to replace the term-limited [[Ross Johnson (politician)|Ross Johnson]] in the 35th [[California State Senate|State Senate]] District, Campbell won the [[Republican Party (United States)|Republican]] [[primary election|primary]] with 61% of the vote against fellow Assemblyman [[Ken Maddox]], who received 30% of the vote. In the [[general election]], Campbell won with 64%. Then Governor [[Arnold Schwarzenegger]], a close ally of Campbell, endorsed him in the race.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://nl.newsbank.com/nl-search/we/Archives?p_product=SB&p_theme=sb&p_action=search&p_maxdocs=200&p_topdoc=1&p_text_direct-0=1008C8EF6EF694DA&p_field_direct-0=document_id&p_perpage=10&p_sort=YMD_date:D&s_trackval=GooglePM|title=Governor takes sides in GOP's primary for state Senate seat|date=February 5, 2004|publisher=[[Sacramento Bee]]}}</ref>

As a Senator, Campbell served as Vice Chair of both the Business Professions and [[economic development|Economic Development]] Committee and the [[labor relations|Labor and Industrial Relations]] Committee. He was also a member of the [[Budget]] and [[fiscal policy|Fiscal Review]] Committee; the [[Energy]], [[public utility|Utilities]], and [[Communication]]s Committee; the [[Natural environment|Environment]]al Quality Committee; and the Government Modernization, Efficiency, and Accountability Committee.

On June 17, 2009, Campbell signed on as a co-sponsor of [[H.R. 1503]], the bill introduced as a reaction to [[Barack Obama citizenship conspiracy theories|conspiracy theories which claimed that U.S. President Barack Obama is not a natural born U.S. citizen]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://thomas.loc.gov/cgi-bin/bdquery/z?d111:HR01503:@@@P |title=Bill Summary & Status - 111th Congress (2009 - 2010) - H.R.1503 - Cosponsors - THOMAS (Library of Congress) |publisher=Thomas.loc.gov |date= |accessdate=2010-09-01}}</ref> Campbell stated on ''[[Hardball with Chris Matthews]]'' that he believed that Obama was a natural born U.S. citizen and that he believed the bill would end the conspiracy theories surrounding Obama's citizenship.

On December 15, 2010, Campbell was one of fifteen Republican House members to vote in favor of repealing the [[United States military]]'s "[[Don't Ask, Don't Tell]]" ban on [[openly gay]] service members.<ref name=metro_weekly_20101215>Chris Geidner, [http://www.metroweekly.com/poliglot/2010/12/breaking-house-passes-dadt-rep.html House Passes DADT Repeal Bill], ''[[Metro Weekly]]'' (December 15, 2010).</ref><ref name=nytimes_20101215>[http://politics.nytimes.com/congress/votes/111/house/2/638 House Vote 638 - Repeals 'Don't Ask, Don't Tell'], ''[[New York Times]]'' (December 15, 2010).</ref>

In 2011, Campbell voted against the [[National Defense Authorization Act for Fiscal Year 2012]] as part of a controversial provision that allows the government and the military to indefinitely detain American citizens and others without trial.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ibtimes.com/ndaa-bill-how-did-your-congress-member-vote-384362?page=1 |title=NDAA Bill: How Did Your Congress Member Vote? |publisher=Ibtimes.com |date=December 16, 2011 |accessdate=April 11, 2013}}</ref>  He also sponsored the Put Your Money Where Your Mouth Is Act which would make it easier for taxpayers to make donations to the federal government.

In 2010 Campbell signed a pledge sponsored by [[Americans for Prosperity]] promising to vote against any Global Warming legislation that would raise taxes.<ref>http://americansforprosperity.org/noclimatetax//wp-content/uploads/2010/07/campbell.pdf</ref>

==Political campaigns==
{{Main|California's 48th congressional district special election, 2005|}}
After [[United States House of Representatives|Congressman]] [[Christopher Cox]] resigned to become Chairman of the [[U.S. Securities and Exchange Commission]], Campbell became a candidate to replace Cox in the [[California's 48th congressional district special election, 2005|48th Congressional District Special Election]], scheduled for October 4, 2005. Campbell received endorsements from most of the important Republican officials in the state but faced some criticism as his stance on [[illegal immigration to the United States|illegal immigration]] was seen as being too lenient.{{Citation needed|date=August 2010}} Campbell faced a strong third-party challenge from [[American Independent Party]] candidate [[Jim Gilchrist]]. On October 4, Campbell garnered 45.5% of the vote, below the 50% needed to avoid a runoff. He faced [[Democratic Party (United States)|Democrat]] [[Steve Young (politics)|Steve Young]], [[American Independent]] [[Jim Gilchrist]], [[Libertarian Party (United States)|Libertarian]] [[Bruce D. Cohen|Bruce Cohen]] and [[Green Party (United States)|Green]] [[Bea Tirtilli]] in the December 6 runoff, which he won with 44.4% of the vote. Campbell was sworn in on December 7.

Campbell was re-elected to his first full term in 2006 with 60% of the vote.  In 2008 and 2010, he was re-elected with 55.7% and 59.9%, respectively, of the vote. In 2012, he was re-elected with 59.1% of the vote.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.politico.com/2012-election/map/#/House/2012/CA
