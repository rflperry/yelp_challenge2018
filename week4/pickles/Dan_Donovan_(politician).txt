'''Daniel Michael Donovan, Jr.''' (born November 6, 1956) is an attorney and elected official from [[New York City]]. He currently serves as the district attorney for [[Staten Island|Richmond County, New York]] ([[Staten Island]]).

==Early life==
Dan Donovan was born into a [[working-class]] [[Roman Catholic]] family in [[Staten Island]], [[New York]] in 1956. His [[Irish-American]] father, Daniel M. Donovan, Sr., was a [[longshoreman]], and his [[Polish-American]] mother, Katherine Bolewicz Donovan, was a [[garment worker]].<ref>Gray, Geoffrey (09-19-2010) http://nymag.com/news/intelligencer/encounter/68321/. ''New York Magazine''. Retrieved April 1, 2011.</ref><ref>Kern-Jedrychowska, Ewa (December 10, 2010) http://news.feetintwoworlds.org/2010/10/12/half-polish-catholic-ag-candidate-wants-ny-polish-community-to-know-his-background/. ''Feet in 2 Worlds''. Retrieved April 1, 2011.</ref> He was raised in the [[Tompkinsville, Staten Island|Tompkinsville]] section of the borough.<ref name="dandonovan1">[http://www.dandonovan.org/meet_dan.php''. Retrieved 2010-06-03.]{{dead link|date=December 2014}}</ref> He attended [[Monsignor Farrell High School]], an all-boys Catholic school, graduating in 1974.<ref>[http://www.monsignorfarrellalumni.com/article.asp?articleID=239. Retrieved 2010-06-03.]</ref> He went to study Criminal Justice at [[St. John's University (New York)|St. John's University]].<ref name="dandonovan1"/> After graduating from there he attended [[Fordham University School of Law]], earning his [[juris doctor]] in 1988.<ref name="dandonovan1"/>

==Early career==
In 1989 Dan Donovan became an Assistant District Attorney in the office of [[Robert M. Morgenthau]].<ref>[http://rcda.nyc.gov/meet.html. Retrieved 2010-06-03.]</ref> He served in with the office of the [[New York County District Attorney]] under Mr. Morgenthau until 1996. Later that year Donovan became Chief of Staff to then Staten Island [[Borough President]] [[Guy V. Molinari]].<ref name="autogenerated1">Farinacci, Amanda (05-13-2010) http://brooklyn.ny1.com/content/news_beats/politics/118626/da-donovan-set-to-announce-ag-run/?ap=1&MP4. Retrieved June 3, 2010.</ref> He remained in that position until 2002 when he was sworn in as Deputy Borough President of Staten Island; he had been appointed by his immediate predecessor and the then new Borough President [[James Molinaro]].<ref name="autogenerated1"/>

==Richmond County District Attorney==
In 2003, twenty-year incumbent Democrat, [[William L. Murphy]] decided not to seek re-election. Dan Donovan announced his intention to run to succeed him. In the election he faced Chief Assistant District Attorney David Lehr and won with over 53% of the vote.<ref>[http://www.vote.nyc.ny.us/results.html. Retrieved 2010-06-03.]</ref> One of the key parts of his platform was to start the county's first witness protection program, and his office led the city's prosecutors with the highest felony conviction rate in many of the years since he took office.<ref>Wrobleski, Tom (05-30-2010). http://www.silive.com/news/index.ssf/2010/05/swimming_upstream_in_bid_for_s.html. ''Staten Island Advance''. Retrieved June 4, 2010.</ref> He was re-elected in 2007 with over 68% of the vote, defeating local Democratic attorney Michael Ryan.<ref>Lomardi, Frank (July 11, 2007). http://www.nydailynews.com/news/2007/11/07/2007-11-07_staten_island_da_daniel_donovan_survives.html. ''New York Daily News''. Retrieved June 4, 2010</ref> This was in spite of a last minute endorsement for his rival by longtime friend and mentor [[Staten Island]] [[Borough President]] [[James Molinaro]]; Molinaro was angered that Donovan had referred his grandson's case to a special prosecutor.<ref>Hicks, Jonathan P. (March 11, 2007). http://www.nytimes.com/2007/11/03/nyregion/03staten.html?ref=james_p_molinaro. ''New York Times''. Retrieved July 13, 2010.</ref> Donovan's tenure as DA has seen several high-profile cases, including the second conviction of Andre Rand, long suspected in a string of kidnappings on Staten Island.<ref>Grace, Melissa and Bill Hutchinson (10-20-2004). http://www.nydailynews.com/archives/news/2004/10/20/2004-10-20_jury__pedophile_took_holly.html. ''New York Daily News''. Retrieved July 13, 2010.</ref> In 2010, famed rapper [[Method Man]] pled guilty to attempted tax evasion and was forced to pay about $106,000 in restitution and penalties.<ref>Katz, Celeste (06-29-2010). http://www.nydailynews.com/blogs/dailypolitics/2010/06/dan-donovan-versus-method-man.html. ''New York Daily News''. Retrieved July 13, 2010.</ref> Donovan became the focus of a national controversy surrounding the death of [[Eric Garner]] in 2014.

==2010 New York Attorney General Campaign==
On May 17, 2010, Donovan, a registered Republican, announced his candidacy for the [[New York attorney general election, 2010]], automatically becoming the front runner for his party's nomination.<ref>Wrobleski, Tom (May 17, 2010). [http://www.silive.com/news/index.ssf/2010/05/staten_island_da_daniel_donova.html Staten Island D.A. Daniel Donovan announces A.G. run, vows to fight corruption in Albany]. ''Staten Island Advance''. Retrieved 2010-05-22.</ref> In spite of this [[Bob Antonacci]], [[Onondaga County]] [[Comptroller]], announced his intention run for Attorney General.<ref>Mariani, Tom (May 17, 2010). [http://www.syracuse.com/news/index.ssf/2010/05/staten_island_da_to_declare_at.html Staten Island DA Daniel Donovan to declare attorney general candidacy in Syracuse]. ''The Post-Standard''. Retrieved 2010-07-13.</ref> Antonacci stepped aside and endorsed Donovan after earning forty percent of the vote at the 2010 Republican State Convention.<ref>Knauss, Tim (June 2, 2010). [http://www.syracuse.com/news/index.ssf/2010/06/onondaga_comptroller_robert_an.html Staten Island DA Daniel Donovan to declare attorney general candidacy in Syracuse]. ''The Post-Standard''. Retrieved 2010-07-13.</ref> With a sixty percent win of the delegates at the convention and no primary opponent, Donovan became the presumptive nominee of the [[Republican Party (United States)|Republican Party]].<ref>[http://www.nygop.org/page/new-york-state-republicans-nominate-dan-donovan-as-their-candidate-for-attorney-general New York State Republicans Nominate Dan Donovan As Their Candidate for Attorney General]{{dead link|date=December 2014}}. Retrieved July 13, 2010.</ref> A week prior he received the endorsement of the [[Conservative Party of New York]].<ref>Hornak, Robert (May 31, 2010). [http://www.urbanelephants.com/index.php/component/content/article/64/2688-state-conservative-party-meets-endorse-jay-townend-joe-dioguardi-for-senate-dan-donovan-for-attorney-general-harry-wilson-for-comptroller-and-surprise-rick-lazio-for-governor.html State Conservative Party Meets: Endorse Townsend, DioGuardi, Donovan, Wilson and (Surprise!) Rick Lazio]. Retrieved 2010-07-13.</ref> He was defeated on November 2 by the Democratic nominee [[Eric Schneiderman]].
