'''Stephen Knight''' (born December 17, 1966<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.facebook.com/pages/Steve-Knight-for-Senate/283938464089?sk=info|title=Steve Knight Facebook page}}</ref>) is an [[United States|American]] politician. He represents the [[California's 21st Senate district|21st]] district in the [[California State Senate]], encompassing northeastern [[Los Angeles County, California|Los Angeles]] and western [[San Bernardino County, California|San Bernardino]] counties in the [[High Desert (California)|High Desert]] region. He is also congressman-elect for California's 25th Congressional District.  He supported Proposition 8<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.montereyherald.com/ci_11729329|title=Panel backs Prop. 8 challenge|work=montereyherald.com}}</ref> that was ruled unconstitutional by SCOTUS on June 26, 2013.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://abcnews.go.com/Politics/supreme-court-rules-gay-marriage-cases/story?id=19492896|title=The Supreme Court Rules on DOMA and Proposition 8 Cases - ABC News|work=ABC News}}</ref> Knight opposed bi-lingual education in a mostly latino populated city<ref>{{cite web|url=http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Palmdale,_California#Demographics|title=Palmdale, California|publisher=}}</ref> and courted further controversy in voting against the images of the Confederate flag being removed from state property.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.latimes.com/local/political/la-me-pc-steve-knight-flag-20140827-story.html|title=Confederate flag vote puts Knight on spot in congressional race|date=27 August 2014|work=latimes.com}}</ref> Knight has consistently voted against an increase of minimum wage, gun control with semi-automatic weapons and water regulations for the city.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://votesmart.org/candidate/key-votes/71098/steve-knight#.VDc2W_fn_mQ|title=Project Vote Smart - The Voter's Self Defense System|work=Project Vote Smart}}</ref>
He currently lives in [[Palmdale, California|Palmdale]] with his wife Lily and two children.

He also served in the California State Assembly from 2008-2012.

His father, [[William J. Knight|William J. "Pete" Knight]], represented geographically similar Senate and Assembly districts from 1992 to 2004.
