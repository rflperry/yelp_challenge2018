'''Robert Pittenger''' (born August 15, 1948) is an [[United States|American]] [[politician]] who has been the [[United States House of Representatives|U.S. Representative]] for [[North Carolina's 9th congressional district]] since 2013.  The district includes several outer portions of [[Charlotte]], as well as many of that city's northern and eastern suburbs. He is a member of the [[Republican Party (United States)|Republican Party]].

==Early life, education, and business career==
Pittenger was born in Texas and attended the [[University of Texas]]. After graduating he worked for [[Campus Crusade for Christ]] before moving to Charlotte in 1985 and becoming a real estate investor. 

==North Carolina Senate (2003-2007)==
===Elections===
After redistricting, Pittenger decided to run for the 40th senate district of the [[North Carolina General Assembly]] in 2002.<ref name=official_bio/> He defeated Democratic State Senator [[Fountain Odom]] 55%-43%.<ref>http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=24125</ref> In 2004, he decided to run for the 39th senate district and defeated Libertarian nominee Andy Grum 89%-11%.<ref>http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=84971</ref> In 2006, he won re-election to a third term unopposed.<ref>http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=287550</ref>

===Tenure===
He represented the state's 39th [[North Carolina Senate|Senate]] district, which included portions of southeastern [[Mecklenburg County, North Carolina|Mecklenburg County]]. Robert was a lead sponsor of [[Right-to-life movement|Right To Life]] legislation and the [[North Carolina Amendment 1|North Carolina marriage amendment]].<ref name=aboutpage>{{cite web |title = About Robert Pittenger | work = robertpittenger.com (campaign site) | url = http://robertpittenger.com/about | accessdate = 2013-11-05 }}</ref>

In May 2004, he proposed big tax cuts like bringing the state's corporate tax from 6.9% to 4.9% and the income tax rate for the state's top earners from 8.25% to 7.5%.<ref>{{cite web  | title = House Supports Business Incentives, Legislators Give Preliminary OK to $20 Million for N.C. Fund | work = Charlotte Observer |page=B1 Metro | publisher =  | date = May 21, 2004 | url = http://nl.newsbank.com/nl-search/we/Archives?p_product=CO&s_site=charlotte&p_multi=CO&p_theme=realcities&p_action=search&p_maxdocs=200&p_topdoc=1&p_text_direct-0=102BBE554D6FCB0B&p_field_direct-0=document_id&p_perpage=10&p_sort=YMD_date:D&s_trackval=GooglePM | accessdate = 2013-11-05 }}</ref> He proposed over $1.5 billion in spending cuts, including the elimination of waste, fraud, and abuse in [[Medicaid]].<ref name=aboutpage />

In February 2005, he proposed a [[medical malpractice]] bill that would cap non-economic damages at $250,000 for physicians, hospitals, and long-term care facilities.<ref>{{cite web | title = Medical malpractice bill introduced in General Assembly | work = Triad Business Journal | date = Feb 2, 2005 | url = http://www.bizjournals.com/triad/stories/2005/01/31/daily37.html?jst=b_ln_hl | accessdate = 2013-11-05 }}</ref>

In 2006, Pittenger sent a book called "[[The Skeptical Environmentalist]]," to his senate colleagues. He [[Global warming denial|denied]] [[Global warming controversy|global warming]].<ref>{{cite web | title = On Climate, Pittenger Turns Contrarian, State Senator Disputes Global Warming Claims | work = Charlotte Observer | date = May 14, 2006 |page=B1 Metro | url = http://nl.newsbank.com/nl-search/we/Archives?p_product=CO&s_site=charlotte&p_multi=CO&p_theme=realcities&p_action=search&p_maxdocs=200&p_topdoc=1&p_text_direct-0=111A0A0E5A8083F0&p_field_direct-0=document_id&p_perpage=10&p_sort=YMD_date:D&s_trackval=GooglePM | accessdate = 2013-11-05 }}</ref><ref>{{cite web | title = We Aren't Changing Climate, Let's Avoid Snap Judgements and Wishful Thinking on Warming Trend | work = Charlotte Observer  |page= 24A | publisher =  | date = February 5, 2006 | url = http://nl.newsbank.com/nl-search/we/Archives?p_product=CO&s_site=charlotte&p_multi=CO&p_theme=realcities&p_action=search&p_maxdocs=200&p_topdoc=1&p_text_direct-0=10F9C0D578F705E8&p_field_direct-0=document_id&p_perpage=10&p_sort=YMD_date:D&s_trackval=GooglePM | accessdate = 2013-11-05 }}</ref><ref>{{cite web | title = Green Moves Could Create New Jobs | work = digtriad.com | publisher = [[WFMY-TV]] | date = Oct 24, 2007 | url = http://www.digtriad.com/news/local/story.aspx?storyid=91707 | accessdate = 2013-11-05 }}</ref>

==2008 gubernatorial election==
{{Seealso|North Carolina lieutenant governor election, 2008|North Carolina gubernatorial election, 2008}}
Pittenger won the Republican primary on May 6, 2008 to become his party's nominee for [[Lieutenant Governor of North Carolina]] with 59% of the vote, defeating three other candidates.<ref>http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=415029</ref><ref>{{cite web | title = Charlotte candidate announces run | work = Under The Dome (blog) | publisher = Charlotte News & Observer | date = January 10, 2008 | url = http://projects.newsobserver.com/under_the_dome/charlotte_candidate_announces_run | accessdate = 2013-11-05 }}</ref><ref>{{cite web | title = And the winners are... | work = Under The Dome (blog) | publisher = Charlotte News & Observer | date = May 7, 2008 | url = http://projects.newsobserver.com/under_the_dome/and_the_winners_are | accessdate = 2013-11-05 }}</ref> On May 27, 2008, he resigned from the Senate to focus on his campaign.<ref>{{cite web | title = Pittenger resigns from Senate |  work = Under The Dome (blog) | publisher = Charlotte News & Observer | date = May 27, 2008 | url = http://projects.newsobserver.com/under_the_dome/pittenger_resigns_from_senate | accessdate = 2013-11-05 }}  </ref> He lost the general election to fellow State Senator [[Walter H. Dalton]] 51%-46%.<ref>http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=426463</ref>

==U.S. House of Representatives (2013-Present)==
===2012 election===
{{Main|United States House of Representatives elections in North Carolina, 2012#District 9}}
After [[Sue Myrick]] announced her retirement as the [[United States House of Representatives|Representative]] of [[North Carolina's 9th congressional district]] in early 2012, Pittenger announced that he would run to replace her.<ref>[http://projects.newsobserver.com/under_the_dome/robert_pittenger_preparing_to_enter_congressional_race News & Observer: Robert Pittenger preparing to enter congressional race]</ref> He failed to win the primary outright on May 8, 2012, but ranked first with 32% of the vote in the eleven-candidate field.<ref>http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=751734</ref> In the primary run-off election held on July 17, 2012, he defeated former [[Mecklenburg County Sheriff's Office|Mecklenburg County Sheriff]] Jim Pendergraph 53%-47%.<ref>http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=771271</ref><ref>{{cite journal | last1 = Morrill | first1 = Jim | last2 = Funk | first2 = Tim | date = 2012-07-18 | title = Pittenger wins bitter race in 9th, will face Roberts | journal = Charlotte Observer | pages = Page 1 | location = Charlotte, North Carolina | publisher = The McClatchy Company | accessdate = 2012-07-18 | url = http://www.charlotteobserver.com/2012/07/17/3388767/pittenger-wins-will-face-roberts.html}}</ref>

Pittenger won the general election on November 6, defeating Democratic Mecklenburg County Commission Chairwoman Jennifer Roberts 52%-47%.<ref>http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=740216</ref><ref>[http://results.enr.clarityelections.com/NC/42923/114047/Web01/en/summary.html NC State Board of Elections]</ref>  While Pittenger lost the district's share of Mecklenburg County (47%), he ran up huge margins in the [[Union County, North Carolina|Union]] (63%) and [[Iredell County|Iredell]] (64%) portions of the district. Still, it was the closest that a Republican had come to losing this district since 1986. He took office in January 2013.

===Tenure===
On December 2, 2013, Pittenger introduced the [[Kilah Davenport Child Protection Act of 2013]], which became {{USPL|113|104}}.<ref name=3627allactions>{{cite web|title=H.R. 3627 - All Actions|url=http://beta.congress.gov/bill/113th-congress/house-bill/3627/actions|publisher=United States Congress|accessdate=23 May 2014}}</ref> The law broadens the coverage of current laws that address domestic assaults by certain repeat offenders.<ref name=cbo3627>{{cite web|title=CBO - H.R. 3627|url=http://cbo.gov/publication/44948|publisher=Congressional Budget Office|accessdate=23 May 2014}}</ref> The law also requires the [[United States Department of Justice]] to write a report on [[child abuse]] prevention laws in all U.S. states and territories, "with a particular focus on penalties for cases of severe child abuse."<ref name=PRpittenger1>{{cite web|title=Kilah's Law|url=http://pittenger.house.gov/KilahsLaw|publisher=House Office of Congressman Pittenger|accessdate=23 May 2014}}</ref> Pittenger said that the bill "will strengthen laws and help prevent child abuse," noting that "it is sickening to realize that we need such laws."<ref name="PRpittenger1"/> After the bill's passage, Pittenger called it "a victory on behalf of children," but said that "no happy, bright, little girl should ever become the face of child abuse legislation."<ref name=WBTVmay20>{{cite news|title=President signs Kilah Davenport Child Protection Act into law|url=http://www.wbtv.com/story/25567793/president-signs-kilah-davenport-child-protection-act-into-law|accessdate=23 May 2014|newspaper=WBTV|date=20 May 2014}}</ref>

**[[United States House Financial Services Subcommittee on Financial Institutions and Consumer Credit|Subcommittee on Financial Institutions and Consumer Credit]]

==Personal life==
Pittenger lives in south Charlotte with his wife, Suzanne.  A former longtime member of Central [[Church of God (Cleveland, Tennessee)|Church of God]], he now attends another large evangelical church in Charlotte, Forest Hill Church.

Pittenger's father-in-law was [[Cy Bahakel]], a former state senator and longtime owner of [[WCCB]] in Charlotte.

'''Robert Pittenger''' (born August 15, 1948) is an [[United States|American]] [[politician]] who has been the [[United States House of Representatives|U.S. Representative]] for [[North Carolina's 9th congressional district]] since 2013.  The district includes several outer portions of [[Charlotte]], as well as many of that city's northern and eastern suburbs. He is a member of the [[Republican Party (United States)|Republican Party]].

==Early life, education, and business career==
Pittenger was born in Texas and attended the [[University of Texas]]. After graduating he worked for [[Campus Crusade for Christ]] before moving to Charlotte in 1985 and becoming a real estate investor. 

==North Carolina Senate (2003-2007)==
===Elections===
After redistricting, Pittenger decided to run for the 40th senate district of the [[North Carolina General Assembly]] in 2002.<ref name=official_bio/> He defeated Democratic State Senator [[Fountain Odom]] 55%-43%.<ref>http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=24125</ref> In 2004, he decided to run for the 39th senate district and defeated Libertarian nominee Andy Grum 89%-11%.<ref>http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=84971</ref> In 2006, he won re-election to a third term unopposed.<ref>http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=287550</ref>

===Tenure===
He represented the state's 39th [[North Carolina Senate|Senate]] district, which included portions of southeastern [[Mecklenburg County, North Carolina|Mecklenburg County]]. Robert was a lead sponsor of [[Right-to-life movement|Right To Life]] legislation and the [[North Carolina Amendment 1|North Carolina marriage amendment]].<ref name=aboutpage>{{cite web |title = About Robert Pittenger | work = robertpittenger.com (campaign site) | url = http://robertpittenger.com/about | accessdate = 2013-11-05 }}</ref>

In May 2004, he proposed big tax cuts like bringing the state's corporate tax from 6.9% to 4.9% and the income tax rate for the state's top earners from 8.25% to 7.5%.<ref>{{cite web  | title = House Supports Business Incentives, Legislators Give Preliminary OK to $20 Million for N.C. Fund | work = Charlotte Observer |page=B1 Metro | publisher =  | date = May 21, 2004 | url = http://nl.newsbank.com/nl-search/we/Archives?p_product=CO&s_site=charlotte&p_multi=CO&p_theme=realcities&p_action=search&p_maxdocs=200&p_topdoc=1&p_text_direct-0=102BBE554D6FCB0B&p_field_direct-0=document_id&p_perpage=10&p_sort=YMD_date:D&s_trackval=GooglePM | accessdate = 2013-11-05 }}</ref> He proposed over $1.5 billion in spending cuts, including the elimination of waste, fraud, and abuse in [[Medicaid]].<ref name=aboutpage />

In February 2005, he proposed a [[medical malpractice]] bill that would cap non-economic damages at $250,000 for physicians, hospitals, and long-term care facilities.<ref>{{cite web | title = Medical malpractice bill introduced in General Assembly | work = Triad Business Journal | date = Feb 2, 2005 | url = http://www.bizjournals.com/triad/stories/2005/01/31/daily37.html?jst=b_ln_hl | accessdate = 2013-11-05 }}</ref>

In 2006, Pittenger sent a book called "[[The Skeptical Environmentalist]]," to his senate colleagues. He [[Global warming denial|denied]] [[Global warming controversy|global warming]].<ref>{{cite web | title = On Climate, Pittenger Turns Contrarian, State Senator Disputes Global Warming Claims | work = Charlotte Observer | date = May 14, 2006 |page=B1 Metro | url = http://nl.newsbank.com/nl-search/we/Archives?p_product=CO&s_site=charlotte&p_multi=CO&p_theme=realcities&p_action=search&p_maxdocs=200&p_topdoc=1&p_text_direct-0=111A0A0E5A8083F0&p_field_direct-0=document_id&p_perpage=10&p_sort=YMD_date:D&s_trackval=GooglePM | accessdate = 2013-11-05 }}</ref><ref>{{cite web | title = We Aren't Changing Climate, Let's Avoid Snap Judgements and Wishful Thinking on Warming Trend | work = Charlotte Observer  |page= 24A | publisher =  | date = February 5, 2006 | url = http://nl.newsbank.com/nl-search/we/Archives?p_product=CO&s_site=charlotte&p_multi=CO&p_theme=realcities&p_action=search&p_maxdocs=200&p_topdoc=1&p_text_direct-0=10F9C0D578F705E8&p_field_direct-0=document_id&p_perpage=10&p_sort=YMD_date:D&s_trackval=GooglePM | accessdate = 2013-11-05 }}</ref><ref>{{cite web | title = Green Moves Could Create New Jobs | work = digtriad.com | publisher = [[WFMY-TV]] | date = Oct 24, 2007 | url = http://www.digtriad.com/news/local/story.aspx?storyid=91707 | accessdate = 2013-11-05 }}</ref>

==2008 gubernatorial election==
{{Seealso|North Carolina lieutenant governor election, 2008|North Carolina gubernatorial election, 2008}}
Pittenger won the Republican primary on May 6, 2008 to become his party's nominee for [[Lieutenant Governor of North Carolina]] with 59% of the vote, defeating three other candidates.<ref>http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=415029</ref><ref>{{cite web | title = Charlotte candidate announces run | work = Under The Dome (blog) | publisher = Charlotte News & Observer | date = January 10, 2008 | url = http://projects.newsobserver.com/under_the_dome/charlotte_candidate_announces_run | accessdate = 2013-11-05 }}</ref><ref>{{cite web | title = And the winners are... | work = Under The Dome (blog) | publisher = Charlotte News & Observer | date = May 7, 2008 | url = http://projects.newsobserver.com/under_the_dome/and_the_winners_are | accessdate = 2013-11-05 }}</ref> On May 27, 2008, he resigned from the Senate to focus on his campaign.<ref>{{cite web | title = Pittenger resigns from Senate |  work = Under The Dome (blog) | publisher = Charlotte News & Observer | date = May 27, 2008 | url = http://projects.newsobserver.com/under_the_dome/pittenger_resigns_from_senate | accessdate = 2013-11-05 }}  </ref> He lost the general election to fellow State Senator [[Walter H. Dalton]] 51%-46%.<ref>http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=426463</ref>

==U.S. House of Representatives (2013-Present)==
===2012 election===
{{Main|United States House of Representatives elections in North Carolina, 2012#District 9}}
After [[Sue Myrick]] announced her retirement as the [[United States House of Representatives|Representative]] of [[North Carolina's 9th congressional district]] in early 2012, Pittenger announced that he would run to replace her.<ref>[http://projects.newsobserver.com/under_the_dome/robert_pittenger_preparing_to_enter_congressional_race News & Observer: Robert Pittenger preparing to enter congressional race]</ref> He failed to win the primary outright on May 8, 2012, but ranked first with 32% of the vote in the eleven-candidate field.<ref>http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=751734</ref> In the primary run-off election held on July 17, 2012, he defeated former [[Mecklenburg County Sheriff's Office|Mecklenburg County Sheriff]] Jim Pendergraph 53%-47%.<ref>http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=771271</ref><ref>{{cite journal | last1 = Morrill | first1 = Jim | last2 = Funk | first2 = Tim | date = 2012-07-18 | title = Pittenger wins bitter race in 9th, will face Roberts | journal = Charlotte Observer | pages = Page 1 | location = Charlotte, North Carolina | publisher = The McClatchy Company | accessdate = 2012-07-18 | url = http://www.charlotteobserver.com/2012/07/17/3388767/pittenger-wins-will-face-roberts.html}}</ref>

Pittenger won the general election on November 6, defeating Democratic Mecklenburg County Commission Chairwoman Jennifer Roberts 52%-47%.<ref>http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=740216</ref><ref>[http://results.enr.clarityelections.com/NC/42923/114047/Web01/en/summary.html NC State Board of Elections]</ref>  While Pittenger lost the district's share of Mecklenburg County (47%), he ran up huge margins in the [[Union County, North Carolina|Union]] (63%) and [[Iredell County|Iredell]] (64%) portions of the district. Still, it was the closest that a Republican had come to losing this district since 1986. He took office in January 2013.

===Tenure===
On December 2, 2013, Pittenger introduced the [[Kilah Davenport Child Protection Act of 2013]], which became {{USPL|113|104}}.<ref name=3627allactions>{{cite web|title=H.R. 3627 - All Actions|url=http://beta.congress.gov/bill/113th-congress/house-bill/3627/actions|publisher=United States Congress|accessdate=23 May 2014}}</ref> The law broadens the coverage of current laws that address domestic assaults by certain repeat offenders.<ref name=cbo3627>{{cite web|title=CBO - H.R. 3627|url=http://cbo.gov/publication/44948|publisher=Congressional Budget Office|accessdate=23 May 2014}}</ref> The law also requires the [[United States Department of Justice]] to write a report on [[child abuse]] prevention laws in all U.S. states and territories, "with a particular focus on penalties for cases of severe child abuse."<ref name=PRpittenger1>{{cite web|title=Kilah's Law|url=http://pittenger.house.gov/KilahsLaw|publisher=House Office of Congressman Pittenger|accessdate=23 May 2014}}</ref> Pittenger said that the bill "will strengthen laws and help prevent child abuse," noting that "it is sickening to realize that we need such laws."<ref name="PRpittenger1"/> After the bill's passage, Pittenger called it "a victory on behalf of children," but said that "no happy, bright, little girl should ever become the face of child abuse legislation."<ref name=WBTVmay20>{{cite news|title=President signs Kilah Davenport Child Protection Act into law|url=http://www.wbtv.com/story/25567793/president-signs-kilah-davenport-child-protection-act-into-law|accessdate=23 May 2014|newspaper=WBTV|date=20 May 2014}}</ref>

==Personal life==
Pittenger lives in south Charlotte with his wife, Suzanne.  A former longtime member of Central [[Church of God (Cleveland, Tennessee)|Church of God]], he now attends another large evangelical church in Charlotte, Forest Hill Church.

Pittenger's father-in-law was [[Cy Bahakel]], a former state senator and longtime owner of [[WCCB]] in Charlotte.
