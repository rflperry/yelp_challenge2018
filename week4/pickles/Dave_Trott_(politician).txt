'''David A. "Dave" Trott''' (born October 16, 1960) is an American lawyer from [[Birmingham, Michigan]]. On September 4, 2013, Trott announced his candidacy for [[Michigan's 11th congressional district]]. He is a Republican.

==Early life and education==
Trott was born in 1960.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://info.cqrollcall.com/rs/cqrc/images/CQNews_NewMemberGuide2014.pdf|title=Guide to the New Congress|publisher=[[Roll Call]]|accessdate=December 6, 2014}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.nationaljournal.com/article/533669|title=Michigan-11: Dave Trott (R)|work=www.nationaljournal.com}}</ref> He graduated from [[Cranbrook Schools|Cranbrook]] in 1978, and obtained a bachelorâ€™s degree from the [[University of Michigan]] in 1981. He graduated from the [[Duke University School of Law]] in 1985, receiving his [[Juris Doctor]].<ref name="Crain's">{{cite news|last=Halcom|first=Chad|title=Farmington Hills exec David Trott declares candidacy for Bentivolio's U.S. House seat|url=http://www.crainsdetroit.com/article/20130904/NEWS/130909947/farmington-hills-exec-david-trott-declares-candidacy-for-bentivolios|accessdate=September 11, 2013|newspaper=Crain's Business Detroit|date=September 4, 2013}}</ref>

==Career==
Trott is the chairman and CEO of Trott & Trott PC, which represents banks and lenders in homeowner foreclosure and bankruptcy litigation. He is the owner of Attorneys Title Agency LLC and its subsidiaries. Trott is also the co-owner of Dietz Trott Sports & Entertainment,<ref name="Crain's"/> and owns Trott Recovery Services.<ref name=Pres>{{cite news|last=Korte|first=Gregory|title=Romney super PAC supporter has ties to Freddie and Fannie|url=http://usatoday30.usatoday.com/news/politics/story/2012-02-02/mitt-romney-super-pac-trott-and-trott-freddie-mac-fannie-mae/52939706/1|accessdate=September 11, 2013|newspaper=USA Today|date=February 3, 2012}}</ref> Trott has also served as chairman and CEO of NDeX, and has been affiliated with Detroit Legal News Publishing, the U.S. Foreclosure Network, and [[First American Financial Corporation]]. Trott's legal career and impact on the community was profiled on the front page of the ''[[Detroit Free Press]]'' on May 18, 2014.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.freep.com/article/20140518/NEWS06/305180087/david-trott-foreclosure-mortgage-housing-real-estate-michigan|title=U.S. House candidate David Trott made millions in mortgage crisis|work=Detroit Free Press}}</ref>

Trott has served on the boards of the [[University of Michigan]], On My Own, the Detroit Country Day School, The Community House, and the Karmanos Cancer Institute.<ref name=Forbes>{{cite web|title=David Trott|url=http://www.forbes.com/profile/david-trott/|publisher=Forbes|accessdate=September 11, 2013}}</ref> He is additionally a partner of 23 energy companies.<ref>http://clerk.house.gov/public_disc/financial-pdfs/2013/9103183.pdf</ref>

Trott chairs the [[Oakland County, Michigan|Oakland County]] Lincoln Day Dinner, an important GOP fundraising event.<ref name=Oakland>{{cite news|last=AlHajal|first=Khalil|title=U.S. Rep. Kerry Bentivolio faces primary challenge from Oakland County Republican David Trott|url=http://www.mlive.com/news/detroit/index.ssf/2013/09/us_rep_kerry_bentivolio_faces.html|accessdate=September 11, 2013|newspaper=MLive|date=September 4, 2013}}</ref> Trott was a major donor to the [[Republican National Committee]] and the presidential campaigns of [[John McCain]] and [[Mitt Romney]].<ref name="Pres"/>

==2014 United States House of Representatives Campaign==
On September 4, 2013, Trott announced his candidacy for [[Michigan's 11th congressional district]], challenging incumbent freshman Congressman [[Kerry Bentivolio]].<ref name="Crain's"/> Former Michigan State Senate Majority Leader Mike Bishop, Wayne County Commissioner Laura Cox, former Congressional candidate Rocky Raczkowski, and Republican Committeewoman Ronna Romney McDaniel were announced as his campaign co-chairs. Both the Michigan Chamber of Commerce and the [[United States Chamber of Commerce]]<ref>{{cite web|url=http://atr.rollcall.com/u-s-chamber-endorses-kerry-bentivolios-gop-challenger/|title=U.S. Chamber Endorses Kerry Bentivolio's GOP Challenger|work=At the Races}}</ref> have endorsed Trott's campaign, joining more than 350 other individuals and elected officials.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://trottforcongress.com/endorse-dave-trott/endorsements/|title=List of Endorsements|work=Dave Trott for Congress}}</ref>

On May 27 MIRS - Michigan Information and Research Service published the results of an initial Republican primary poll for the 11th Congressional District which showed incumbent Kerry Bentivolio at 33% to Trott's 21%, with a large portion of the district undecided.<ref>http://www.mirsnews.com/alert.php?alert_id=1782 {{subscription required}}</ref>

Trott enjoyed a significant advantage in resources over his opponent throughout the campaign, with more than $1 million in the bank following the first quarter of 2014, compared to Bentivolio's $130,000.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.freep.com/article/20140416/NEWS15/304160194/Campaign-financial-reports-show-lots-activity-tough-race-11th-district|title=Bentivolio reports $130,000 in campaign war chest for Congress to Trott's $1M|work=Detroit Free Press}}</ref> According to Federal Election Campaign filings Trott had personally contributed over $2,400,000 to his own primary run and outspent the incumbent approximately 20:1.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.fec.gov/fecviewer/CandidateCommitteeDetail.do|title=Committee/Candidate Search|publisher=}}</ref><ref>http://www.detroitnews.com/article/20140915/POLITICS/309150086/Bentivolio-seriously-considering-write-campaign</ref> The congressional primary election was held on August 5, 2014 in which he defeated Bentivolio.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.freep.com/article/20140805/NEWS06/308050217/Congress-11th-Bentivolio-Trott|title=Michigan news - Detroit Free Press - freep.com|work=Detroit Free Press}}</ref> However, Bentivolio has initiated a write-in campaign for the November, 2014 election to run against Trott and Democrat Bobby McKenzie and continue serving the people of Michigan in Congress, however that effort failed.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.detroitnews.com/story/news/politics/2014/10/02/bentivolio-write/16605253/|title=Bentivolio plans write-in campaign for Congress|author=Chad Livengood and Steve Pardo, The Detroit News|date=October 2, 2014|publisher=}}</ref>

==Political views==
A staunch [[fiscal conservatism|fiscal]] and [[social conservatism in the United States|social conservative]], Trott is [[pro-life]][http://trottforcongress.com/issues/], supports [[school choice]] programs [http://trottforcongress.com/issues/], supports [[gun rights]][http://trottforcongress.com/issues/], and is opposed to [[same-sex marriage]], but believes that the issue should be left to the states to decide [http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=dwzLZM1Fpa0].
