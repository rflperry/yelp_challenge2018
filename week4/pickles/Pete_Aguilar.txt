'''Peter R. "Pete" Aguilar''' (born June 19, 1979) serves as the Mayor of [[Redlands, California]] and is also the President of the Inland Empire Division of the [[League of California Cities]].<ref name="About the Inland Empire Division of the League of California Cities">{{cite web|last=Unknown|title=About the Inland Empire Division of the League of California Cities|url=http://www.cacities.org/Member-Engagement/Regional-Divisions/Inland-Empire|publisher=League of California Cities|accessdate=8 May 2012}}</ref>  Aguilar has served on the Redlands City Council since 2006.

==Personal==
Aguilar was born in [[Fontana, California]] and grew up in nearby [[San Bernardino, California]]. Aguilar attended grade school in [[Yucaipa, California]], and attended the [[University of Redlands]], where he graduated with degrees in both Government and Business Administration.<ref name="Mayor Aguilar Biography - City of Redlands">{{cite web|title=City Elected Officials Information Page|url=http://www.ci.redlands.ca.us/clerk/aguilar.htm|publisher=City of Redlands|accessdate=May 8, 2012}}</ref> One of Aguilar's first jobs was working at the San Bernardino County Courthouse Cafeteria where his blind grandfather was the operator. He has lived in [[Redlands, California]] since 1997, with his wife Alisha. Together they have two sons.<ref name=Aguilar>{{cite web|title=Pete Aguilar (D-CA-31)|url=http://teapartycheer.com/bios/west/california/pete-aguilar-ca-bio/|publisher=Tea Party Cheer|accessdate=13 March 2013}}</ref>

==Career==
{{update-section|date=December 2014}}

Aguilar began his career in public service in 2001 when then [[Governor of California]], [[Gray Davis]], appointed him Deputy Director of the Inland Empire Regional Office of the Governor. Aguilar subsequently became the Interim Director of the office.

In 2006, Aguilar was appointed to the Redlands City Council, making him the youngest serving Council Member in the city's 140-year history. In 2010, he was elected Mayor.<ref name="Redlands: Council elects new mayor">{{cite web|last=Sears|first=Jan|title=Redlands: Council elects new mayor|url=http://www.pe.com/local-news/san-bernardino-county/san-bernardino-county-headlines-index/20101208-redlands-council-elects-new-mayor.ece|publisher=The Press-Enterprise|accessdate=8 May 2012}}</ref>

In January 2012 Aguilar announced he would run for [[United States Congress]] in California's 31st congressional district.<ref name="Redlands mayor announces congressional bid">{{cite web|last=Goad|first=Ben|title=Elections: Redlands mayor announces congressional bid|url=http://www.pe.com/local-news/politics/ben-goad-headlines/20120112-elections-redlands-mayor-announces-congressional-bid.ece|publisher=The Press-Enterprise|accessdate=8 May 2012}}</ref> Although the top Democratic vote-getter, with 22.6% of the vote, he finished behind two Republicans, and - because of California's open primary - those two advanced to the November general election.<ref>{{cite web |url= http://nbclatino.com/2013/03/18/on-the-trail-after-2012-disappointment-aguilar-readies-re-run/ |title=On the Trail: After 2012 disappointment, Aguilar readies re-run |author=Nathan L. Gonzale|publisher=NBCLatino.com |date=March 18, 2013 |accessdate=June 6, 2014}}</ref>

In March 2013 Aguilar announced he would run again for [[United States House of Representatives elections in California, 2014#District 31|election from California's 31st congressional district]]. <ref name="Redlands Mayor Pete Aguilar announces another bid for Congress">{{cite web|last=Cappis|first=Greg|title=Redlands Mayor Pete Aguilar announces another bid for Congress|url=http://www.redlandsdailyfacts.com/news/ci_22918664/redlands-mayor-pete-aguilar-announces-another-bid-congress|publisher=The Redlands Daily Facts|date=April 1, 2013}}</ref> In the June 2014 primary, Aguilar finished second in unofficial returns with 17.4% of the vote, and currently has a 179 vote lead to his nearest rival, Republican Lesli Gooch, who is not conceding the runoff spot while ballot counts are still ongoing.<ref name="San Bernardino Sun">{{cite web|last1=Hagen|first1=Ryan|title=Pete Aguilarâ€™s standing solidifies as attacks begin in 31st Congressional District|url=http://www.sbsun.com/government-and-politics/20140606/pete-aguilars-standing-solidifies-as-attacks-begin-in-31st-congressional-district|accessdate=8 June 2014}}</ref> Aguilar facee Republican [[Paul Chabot]] in the November 2014 general election. <ref name="Democrat Pete Aguilar advances to general election in 31st Congressional District">{{cite web|last=Hagen|first=Ryan|title=Democrat Pete Aguilar advances to general election in 31st Congressional District|url=http://www.redlandsdailyfacts.com/government-and-politics/20140605/democrat-pete-aguilar-advances-to-general-election-in-31st-congressional-district|publisher=The Redlands Daily Facts|date=June 5, 2014}}</ref>
