'''Thomas Anthony Marino''' (born August 13, 1952) is an [[United States|American]] politician who has been the [[United States House of Representatives|U.S. Representative]] for {{ushr|PA|10}} since 2011. He is a member of the [[Republican Party (United States)|Republican Party]]. 

The district, located in northern Pennsylvania, includes Bradford County, Juniata County, Lycoming County, Mifflin County, Pike County, Snyder County, Sullivan County, Susquehanna County, Union County, Wayne County, and portions of Perry County, Tioga County, Lackawanna County, Monroe County, and Northumberland County. 

==Early life and education==
Marino was born and raised in [[Lycoming County, Pennsylvania]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://dailyitem.com/0100_news/x1324602326/Marino-wants-less-government-lower-taxes |title=Marino wants less government, lower taxes |publisher=[[The Daily Item (Sunbury)|The Daily Item]] |date=2010-10-12 |accessdate=2014-02-04}}</ref> Marino received his undergraduate degree from [[Lycoming College]] and his [[juris doctor]] from [[Dickinson School of Law]].<ref>{{cite web|url= http://bioguide.congress.gov/scripts/biodisplay.pl?index=M001179 |title=
MARINO, Thomas A., (1952 - )|publisher=[[Biographical Directory of the United States Congress]]|accessdate= November 6, 2012}}</ref>

==Law career==
Marino served as a Lycoming County District Attorney from 1992–2002, and was then selected as a [[United States Attorney|U.S. Attorney]].

In 2007, Marino resigned from office as the U.S. Attorney for the Middle District of Pennsylvania.  Anonymous sources claimed that Marino resigned his position while under review by the [[United States Department of Justice|Department of Justice]]. <ref>{{cite news|last=Birkbeck|first=Matt|title=Source: Marino resigned while under review|url=http://articles.mcall.com/2010-10-01/news/mc-marino-investigation-20101001_1_denaples-investigations-marino-democrat-chris-carney|accessdate=13 February 2013|newspaper=[[The Morning Call]]|date=1 October 2010}}</ref> In 2013, the current US Attorney for the Middle District of Pennsylvania, Peter Smith, confirmed in ''United States v. Healy''<ref>{{cite case|title=United States of America v. Sean Healy|url=http://bloximages.chicago2.vip.townnews.com/dailyitem.com/content/tncms/assets/v3/editorial/7/93/793f495e-65e5-11e4-9caa-5704cb4928f9/545bc2b9ebb97.pdf.pdf|accessdate=12 November 2014}}</ref> that neither Marino, nor his office, were ever under review or investigation, saying, "Mr. Marino was never under investigation by this office... there was no investigation of Mr. Marino by the Department of Justice." <ref>{{cite news|last=Scarcella|first=Francis|title=Source: Marino calls accusations 'lies'|url=http://www.dailyitem.com/news/marino-calls-accusations-lies/article_a6f77f96-6618-11e4-b6f2-ef69c0a20f69.html|accessdate=12 November 2014|newspaper=[[The Daily Item]]|date=6 November 2014}}</ref> After his resignation, Marino accepted a position as an in-house attorney for DeNaples Management for $250,000 per year.<ref>{{cite news|last=Birkbeck|first=Matt|title=Source: Marino resigned while under review|url=http://articles.mcall.com/2010-10-01/news/mc-marino-investigation-20101001_1_denaples-investigations-marino-democrat-chris-carney|accessdate=12 November 2014|newspaper=[[The Morning Call]]|date=1 October 2010}}</ref>

==U.S. House of Representatives==
===Elections===
;2010
{{See also|United States House of Representatives elections in Pennsylvania, 2010#District 10}}

In 2010, Marino decided to challenge incumbent Democrat U.S. Congressman [[Chris Carney]] of [[Pennsylvania's 10th congressional district]]. He won the three-candidate Republican primary with 41% of the vote, defeating Dave Madeira (31%) and [[Snyder County, Pennsylvania|Snyder County]] [[County commission|Commissioner]] [[Malcolm Derk]] (28%).<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=494785 |title=PA - District 10 - R Primary Race - May 18, 2010 |publisher=Our Campaigns |date= |accessdate=2014-02-04}}</ref> On November 2, 2010, Marino defeated Carney 55-45%. He won every county in the district except [[Luzerne County, Pennsylvania|Luzerne]] and [[Lackawanna County, Pennsylvania|Lackawanna]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=493132 |title=PA - District 10 Race - Nov 02, 2010 |publisher=Our Campaigns |date= |accessdate=2014-02-04}}</ref><ref>[http://thetimes-tribune.com/news/carney-concedes-to-marino-in-10th-1.1057990 ]{{dead link|date=February 2014}}</ref>

;2012
{{See also|United States House of Representatives elections in Pennsylvania, 2012#District 10}}
After redistricting, he decided to run in the newly redrawn 10th district, which is more reliably Republican and includes more counties in the central part of the state. He won re-election to a second term, defeating Democratic nominee Philip Scollo 66%-34%.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=705248 |title=PA - District 10 Race - Nov 06, 2012 |publisher=Our Campaigns |date= |accessdate=2014-02-04}}</ref>

;2014
Congressman Marino faced off against Independent Nick Troiano and Democrat Scott Brion. Marino garnered 62.5% of the vote; Troiano got 12.7%; Brion got 24.8%. <ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.politico.com/2014-election/results/map/house/pennsylvania/#.VGYlP0td8ds|publisher=Politico|date= |accessdate=2014-11-12}}</ref>

===Tenure===
Marino is one of the most conservative members of the Pennsylvania delegation. He ranked third among PA members in [[Americans for Prosperity]]’s scorecard (70%) and fifth in [[Club for Growth]]'s scorecard (63%).<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.politicspa.com/exclusive-bradford-commissioner-might-primary-marino/46434/ |title=Exclusive: Bradford Commissioner Might Primary Marino |publisher=PoliticsPA |date=2013-03-05 |accessdate=2014-02-04}}</ref>

He's the co-chair of the Congressional Caucus on Foster Youth <ref>{{cite web|title=Congressional Caucus on Foster Youth|url=http://fosteryouthcaucus-karenbass.house.gov/|website=http://fosteryouthcaucus-karenbass.house.gov/|accessdate=12 November 2014}}</ref> and co-chair of the Cystic Fibrosis Caucus. <ref>{{cite web|title=Cystic Fibrosis Caucus|url=http://www.cff.org/GetInvolved/Advocate/CFCaucus//|website=http://www.cff.org/GetInvolved/Advocate/CFCaucus/|accessdate=12 November 2014}}</ref>

In 2011, Rep. Marino became a co-sponsor of Bill H.R.3261 otherwise known as the [[Stop Online Piracy Act]].<ref name="govtrack">[http://www.govtrack.us/congress/bill.xpd?bill=h112-3261 Bill H.R.3261 ]</ref>

Marino supports the [[death penalty]]. He believes that the mentally ill and criminals should not be able to obtain [[Firearms law|guns]].<ref>{{cite web|last=Brady |first=Chris |url=http://www.standard-journal.com/news/local/article_23e4f56c-960c-11e2-99b7-001a4bcf887a.html |title=Marino: Keep guns away from mentally ill, felons |publisher=standard-journal.com |date=2013-03-26 |accessdate=2014-02-04}}</ref>

In July 2012, Marino introduced a bill to help fund local and state governments, about $800 million per year, to sustain various law enforcement activities such as prosecution, prevention, education, training, and corrections called the "[[Edward Byrne (police officer)|Edward Byrne]] Memorial Justice Assistance Grant Program Reauthorization Act of 2012". Marino said, "Local law enforcement agencies and officials need nothing less than our full support in combating crime on every level".<ref>{{cite web|url=http://marino.house.gov/press-release/house-passes-byrne-jag-program |title=House Passes Byrne-JAG Program &#124; Congressman Thomas Marino |publisher=Marino.house.gov |date=2012-08-02 |accessdate=2014-02-04}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://marino.house.gov/press-release/rep-calls-bureau-prisons-make-improvements |title=Rep. Calls on Bureau of Prisons to Make Improvements &#124; Congressman Thomas Marino |publisher=Marino.house.gov |date=2013-03-15 |accessdate=2014-02-04}}</ref>

In July 2013, Marino voted "NO" to Rep. [[Justin Amash]]'s amendment #413 to H.R. 2397 "To end authority for the blanket collection of records under the [[Patriot Act]] and bar the NSA and other agencies from using Section 215 of the Patriot Act to collect records, including telephone call records, that pertain to persons who are not subject to an investigation under Section 215"<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.govtrack.us/congress/votes/113-2013/h412 |title=H.Amdt. 413 (Amash) to H.R. 2397: Amendment sought to end authority for the blanket collection of records under the Patriot ... |publisher=GovTrack.us |date=2013-07-24 |accessdate=2014-02-04}}</ref> which Rep. [[Jim Sensenbrenner]], an author of the Patriot Act, considers un-American.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.huffingtonpost.com/2013/06/06/jim-sensenbrenner-nsa_n_3397440.html |title=Jim Sensenbrenner, Patriot Act Author, Slams 'Un-American' NSA Verizon Phone Records Grab |publisher=[[HuffPo]] |date= |accessdate=2014-02-04}}</ref>

In August 2014, Marino had a confrontation on the House floor with Minority Leader [[Nancy Pelosi]], after he blamed her for the current immigration crisis, claiming that she could have dealt with the issue when the [[Democratic Party (United States)|Democrats]] held a majority in both houses of Congress at the beginning of President [[Barack Obama]]'s first term. <ref>{{cite web|last1=Itkowitz|first1=Colby|title=Rep. Marino: Pelosi confrontation was ‘walk in the park’|url=http://www.washingtonpost.com/blogs/in-the-loop/wp/2014/08/04/rep-marino-pelosi-confrontation-was-walk-in-the-park/|website=www.washingtonpost.com|publisher=The Washington Post|accessdate=4 August 2014}}</ref>

===Legislation sponsored===

Marino is the author of the Animal Fighting Spectator Prohibition Act that was signed into law by President Obama in February of 2014. The law makes it a federal offense to watch animals fight. <ref name=marinoforcongress>{{cite news|title=Marino's animal fighting bill signed into law|url=http://marino.house.gov/press-release/marinos-animal-fighting-bill-signed-law|accessdate=12 November 2014}}</ref>

On July 10, 2013, Marino introduced the [[Responsibly And Professionally Invigorating Development Act of 2013 (H.R. 2641; 113th Congress)]] into the House.<ref name=2641sum>{{cite web|title=H.R. 2641 - Summary|url=http://beta.congress.gov/bill/113th-congress/house-bill/2641|publisher=United States Congress|accessdate=6 March 2014}}</ref> The bill aims to expedite the review process required by the [[National Environmental Policy Act]] (NEPA) for construction projects that are partly or fully financed with federal funds or require permits or approvals from federal regulatory agencies.<ref name=2641cbo>{{cite web|title=H.R. 2641 - CBO|url=http://www.cbo.gov/publication/44534|publisher=Congressional Budget Office|accessdate=6 March 2014}}</ref> On March 6, 2014, the House voted 229-179 to pass the bill.<ref name=HouseLimitsPete>{{cite news|last=Kasperowicz|first=Pete|title=House votes to limit environmental reviews|url=http://thehill.com/blogs/e2-wire/e2-wire/200129-house-votes-to-limit-environmental-reviews|accessdate=7 March 2014|newspaper=The Hill|date=6 March 2014}}</ref>
