'''Filemon Bartolome Vela, Jr.''' (born February 13, 1963)<ref name=NationalJournal>{{cite web|last=Hopkins|first=Christopher Snow|title=Texas, 34th House District|url=http://www.nationaljournal.com/congress-legacy/texas-34th-house-district-20121102|work=nationaljournal.com|accessdate=8 November 2012}}</ref> is an American lawyer and politician who has been the [[United States House of Representatives|U.S. Representative]] for {{ushr|TX|34}} since 2013. He is a member of the [[Democratic Party (United States)|Democratic Party]].

==Early life and education==

Vela was born in [[Harlingen, Texas]] and raised in [[Brownsville, Texas]]. His father, [[Filemon Bartolome Vela, Sr.]], was a long-serving United States federal judge.  The Reynaldo G. Garza-Filemon B. Vela United States Courthouse in Brownsville is named in Judge Vela's honor. His mother, [[Blanca Vela|Blanca Sanchez Vela]], served as Brownsville’s first female mayor from 1999 to 2003.<ref>vela.house.gov</ref><ref name=monitor>{{cite news|first=Ty|last=Johnson|title=Former Brownsville mayor, feminist 'trailblazer' Blanca Vela dies at 78 |url=http://www.themonitor.com/news/local/article_93ce870a-99d3-11e3-8d61-001a4bcf6878.html |work=[[The Monitor (Texas)]] |publisher= |date=2014-02-18 |accessdate=2014-03-13}}</ref><ref name=kveo>{{cite news|first=|last=|title=Brownsville's former and only female mayor, Blanca Vela, passes away |url=http://www.kveo.com/news/brownsvilles-former-and-only-female-mayor-blanca-vela-passes-away |work=[[KVEO]] |publisher= |date=2014-02-19 |accessdate=2014-03-13}}</ref>

Filemon attended [[Saint Joseph Academy (Brownsville, Texas)|Saint Joseph Academy]] in Brownsville, Texas, and went on to graduate from [[Georgetown University]] in 1985. During his time at Georgetown, he served as an intern at the [[Federal Judicial Center]] – the research and education agency of the federal judicial system. He also served as an intern in [[Solomon P. Ortiz|Congressman Solomon P Ortiz’s]] Washington, D.C. office. Vela earned his J.D. from [[University of Texas School of Law|University of Texas at Austin School of Law]] in 1987. <ref>vela.house.gov</ref>

==Law career==
For more than 20 years, Filemon practiced law in [[South Texas]], helping countless individuals seek justice in state and federal courts across the country.  His legal practice focused on complex civil litigation. As a trial attorney, Filemon successfully represented clients who were the victims of racial discrimination, consumers who had been severely injured by defective products, and employees injured during work.

Some notable cases include Earl Shinhoster v. [[Ford Motor Company]] where Vela represented the family of deceased Director of the [[NAACP]] who was killed in a car rollover accident in what was the largest wrongful death settlements in Alabama history and Robert Trevino v. [[Walmart]] where Vela represented a group of American farm workers who were told they could not shop in a Mississippi store because of discrimination against Hispanics.[4]  

As an attorney, Vela also represented several South Texas school districts. In [[Edinburg Consolidated Independent School District|Edinburg School District]] v. Landmark, Vela represented Edinburg to fight for more funding and in Pharr-San Juan-Alamo Independent School District v. Landmark, he represented the district in fighting contractors accused of building another poorly constructed school facility.

==U.S. House of Representatives==
===2012 election===
{{Main|United States House of Representatives elections in Texas, 2012#District 34}}
Vela decided to run in the newly created [[Texas's 34th congressional district]] as a Democrat. In the May 29 primary, Vela ranked first in an 8-candidate field with 40% of the vote.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=760010 |title=Ourcampaigns.com |publisher=Ourcampaigns.com |date= |accessdate=2014-08-19}}</ref> In the July 31 run-off primary, Vela defeated Denise Saenz Blanchard 67%-33%.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=767642 |title=Ourcampaigns.com |publisher=Ourcampaigns.com |date= |accessdate=2014-08-19}}</ref><ref>http://enr.sos.state.tx.us/enr/results/july31_163_state.htm</ref> 

In the general election, Vela defeated Republican Jessica Bradshaw 62% - 36%, winning the election.<ref>{{cite web|title=TX-TopRaces-Glance-Sum|url=http://www.kxxv.com/story/20031305/tx-topraces-glance-sum?clienttype=printable|work=kxxv.com|accessdate=8 November 2012}}</ref>

===Tenure===
In July 2013, he decided to quit the [[Congressional Hispanic Caucus]] because of his opposition to the [[John Hoeven|Hoeven]]-[[Bob Corker|Corker]] Amendment that tied border security with a [[amnesty|pathway to citizenship]].
