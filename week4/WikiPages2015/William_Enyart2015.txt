'''William Lee Enyart, Jr.''' (born September 22, 1949) is the [[United States House of Representatives|U.S. Representative]] for [[Illinois's 12th congressional district]]. Enyart is a member of the [[Democratic Party (United States)|Democratic Party]]. He was elected on November 6, 2012 and assumed office on January 3, 2013.<ref name=times>{{cite news|title=William 'Bill' L. Enyart, Jr.|url=http://www.washingtontimes.com/campaign-2012/candidates/william-bill-l-enyart-jr-62250/|accessdate=May 23, 2014|newspaper=Washington Times}}</ref><ref name=results>{{cite news|title=Bill Enyart officially becomes 12th District's congressman|url=http://thesouthern.com/news/local/govt-and-politics/bill-enyart-officially-becomes-th-district-s-congressman/article_16f5670c-55e2-11e2-941c-0019bb2963f4.html|accessdate=May 23, 2014|newspaper=The Southern|date=1-3-2013}}</ref> Enyart is a former attorney and [[Adjutant general of Illinois|Adjutant General of Illinois]].

Enyart ran for re-election to Congress in 2014. He faced Republican [[Mike Bost]] in the general election. Enyart was viewed as one the of the most vulnerable freshman Democrats.<ref>{{cite news|last=Sweet|first=Lynn|title=Three Illinois freshmen Democrats--Schneider, Bustos, Enyart--seen as vulnerable in 2014|url=http://blogs.suntimes.com/sweet/2013/03/three_illinois_freshmen_democr.html|accessdate=23 May 2014|newspaper=Chicago Sun-Times|date=3-5-2013}}</ref><ref name=ivn>{{cite news|last=Wicklander|first=Carl|title=Large Percentage of Undecided Voters in IL-12 Leaves Election a Toss-Up|url=http://ivn.us/2014/02/03/large-percentage-undecided-voters-il-12-leaves-election-toss|accessdate=May 22, 2014|newspaper=Independent Voter Network|date=February 3, 2014}}</ref> Enyart lost to Bost on November 4, 2014.

==Education==
Enyart earned his [[Bachelor of Arts]] in journalism and political science in 1974 from [[Southern Illinois University Edwardsville]] and his [[Juris Doctor]]ate from [[Southern Illinois University School of Law]] in 1979. In 2000, Enyart graduated from the [[United States Army War College]] with a [[Master of Science]] in Strategic Studies.<ref>{{cite web|title=Biography|url=http://www.enyartforillinois.com/about|work=Campaign Website|publisher=Bill Enyart for Congress|accessdate=23 May 2014}}</ref> In 2012, Enyart was awarded an honorary degree from [[Lindenwood University]] in Belleville, Illinois.<ref>{{cite web|title=‘Be Prepared,’ Rep. Enyart Challenges First Lindenwood-Belleville Grads|url=http://enyart.house.gov/media-center/press-releases/be-prepared-rep-enyart-challenges-first-lindenwood-belleville-grads|work=United States House of Representatives|publisher=William Enyart|accessdate=23 May 2014}}</ref>

appointed to lead the National Guard and the Illinois Department of Military Affairs as the 37th Adjutant General in 2007, and was reappointed in 2009 and in 2011.<ref>{{cite news|title=Enyart Reappointed As Illinois National Guard's Adjutant General|url=http://www.il.ngb.army.mil/PAO/newsrelease.aspx?id=374|accessdate=23 May 2014|newspaper=Illinois National Guard|date=2011-04-26}}</ref> As 2014|newspaper=Chicago Tribune|date=2011-11-27}}</ref> and Illinois's response to the [[2011 Mississippi River floods]].<ref>{{cite news|title=Quinn, Illinois National Guard Adjutant General, Metropolis Mayor Flood|url=http://readme.readmedia.com/Quinn-Illinois-National-Guard-Adjutant-General-Metropolis-Mayor-Flood/2314863|accessdate=23 May 2014|newspaper=Read Order of Merit of the Republic of Poland from [[Bronislaw Komorowski]], the President of Poland.<ref>{{cite news|title=Polish President Visits Illinois Soldiers At Marseilles Training ==United States House of Representatives==

===Elections===
;2012
{{Main|United States House of Representatives elections in Illinois, 2012#District 12}}
On June 23, 2012, Enyart was selected as the Democratic nominee for the U.S. House of Representatives [[Illinois's 12th congressional district]] after the original nominee, Brad Harriman, withdrew from the race for health reasons.<ref name=times/> Enyart defeated Republican nominee Jason Plummer 52%-43%.<ref name=results/>

During the election, Plummer stated that Enyart and his wife, a retired circuit judge, receive a total of three taxpayer-funded pensions. The ''[[St. Louis Post-Dispatch]]'' reported that while Enyart did not currently receive a pension, his wife does collect on three taxpayer pension funds.<ref>{{cite news|last1=Pistor|first1=Nicholas|title=Enyart fights back against political attacks on his wife|url=http://www.stltoday.com/news/local/govt-and-politics/nick-pistor/enyart-fights-back-against-political-attacks-on-his-wife/article_eb64721f-e10f-570c-84b3-30dbece67c3b.html|accessdate=1 August 2014|publisher=St. Louis Post-Dispatch|date=10/31/12}}</ref>

;2014
{{Main|United States House of Representatives elections in Illinois, 2014#District 12}}
Enyart ran for re-election in 2014.<ref>{{cite news|last=Fitzgerald|first=Mike|title=Enyart announces re-election campaign for Congress|url=http://www.bnd.com/2013/11/26/2927027/us-rep-enyart-officially-announces.html|accessdate=23 May 2014|newspaper=News-Democrat|date=2013-11-26}}</ref> He ran unopposed in the Democratic primary. He faced [[Mike Bost]] in the general election on 4 November. Enyart was a member of the [[Democratic Congressional Campaign Committee|Democratic Congressional Campaign Committee's]] Frontline Program, which is designed to help protect vulnerable Democratic incumbents.<ref>{{cite news|last=Livingston|first=Abby|title=DCCC Announces 26 Members on Frontline Incumbent Retention Program|url=http://atr.rollcall.com/dccc-announces-26-members-on-frontline-incumbent-retention-program/?dcz=|accessdate=23 May 2014|newspaper=Roll Call|date=3-5-2013}}</ref>

Bost defeated Enyart in the general election.

==Political positions==

===Immigration===
Enyart supports the [[DREAM Act]], particularly a provision that would give immigrants who join the military an expedited path to citizenship.<ref name=debate>{{cite news|last=Rosenbaum|first=Jason|title=Plummer and Enyart set acrimonious tone in second debate|url=https://www.stlbeacon.org/#!/content/27152/enyart_plummer_debate_marion|accessdate=23 May 2014|newspaper=St. Louis Beacon|date=2012-09-20}}</ref>

===Health care===
Enyart is against repealing the [[Patient Protection and Affordable Care Act]]. Speaking about the health care law, Enyart said "Now are there problems with it? Of course there are problems with it. Let’s not throw the baby out with the bathwater. Let’s change the things that are problems and move on."<ref>{{cite news|last=Rosenbaum|first=Jason|title=Enyart and Plummer compete in high-stakes battle for Illinois' 12th District seat|url=https://www.stlbeacon.org/#!/content/27291/illinois_12th_district_overview|accessdate=23 May 2014|newspaper=St. Louis Beacon|date=10-4-2012}}</ref> In an analysis of 2014 campaign websites, ''[[The Hill (newspaper)|The Hill]]'' found that Enyart is among six congressional Democrats whose websites do not mention the Affordable Care Act, noting that "Most Democrats in competitive elections are seeking to avoid the topic, opting not to tout the controversial law on their campaign websites."<ref>{{cite news|last=Timmons|first=Sheila|title=Dems don't want to talk O-Care|url=http://thehill.com/policy/healthcare/204426-dems-dont-want-to-talk-about-o-care|accessdate=23 May 2014|newspaper=The Hill|date=2014-04-26}}</ref>

===Taxes===
Enyart has stated his support for raising taxes on higher income individuals. Enyart has said "there is class warfare."<ref name=debate/>

===Women's issues===
Enyart voted for the [[Violence Against Women Act]].<ref>{{cite web|title=House Vote 55 - Passes Reauthorization of the Violence Against Women Act|url=https://politics.nytimes.com/congress/votes/113/house/1/55|publisher=New York Times|accessdate=23 May 2014}}</ref>

===Privacy issues===
Enyart co-sponsored the [[Cyber Intelligence Sharing and Protection Act]] (CISPA),<ref>{{cite news|last=Wicklander|first=Carl|title=Illinois State Rep. Mike Bost to Enter US House Race|url=http://ivn.us/2013/07/29/illinois-state-rep-mike-bost-to-enter-us-house-race-2/|accessdate=23 May 2014|newspaper=Independent Voter Network|date=2013-07-29}}</ref> which would have allowed for the sharing of Internet traffic information between the U.S. government and technology and manufacturing companies for cybersecurity purposes.<ref>{{cite news|last=Nesbit|first=Jeff|title=CISPA Rolls Along|url=http://www.usnews.com/news/blogs/at-the-edge/2013/05/06/cispa-rolls-along|accessdate=23 May 2014|newspaper=US News & World Report|date=5-6-2013}}</ref> CISPA passed in the House but was not passed by the U.S. Senate. President Obama's advisers argued that the bill lacked confidentiality and civil liberties safeguards.<ref>{{cite news|last=Smith|first=Gerry|title=White House Threatens CISPA Veto, Says Revised Cybersecurity Bill Doesn't Adequately Protect Privacy, Civil Liberties|url=http://www.huffingtonpost.com/2013/04/16/white-house-cispa-veto-threat_n_3094865.html|accessdate=23 May 2014|newspaper=Huffington Post|date=2013-04-16}}</ref>

Enyart co-sponsored the Preserving American Privacy Act, which would have required federal agencies that used drones domestically to minimize the collection of personally identifiable information. The bill did not make it out of committee.<ref>{{cite web|title=H.R. 637: Preserving American Privacy Act of 2013|url=https://www.govtrack.us/congress/bills/113/hr637|publisher=GovTrack|accessdate=23 May 2014}}</ref>

Enyart voted against a proposed amendment by [[Justin Amash]] (R-MI) that would have defunded warrantless domestic surveillance operations by the [[National Security Agency]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Amash Amendment On NSA Data Collection: House Roll Call Vote|url=http://www.huffingtonpost.com/2013/07/24/amash-amendment-roll-call-vote_n_3648737.html|publisher=Huffington Post|accessdate=23 May 2014}}</ref>

===Veterans' issues===
Enyart introduced the Veterans Backlog Reduction Act, which would provide provisional benefits to veterans who have requested benefits from the VA and been left waiting for more than 125 days.<ref>{{cite news|title=Enyart introduces veteran claims bill|url=http://carbondaletimes.com/051713veterans|accessdate=23 May 2014|newspaper=Carbondale Times|date=2013-05-17}}</ref>

In October 2013, Enyart was the lone member of Congress to vote against a bill that would have restored chaplain services to military personnel in the wake of the [[government shutdown of 2013]].<ref>{{cite news|last1=Mariano|first1=Nick|title=Enyart lone no-vote on military bill|url=http://thesouthern.com/news/local/enyart-lone-no-vote-on-military-bill/article_50dea2a6-2f0a-11e3-86ea-001a4bcf887a.html|accessdate=27 June 2014|publisher=The Southern|date=10-7-2013}}</ref> Enyart defended his vote, calling the measure "an absolutely phony, feel-good bill that had nothing to do with reality," further noting that the bill provided no way to pay the chaplains if they resumed their duties.<ref>{{cite news|last=Suhr|first=Jim|title=Thousands of Illinois Military Workers Back on Job|url=http://www.nbcchicago.com/news/local/Most-Furloughed-Workers-Back-on-Job-at-Illinois-Base-226751191.html#ixzz3JZFYgr4W|accessdate=19 November 2014|publisher=NBC Chicago}}</ref>

===National security===
Enyart voted in favor of proposed amendments by [[Adam Smith]] (D-WA) that would have eliminated indefinite detention for any individual held in the United States<ref>{{cite web|title=H.Amdt. 152 (Smith) to H.R. 1960: Amendment sought to eliminate indefinite military detention of any person detained under AUMF authority|url=https://www.govtrack.us/congress/votes/113-2013/h228|publisher=GovTrack|accessdate=23 May 2014}}</ref> and provided a framework  to close the detention facility at [[Guantanamo Bay, Cuba]], by December 1, 2014.<ref>{{cite web|title=H.Amdt. 168 (Smith) to H.R. 1960: Amendment sought to provide framework to close the detention facility at Guantanamo Bay, Cuba|url=https://www.govtrack.us/congress/votes/113-2013/h237|publisher=GovTrack|accessdate=23 May 2014}}</ref>

===Federal budget===
Enyart voted for the [[No Budget, No Pay Act of 2013]], which temporarily suspended the [[United States debt ceiling]] and placed temporary restrictions on congressional salaries.<ref>{{cite news|last=Huston|first=Warner Todd|title=Illinois Congressmen Sign Onto ‘No Budget, No Pay’ Act|url=http://www.prairiestatereport.us/2013/01/24/illinois-congressmen-sign-onto-no-budget-no-pay-act/|accessdate=23 May 2014|newspaper=The Prairie State Report|date=2013-1-24}}</ref>
