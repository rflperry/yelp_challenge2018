'''David Wilson Jolly''' (born October 31, 1972) is an [[United States|American]] attorney, former lobbyist and the [[United States House of Representatives|U.S. Representative]] for [[Florida's 13th congressional district]]. A [[Republican Party (United States)|Republican]], he previously served as general counsel to his predecessor, U.S. Rep. [[Bill Young]].<ref name="Jolly">{{cite news |url=http://www.tampabay.com/news/politics/local/rick-baker-wont-run-for-youngs-seat-but-david-jolly-will/2150510|title=Rick Baker won't run for Young's seat, but David Jolly will|newspaper=Tampa Bay Times|date=November 2, 2013|accessdate=November 2, 2013}}</ref> He won the race for Young's seat as the Republican candidate in a [[Florida's 13th congressional district special election, 2014|2014 special election]] against [[Democratic Party (United States)|Democrat]] [[Alex Sink]] and [[Libertarian Party of Florida|Libertarian]] [[Lucas Overby]].<ref>{{cite news|last=Bradshaw|first=Kate|title=David Jolly takes District 13 election|url=http://tbo.com/pinellas-county/polls-close-in-contentious-congressional-race-20140311/|accessdate=March 12, 2014|newspaper=St. Petersburg Tribune|date=March 11, 2014}}</ref>

==Early life, education and career==
Jolly was born in [[Dunedin, Florida]].<ref name=meet/> He received his [[Bachelor of Arts]] in history from [[Emory University]] and a [[juris doctorate]] from the [[George Mason University]] School of Law. He worked for U.S. Rep. Young as an adviser and General Counsel until 2007. 

In 2007, Jolly began work as a [[lobbying in the United States|lobbyist]] with Washington, D.C. firm Van Scoyoc Associates. Eventually, he opened his own firm, Three Bridges Advisors in 2011, and contributed political donations to both Republicans and Democrats during his time as a lobbyist.<ref>{{cite web|title=David Jolly gave 'almost $30,000 to keep Democrats in Congress' as a lobbyist, Kathleen Peters says|url=http://www.politifact.com/florida/statements/2013/dec/27/kathleen-peters/david-jolly-gave-almost-30000-keep-democrats-congr/|work=PolitiFact|date=December 27, 2013|publisher=Tampa Bay Times}}</ref> Jolly officially took his name off the Lobby Registry in order to run for the vacant House seat.<ref>{{cite news|last=Tau|first=Byron|title=GOP candidate's Democratic giving past|url=http://www.politico.com/story/2013/11/david-jolly-florida-donations-99922.html|newspaper=Politico|date=November 15, 2013}}</ref>

Jolly lives in [[Indian Shores, Florida]].<ref name=meet>[http://davidjolly.com/meet-david David Jolly for Congress website]</ref> His divorced from his wife Carrie was finalized on January 16, 2014.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.tampabay.com/blogs/the-buzz-florida-politics/update-to-profile-on-pinellas-congressional-candidate-david-jolly/2160390|title=Congressional candidate David Jolly's girlfriend once named one of Washington's most beautiful people|last1=Krueger|first1=Curtis|date=January 9, 2014|publisher=Tampa Bay Times|accessdate=March 9, 2014}}</ref>

==Political positions==
Jolly opposes the [[Patient Protection and Affordable Care Act]], also known as "[[Obamacare]]", and says he has goals of lowering taxes and cutting spending. He believes [[Israel]] is one of the premier allies of the United States and has a goal of not cutting the United States commitment to the military and to its allies. He is [[pro-life]], says that he "support[s] the constitutional [[right to keep and bear arms in the United States|right to keep and bear arms]]", and opposes amnesty for illegal immigrants. He supports the [[Balanced Budget Amendment]],<ref name=friends>[http://davidjolly.com/issues Issues], davidjolly.com; accessed November 12, 2014.</ref> and says he would have voted to raise the debt limit in early 2014.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.tampabay.com/news/politics/national/david-jolly-campaigns-as-a-cw-bill-young-republican-but-which-one/2168337|title=David Jolly campaigns as a C.W. Bill Young Republican, but which one?|last1=Smith|first1=Adam C.|date=March 3, 2014|newspaper=Tampa Bay Times|accessdate=March 9, 2014}}</ref> 

On July 21, 2014, Jolly announced his support for same-sex marriage, stating that he believes that it is "fully appropriate" for a state to recognize both same-sex marriages and "traditional" ones.<ref>[http://www.washingtonpost.com/blogs/post-politics/wp/2014/07/21/republican-rep-david-jolly-fla-supports-gay-marriage Rep. Dave Jolly (R-FL) supports gay marriage], washingtonpost.com, July 21, 2014; accessed November 12, 2014.</ref>

==Special election campaign (2014)==
On January 14, 2014, Jolly won the Republican nomination over [[Mark Bircher]] and [[Kathleen Peters]].<ref name=AP/>

After Jolly won the Republican nomination, the [[National Republican Congressional Committee]] spent nearly $500,000 on advertising on his behalf.<ref name="Isenstadt">{{cite web| last =Isenstadt | first = Alex | title = National GOP turns on Florida candidate| publisher = ''[[Politico]]''| date = March 7, 2014 | url = http://www.politico.com/story/2014/03/david-jolly-alex-sink-florida-special-election-2014-104397.html#ixzz2vRbfkXot| accessdate = 2014-03-10 }}</ref>  Nevertheless, there was friction between the national committee and Jolly, who criticized the advertising.<ref name="Isenstadt"/>  Jolly downplayed the differences, stating that he and the Republican Party stood for the same things.<ref name="Isenstadt"/>

Ultimately, Jolly defeated Democratic nominee [[Alex Sink]] on March 11, 2014 and was sworn in on March 13.<ref name=swornin>{{cite news|last=Cassata|first=Donna|title=Florida's Jolly Sworn in as Newest Congressman|url=http://abcnews.go.com/Politics/wireStory/floridas-jolly-sworn-newest-congressman-22900163|accessdate=13 March 2014|newspaper=Associated Press|date=13 March 2014}}</ref>
