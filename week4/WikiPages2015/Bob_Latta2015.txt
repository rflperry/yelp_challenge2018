'''Robert Edward 'Bob' Latta''' (born April 18, 1956) is the [[United States House of Representatives|U.S. Representative]] for {{ushr|OH|5}}, serving since 2007. He is a member of the [[Republican Party (United States)|Republican Party]]. The district includes most of  [[Toledo, Ohio|Toledo]]'s suburbs including [[Bowling Green, Ohio|Bowling Green]] and [[Perrysburg, Ohio|Perrysburg]].  It also includes [[Findlay, Ohio|Findlay]]. He is also the head of the controversial H.R. 4752 bill, which threatens the neutrality and future of the internet.<ref>{{cite web|title=Net Neutrality is under Attack|url=http://act.freepress.net/sign/internet_nn_congress_latta/?source=conf}}</ref> 

==Early life, education and career==
Born in [[Bluffton, Ohio]], Latta earned his [[Bachelor of Arts|B.A.]] at [[Bowling Green State University]] in 1978 and his [[Juris Doctor|J.D.]] at the [[University of Toledo College of Law]] in 1981. His father, [[Del Latta]], represented the 5th from 1959 to 1989 and served as ranking Republican on the [[United States House Committee on the Budget|House Budget Committee]] from 1975 to his retirement.

Latta worked as a private practice attorney before entering politics.

==Ohio political career==
Latta served as a [[Wood County, Ohio|Wood County]] Commissioner from 1991 to 1997. He then represented the [[Ohio's 2nd senatorial district|2nd senate district]] in the [[Ohio Senate]] from 1997 to 2001 and the 6th house district in the [[Ohio House of Representatives]] from 2001 to 2007.

===Legislation sponsored===
Latta introduced the [[Sportsmenâ€™s Heritage And Recreational Enhancement Act of 2013 (H.R. 3590; 113th Congress)]] into the United States House of Representatives on November 21, 2013.<ref name=3590sum>{{cite web|title=H.R. 3590 - Summary|url=http://beta.congress.gov/bill/113th-congress/house-bill/3590|publisher=United States Congress|accessdate=5 February 2014}}</ref> The bill is an [[omnibus bill]] that covers several firearms, fishing, hunting, and federal land laws.<ref name=TuesdayFirearms>{{cite news|last=Kasperowicz|first=Pete|title=Tuesday: Firearms in the House, farm bill in the Senate|url=http://thehill.com/blogs/floor-action/scheduling/197342-tuesday-firearms-in-the-house-farm-bill-in-the-senate|accessdate=6 February 2014|newspaper=The Hill|date=4 February 2014}}</ref> The bill passed the House on February 5, 2014.

On July 22, 2014, Latta introduced the [[E-LABEL Act (H.R. 5161; 113th Congress)]], a bill that would direct the [[Federal Communications Commission]] (FCC) to allow manufacturers of [[electronic devices]] with a screen to display information required by the agency digitally on the screen rather than on a label affixed to the device.<ref name=cbo5161>{{cite web|title=CBO - H.R. 5161|url=http://www.cbo.gov/publication/45666|publisher=Congressional Budget Office|accessdate=12 September 2014}}</ref><ref name=MarcosHousePasses1>{{cite news|last1=Marcos|first1=Cristina|title=House passes 'E-labeling' bill|url=http://thehill.com/blogs/floor-action/house/217448-house-passes-e-labeling-bill|accessdate=12 September 2014|publisher=The Hill|date=11 September 2014}}</ref> Latta argued that e-labeling would "give greater flexibility to design consumer products" and that "by some estimates e-labeling will save manufactures over $80 million a year."<ref name="MarcosHousePasses1"/>

==Political campaigns==

===1988===
When Del Latta decided to retire from Congress in 1988, Bob Latta ran in the Republican primary. However, he lost by twenty-seven votes to then [[Ohio State Senate]] president [[Paul Gillmor]], who went on to win the general election.

===2007===
After Gillmor's sudden death in September 2007, Latta decided to run again for the open seat. He defeated state senator [[Steve Buehrer]], among other candidates, in the November primary.  He benefited not only from his name recognition, but because [[Wood County, Ohio|Wood County]], home to Bowling Green, is the largest whole county in the sprawling district.

In the [[Ohio's 5th congressional district special election, 2007|special general election]], Latta defeated [[Democratic Party (United States)|Democratic]] candidate [[Robin Weirauch]] on December 11, 2007 by a margin of 57% to 43%. He was sworn in on December 13, 2007.<ref>[http://www.cleveland.com/news/plaindealer/index.ssf?/base/news/1197624921112340.xml&coll=2 Ohio's new congressman Bob Latta sworn in ]</ref>

===2010===
{{See also|United States House of Representatives elections in Ohio, 2010#District 5*}}
Latta faced Democratic nominee Caleb Finkenbiner and Libertarian nominee Brian L. Smith in the general election, he won the election.

===2012===
{{Main|United States House of Representatives elections in Ohio, 2012#District 5}}
Latta was re-elected in 2012.  He beat Democratic nominee, Angela Zimmann and Libertarian nominee, Eric Eberly.<ref>{{cite news|title=House Election Results|url=http://elections.huffingtonpost.com/2012/results/house | work=Huffington Post}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=Ohio Secretary of State|url=http://www.sos.state.oh.us/sos/upload/elections/2012/gen/UScongress.pdf}}</ref>  He was endorsed by the United States Chamber of Commerce, the NFIB, the NRA and National Right to Life.<ref>{{cite web|title=Latta For Congress|url=http://www.lattaforcongress.com/}}</ref>

==Personal life==
Latta and his wife Marcia have two children, Elizabeth and Maria.
