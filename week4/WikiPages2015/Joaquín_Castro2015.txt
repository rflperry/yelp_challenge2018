'''Joaquín Castro''' (born September 16, 1974)<ref>[http://www.vote-smart.org/bio.php?can_id=MTX03265 Project Vote Smart - Representative Joaquin Castro - Biography<!-- Bot generated title -->]</ref> is an American politician who has served in the [[United States House of Representatives]] since 2013, representing [[Texas's 20th congressional district]].  The district includes just over half of [[San Antonio]], as well as some of its closer-in suburbs. From 2003 to 2013, he was a representative in the [[Texas Legislature]] representing District 125.<ref name="txgov">[http://www.house.state.tx.us/members/dist125/castro.htm Texas House of Representatives membership summary]</ref> While in the Texas legislature Castro served as Vice-Chair of the Higher Education Committee and was a member of the Judiciary & Civil Jurisprudence Committee. He has previously served on the County Affairs Committee, Border & International Affairs Committee and the Juvenile Justice & Family Issues Committee.<ref name="txgov" /> Castro's identical twin brother, [[Julian Castro|Julián]], served as [[List of mayors of San Antonio|Mayor of San Antonio]] from 2009 to 2014 and is now the 16th [[United States Secretary of Housing and Urban Development]] in the [[United States Cabinet|Cabinet]] of [[Barack Obama|President Barack Obama]].<ref name="SecHousing">{{cite web | url=http://trailblazersblog.dallasnews.com/2014/07/julian-castro-to-take-office-monday-as-housing-secretary.html/ | title=Julián Castro to take office Monday as Housing Secretary | publisher=[[Dallas Morning News]] | date=25 July 2014 | author=Gillman, Todd J}}</ref>

==Early life, education, and early career==
Castro was raised in San Antonio and attended [[Thomas Jefferson High School (San Antonio, TX)|Thomas Jefferson High School]]. Castro has stated that his interest in public service developed at a young age from watching his parents' involvement in political campaigns and civic causes. His father was a retired teacher from the [[Edgewood Independent School District (Bexar County, Texas)|Edgewood Independent School District]], and his mother, a community activist. He graduated with honors from [[Stanford University]] with a [[Bachelor of Arts]] in [[political science]] and [[communications]] and went on to earn a [[juris doctor]] with his twin brother at [[Harvard Law School]].<ref name="house.state.tx.us">[http://www.house.state.tx.us/members/member-page/?district=125#member-biography Member biography, Texas state legislature]</ref> After law school, the two brothers continued together to work for the law firm [[Akin Gump Strauss Hauer & Feld]] before starting their own firm in 2005.<ref>{{cite web|title=TRIBPEDIA: Julián Castro|publisher="[[The Texas Tribune]]"|accessdate=2013-08-29|url=http://www.texastribune.org/tribpedia/julian-castro/about/}}</ref>

He worked in [[public education]], [[health care]], and the juvenile justice system.<ref name="house.state.tx.us"/> Castro is a member of the National College Advising Corps, [[St. Mary's University, Texas|St. Mary's University]] Mission and Identity Taskforce, [[St. Philip's College (United States)|St. Philip's College]] President's Advisory Board, and Texas Family Impact Seminar.

==Texas House of Representatives==

===Elections===
Castro ran for Texas's 125th House district in 2002. He defeated incumbent State Representative [[Arthur Reyna]] in the Democratic primary 64%-36%.<ref>http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=290782</ref> In the general election, he defeated Republican Nelson Balido 60%-40% and was elected to the [[Texas House of Representatives]] in 2002 at the age of 28.<ref>http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=5909</ref> In 2004, he won re-election to a second term unopposed.<ref name="elections.sos.state.tx.us">http://elections.sos.state.tx.us/elchist.exe</ref> In 2006, he won re-election to a third term, defeating Republican Nelson Balido, 58%-38%.<ref name="elections.sos.state.tx.us"/> In 2008, he won re-election to a fourth term unopposed.<ref name="elections.sos.state.tx.us"/> In 2010, he won re-election to a fifth term, defeating Libertarian Jeffrey Blunt, 78%-22%.<ref name="elections.sos.state.tx.us"/>

==U.S. House of Representatives==

===Elections===
In June 2011, Castro announced that he was running for a seat in the [[United States House of Representatives]] in the newly-drawn {{ushr|TX|35}}.  He was initially set to challenge fellow Democrat and nine-term incumbent [[Lloyd Doggett]], whose home in [[Austin, Texas|Austin]] had been drawn into the district, in the Democratic primary<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.texastribune.org/texas-politics/2012-congressional-election/castro-to-take-on-doggett-for-congressional-seat/|title=Castro To Take On Doggett for New Congressional Seat — 2012 Congressional Election |last=Ramshaw|first=Emily|date=June 24, 2011|work=Texas Tribune|accessdate=5 September 2012}}</ref> However, on November 28, after [[Charlie Gonzalez]] of the neighboring [[Texas's 20th congressional district|20th District]] announced his retirement after seven terms, Castro announced his intent to run instead for the 20th District seat.  He was unopposed in the Democratic primary, all but assuring him of being the next congressman from this heavily Democratic, Hispanic-majority district. At the [[2012 Democratic National Convention]], he introduced his brother Julián as keynote speaker.<ref name = "Garrett">{{cite news|url=http://www.dallasnews.com/news/politics/national-politics/20120904-with-his-twin-brother-in-the-spotlight-joaquin-castro-prepares-for-prominent-role-of-his-own.ece|title=With his twin brother in the spotlight, Joaquin Castro prepares for prominent role of his own|last=Garrett|first=Robert T.|date=September 4, 2012|work=Dallas Morning News|accessdate=5 September 2012}}</ref> In November 2012, Castro defeated Republican David Rosa 64%-34%.<ref>http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=750688</ref> becoming only the fifth person to represent this district since its creation in 1935.

==Personal life==
Castro is the son of Jesse Guzman and Rosie Castro and the identical twin brother of [[Julian Castro|Julián Castro]], the former [[List of mayors of San Antonio|Mayor of San Antonio]] and the 16th [[United States Secretary of Housing and Urban Development]] in the [[United States Cabinet|Cabinet]] of [[Barack Obama|President Barack Obama]].<ref name="SecHousing" /> In addition to his work in the [[Texas Legislature]], Castro practices law in San Antonio. He has also taught as a [[visiting professor]] of law at [[St. Mary's University, Texas|St. Mary's University]] and as an [[adjunct professor]] at [[Trinity University (Texas)|Trinity University]] in San Antonio.

Castro sits on several boards of [[nonprofit organization]]s and institutions of higher education, including: Achieving the Dream, the National College Advising Corps, St. Phillip's College President's Advisory Board, St. Mary's University Mission and Identity Taskforce, and the [[National Association of Latino Elected and Appointed Officials]]' (NALEO) Taskforce on Education.<ref name="house.state.tx.us"/>

In early summer of 2013, Castro became engaged to his girlfriend, Anna Flores. The announcement was made by his twin brother, Julian, on his Facebook page.<ref>http://www.mysanantonio.com/news/local_news/article/Mayor-says-Congressman-Castro-engaged-4606058.php</ref>
