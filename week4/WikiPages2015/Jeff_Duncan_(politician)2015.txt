'''Jeffrey D. Duncan''' (born January 7, 1966)<ref>[http://news.yahoo.com/s/cq/20101103/pl_cq_politics/politics000003758169_2 112th Congress: Jeff Duncan, R-S.C. (3rd District)] ''[[CQ Politics]]''</ref> is an [[United States|American]] politician who has been the [[United States House of Representatives|United States Representative]] for {{ushr|SC|3}} since 2011. Duncan, a [[Republican Party (United States)|Republican]], previously served as a member of the [[South Carolina House of Representatives]].

== Early life, education, and business career==
In 1984, he graduated from [[Ware Shoals, South Carolina|Ware Shoals High School]]. He is a 1988 graduate of [[Clemson University]]; he was a [[Wide receiver (American football)|wide receiver]] on the [[Clemson Tigers football|football team]] under Head Coach [[Danny Ford]].

He is currently the President and CEO of J. Duncan Associates, a real estate marketing firm.

==South Carolina House of Representatives==

===Elections===
After redistricting, Duncan ran for South Carolina's 15th House District in 2002. In the Republican primary, he defeated David Tribble Jr, Clinton County Councilman, 56%-44%.<ref>[http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=42568 Our Campaigns - SC State House 015 - R Primary Race - Jun 11, 2002<!-- Bot generated title -->]</ref> He won the general election with 62% of the vote.<ref>[http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=40161 Our Campaigns - SC State House 015 Race - Nov 05, 2002<!-- Bot generated title -->]</ref> In 2004, he won re-election to a second term unopposed.<ref>[http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=41626 Our Campaigns - SC State House 015 Race - Nov 02, 2004<!-- Bot generated title -->]</ref> In 2006, he won re-election to a third term with 63% of the vote.<ref>[http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=168455 Our Campaigns - SC State House 015 Race - Nov 07, 2006<!-- Bot generated title -->]</ref> In 2008, he won re-election to a fourth term unopposed.<ref>[http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=451667 Our Campaigns - SC State House 015 Race - Nov 04, 2008<!-- Bot generated title -->]</ref> In 2010, he retired in order to run for the [[U.S. House of Representatives]]. David Tribble, Duncan's primary opponent in 2002, won Duncan's seat.

===Tenure===
Governor [[Mark Sanford]] called him a “Tax Payer Hero.” He was given the “Guardian of Small Business” award from the [[National Federation of Independent Businesses]], an A+ rating from the [[Club for Growth]], the Palmetto Leadership Award from the SC Policy Council, and “Legislator of the Year” from the SC Recreation and Parks Association and SC Wildlife Federation.<ref>http://www.scstatehouse.gov/members/bios/0506818121.html</ref>

===Committee assignments===
Duncan was named Chairman of the House Agriculture, Natural Resources and Environmental Affairs Committee in 2007. He was appointed by the Speaker of the House to be the Chair of the Education Finance Study Committee, Natural Gas Offshore Drilling Study Committee and was appointed to represent the state of South Carolina on the Southern States Energy Board.<ref>http://www.jeffduncan.com/site/about-2/</ref>

==U.S. House of Representatives==

===Elections===
;2010
{{see also|United States House of Representatives elections in South Carolina, 2010#District 3}}
He ran for [[South Carolina's 3rd congressional district]] when Republican incumbent U.S. Congressman [[J. Gresham Barrett]] ran for [[Governor of South Carolina]]. He was endorsed by the [[Club for Growth]]<ref>[http://www.clubforgrowth.org/endorsedcandidates/?subsec=0&id=702 Congressional Scorecard<!-- Bot generated title -->]</ref> and the [[National Right to Life Committee]].<ref>http://www.jeffduncan.com/site/2010/09/national-right-to-life-endorses-duncan/</ref> In the Republican primary, businessman Richard Cash ranked first with 25% but failed to reach the 50% threshold to win outright. Duncan ranked second in the six candidate field with 23%.<ref>[http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=523030 Our Campaigns - SC - District 03 - R Primary Race - Jun 08, 2010<!-- Bot generated title -->]</ref> In the run-off election, Duncan defeated Cash 51%-49%, a vote difference of 2,171. Duncan won five of the district's ten counties, and were mostly located in the southern part of the CD.<ref>[http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=657940 Our Campaigns - SC District 03 - R Runoff Race - Jun 22, 2010<!-- Bot generated title -->]</ref> He won the general election with 62% of the vote, 2% less than [[John McCain]]'s 64% vote in`2008. He won nine of the district's ten counties, losing just McCormick (52%-47%).  Duncan spent $935,503; [[Democratic Party (United States)|Democrat]] [[Jane Dyer]] spent $272,698.<ref name=Barone2012/><ref>{{cite web
  |accessdate=2013-09-08 |url=http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=511274
  |title=SC - District 03 Race |date=Nov 2, 2010 |work=Our Campaigns}}</ref>

;2012
{{see also|United States House of Representatives elections in South Carolina, 2012#District 3}}
Duncan successfully ran for re-election in the newly redrawn 3rd district, which excludes [[Aiken County, South Carolina|Aiken County]] (McCain won with 62%),<ref>[http://www.aikenstandard.com/story/0901-jeff-duncan U.S. Rep. Duncan promises to represent Aiken despite congressional redistricting | Aiken Standard<!-- Bot generated title -->]</ref> and includes two new counties: [[Newberry County, South Carolina|Newberry]] (McCain won with 58%) and [[Greenville County, South Carolina|Greenville]] (McCain won with 57%). Duncan won re-election to a second term with 67% of the vote.<ref>[http://www.politico.com/2012-election/map/#/House/2012/SC 2012 Election Results Map by State - Live Voting Updates - POLITICO.com<!-- Bot generated title -->]</ref>

===Tenure===
On the 1st of August, Duncan took part in the ´Chick-fil-a appreciation day´, using Twitter to publicise his purchase of large quantities of fried chicken produce in an apparent endorsement of CEO Dan Cathy´s anti-same-sex marriage sentiments.<ref>[http://www.guardian.co.uk/lifeandstyle/us-news-blog/2012/aug/01/chick-fil-a-appreciation-day Chick-fil-A appreciation day brings huge crowds to fast-food chain | Life and style | guardian.co.uk<!-- Bot generated title -->]</ref>

In January 2012, [[The New American]]‘s ''Freedom Index'' gave Duncan a perfect 100% score for his votes in 2011. He was just one of three members of congress to receive a perfect score, the other two being [[Walter B. Jones, Jr.|Walter B. Jones]] (R-NC) and [[Ron Paul]] (R-Texas).<ref>[http://www.fitsnews.com/2012/01/02/sc-freshmen-soar-on-freedom-index/ SC Freshmen Soar On "Freedom Index" - FITSNews<!-- Bot generated title -->]</ref>

On November 19, 2012, Duncan led a group of 97 Republican U.S. Representatives in writing a letter to President Obama, expressing opposition to the possible nomination of [[U.S. Ambassador to the United Nations|U.S. Ambassador to the U.N.]] [[Susan Rice]] to [[U.S. Secretary of State]].<ref>http://jeffduncan.house.gov/sites/jeffduncan.house.gov/files/Rep.%20Duncan%20Letter%20to%20President%20Obama%20on%20Ambassador%20Susan%20Rice%20(11.19.12).pdf</ref><ref>[http://www.politico.com/news/stories/1112/84072.html?hp=l4 97 House members oppose Susan Rice - Kevin Robillard - POLITICO.com<!-- Bot generated title -->]</ref>

===Legislation===

In February, 2011, Duncan introduced a resolution to create a new Committee on the Elimination of Nonessential Federal Programs in an attempt to reduce federal outlays.<ref>{{cite web|title=Jeff Duncan Full Biography|url=http://jeffduncan.house.gov/about-me/full-biography|website=U.S. Congressman Jeff Duncan|accessdate=8 December 2014}}</ref> 

On March 13, 2014, Duncan introduced the [[DHS Acquisition Accountability and Efficiency Act (H.R. 4228; 113th Congress)]], a bill that would direct the [[United States Department of Homeland Security]] (DHS) to improve the accountability, transparency, and efficiency of its major acquisition programs.<ref name=cbo4228>{{cite web|title=CBO - H.R. 4228|url=http://www.cbo.gov/publication/45385|publisher=Congressional Budget Office|accessdate=9 June 2014}}</ref> The bill would specify procedures for the department to follow if it fails to meet timelines, cost estimates, or other performance parameters for these programs.<ref name="cbo4228"/><ref name=FTjune10>{{cite news|last1=Medici|first1=Andy|title=House passes bill reforming DHS acquisition management|url=http://www.federaltimes.com/article/20140610/ACQ02/306100013/House-passes-bill-reforming-DHS-acquisition-management?odyssey=nav%7Chead|accessdate=10 June 2014|publisher=Federal Times|date=10 June 2014}}</ref> Duncan argued that "for years, DHS's purchases of major homeland security systems have been late, cost more, and done less than promised. This bill will save taxpayer dollars by forcing DHS to improve its management."<ref name="FTjune10"/>

Duncan is married to Melody, with whom he has three sons: Graham, John Philip, and Parker.
