'''Lois Jane Frankel''' (born May 17, 1948) is an [[United States|American]] [[politician]] and [[lawyer]] who has been the [[United States House of Representatives|United States Representative]] for [[Florida's 22nd congressional district]] since 2013. She is a member of the [[Democratic Party (United States)|Democratic Party]]. 

Frankel was member of the [[Florida House of Representatives]] for fourteen years, becoming the first woman to serve as [[Minority Leader]] of the State House in Florida's history.<ref name=brn>{{cite news|first=|last=|title=Frankel beats Daves for West Palm Mayor |url=http://news.google.com/newspapers?id=pSNUAAAAIBAJ&sjid=5Y0DAAAAIBAJ&pg=3032,6460473&dq=lois+frankel+mayor&hl=en |work=[[Associated Press]] |publisher=[[Boca Raton News]] |date=2003-03-12 |accessdate=2011-03-21}}</ref> She was elected [[Mayor]] of [[West Palm Beach, Florida]], in 2003,<ref name=brn/> serving two terms in office until leaving office in 2011 due to term limits. On November 6, 2012, Frankel was elected to the U.S. House of Representatives from Florida's 22nd congressional district; she was sworn in on January 3, 2013.

==Early life and education==
Frankel was born in 1948<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.sun-sentinel.com/topic/politics/lois-frankel-PEPLT007453.topic|title=Lois Frankel|publisher=South Florida Sun-Sentinel |accessdate=2011-03-23}}</ref> in [[New York City]] and received a [[bachelor's degree]] from [[Boston University]] in 1970. She earned a [[law degree]] from [[Georgetown University Law Center]] in 1973.<ref name=wpb>{{cite news|first=|last=|title=Lois J. Frankel, Mayor of the City of West Palm Beach |url=http://www.wpb.org/mayor/ |work=City of [[West Palm Beach]] government |publisher= |date= |accessdate=2011-03-21|archiveurl=http://www.webcitation.org/5xOBhIbfU|archivedate=2011-03-23}}</ref> Frankel moved to [[West Palm Beach, Florida]], in 1974.<ref name=brn/>

==Florida House of Representatives (1987-2003)==

===Elections===
In 1986, [[incumbent]] Democratic state representative Eleanor Weinstock of the 83rd district decided to run for a seat in the [[Florida Senate]]. Frankel ran for Weinstock's open seat in the Florida House, and defeated Republican nominee Gerald Adams 69%-31% in November 1986.<ref name="ourcampaigns.com">http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=226227</ref> In 1988, she won re-election to a second term unopposed;<ref>http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=226662</ref> in 1990, she again was unopposed.<ref>http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=217188</ref>

In November 1991, Frankel resigned as state representative, to run for Congress in 1992.<ref>{{cite news |url=http://www.browardpalmbeach.com/content/printVersion/132715/ |title=Florida House minority leader Lois Frankel is waging an impossible campaign for governor|author=Ashley Fantz |date=November 15, 2001 |work=Broward/Long Beach New Times}}</ref> Mimi McAndrews, a former aide of Frankel's, was elected to replace her. Frankel lost to fellow Democratic representative [[Alcee Hastings]] in the 1992 congressional primary.

In 1994 Frankel challenged, and after a bitter campaign, defeated McAndrews in the Democratic primary for her old State House seat.<ref>{{cite news |url=http://articles.sun-sentinel.com/1994-09-09/news/9409090129_1_lois-frankel-florida-house-rep-suzanne-jacobs |title=Frankel Scores Victory In Bitter House Race |date=September 9, 1994|author =Steve Nichol, Robin Fields, Jane Musgrave, and Glenn Singer |work=Sun Sentinel }}</ref> Frankel won the November 1994 general election with 55% of the vote.<ref>http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=219139</ref> In 1996, she won re-election to a fifth term with 68% of the vote.<ref>http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=216249</ref> In 1998, she won re-election to a sixth term with 64% of the vote.<ref>http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=35653</ref> In 2000, she won re-election to a seventh term with 63% of the vote.<ref>http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=32914</ref>

===Tenure===

During her first period as a state legislator, Frankel was State House Majority Whip.<ref>[http://www.myfloridahouse.gov/sections/Representatives/details.aspx?MemberId=3046&SessionId=48 Representative  Lois J. Frankel]</ref> While in office from 1995 to 2003, Frankel became the first female House [[Minority Leader]] in Florida's history and co-authored a change to Florida's already existing [[AIDS]] omnibus law originally passed in 1988.<ref name=brn/> She left office due to term limits in 2002 after serving fourteen years in the State House.<ref name=brn/><ref name=wpb/>

==1992 congressional election==
{{See also|United States House of Representatives elections, 1992}}
In 1992, she decided to retire from the State House to run for the newly created [[Florida's 23rd congressional district|Florida's 23rd district]]. In the Democratic primary, Frankel ranked first with 35% of the vote, but failed to reach the 50% threshold necessary to win outright and avoid a run-off election.<ref>http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=376676</ref> In the run-off election, former [[United States District Court for the Southern District of Florida|U.S. District Court Judge]] [[Alcee Hastings]] defeated Frankel 57%-43%.<ref name=brn/><ref>http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=376680</ref>

==2002 gubernatorial election==
{{See also|Florida gubernatorial election, 2002}}
In 2002, Frankel entered and then dropped out of the [[United States gubernatorial elections, 2002|2002 election for]] [[Governor of Florida]], in which [[Governor of Florida|Governor]] [[Jeb Bush]] won re-election.<ref name=brn/>

==Mayor of West Palm Beach (2003-2011)==
On March 11, 2003, Frankel defeated incumbent Democratic [[West Palm Beach]] Mayor Joel Daves in the mayoral election.<ref name=brn/> Frankel was endorsed in the race by former West Palm Beach Mayor Nancy Graham.<ref name=brn/> Frankel won with 56% of the vote to 38% for Daves.<ref name=brn/><ref>http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=6944</ref>  She was sworn into office on March 27, 2003, as the city's third [[strong mayor]].

In 2007, Frankel won re-election to a second term, defeating Al Zucaro 58%-42%.<ref>http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=218409</ref>

On March 31, 2011, due to term limits, Frankel left office as Mayor of West Palm Beach after two-terms. In the race to succeed her, West Palm Beach city commissioner [[Jeri Muoio]], was elected that month, with 51% of the vote, on a platform of business development and pension reform.<ref name=ss>{{cite news|first=Angel|last=Streeter|title=Jeri Muoio elected mayor of West Palm Beach |url=http://articles.sun-sentinel.com/2011-03-08/news/fl-election-west-palm-beach-mayor-20110308_1_jeri-muoio-molly-douglas-pension-fight |work=[[Sun Sentinel]] |publisher= |date=2003-03-08 |accessdate=2011-03-21}}</ref>

==U.S. House of Representatives==

===2012 congressional election===
{{main|United States House of Representatives elections in Florida, 2012#District 22}}
On March 21, 2011, Frankel announced that she would run in the newly redrawn [[Florida's 22nd congressional district]] in the upcoming [[United States House of Representatives elections, 2012|2012 House election]].<ref name=rollcall>{{cite news|first=Kyle|last=Trygstad|title=Lois Frankel Launches Bid Against Allen West |url=http://www.rollcall.com/news/Lois-Frankel-Allen-West-Florida-22-204261-1.html |work=[[Roll Call]] |publisher= |date=2003-03-21 |accessdate=2011-03-21}}</ref> On August 14 she defeated Democrat primary rival Kristin Jacobs, and moved forward to the November election against Republican [[Adam Hasner]].<ref>http://enight.dos.state.fl.us/FederalOffices/Representative/</ref>

Frankel was criticized for acceptance of $20,000.00 from Digital Domain Media Group for her election campaign five months after that company was awarded a downtown project which included incentives fron the city of West Palm Beach, and in response vowed to give the contribution to charity.<ref>http://www.palmbeachpost.com/news/news/national-govt-politics/frankel-to-give-digital-domains-20000-in-campaign-/nSBwy/</ref>

Frankel went on to win the general election on November 6, 2012, defeating Hasner 54.7% to 45.3%.<ref>{{cite web|title=Frankel beats out Hasner in race for U.S. Congress|url=http://india.nydailynews.com/politicsarticle/ac173c124c3d7862c440a6cc1a9edc7b/frankel-beats-out-hasner-in-race-for-us-congress|work=nydailynews.com|accessdate=8 November 2012}}</ref>
