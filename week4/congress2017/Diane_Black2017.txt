'''Diane Lynn Black'''<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.opensecrets.org/races/summary.php?cycle=2010&id=TN06 |title=Campaign contributions |work=OpenSecrets.org |accessdate=2010-07-12}}</ref> (''née'' Warren; born January 16, 1951) is an [[United States|American]] politician who has been the [[United States House of Representatives|U.S. Representative]] for {{ushr|TN|6}} since 2011. The district includes several suburban and rural areas east of [[Nashville]].  She is a member of the [[Republican Party (United States)|Republican Party]]. Previously she was a member of the [[Tennessee Senate]] for the 18th district, which encompasses [[Robertson County, Tennessee|Robertson County]] and part of [[Sumner County, Tennessee|Sumner County]]. She was floor leader of the State Senate Republican Caucus.

==Early life and education==
Born in [[Baltimore, Maryland]] to Joseph and Audrey Warren, Black graduated from Andover High School in [[Linthicum, Maryland]], in 1969. She became the first member of her family to earn a college degree <ref>{{cite web|url=http://thehill.com/capital-living/new-member-of-the-week/240911-rep-blacks-healthcare-prescription-doesnt-include-the-government|title=Rep. Black’s healthcare prescription doesn’t include the government|author=Sterling C. Beard|work=TheHill}}</ref> after graduating from [[Anne Arundel Community College]] in [[Arnold, Maryland]] with an [[associate's degree]] in [[nursing]].  She received a [[bachelor's degree]] in nursing in 1991 from [[Belmont University]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.tennessean.com/article/20121003/NEWS/310020091/Rep-Diane-Black-won-t-end-push-more-spending-cuts?nclick_check=1|title=Rep. Diane Black won't end push for more spending cuts|work=The Tennessean}}</ref><ref name="wapobio">{{cite news |url= |title=Diane Black (R-Tenn.) |accessdate=May 23, 2012 | work=The Washington Post |first1=Nia-Malika |last1=Henderson |first2=Jackie |last2=Kucinich}}</ref>

==Career==
Black worked as a [[registered nurse]] until she ran for the [[Tennessee House of Representatives]] in 1998. Later she served as an educator at [[Volunteer State Community College]]<ref name="About Diane"/><ref>[http://health.state.tn.us/licensure/Practitioner.aspx?ProfessionCode=5005&LicenseNumber=13146&FileNumber=13146 Practitioner Profile Data – Tennessee Department of Health]</ref> in [[Gallatin, Tennessee]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.dianeblack.org/ContentPage.aspx?WebPageId=23954&GroupId=825 |title=Biography |work=Diane Black for State Senate |accessdate=2010-07-12}}</ref>

=== Tennessee legislature ===
Before becoming a [[Tennessee Senate|state senator]] in 2004, she had previously served as a [[Tennessee House of Representatives|state representative]] for six years from 1998. Black was the Assistant Floor Leader of the Senate Republican Caucus, a member of the Senate Government Operations Committee, and the Vice-Chair of the Senate General Welfare, Health and Human Resources Committee. She was elected the Tennessee Senate Republican [[Caucus]] Chairman in 2006.<ref name="About Diane">{{cite web |url=http://www.votedianeblack.com/index.php/about-diane.html |title=About Diane |work=Diane Black for Congress |accessdate=2010-07-12}}</ref>

In May 2009, Black's legislative aide forwarded an email depicting a collage of United States Presidents. President [[Barack Obama]]'s section of the collage was represented by a black square with two eyeballs.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.politicsdaily.com/2009/06/16/the-gops-minority-outreach/|title=The GOP's Minority Outreach?|work=Politics Daily}}</ref>  Black's reprimand of her aide was criticized as too lenient by two political blogs<ref>{{cite web|url=http://newscoma.com/2009/06/15/racist-and-ridiculous/|title=Racist And Ridiculous|work=newscoma.com}}</ref> and Tennessee Democratic Party Chair [[Chip Forrester]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://tndp.org/blog/2009/06/15/forrester-demands-sen-diane-black-fire-staffer-who-sent-racist-email/|title=Forrester Demands Sen. Diane Black Fire Staffer Who Sent Racist Email|work=TNDP News}}</ref>  Black said the email did not represent her views and that the reprimand of her aide was in keeping with the legislator's human resource policy for email guideline violations.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://blogs.tennessean.com/politics/2009/black-says-she-followed-hr-rules-on-senate-staffer-email/|title=Black says she followed HR rules on Senate staffer email – In Session|work=tennessean.com}}</ref>

===U.S. House of Representatives===
Black is one of three female U.S. Representatives in Congress who self-identifies as a "congressman"; the others are Republicans [[Marsha Blackburn]] of Tennessee and [[Cynthia Lummis]] of Wyoming.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://editions.lib.umn.edu/smartpolitics/2013/06/13/meet-the-three-house-women-who/ |title= Meet the Three House Women Who Go by "Congressman" |work=Smart Politics |first=Eric |last=Ostermeier |date= June 13, 2013}}</ref>

====2010 election====
In December, 2009, she became a candidate for [[Tennessee's 6th congressional district]] to succeed [[Bart Gordon]], who did not run for reelection.<ref>{{cite news |url=http://www.newschannel5.com/global/story.asp?s=11696944 |title=Diane Black Joins Race To Succeed Gordon In Congress |date=December 18, 2009 |agency=Associated Press |work=WTVF |accessdate=2010-07-12}}</ref> Her biggest competition in the Republican primary came from former [[Rutherford County, Tennessee|Rutherford County]] GOP chairwoman [[Lou Ann Zelenik]] and State Senator [[Jim Tracy (politician)|Jim Tracy]]. On August 5, 2010, Black won the Republican primary with 31% of the vote, over Zelenik and Tracy, who earned 30% each.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://elections.nytimes.com/2010/house/TENNESSEE/6 |title=Tennessee 6th District Race Profile – Election 2010 |work=The New York Times |accessdate=2010-08-07 |deadurl=yes |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20100620100926/http://elections.nytimes.com/2010/house/tennessee/6 |archivedate=June 20, 2010 }}</ref> [[Brett Carter (politician)|Brett Carter]] won the [[Democratic Party (United States)|Democratic]] nomination after well-known elected officials declined the candidacy, leading most Democratic observers to write off the seat as a Republican pickup.  [[CQ Politics]] rating this race as "Safe Republican".<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.cqpolitics.com/wmspage.cfm?docID=news-000003635279 |date=March 31, 2010 |title=Gordon’s Tennessee Seat All But Gone for Democrats |first=John |last=McArdle |work=CQ Politics |accessdate=2010-07-23}}</ref>  In the November election, Black won with 67 percent of the vote.{{citation needed|date=March 2015}}

====2012 to present====
In the 2012 general election, Black was a surrogate for Republican Presidential candidate Mitt Romney.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://abcnews.go.com/blogs/politics/2012/10/romney-may-have-edge-in-battle-of-the-spinners/|title=Romney May Have Edge in Battle of The Spinners|author=ABC News|work=ABC News Blogs}}</ref> Black made appearances for Romney in Pennsylvania,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://readingeagle.com/article.aspx?id=419890|title=Romney surrogate at GOP headquarters|work=readingeagle.com}}</ref> Wisconsin,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.knoxnews.com/news/2012/sep/22/tennesseans-head-elsewhere-for-presidential/?print=1|title=Tennesseans head elsewhere for presidential campaigning|author=Tom Humphrey|work=KNS}}</ref> and in various cable news interviews.<ref>{{YouTube|XWXjIfBwfBY}}</ref>  Black has been a member of the following committees: [[United States House Committee on the Budget|Committee on the Budget]], [[United States House Committee on Ways and Means|Committee on Ways and Means]], [[United States House Ways and Means Subcommittee on Human Resources|Subcommittee on Human Resources]], [[Tea Party Caucus]] and the [[Republican Study Committee]].

In October 2013, Black introduced the [[Student and Family Tax Simplification Act (H.R. 3393; 113th Congress)]], a bill that would amend the [[Internal Revenue Code]] to consolidate several different education tax incentives into an expanded [[American Opportunity Tax Credit]].<ref name=AccountingTodayCohn>{{cite news|last1=Cohn|first1=Michael|title=House Passes Student Tax Credit Simplification Bill|url=http://www.accountingtoday.com/news/government-news/house-passes-student-tax-credit-simplification-bill-71453-1.html|accessdate=25 July 2014|publisher=Accounting Today|date=24 July 2014}}</ref><ref name=3393sum>{{cite web|title=H.R. 3393 – Summary|url=https://beta.congress.gov/bill/113th-congress/house-bill/3393|publisher=United States Congress|accessdate=25 July 2014}}</ref> The American Opportunity Tax Credit, under this legislation, would provide a maximum credit of $2,500.<ref name=MarcosTaxCredit>{{cite news|last1=Marcos|first1=Cristina|title=House passess tax credit for college expenses|url=http://thehill.com/blogs/floor-action/house/213291-house-passes-tax-credit-for-college-expenses|accessdate=25 July 2014|publisher=The Hill|date=24 July 2014}}</ref>

Black has received endorsements from Governor Sarah Palin,<ref>[http://www.sarahpac.com/candidates?page=7] {{webarchive |url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131031121348/http://www.sarahpac.com/candidates?page=7 |date=October 31, 2013 }}</ref> The National Federation of Independent Business (NFIB),<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.nfib.com/article/?cmsid=60648|title=NFIB Endorses Diane Black for Congress|work=National Federation of Independent Business}}</ref> Governor Mitt Romney,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://blogs.tennessean.com/politics/2010/mitt-romney-endorses-diane-black/|title=Mitt Romney endorses Diane Black – In Session|work=tennessean.com}}</ref> The National Rifle Association (NRA) <ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.nba.com/|title=NBA.com|work=nba.com}}</ref> Congresswoman Michele Bachmann,<ref name="realclearpolitics.com">{{cite web|url=http://www.realclearpolitics.com/news/ap/politics/2012/Jul/29/black__zelenik_battle_for_6th_district_again.html|title=RealClearPolitics – Politics – Jul 29, 2012 – Black, Zelenik battle for 6th District again|work=realclearpolitics.com}}</ref> former Congressman Allen West,<ref name="realclearpolitics.com"/> Tennessee Right to Life, {{citation needed|date=March 2015}} Susan B. Anthony List,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.sba-list.org/endorsed-candidates|title=2014 Endorsed Candidates|work=sba-list.org}}</ref> and The U.S. Chamber of Commerce.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://blogs.tennessean.com/politics/2012/diane-black-announces-chamber-endorsement/|title=Diane Black announces Chamber endorsement – In Session|work=tennessean.com}}</ref>

In October 2015, Black was named to serve on the [[United States House Committee on Energy and Commerce Select Investigative Panel on Planned Parenthood|Select Investigative Panel on Planned Parenthood]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.washingtonpost.com/news/powerpost/wp/2015/10/23/boehners-next-select-committee-focusing-on-planned-parenthood-to-be-led-by-marsha-blackburn/|title=Boehner’s next select committee, focusing on Planned Parenthood, to be led by Marsha Blackburn|author=Paul Kane|date=October 23, 2015|work=Washington Post|accessdate=October 23, 2015}}</ref>

==Personal life==
Black's net worth is "almost $29 million" which includes $8.5 million in real estate and her husband's stake in Aegis Sciences Corporation.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.rollcall.com/news/-202702-1.html|title=New Members Are Among Richest in Congress|work=Roll Call}}</ref>
