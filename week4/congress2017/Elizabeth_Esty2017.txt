'''Elizabeth Henderson Esty''' (born August 25, 1959) is the [[United States House of Representatives|U.S. Representative]] for {{ushr|CT|5}}, having been elected on November 6, 2012 and reelected on November 4, 2014.  She is a member of the [[Democratic Party (United States)|Democratic Party]]. Previously she was a member of the [[Connecticut House of Representatives]], representing the 103rd Assembly District, which consisted of [[Cheshire, Connecticut|Cheshire]] and parts of [[Hamden, Connecticut|Hamden]] and [[Wallingford, Connecticut|Wallingford]]. She also served two terms on the Cheshire Town Council. The 5th congressional district spans central and northwest Connecticut, including Waterbury, Danbury, New Britain, Meriden, Torrington, Litchfield County, the Farmington Valley, Newtown, and Esty's hometown of Cheshire.

Esty defeated two challengers in the August 14, 2012, Democratic primary to become the Democratic nominee in [[Connecticut's 5th congressional district]], and on November 6, 2012, she defeated challenger [[Andrew Roraback]]. Esty defeated Mark Greenberg on November 4, 2014, in the state's most competitive district to win her first reelection.<ref>{{Cite web|title = Esty beats Greenberg in Key 5th District|url = http://ctmirror.org/2014/11/04/esty-beats-greenberg-in-5th-district/|website = The CT Mirror|accessdate = 2015-11-07|first = Arielle Levin|last = Becker|first2 = Melissa|last2 = Collins}}</ref>

==Early life, education, and career==
Elizabeth Henderson was born in 1959 in [[Oak Park, Illinois]].<ref>[http://www.rollcall.com/new-members/elizabeth-esty-house-d-conn-5.html]</ref> Her father worked as an engineer in a construction company and the family moved numerous times during her childhood. She was raised in [[Minnesota]] and graduated from [[Winona Senior High School]]. She earned an A.B. from [[Harvard College]] in 1981 and a J.D. from [[Yale Law School]] in 1985. She also studied International Relations at [[L'Institut d'études politiques]] in Paris for a year on a [[Rotary Scholarship]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://ct5thdistrict.registercitizen.com/elizabeth-esty/|title=Redlands Daily Facts Blogs|publisher=}}</ref> Esty volunteered for [[Planned Parenthood]] in college.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://ct5thdistrict.registercitizen.com/elizabeth-esty/4/|title=Redlands Daily Facts Blogs|publisher=}}</ref>

Esty has been a law clerk for a federal judge, a Supreme Court lawyer at [[Sidley Austin LLP]] in Washington, DC, and Professor at [[American University]]. She is a member of the Cheshire Public Library Board, Legal Advisor to the [[League of Women Voters|Connecticut League of Women Voters Consensus Project]], Chair of the Board of Trustees for the First Congregational Church of Cheshire, lay member of the Committee on Ministry New Haven Association of the [[United Church of Christ]], and a member of the Parent-Teacher Association.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://votesmart.org/candidate/biography/72826|title=Elizabeth Esty's Biography - The Voter's Self Defense System - Vote Smart|work=Project Vote Smart}}</ref>

==Connecticut House of Representatives==

===Elections===
In 2008, she challenged Republican State Representative [[Al Adinolfi]] of Connecticut's 103rd Assembly District. She defeated him 51%-49%.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=455786|title=Our Campaigns - CT State House 103 Race - Nov 04, 2008|publisher=}}</ref> In 2010, Adinolfi challenged her in a rematch and defeated her 51%-49%.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=664825|title=Our Campaigns - CT State House 103 Race - Nov 02, 2010|publisher=}}</ref>

===Tenure===
She opposed Governor [[Jodi Rell]]'s proposal to eliminate the state’s Office of Consumer Counsel. She cut her own pay by 10%. When [[aerospace]] manufacturer [[Pratt & Whitney]] closed its Cheshire plant in 2009, Esty was among several politicians who fought to get workers new job placements or early retirement packages. She opposes the [[Capital punishment in Connecticut|death penalty]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://ct5thdistrict.registercitizen.com/elizabeth-esty/8/|title=Redlands Daily Facts Blogs|publisher=}}</ref>

==U.S. House of Representatives==

===2012 election===
{{Main article|United States House of Representatives elections in Connecticut, 2012#District 5}}
In 2012, Democratic U.S. Congressman [[Chris Murphy (Connecticut politician)|Chris Murphy]] of [[Connecticut's 5th congressional district]] decided to retire in order to run for the U.S. Senate. Esty decided to run. She was endorsed by [[EMILY's List]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://atr.rollcall.com/connecticut-elizabeth-esty-wins-5th-district-democratic-nomination/|title=Connecticut: Elizabeth Esty Wins 5th District Democratic Nomination|author=AbLivingston|date=15 August 2012|work=Roll Call}}</ref> She also won the newspaper endorsements from the ''[[New York Times]]'', ''[[Hartford Courant]]'' and the ''[[Torrington, Connecticut|Torrington Register-Citizen]]''. She defeated Daniel Roberti and State House Speaker [[Christopher G. Donovan|Chris Donovan]] in the primary.

In the November 6 [[general election]], Esty defeated State Senator [[Andrew Roraback]] to become the district's next representative.<ref>{{cite web|last=Christoffersen|first=John|title=Democrat Esty wins Conn.'s 5th District seat|url=http://www.sfgate.com/news/article/Democrat-Esty-wins-Conn-s-5th-District-seat-4015182.php|work=sfgate.com|accessdate=8 November 2012}}</ref>  Esty won despite the opposition of New York Mayor [[Michael Bloomberg]] whose PAC channeled over a million dollars to her opponent.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://politicker.com/2012/11/bloombergs-super-pac-narrowly-misses-most-congressional-targets/|title=Bloomberg’s Super PAC Narrowly Misses Most Congressional Targets|author=Colin Campbell|date=7 November 2012|work=Observer}}</ref>

===Tenure ===
Esty introduced the Collinsville Renewable Energy Promotion Act in February 2013 allow the town of [[Canton, Connecticut]] to take over two lapsed licenses from the [[Federal Energy Regulatory Commission|Federal Energy Regulatory Commission (FERC)]] in order to refurbish two old local dams.<ref>{{cite news|title=Elizabeth Esty's first bill, reintroduced Collinsville dams legislation, passes House|url=http://www.foothillsmediagroup.com/articles/2013/02/18/canton/news/doc51228c29d578e656011125.txt|accessdate=12 April 2013|newspaper=The Avon News|date=February 18, 2013}}</ref>  The dams would be used to produce [[Hydroelectricity|hydroelectric power]].<ref name=316sum>{{cite web|title=H.R 316 - 113th Congress|url=http://beta.congress.gov/bill/113th-congress/house-bill/316|publisher=United States Congress|accessdate=12 April 2013}}</ref>

In May 2013, Esty voted against repeal of the [[Patient Protection and Affordable Care Act]].  She then subsequently voted to delay the individual and business mandates in the law by one year.;<ref>{{cite web |title=Esty Statement on Bills to Delay ACA Penalties for One Year |url=http://www.stamfordplus.com/stm/information/nws1/publish/News_1/Esty-Statement-on-Bills-to-Delay-ACA-Penalties-for-One-Year20533.shtml |publisher=StamfordPlus.com |accessdate=7 November 2013}}</ref>

Esty co-authored<ref>{{Cite web|title = Esty bill on tech education passes House|url = http://blog.ctnews.com/politics/2015/02/25/esty-bill-on-tech-education-passes-house/|website = CT Politics|accessdate = 2015-11-07|language = en-US}}</ref> the [[STEM Education Act of 2014 (H.R. 5031; 113th Congress)|STEM Education Act (H.R. 5031; 113th Congress)]] [https://www.congress.gov/bill/114th-congress/house-bill/1020?q=%7B%22search%22%3A%5B%22stem+education%22%5D%7D&resultIndex=1 (H.R. 1020; 114th Congress)]. This bill strengthens science, technology, engineering and math (STEM) education efforts and expands the definition of STEM to include computer science.<ref>{{Cite web|title = Smith’s STEM Education Act Signed Into Law|url = https://science.house.gov/news/press-releases/smith-s-stem-education-act-signed-law|website = Committee on Science, Space, and Technology|accessdate = 2015-11-07}}</ref> She said that "STEM education is critical to preparing our students for high-demand careers in [[engineering]], [[manufacturing]], and [[information technology]]."<ref name=Callahan1>{{cite news|last1=Callahan|first1=Molly|title=House passes Rep. Esty's STEM Education Act|url=http://www.myrecordjournal.com/meriden/meridennews/5036761-129/house-passes-rep-estys-stem-education-act.html|accessdate=16 July 2014|publisher=My Record Journal|date=15 July 2014}}</ref> According to Esty, she frequently hears from "manufactures and small business owners that it's increasingly difficult to find workers with the right skill sets to fill the jobs in demand." The House easily approved this bill with a vote of 412-8<ref>{{Cite web|title = Esty wins on STEM bill|url = http://ctmirror.org/2015/02/25/esty-wins-on-stem-bill/|website = The CT Mirror|accessdate = 2015-11-07|first = Ana|last = Radelat}}</ref> and it was signed into law in October 2015.<ref>{{Cite web|title = Plainville High STEM best in state, Esty says|url = http://www.centralctcommunications.com/bristolpress/article_ae0498f4-7c4d-11e5-8cad-03fc987153c3.html|website = Central Connecticut Communications|accessdate = 2015-11-07}}</ref>

==Personal life==
[[File:Esty-official-portrait-114.jpg|thumb|Rep. Elizabeth H. Esty's official portrait]]
Elizabeth Henderson married [[Daniel C. Esty]] in 1984. Several years later, their first child, Sarah, was born while they were working in [[Washington, D.C.]] Elizabeth became a stay-at-home mother. The family moved to Connecticut in 1994 when Dan Esty started the environmental law and policy program at Yale.,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://ct5thdistrict.registercitizen.com/elizabeth-esty/5/|title=Redlands Daily Facts Blogs|publisher=}}</ref> before accepting appointment as Commissioner of the [[Connecticut Department of Energy and Environmental Protection]] from Governor [[Dannel P. Malloy]] in March 2011.<ref>{{cite web|author=Department of Environmental Protection |url=http://www.ct.gov/dep/cwp/view.asp?A=4013&Q=474242&depNav_GID=1511 |title=DEP: Governor Malloy Selects Daniel Esty to Head Newly Fused Department of Energy & Environmental Protection |publisher=Ct.gov |date=2011-02-10 |accessdate=2012-06-07}}</ref>
