'''Thomas Anthony Marino''' (born August 13, 1952) is an [[United States|American]] politician and attorney. He is currently serving his third term as the [[United States House of Representatives|U.S. Representative]] for {{ushr|PA|10}}. He is a member of the [[Republican Party (United States)|Republican Party]].

==Early life and education==
Marino was born and raised in [[Lycoming County, Pennsylvania]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://dailyitem.com/0100_news/x1324602326/Marino-wants-less-government-lower-taxes|title=Marino wants less government, lower taxes|publisher=[[The Daily Item (Sunbury)|The Daily Item]]|date=2010-10-12|accessdate=2014-02-04}}</ref> After graduating from High School, Marino went to work in the factories of Central Pennsylvania. At 30 years old, Marino enrolled in the former Williamsport Area Community College. Marino would then transfer to Lycoming College, where he graduated ''magna cum laude'', before completing his law degree at Dickinson School of Law. He received his undergraduate degree from [[Lycoming College]] and his [[juris doctor]] from [[Dickinson School of Law]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://bioguide.congress.gov/scripts/biodisplay.pl?index=M001179|title=MARINO, Thomas A., (1952 – )|publisher=[[Biographical Directory of the United States Congress]]|accessdate=November 6, 2012}}</ref>

==Law career==
Marino served as a Lycoming County District Attorney from 1992–2002.  In 2002, Tom was appointed the United States Attorney for the Middle District of Pennsylvania by President George W. Bush.

==U.S. House of Representatives==

=== District ===
The district, located in central and northeastern Pennsylvania, includes Bradford County, Juniata County, Lycoming County, Mifflin County, Pike County, Snyder County, Sullivan County, Susquehanna County, Union County, Wayne County, and portions of Perry County, Tioga County, Lackawanna County, Monroe County, and Northumberland County.

===Political Positions===
Marino is one of the most conservative members of the Pennsylvania delegation. He ranked third among PA members in [[Americans for Prosperity]]’s scorecard (70%) and fifth in [[Club for Growth]]'s scorecard (63%).<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.politicspa.com/exclusive-bradford-commissioner-might-primary-marino/46434/|title=Exclusive: Bradford Commissioner Might Primary Marino|date=2013-03-05|publisher=PoliticsPA|accessdate=2014-02-04}}</ref>
* He's the co-chair of the Congressional Caucus on Foster Youth <ref>{{cite web|title=Congressional Caucus on Foster Youth|url=http://fosteryouthcaucus-karenbass.house.gov/|website=http://fosteryouthcaucus-karenbass.house.gov/|accessdate=12 November 2014}}</ref> and co-chair of the Cystic Fibrosis Caucus.<ref>{{cite web|title=Cystic Fibrosis Caucus|url=http://www.cff.org/GetInvolved/Advocate/CFCaucus//|website=http://www.cff.org/GetInvolved/Advocate/CFCaucus/|accessdate=12 November 2014}}</ref>

In 2011, Rep. Marino became a co-sponsor of Bill H.R.3261 otherwise known as the [[Stop Online Piracy Act]].<ref name="govtrack">[http://www.govtrack.us/congress/bill.xpd?bill=h112-3261 Bill H.R.3261]</ref>

Marino supports the [[Death penalty]]. He believes that the mentally ill and criminals should not be able to obtain [[Firearms law|guns]].<ref>{{cite web|last=Brady|first=Chris|url=http://www.standard-journal.com/news/local/article_23e4f56c-960c-11e2-99b7-001a4bcf887a.html|title=Marino: Keep guns away from mentally ill, felons|publisher=standard-journal.com|date=2013-03-26|accessdate=2014-02-04}}</ref>

In July 2012, Marino introduced a bill to help fund local and state governments, about $800 million per year, to sustain various law enforcement activities such as prosecution, prevention, education, training, and corrections called the "[[Edward Byrne (police officer)|Edward Byrne]] Memorial Justice Assistance Grant Program Reauthorization Act of 2012". Marino said, "Local law enforcement agencies and officials need nothing less than our full support in combating crime on every level".<ref>{{cite web|url=http://marino.house.gov/press-release/house-passes-byrne-jag-program|title=House Passes Byrne-JAG Program &#124; Congressman Thomas Marino|publisher=Marino.house.gov|date=2012-08-02|accessdate=2014-02-04}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://marino.house.gov/press-release/rep-calls-bureau-prisons-make-improvements|title=Rep. Calls on Bureau of Prisons to Make Improvements &#124; Congressman Thomas Marino|publisher=Marino.house.gov|date=2013-03-15|accessdate=2014-02-04}}</ref>

In July 2013, Marino voted "NO" to Rep. [[Justin Amash]]'s amendment #413 to H.R. 2397 "To end authority for the blanket collection of records under the [[Patriot Act]] and bar the NSA and other agencies from using Section 215 of the Patriot Act to collect records, including telephone call records, that pertain to persons who are not subject to an investigation under Section 215"<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.govtrack.us/congress/votes/113-2013/h412|title=H.Amdt. 413 (Amash) to H.R. 2397: Amendment sought to end authority for the blanket collection of records under the Patriot ...|publisher=GovTrack.us|date=2013-07-24|accessdate=2014-02-04}}</ref> which Rep. [[Jim Sensenbrenner]], an author of the Patriot Act, considers un-American.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.huffingtonpost.com/2013/06/06/jim-sensenbrenner-nsa_n_3397440.html|title=Jim Sensenbrenner, Patriot Act Author, Slams 'Un-American' NSA Verizon Phone Records Grab|publisher=[[HuffPo]]|date=|accessdate=2014-02-04}}</ref>

===Legislation===
As of May 2016, Tom Marino has sponsored 40 bills, none of which have become law.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.congress.gov/member/tom-marino/M001179?pageSize=250|title=Tom Marino|last=Marino|first=Tom|website=www.congress.gov|access-date=2016-05-08}}</ref>

Marino introduced H.J.Res. 40 on March 26, 2015. This bill proposes an amendment to the U.S. Constitution to require that each law enacted by Congress be limited to only one subject and that the subject be clearly and descriptively expressed in the title of the law.

Marino introduced the Animal Fighting Spectator Prohibition Act of 2013. After multiple committee considerations, the bill was in part added as an amendment in the ''Federal Agriculture Reform and Risk Management Act (FARRM Act),'' which was signed into law by President Obama in February 2014.<ref name="marinoforcongress">{{cite news|title=Marino's animal fighting bill signed into law|url=http://marino.house.gov/press-release/marinos-animal-fighting-bill-signed-law|accessdate=12 November 2014}}</ref>

Marino introduced the [[Responsibly And Professionally Invigorating Development Act of 2013 (H.R. 2641; 113th Congress)|Responsibly And Professionally Invigorating Development Act of 2013]] into the House.<ref name="2641sum">{{cite web|title=H.R. 2641 – Summary|url=http://beta.congress.gov/bill/113th-congress/house-bill/2641|publisher=United States Congress|accessdate=6 March 2014}}</ref> Marino introduced this same bill in the 114th Congress. The bill aims to expedite the review process required by the [[National Environmental Policy Act]] (NEPA) for construction projects that are partly or fully financed with federal funds or require permits or approvals from federal regulatory agencies.<ref name="2641cbo">{{cite web|title=H.R. 2641 – CBO|url=http://www.cbo.gov/publication/44534|publisher=Congressional Budget Office|accessdate=6 March 2014}}</ref>

== Resignation from U.S Attorney's Office ==
In 2007, Marino resigned from office of U.S. Attorney for the Middle District of Pennsylvania.  Anonymous sources claimed that Marino resigned his position while under review by the Department of Justice<ref>{{cite news|last1=Birkbeck|first1=Matt|title=Source: Marino resigned while under review|url=http://articles.mcall.com/2010-10-01/news/mc-marino-investigation-20101001_1_denaples-investigations-marino-democrat-chris-carney|accessdate=1 October 2010|publisher=The Morning Call}}</ref>  In 2007, the current US Attorney for the Middle District of PA, Peter Smith confirmed that neither Marino, nor his office, were ever under review or investigation<ref name="Marino calls accusations 'lies'">{{cite news|last1=Scarcella|first1=Francis|title=Marino calls accusations 'lies'|url=http://www.dailyitem.com/news/marino-calls-accusations-lies/article_a6f77f96-6618-11e4-b6f2-ef69c0a20f69.html|accessdate=6 November 2014|publisher=Sunbury Daily Item}}</ref>  "At no time was Marino under investigation by the Justice Department,” said Smith<ref name="Marino calls accusations 'lies'"/>  After his resignation in 2007, Marino accepted a position as an in-house attorney for DeNaples Management.

Marino resides outside [[Williamsport, Pennsylvania|Williamsport]], Pennsylvania, with his wife, Edie, and his two children.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.marinoforuscongress.com/index.php?option=com_content&view=article&id=22&Itemid=28|title=Marino for US Congress|deadurl=yes|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20101130163334/http://www.marinoforuscongress.com/index.php?option=com_content&view=article&id=22&Itemid=28|archivedate=November 30, 2010}}</ref>
