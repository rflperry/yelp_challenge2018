{{Nihongo|'''Doris Okada Matsui'''|松井ドリス岡田|Matsui Dorisu Okada}} (born September 25, 1944) is the [[United States House of Representatives|U.S. Representative]] for {{ushr|CA|6}}. She is a member of the [[Democratic Party (United States)|Democratic Party]]. The district, numbered as the 5th District from 2005 to 2013 consists of the city of Sacramento and the surrounding area. Following the death on January 1, 2005 of her husband, [[Bob Matsui]], she was elected as his replacement in a special election on March 8, 2005, and took the oath on March 10, 2005.

==Early life and career==

Matsui was born in the [[Poston War Relocation Center]] [[Japanese American internment|internment camp]] in [[Poston, Arizona|Poston]], [[Arizona]] and grew up in [[Dinuba, California|Dinuba]], in California's [[Central Valley (California)|Central Valley]]. While attending the [[University of California, Berkeley]], where she earned a [[Bachelor of Arts|B.A.]] in [[psychology]], she met her husband. They had one child, Brian.

Matsui was a housewife and socialite and was active in the group "Lawyers' Wives", now called the Legal Auxiliary of Sacramento, while her husband was a local attorney and served on the [[Sacramento, California|Sacramento]] city council before his election to congress in 1979. The Matsuis moved to Washington DC shortly thereafter where they raised their son Brian.

Doris Matsui was a volunteer on the Clinton for President campaign. When he was elected, Matsui served on his transition team. Following the inauguration, she was appointed deputy special assistant to the president and deputy director of public liaison, working under [[Alexis Herman]]. One of her duties was to work with the Asian American community. {{Citation needed|date=April 2010}} The President appointed her to the board of the [[Woodrow Wilson International Center for Scholars]] in September 2000.

She served in the [[White House]] from 1993 to 1998. Later she became a lobbyist in Washington where she represented corporate clients until 2005 when she returned to California to run for Congress against a field of local Democrats.

==U.S. House of Representatives==

===Tenure===
Matsui's husband, Congressman Bob Matsui, died from complications of [[myelodysplastic syndrome]] on {{nowrap|January 1, 2005}}. On January 9, 2005, the day after his funeral, Matsui told supporters she was running for his open seat. In the special election she garnered 68% of the vote. Press reports said that Matsui won the election before the polls opened as most votes in the election were [[absentee ballot]]s, which she won overwhelmingly.{{citation needed|reason=Mentions "press reports" but does not link to them|date=May 2016}} Congresswoman Matsui was elected to a full term in 2006 and has been reelected four more times without serious difficulty. The 6th is the most Democratic district in interior California; it and its predecessors have been in Democratic hands without interruption since 1953.

In her inaugural speech, she spoke of the many people who encouraged her to run and her family. She pledged to continue the work of her husband, especially regarding [[flood control]] projects in Sacramento, the main city in the district.

Matsui is a member of the [[House Committee on Energy and Commerce]]. As a member, she has been focused on making the Sacramento area a hub clean technology.<ref name="Matsui Arena">{{cite web|title=Rep. Doris Matsui|url=http://www.politico.com/arena/bio/rep_doris_matsui_.html|work=The Arena|publisher=Politico|accessdate=August 17, 2012}}</ref>

In 2007, Speaker [[Nancy Pelosi]] appointed Matsui to the [[Smithsonian Institution]]'s Board of Regents, where she served until 2011. Matsui was one of only three House Members to sit on the board. In 2007, Matsui was instrumental in developing an overhaul of the oversight and accountability practices of the Smithsonian.

Matsui served as convention [[Parliamentarian (consultant)|parliamentarian]] of the [[2008 Democratic National Convention]] in [[Denver, Colorado|Denver]], [[Colorado]].

She supports full disclosure of campaign finances for politicians and desires more fair elections, as well as extending election periods to grab more of the population.<ref name=issues/> She has also expressed support for whistle-blowers' protection to promote transparency in both business and government.<ref name=issues/>

====Budget====
Matsui has been a supporter of [[earmark (politics)|earmarks]], which have been a controversial topic. She defended her position, saying "members of Congress know their districts pretty well and know what they need."<ref name="Earmark ban">{{cite news|last=Kindy|first=Kimberly|title=Despite earmark ban, lawmakers try to give money to hundreds of pet projects|url=http://www.washingtonpost.com/politics/after-earmark-ban-lawmakers-try-to-direct-money-to-hundreds-of-pet-projects/2011/11/29/gIQA2L2WAO_story.html|accessdate=August 17, 2012|newspaper=The Washington Post|date=November 19, 2011}}</ref>

Matsui has supported raising the debt limit by 2.4 trillion dollars for federal spending<ref name=issues/> and has supported numerous bailouts and federal funds injections.<ref name=issues/> In 2008 she supported a 15 billion dollar bailout for GM and another 60 billion dollar stimulus in the hopes to stimulate the US economy. She supported the initial TARP bailout funds and the 825 billion dollar continuation of 2009 in the hopes of avoiding recession.<ref name=issues/> She later supported an additional 198 billion dollar stimulus package.<ref name=issues/> She supports expanding agencies to meet the needs of citizens, rather than cutting spending and reform.<ref name=issues/>

Matsui voted to raise Senator's salary in 2009.<ref name=issues/> She also voted to raise the minimum wage in 2007 and extend unemployment benefits from 39 to 59 weeks.<ref name=issues/>

====Taxes====
Matsui supports a progressive tax system and seeks to shut down off-shore loopholes for business.<ref name=issues/> She voted against continuing capital gains and dividend tax breaks.<ref name=issues/> She supports extending AMT exemptions which benefit higher-income taxpayers in states like California with high state income taxes.<ref name=issues/>

====Corporations====
Matsui is a pro-labor politician and supports an initiative to have shareholders vote for executive compensation at companies.<ref name=issues/>

====Social security====
Matsui is strongly in favor of continuing social security as it is now, and opposes any move to privatize it or allow citizens the option to have alternative retirement funds.<ref name=issues/> She also opposes raising the retirement age, despite the significantly increased longevity of the average American since the establishment of social security.<ref name=issues/>

====Healthcare====
In a discussion about the [[Patient Protection and Affordable Care Act]], Matsui said that as "more Americans get to know and understand the law, and feel its effects in their lives, the less the public will want to see us take steps back to the broken health care system we have experienced for decades in this country."<ref name="Matsui Arena"/> She has opposed many attempts to repeal, reduce, or privatize medicare or medicaid.<ref name=issues>{{cite web| last =| first =| coauthors =| title =Doris Matsui: (Democrat, district 6) | work =On the Issues| url =http://www.ontheissues.org/CA/Doris_Matsui.htm}}</ref> In addition she has sought to expand medical coverage to children and mental patients.<ref name=issues/> She voted against patients being denied treatment for non-emergency issues without a medicare copay.<ref name=issues/>

She seeks to establish databases for childhood cancer and diabetes to better meet the needs of patients and diffuse information for better treatment.<ref name=issues/> She supports tobacco being regulated as a drug.<ref name=issues/>

====Drugs====
She voted to increase funding to Mexico to fight against the drug cartels.<ref name=issues/> Her rating by NORML indicates that she is 'hard on drugs'.<ref name=issues/> Matsui supports the distribution of clean and sterile syringes to reduce spread of HIV and Hepatitis.<ref name=issues/>

====Abortion====
Matsui is pro-choice and received an endorsement from NARAL<ref name=issues/> She supports federal health funding that includes abortion funding. She has a focus on preventing unwanted pregnancies all together, through funding contraception programs and make them readily available.<ref name=issues/> She supports emergency contraceptive capabilities in hospitals for rape victims.<ref name=issues/> Matsui opposes the restriction of minors traveling across states for abortion procedure.<ref name=issues/>

She has voted to continue human embryonic stem cell research.<ref name=issues/>

====Gay rights====
Matsui is a strong supporter of gay rights and was given a rating of 100% by the HRC.<ref name=issues/> Her definition of marriage does not prohibit same-sex partners.<ref name=issues/> She opposes discrimination in the workplace and in schools based on sexual orientation.<ref name=issues/> She has also voted to enforce against anti-gay crimes.<ref name=issues/> She supported the repeal of Don't-Ask-Don't-Tell and sought the reinstatement of gay soldiers who had been discharged from the military.<ref name=issues/>

====Energy====
Matsui supports American energy independence and desires that the US will be running on at least 25% renewable energy by the year 2025.<ref name=issues/> She opposes the expansion of oil production and has voted against building new refineries, off-shore drilling, and subsidies for oil and gas exploration.<ref name=issues/> She voted to provide tax subsidies for investment in renewable, alternative sources of energy.<ref name=issues/>

====Environment====
Matsui supports an initiative to develop green public schools across the nation.<ref name=issues/> She endorses cash-for-clunkers and voted to provide 2 billion dollars more for the program.<ref name=issues/> She is a pro-animal supporter and seeks to regulate dog kennels and hold tighter prohibition against animal fighting.<ref name=issues/> She has voted to increase wildlife protection from endangerment.<ref name=issues/> Matsui was a supporter of the Clean Water Act and thus seeks cleaner beaches, lakes, and other bodies of water.<ref name=issues/> She voted to allow the EPA to regulate green house gases and promotes strict limits to pollution levels for industries.<ref name=issues/>  She supports individual states creating even stricter emission standards than the federal government.<ref name=issues/>

She has supported the expansion of [[Amtrak]] to provide a better public transportation option for the public.<ref name=issues/>

====Gun control====
Matsui seeks to expand [[gun control]] and supports stricter regulations on gun purchases and sales.<ref name=issues/> She supports banning large-scale purchases of ammunition and seeks to end the [[gun show loophole]].<ref name=issues/> Matsui supports firearms manufacturers being held responsible for product misuse cases and lawsuits.<ref name=issues/>
