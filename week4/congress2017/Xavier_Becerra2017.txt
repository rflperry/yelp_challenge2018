'''Xavier Becerra''' ({{IPAc-en|h|ɑː|v|i|ˈ|ɛər|_|b|ɨ|ˈ|s|ɛr|ə}} {{respell|hah-vee|AIR|'}}; born January 26, 1958) is an American politician and a member of the [[United States House of Representatives]] for {{ushr|CA|34}}, who has represented [[Downtown Los Angeles]] in Congress since 1993. Becerra, a member of the [[Democratic Party (United States)|Democratic Party]], is [[Democratic Caucus Chairman of the United States House of Representatives|Chairman of the House Democratic Caucus]].

Born in [[Sacramento, California]], Becerra is a graduate of [[Stanford University]], receiving his [[Juris Doctor|J.D.]] from [[Stanford Law School]]. He worked as a Lawyer at the Legal Assistance Corporation of Central Massachusetts, before returning to California in 1986 to work as an Administrative Assistant for State Senator [[Art Torres]]. He served as Deputy Attorney General in the [[California Department of Justice]] from 1987 to 1990 before he was elected to the [[California State Assembly]], where he served one term from 1990 to 1992.

Becerra was elected to the U.S. House of Representatives in 1992, previously representing [[California's 34th congressional district]] from 1993 to 2003 and [[California's 31st congressional district]] from 2003 to 2013. During his tenure in the House, he has served as Chairman of the [[Congressional Hispanic Caucus]] from 1997 to 1999, Vice Chairman of the [[House Democratic Caucus]] from 2009 to 2013, and was a member of the [[United States Congress Joint Select Committee on Deficit Reduction|Joint Select Committee on Deficit Reduction]].

[[Governor of California|Governor]] [[Jerry Brown]] selected Becerra to succeed [[Kamala Harris]] as the [[Attorney General of California]], following Harris's election to the [[United States Senate]] in 2016.

==Early life, education and law career==
Born in [[Sacramento, California]], Becerra is the son of working-class immigrants who immigrated from [[Jalisco, Mexico]].<ref>{{cite news| url=http://transcripts.cnn.com/TRANSCRIPTS/0910/22/ec.01.html | work=CNN | title=CNN.com}}</ref> As a young child Becerra grew up in a one-room house with his three sisters.<ref>{{cite web | url=http://www.clotureclub.com/2011/08/member-week-xavier-becerra-representing-californias-31st-district/ | title =Member of the Week: Xavier Becerra – Representing California’s 31st District | publisher =clotureclub.com | date =August 31, 2011}}</ref> He graduated in 1976 from [[C.K. McClatchy High School]] located in central city Sacramento.<ref>{{cite book | url=https://books.google.com/books?id=fVSkbYQp6qgC&pg=PA34&lpg=PA34&dq=Xavier+Becerra+C.K.+McClatchy+High+School&source=bl&ots=dfpP6ygsCc&sig=Kx34EQOwvr_V4Zch8wJfIMG2PCY&hl=en&sa=X&ei=s04NUYfcL8KFywHoqYGQDA&ved=0CG4Q6AEwCTgK#v=onepage&q=Xavier%20Becerra%20C.K.%20McClatchy%20High%20School&f=false | title =Congressional Directory 2011-2012 112th Congress| publisher =books.google.com}}</ref> He attended [[University of Salamanca]] in [[Salamanca, Spain]] from 1978 to 1979, before earning his B.A. in [[Economics]] from [[Stanford University]] in 1980, becoming the first person in his family to graduate from college.<ref>{{cite web | url=http://www.gpo.gov/fdsys/pkg/CDIR-2011-12-01/pdf/CDIR-2011-12-01-CA-H-31.pdf | title =Congressional Directory CALIFORNIA THIRTY-FIRST DISTRICT | publisher =gpo.gov}}</ref><ref>{{cite news | url=http://www.nytimes.com/2008/12/04/us/politics/04web-becerra.html?_r=0 | title =THE NEW TEAM Xavier Becerra | publisher =nytimes.com | author =Mark Landler | date =December 4, 2008}}</ref>  He received his [[Juris Doctor]] from [[Stanford Law School]] in 1984 and was admitted to the [[State Bar of California]] in 1985.<ref>{{cite web | url=http://members.calbar.ca.gov/fal/Member/Detail/118517 | title =ATTORNEY SEARCH Xavier Becerra - #118517 | publisher =calbar.ca.gov}}</ref> He was initially a lawyer, working on cases involving individuals who had mental disorders for the Legal Assistance Corporation of Central Massachusetts.<ref>{{cite web | url=http://www.dems.gov/members/xavier-becerra | title =Xavier Becerra Caucus Chairman Representing the 34th District of CA | publisher =dems.gov}}</ref>

==Early political career==

Becerra worked as an Administrative Assistant for [[California State Senate|California State Senator]] [[Art Torres]] in 1986.<ref>{{cite web | url=http://www.gpo.gov/fdsys/pkg/CDIR-2004-01-01/pdf/CDIR-2004-01-01-CA-H-31.pdf | title =108th Congress Directory CALIFORNIA THIRTY-FIRST DISTRICT | publisher =gpo.gov =accessdate = February 2, 2013}}</ref> He served the Deputy Attorney-General in the [[California Department of Justice]] under [[California Attorney General|Attorney General]] [[John Van de Kamp]] from 1987 to 1990.<ref>{{cite news | url=http://projects.washingtonpost.com/congress/members/B000287# | title =Xavier Becerra (D) House - California, District 34 - Up for re-election in 2012 | publisher =projects.washingtonpost.com | accessdate = February 2, 2013}}</ref>

After incumbent State Assemblyman [[Charles Calderon]] decided to seek a seat in the California Senate, Becerra launched a grassroots campaign for the [[California State Assembly]], defeating Calderon's Senate aide Marta Maestas in the Democratic primary.<ref>{{cite news | url=http://articles.latimes.com/1990-06-08/local/me-535_1_xavier-becerra | title =COLUMN LEFT / RODOLFO ACUNA : The Candidate Who Upset Latino Politics : Xavier Becerra owes his victory to the people, not to the blessings of a papacito. | publisher =latimes.com | author =Rodolfo Acuna | date =June 8, 1990 | accessdate =February 2, 2013}}</ref> He later went on to beat [[Republican Party (United States)|Republican]] Lee Lieberg and [[Libertarian Party (United States)|Libertarian]], receiving 60% of the vote.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.sos.ca.gov/elections/sov/1990-general/assemblymember.pdf |title=Member of the State Assembly |publisher=sos.ca.gov |accessdate=February 2, 2013 |deadurl=yes }}</ref> Becerra served one-term in the State Assembly representing California's 59th district from 1990 to 1992.<ref>{{cite web | url=http://bioguide.congress.gov/scripts/biodisplay.pl?index=b000287 | title =BECERRA, Xavier, (1958 - )
 | publisher =bioguide.congress.gov | accessdate =February 2, 2013}}</ref>

==U.S. House of Representatives==

===Elections===
In 1992, 25th District Congressman [[Edward Roybal]] announced his retirement after 30 years in Congress.  Becerra entered the race for the seat, which had been renumbered as the 30th District after redistricting.

He won the Democratic primary with a plurality of 32% of the vote.<ref>[http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=207769 CA - District 30 - Democratic Primary] (1992) Our Campaigns</ref> In the general election, he defeated Republican nominee Morry Waksberg 58%–24%.<ref>[http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=27863 CA District 30 – General Election] (1992) Our Campaigns</ref>

In 1994, he won re-election to a second term with 66%,<ref>[http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=28733 CA District 30] (1994) Our Campaigns</ref> the lowest re-election winning percentage of his career. After, he never won re-election with less than 72%. His district was renumbered as the 31st District after the 2000 census.

After redistricting, ahead of the [[United States House of Representatives elections in California, 2012|2012 elections]], most of Becerra's old district became the 34th District. Becerra announced he would run there; it includes his home.<ref>[http://xavierbecerra.ngphost.com/node/100] {{dead link|date=August 2015}}</ref> He defeated Republican Stephen Smith 85.6% to 14.4%.

===Tenure===
Becerra is a prominent member of the [[Congressional Hispanic Caucus]], of which he served as chairman during the [[105th United States Congress|105th Congress]].<ref>{{cite web | url=http://www.loc.gov/today/pr/2007/07-167.html | title =Congressman Xavier Becerra To Deliver Hispanic Heritage Month Keynote | publisher =loc.gov | date =August 29, 2007}}</ref> He was featured on ''[[The Colbert Report|The Colbert Report's]]'' [[Better Know a District]] on August 17, 2006.<ref>{{cite web | url=http://www.colbertnation.com/the-colbert-report-videos/73148/august-17-2006/better-know-a-district---california-s-31st---javier-becerra-pt--2 | title =Better Know a District - California's 31st - Javier Becerra | publisher =colbertnation.com | date =August 17, 2006}}</ref>

On September 29, 2008 Becerra voted against the [[Emergency Economic Stabilization Act of 2008]] because he "wanted to see direct protections for responsible homeowners" in the bill.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.house.gov/htbin/blog_inc?BLOG,ca31_becerra,blog,999,All,Item%20not%20found,ID=081001_2449,TEMPLATE=postingdetail.shtml |title=On the Emergency Economic Stabilization Act |accessdate=December 6, 2008 |last=Becerra |first=Xavier |date=October 1, 2008 |work=The Becerra Blog |publisher=[[US House of Representatives]] |deadurl=yes |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20081211021139/http://www.house.gov/htbin/blog_inc?BLOG,ca31_becerra,blog,999,All,Item%20not%20found,ID=081001_2449,TEMPLATE=postingdetail.shtml |archivedate=December 11, 2008 }}</ref>

Becerra considered running for Democratic Caucus Vice Chair for the [[110th Congress]]; however, he deferred to [[John Larson]] when [[Democratic Congressional Campaign Committee|DCCC]] chairman [[Rahm Emanuel]] decided to run for [[Democratic Caucus Chairman of the United States House of Representatives|Caucus Chair]], the position Larson had been running for. Instead, Becerra was appointed assistant to the Speaker of the House for the 110th Congress.

Before the opening of the [[111th Congress]], Emanuel accepted a position as [[White House Chief of Staff]] in the [[Obama Administration]]. Larson succeeded Emanuel as caucus chair, and Becerra won his bid to succeed Larson as [[Democratic Caucus Vice-Chairman of the United States House of Representatives|Vice-Chair]]. He defeated [[Marcy Kaptur]] of Ohio by a vote of 175–67.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://thehill.com/leading-the-news/dems-back-off-leadership-challenges-2008-11-10.html|title=Dems back off leadership challenges|publisher=TheHill.com|last=Allen|first=Jared|accessdate=November 10, 2008}}</ref>

In 2011, Becerra successfully ran for a second-term as [[Democratic Caucus Vice-Chairman of the United States House of Representatives|Vice-Chair]] to serve during the [[112th Congress]].

During the [[111th Congress]] and [[112th Congress]], Becerra served on several high-profile committees. On March 24, 2010 Becerra was appointed to serve on the [[National Commission on Fiscal Responsibility and Reform]] (often called Bowles-Simpson/Simpson-Bowles). On August 11, 2011 Becerra was selected to serve on the [[Joint Select Committee on Deficit Reduction]] (also known as the Super Committee). And on December 23, 2011 Becerra was appointed to serve on a bicameral conference committee to find bipartisan solutions on the middle class tax cuts, unemployment insurance, and the Medicare physician payment rate.

====Taxes====
Becerra has consistently expressed opposition to Social Security and Medicare cuts and tax provisions seen to benefit outsourcers.<ref>[http://votesmart.org/public-statement/738585/xavier-becerra-dnc-speech#.UKrzPaX3C8o Xavier Becerra DNC Speech] (Sept. 6, 2012) Vote Smart</ref> He argued against the Job Protection Act and Recession Prevention Act of 2012 which would extend certain tax provisions enacted in 2001 and 2003 under G.W. Bush, on which Becerra voted against despite it passing through the House.<ref>Job Protection and Recession Prevention Act of 2012, [http://votesmart.org/public-statement/729866/job-protection-and-recession-prevention-act-of-2012#.UKrzZ6X3C8o  Floor Speech] (Aug. 1, 2012) Vote Smart</ref> He has consistently voted against budget plans that would protect tax cuts for higher income brackets by cutting Medicare, Medicaid, SNAP, and vital federal services.<ref>[http://votesmart.org/public-statement/692035/representative-becerra-votes-against-budget-plan-that-picks-millionaires-over-middle-class#.UKrzoaX3C8o Representative Becerra Votes Against Budget Plan that Picks Millionaires Over Middle Class] (May 10, 2012) Vote Smart</ref> He supported legislation like the Middle Class Tax Relief and Job Creation Act of 2012.

====Immigration====
As a Representative from California, immigration is a pertinent issue for Rep. Becerra because of its proximity to the border and the large number of undocumented immigrants in California. Rep. Becerra is a strong supporter of the DREAM Act and comprehensive immigration reform.<ref>[http://votesmart.org/public-statement/754608/dream-act Dream Act Statement] (Oct. 17, 2012) Vote Smart</ref> Additionally, Rep. Becerra supports the [[Deferred Action for Childhood Arrivals]] program put in place by President Obama.<ref>[http://votesmart.org/public-statement/735091/rep-becerra-a-new-day-for-dreamers#.UKrzG6X3C8o Rep. Becerra: A New Day for Dreamers] (Aug. 15, 2012) Vote Smart</ref>

====Women's issues====
Becerra is pro-choice and supports the right of access to abortion.<ref>{{cite web | url=http://www.ontheissues.org/CA/Xavier_Becerra_Abortion.htm | title =Xavier Becerra on Abortion Democratic Representative (CA-31) | publisher =ontheissues.org}}</ref> On May 31, 2012, Becerra voted against H.R. 3541, the Prenatal Nondiscrimination Act (PRENDA), which would have imposed civil and criminal penalties on anyone knowingly attempting to perform a [[sex-selective abortion]]. The bill also would have required healthcare providers to report known or suspected violations to law enforcement, including suspicions about a woman's motives for seeking an abortion.<ref>[http://votesmart.org/public-statement/698938/capac-members-denounce-attack-on-reproductive-rights#.UKrwMaX3C8o CAPAC Members Denounce Attack on Reproductive Rights] (May 31, 2012) Vote Smart</ref> Becerra received a 100% rating from [[Planned Parenthood]] and [[NARAL Pro-Choice America]] in 2012.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.plannedparenthoodaction.org/elections-politics/congressional-scorecard.htm#/california/20 |title=2013 CONGRESSIONAL SCORECARD |publisher=plannedparenthoodaction.org |deadurl=yes }}</ref><ref>{{cite web | url=http://www.prochoiceamerica.org/assets/download-files/2012-congressional-record.pdf | title =2012 Congressional Record on Choice | publisher =prochoiceamerica.org}}</ref> Becerra voted in favor of the [[Lilly Ledbetter Fair Pay Act of 2009]] which made the 180-day statute of limitations for filing an equal-pay lawsuit regarding pay discrimination reset with each new paycheck affected by that discriminatory action.<ref>{{cite web | url=http://clerk.house.gov/evs/2009/roll037.xml | title =FINAL VOTE RESULTS FOR ROLL CALL 37 Lilly Ledbetter Fair Pay Act of 2009 | publisher =clerk.house.gov | date =January 27, 2009}}</ref><ref>[http://votesmart.org/public-statement/685613/representative-becerra-on-equal-pay-day Representative Becerra on Equal Pay Day] (April 18, 2012) Vote Smart</ref>

==Other political ambitions==

===2001 run for L.A. Mayor===
Becerra ran for [[Los Angeles mayoral election, 2001|mayor of Los Angeles]] in 2001. He finished with 6 percent of the primary vote, finishing behind businessman [[Steve Soboroff]], Councilman [[Joel Wachs]], former California State Assembly Speaker [[Antonio Villaraigosa]], and the eventual winner, then-City Attorney [[James Hahn]].

===Obama administration===
On January 27, 2008, Becerra endorsed U.S. Senator [[Barack Obama]] for President.<ref>{{cite web| title = Congressman Xavier Becerra Endorses Barack Obama | url = http://www.americanchronicle.com/articles/50337}}</ref>

Becerra was considered for the position of [[Office of the United States Trade Representative|US Trade Representative]] in the administration of President-elect Obama. While it was reported that he had already accepted,<ref>[http://www.cqpolitics.com/wmspage.cfm?parm1=5&docID=news-000002992696 Rep. Becerra Offered Trade Representative Post] {{webarchive |url=https://web.archive.org/web/20081207173246/http://www.cqpolitics.com/wmspage.cfm?parm1=5&docID=news-000002992696 |date=December 7, 2008 }}</ref> on December 15, 2008, he announced that he would not accept the position.<ref>AP (2008). [https://www.google.com/hostednews/ap/article/ALeqM5iDgcCmOtgguknMccxs5kC2yPrNDwD95453NG0 Becerra won't take trade job]. Retrieved December 17, 2008.</ref>

==California Attorney General==
On December 1, 2016, Becerra accepted [[Governor of California|Governor]] [[Jerry Brown]]'s offer to make Becerra the [[Attorney General of California]]. If confirmed by the [[California Legislature]], he will succeed [[Kamala Harris]], who was elected to the [[United States Senate]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.latimes.com/politics/essential/la-pol-ca-essential-politics-updates-gov-brown-taps-california-s-rep-1480609606-htmlstory.html|title=Gov. Xavier Becerra to be state's first Latino attorney general|first1=Sarah D.|last1=Wire|first2=John|last2=Myers|work=Los Angeles Times|date=December 1, 2016|accessdate=December 1, 2016}}</ref>

==Personal life==
Becerra is married to physician Carolina Reyes<ref>{{Cite news|first= Matea|last= Gold |title= Congressman Tests His Winning Streak |url= |work=Los Angeles Times |publisher= Accessed through [[ProQuest]] |date= March 12, 2001 |accessdate=December 6, 2008 }}</ref> and has three children.  He owns a home in Chevy Chase, Maryland where he, his wife and three children reside when Congress is in session.<ref>http://www.huffingtonpost.com/2011/08/25/becerra-visclosky-property-tax-breaks-maryland_n_926823.html</ref> He has a home in his district in Los Angeles, California.
