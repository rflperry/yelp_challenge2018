'''Steven Ernst "Steve" Stivers''' (born March 24, 1965) is an [[United States|American]] politician who has been the [[United States House of Representatives|U.S. Representative]] for {{ushr|OH|15}}<ref name="http://www.cnn.com">[http://www.cnn.com/ELECTION/2010/results/individual/#OHH15]</ref> since 2011. He is a member of the [[Republican Party (United States)|Republican Party]]. Stivers previously served in the [[Ohio Senate]], representing the 16th district. He is a [[Colonel (United States)|Colonel]] in the [[Ohio Army National Guard]] and served [[active duty]] in [[Iraq]] as [[Battalion#United States Army and Marine Corps|Battalion]] [[Commanding officer|Commander]] until December 2005.

==Early life, education, and career==

Stivers was born and grew up in [[Ripley, Ohio]], the son of Carol Sue (née Pulliam) and Ernst Bambach Stivers.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://freepages.genealogy.rootsweb.ancestry.com/%7Ebattle/reps/stivers.htm|title=Steve Stivers ancestry|publisher=|accessdate=January 1, 2016}}</ref> Steve is a recipient of the [[Eagle Scout (Boy Scouts of America)|Eagle Scout]] Award.<ref name="trivillagerotary.org">[http://www.trivillagerotary.org/Newsletters/2008/3-26-08.doc]</ref>

Stivers attended The [[Ohio State University]] where he earned a [[Bachelor of Arts]] degree in [[economics]] and [[international relations]] in 1989 and an [[Master of Business Administration|MBA]] in 1996.<ref name="test2">[http://www.stivers4congress.com/?q=biography Steve Stivers Biography]</ref> While attending Ohio State he joined the [[Delta Upsilon]] Fraternity.

Stivers spent seven years at [[Bank One]], three years at the Ohio Company, two years as Finance Director for the [[Franklin County, Ohio|Franklin County]] Republican Party and five years as a staff member in the [[Ohio Senate]].<ref name=about>{{cite web

==Military service==
Stivers has served in the [[Ohio Army National Guard]] since 1985 and holds the rank of Colonel in the [[United States Army Logistics Branch|Logistics branch]]. Stivers was called to [[active duty]] while serving in the Ohio Senate in October 2004. It was then that Stivers served in [[Iraq]], [[Kuwait]], [[Qatar]], and [[Djibouti]] as Battalion Commander until December 2005. He was awarded a [[Bronze Star]] for his accomplishments as a battalion commander during [[Operation Iraqi Freedom]].<ref name=about/>

==Ohio Senate==

===Elections===
In December 2002, incumbent Republican [[Priscilla Mead]] decided to resign after only serving in the [[Ohio Senate]] for a year.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://news.google.com/newspapers?id=cgskAAAAIBAJ&sjid=wxIEAAAAIBAJ&pg=3556,12300&dq=priscilla+mead&hl=en|title=Westland News - Google News Archive Search|publisher=|accessdate=January 1, 2016}}</ref> Stivers was recommended by a Senate screening committee and was appointed by election of the Senate Republicans on January 4, 2003.  
He then won re-election in 2004 to a full senate term with 58% of the vote.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=70709|title=Our Campaigns - OH State Senate 16 Race - Nov 02, 2004|publisher=|accessdate=January 1, 2016}}</ref>

===Tenure===
Stivers served in the [[Ohio Senate]] from January 9, 2003, until December 2008.

;Legislation
He sponsored 12 bills while in office which became law, 11 of which were bipartisan.<ref name="trivillagerotary.org"/> He was the lead sponsor of the following bills: 
* A comprehensive [[tort]] reform bill which was enacted in 2004.<ref name="test2"/>
* Allowed members of the Armed Forces Reserves who are called to active duty to defer paying property taxes during the length of a deployment.<ref name=test2/>
* Provided doctors limited civil protections if they choose to give free care to uninsured people, resulting in more than $1 million of free care in Franklin County alone.<ref name=test2/>
* Worked to ensure those with disabilities in need of healthcare have the option to buy into Ohio’s Medicaid system.<ref name=test2/>
* Worked to strengthen Ohio's sex offender laws.<ref name=test2/>

He also passed a balanced, fiscally conservative state budget, provided the largest personal property tax cut in Ohio history, and froze tuition rates for Ohio’s college students.<ref name=test2/>

===Committee assignments===
Stivers sat on a variety of Ohio Senate committees. He was the Chairman of the Insurance, Commerce and Labor Committee, Vice-Chair of the Finance and Financial Institutions Committee, served on the Ways and Means Committee, the Judiciary Committee on Civil Justice, the Judiciary Committee for Criminal Justice, and also the Controlling Board.<ref>[http://www.freedomspeaks.com/official/63247/ohio-%28oh%29-state-senator-steve-stivers-%5Boh-senate%5D Freedom Speaks Official Profile]</ref>

==U.S. House of Representatives==

===Elections===
;2008
[[Image:StiversGrandviewParade.jpg|thumb|right|200px|Steve Stivers shaking hands at the Grandview Memorial Day Weekend Parade.]]
{{See also|United States House of Representatives elections in Ohio, 2008#District 15}}

In November 2007, Stivers announced he would run for election to Congress in Ohio's 15th District, a seat held by retiring Republican member [[Deborah Pryce]]. He won the Republican nomination and ran against Democratic nominee Mary Jo Kilroy, [[Libertarian Party (United States)|Libertarian]] Mark Noble and Independent Don Elijah Eckhart. Stivers lost by 2,311 votes, conceding on December 7, 2008, after a long vote  recount.

;2010
[[File:Boehnerandstivers.jpg|thumb|[[John Boehner]], the then House Minority Leader, campaigning for fellow Ohio Congressman Steve Stivers (left) during the [[United States House of Representatives elections in Ohio, 2010|2010 midterm elections]]|right|alt=]]
{{See also |United States House of Representatives elections in Ohio, 2010#District 15*}}

Stivers won the Republican primary with 82% of the vote.<ref>http://vote.franklincountyohio.gov/assets/pdf/2010/primary/EL45A.HTM</ref>
<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.dispatchpolitics.com/live/content/local_news/stories/2010/05/05/copy/dailey-gibbs-fight-for-lead-in-gops-18th.html?adsec=politics&sid=101|title=Dispatch Politics|work=The Columbus Dispatch|accessdate=January 1, 2016}}</ref> He again faced Democratic incumbent [[Mary Jo Kilroy]] along with Constitution Party nominee David Ryon and Libertarian nominee William J. Kammerer. On November 2, 2010, Kilroy conceded to Stivers.

;2012
{{See also|United States House of Representatives elections in Ohio, 2012#District 15}}
Stivers ran again in 2012 against Democratic nominee Pat Lang.<ref>{{cite web|title=Ohio Secretary of State|url=http://www.sos.state.oh.us/sos/upload/elections/2012/gen/UScongress.pdf}}</ref>  He was endorsed by the NRA, National Right to Life, Ohio State Medical Association and United States Chamber of Commerce. Stivers was re-elected by 76,397 votes.<ref>{{cite web|title=Stivers for Congress|url=http://www.stivers4congress.com/endorsements}}</ref>

;2014
{{See also|United States House of Representatives elections in Ohio, 2014#District 15}}
Stivers ran in 2014 against Democratic rival Scott Wharton. Gaining more than 66 percent of the vote, he was reelected for a third term.<ref>http://www.sos.state.oh.us/SOS/elections/Research/electResultsMain/2014Results.aspx</ref>

;2016
Stivers will be once again seeking the Republican nomination for the OH-15 seat. He is again being opposed by Democrat Scott Wharton.<ref>http://www.fairfieldelections.com/candidate.php</ref>

===Tenure===

;Budget
Stivers has voted against raising the debt limit when there was no offset or systemic reform and supports prioritizing spending in the event that the debt limit is reached.<ref name=issues>{{cite web| title =Steve Stivers: (Republican, district 15) | work =On the Issues| url =http://www.ontheissues.org/OH/Steve_Stivers.htm}}</ref><ref name=votebudget>{{cite web| title =Representative Steve Stivers's Voting Records: Budget, Spending and Taxes| work =Vote Smart| url =http://votesmart.org/candidate/key-votes/45333/steve-stivers/10/budget-spending-and-taxes#.UcC4mvk3upo}}</ref>  He was part of a proposal to add a balanced budget amendment to the US Constitution.<ref name=issues/><ref name=votebudget/>  Stivers voted to offset the costs of disaster relief spending through discretionary budget cuts.<ref name=votebudget/>

On December 15, 2011, Stivers introduced a bill that would alter the composition of the [[Penny (United States coin)|penny]], [[Nickel (United States coin)|nickel]], [[Dime (United States coin)|dime]] and [[Quarter (United States coin)|quarter]] to [[steel]], with a [[copper]] coat for the penny, which claimed to save an estimated $433,000,000 over the course of ten years.  The bill was referred to committee and was rejected, but Stivers has resubmitted it twice more, once in April 2013 (again rejected in committee) and again in January 2015 (once more referred to committee).<ref>{{cite web|title=H.R.516:Cents and Sensibility Act|author=GovTrack.us|url=https://www.govtrack.us/congress/bills/114/hr516/text|accessdate=July 19, 2015}}</ref>  In spite of the US Mint releasing a technical report in December 2014 for its Alternative Metals Study in which it reported that steel is an unacceptable material for US coins (due to difficulty in minting, lack of security, and severe impact on both the public and the coin vending industry),<ref>{{cite web|title=Alternative Metals Study Technical Report, 2014 Biennial Report to the Congress|author=United States Mint|url=http://www.usmint.gov/about_the_mint/PDFs/2014-rd-biennial-report-appendix-4.pdf|accessdate=July 19, 2015}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=ksadjhf|author=coinnews.net|url=http://www.coinnews.net/2014/12/15/us-mint-not-recommending-new-coin-compositions/|accessdate=July 19, 2015}}</ref> Stivers kept the wording of his bill identical to the previous two versions.

He voted to audit the Federal Reserve and its recent actions, specifically its involvement in mortgage loans.<ref name=issues/>

;Energy
Stivers supports all energy options, including green, nuclear, and clean coal and supports tax benefits for renewable energy usage.<ref name=issues/><ref name=voteeenergy>{{cite web| title =Representative Steve Stivers's Voting Records: Energy| work =Vote Smart| url =http://votesmart.org/candidate/key-votes/45333/steve-stivers/29/energy#.UcC8rvk3upo}}</ref>  However Stivers opposes federal regulations on efficiency standards<ref name=issues/>

;Gun control
Stivers is a strong supporter of gun rights and opposes any limits to Second Amendment rights.<ref name=issues/>  He supports loosening regulations for interstate gun purchases and supports veterans registering unlicensed firearms acquired from outside the United States.<ref name=issues/>

;Healthcare
Stivers is an opponent of government-run healthcare and has voted on numerous occasions against such.<ref name=issues/><ref name=votehealth>{{cite web| title =Representative Steve Stivers's Voting Records: Health and Healthcare| work =Vote Smart| url =http://votesmart.org/candidate/key-votes/45333/steve-stivers/38/health-and-health-care#.UcDB6_k3upo}}</ref>  He supports privatized healthcare options opposes the use of federal funds for any healthcare program expansions or acts.<ref name=issues/><ref name=votehealth/>

;Social security
Stivers opposes the privatization of social security.<ref name=issues/>  In addition, he also opposes raising the retirement age from its current state.<ref name=issues/>

;Taxes
Stivers took the Taxpayer Protection pledge, insuring he would not support any tax raises.<ref name=issues/>  He supports a flat federal tax rate across the board for all income brackets.<ref name=issues/>

===Legislation===
On November 21, 2013, Stivers introduced the bill [[To amend the Federal Home Loan Bank Act to authorize privately insured credit unions to become members of a Federal home loan bank (H.R. 3584; 113th Congress)]].<ref name=3584sum>{{cite web|title=H.R. 3584 – Summary|url=http://beta.congress.gov/bill/113th-congress/house-bill/3584|publisher=United States Congress|accessdate=May 4, 2014}}</ref> The bill would amend the [[Federal Home Loan Bank Act]] to treat certain privately insured [[credit union]]s as insured depository institutions for purposes of determining eligibility for membership in a federal home loan bank.<ref name="3584sum"/> The bill was scheduled to be voted on under a [[suspension of the rules]] on May 6, 2014.<ref name=HouseExIRS>{{cite news|last=Marcos|first=Cristina|title=The week ahead: House to hold ex-IRS official in contempt|url=http://thehill.com/blogs/floor-action/scheduling/205099-the-week-ahead-house-to-hold-ex-irs-official-in-contempt|accessdate=May 5, 2014|newspaper=The Hill|date=May 2, 2014}}</ref>
