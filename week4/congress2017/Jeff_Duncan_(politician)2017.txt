'''Jeffrey Darren Duncan''' (born January 7, 1966)<ref>[http://news.yahoo.com/s/cq/20101103/pl_cq_politics/politics000003758169_2 112th Congress: Jeff Duncan, R-S.C. (3rd District)] ''[[CQ Politics]]''</ref> is an [[United States|American]] politician who has been the [[United States House of Representatives|United States Representative]] for {{ushr|SC|3}} since 2011. Duncan, a [[Republican Party (United States)|Republican]], previously served as a member of the [[South Carolina House of Representatives]].

==Early life, education, and business career==
In 1984, he graduated from [[Ware Shoals, South Carolina|Ware Shoals High School]]. He is a 1988 graduate of [[Clemson University]]; he was a [[Wide receiver (American football)|wide receiver]] on the [[Clemson Tigers football|football team]] under Head Coach [[Danny Ford]].

He is currently the President and CEO of J. Duncan Associates, a real estate marketing firm.

==South Carolina House of Representatives==

===Elections===
After redistricting, Duncan ran for South Carolina's 15th House District in 2002. In the Republican primary, he defeated David Tribble Jr, Clinton County Councilman, 56%–44%.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=42568|title=Our Campaigns - SC State House 015 - R Primary Race - Jun 11, 2002|publisher=}}</ref> He won the general election with 62% of the vote.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=40161|title=Our Campaigns - SC State House 015 Race - Nov 05, 2002|publisher=}}</ref> In 2004, he won re-election to a second term unopposed.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=41626|title=Our Campaigns - SC State House 015 Race - Nov 02, 2004|publisher=}}</ref> In 2006, he won re-election to a third term with 63% of the vote.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=168455|title=Our Campaigns - SC State House 015 Race - Nov 07, 2006|publisher=}}</ref> In 2008, he won re-election to a fourth term unopposed.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=451667|title=Our Campaigns - SC State House 015 Race - Nov 04, 2008|publisher=}}</ref> In 2010, he retired in order to run for the [[U.S. House of Representatives]]. David Tribble, Duncan's primary opponent in 2002, won Duncan's seat.

===Tenure===
Governor [[Mark Sanford]] called him a “Tax Payer Hero.” He was given the “Guardian of Small Business” award from the [[National Federation of Independent Businesses]], an A+ rating from the [[Club for Growth]], the Palmetto Leadership Award from the SC Policy Council, and “Legislator of the Year” from the SC Recreation and Parks Association and SC Wildlife Federation.<ref>http://www.scstatehouse.gov/members/bios/0506818121.html</ref>

===Committee assignments===
Duncan was named Chairman of the House Agriculture, Natural Resources and Environmental Affairs Committee in 2007. He was appointed by the Speaker of the House to be the Chair of the Education Finance Study Committee, Natural Gas Offshore Drilling Study Committee and was appointed to represent the state of South Carolina on the Southern States Energy Board.<ref>http://www.jeffduncan.com/site/about-2/</ref>

==U.S. House of Representatives==

===Elections===
;2010
{{see also|United States House of Representatives elections in South Carolina, 2010#District 3}}
He ran for [[South Carolina's 3rd congressional district]] when Republican incumbent U.S. Congressman [[J. Gresham Barrett]] ran for [[Governor of South Carolina]]. He was an early Tea Party favorite and was endorsed by the [[Club for Growth]]<ref>[http://www.clubforgrowth.org/endorsedcandidates/?subsec=0&id=702 Congressional Scorecard<!-- Bot generated title -->]</ref> and the [[National Right to Life Committee]].<ref>http://www.jeffduncan.com/site/2010/09/national-right-to-life-endorses-duncan/</ref> In the Republican primary, businessman Richard Cash ranked first with 25% but failed to reach the 50% threshold to win outright. Duncan ranked second in the six candidate field with 23%.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=523030|title=Our Campaigns - SC - District 03 - R Primary Race - Jun 08, 2010|publisher=}}</ref> In the run-off election, Duncan defeated Cash 51%–49%, a vote difference of 2,171. Duncan won five of the district's ten counties, and were mostly located in the southern part of the CD.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=657940|title=Our Campaigns - SC District 03 - R Runoff Race - Jun 22, 2010|publisher=}}</ref> He won the general election with 62% of the vote, 2% less than [[John McCain]]'s 64% vote in 2008. He won nine of the district's ten counties, losing just McCormick (52%–47%).  Duncan spent $935,503; [[Democratic Party (United States)|Democrat]] [[Jane Ballard Dyer]] spent $272,698.<ref name=Barone2012/><ref>{{cite web
  |accessdate=2013-09-08 |url=http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=511274
  |title=SC – District 03 Race |date=Nov 2, 2010 |work=Our Campaigns}}</ref>

;2012
{{see also|United States House of Representatives elections in South Carolina, 2012#District 3}}
Duncan successfully ran for re-election in the newly redrawn 3rd district, which excludes [[Aiken County, South Carolina|Aiken County]] (McCain won with 62%),<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.aikenstandard.com/story/0901-jeff-duncan|title=U.S.   Rep. Duncan promises to represent Aiken despite congressional redistricting|work=Aiken Standard}}</ref> and includes two new counties: [[Newberry County, South Carolina|Newberry]] (McCain won with 58%) and [[Greenville County, South Carolina|Greenville]] (McCain won with 57%). Duncan won re-election to a second term with 67% of the vote.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.politico.com/2012-election/map/#/House/2012/SC|title=2016 Primary Election Results: President Live Map by State, Real-Time Voting Updates|work=Election Hub}}</ref>

Duncan successfully ran for re-election in 2014, winning with a modern-day record of 71.18% of the vote. http://www.enr-scvotes.org/SC/53424/149816/en/summary.html# Duncan won against Democrat candidate Barbara Jo Mullis. http://www.enr-scvotes.org/SC/53424/149816/en/summary.html#

===Tenure===
On the 1st of August 2012, Duncan took part in the ´Chick-fil-a appreciation day´, using Twitter to publicise his purchase of large quantities of fried chicken produce in an apparent endorsement of CEO Dan Cathy´s anti-same-sex marriage sentiments.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.theguardian.com/lifeandstyle/us-news-blog/2012/aug/01/chick-fil-a-appreciation-day|title=Chick-fil-A appreciation day brings huge crowds to fast-food chain|author=Amanda Holpuch|work=the Guardian}}</ref>

In January 2012, [[The New American]]‘s ''Freedom Index'' gave Duncan a perfect 100% score for his votes in 2011. He was just one of three members of congress to receive a perfect score, the other two being [[Walter B. Jones, Jr.|Walter B. Jones]] (R-NC) and [[Ron Paul]] (R-Texas).<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.fitsnews.com/2012/01/02/sc-freshmen-soar-on-freedom-index/|title=SC Freshmen Soar On "Freedom Index" - FITSNews|work=http://www.fitsnews.com}}</ref>

On November 19, 2012, Duncan led a group of 97 Republican U.S. Representatives in writing a letter to President Obama, expressing opposition to the possible nomination of [[U.S. Ambassador to the United Nations|U.S. Ambassador to the U.N.]] [[Susan Rice]] to [[U.S. Secretary of State]].<ref>http://jeffduncan.house.gov/sites/jeffduncan.house.gov/files/Rep.%20Duncan%20Letter%20to%20President%20Obama%20on%20Ambassador%20Susan%20Rice%20(11.19.12).pdf</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.politico.com/news/stories/1112/84072.html?hp=l4|title=97 House members oppose Rice|work=POLITICO}}</ref>

As of 25 April 2016, Jeff Duncan has the most conservative GovTrack ideology score in the House of Representatives.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.govtrack.us/congress/members/jeff_duncan/412472|title=Jeff Duncan, Representative for South Carolina's 3rd Congressional District - GovTrack.us|work=GovTrack.us}}</ref>

===Legislation===

Duncan was a "Tea Party freshman" in the 112th Congress.

In February, 2011, Duncan introduced a resolution to create a new Committee on the Elimination of Nonessential Federal Programs in an attempt to reduce federal outlays.<ref>{{cite web|title=Jeff Duncan Full Biography|url=http://jeffduncan.house.gov/about-me/full-biography|website=U.S. Congressman Jeff Duncan|accessdate=8 December 2014}}</ref>

On March 13, 2014, Duncan introduced the [[DHS Acquisition Accountability and Efficiency Act (H.R. 4228; 113th Congress)]], a bill that would direct the [[United States Department of Homeland Security]] (DHS) to improve the accountability, transparency, and efficiency of its major acquisition programs.<ref name=cbo4228>{{cite web|title=CBO – H.R. 4228|url=http://www.cbo.gov/publication/45385|publisher=Congressional Budget Office|accessdate=9 June 2014}}</ref> The bill would specify procedures for the department to follow if it fails to meet timelines, cost estimates, or other performance parameters for these programs.<ref name="cbo4228"/><ref name=FTjune10>{{cite news|last1=Medici|first1=Andy|title=House passes bill reforming DHS acquisition management|url=http://www.federaltimes.com/article/20140610/ACQ02/306100013/House-passes-bill-reforming-DHS-acquisition-management?odyssey=nav%7Chead|accessdate=10 June 2014|publisher=Federal Times|date=10 June 2014}}</ref> Duncan argued that "for years, DHS's purchases of major homeland security systems have been late, cost more, and done less than promised. This bill will save taxpayer dollars by forcing DHS to improve its management."<ref name="FTjune10"/>

==Personal life==

Duncan is married to Melody, with whom he has three sons: Graham, John Philip, and Parker.{{citation needed|date=August 2012}} Jeff, Melody, John Philip, and Parker are members of First Baptist Church in Clinton, South Carolina, while Graham is a member of the Episcopal Church of the Epiphany in Laurens, South Carolina.<ref>{{cite web|title=Jeff Duncan Biography|url=http://jeffduncan.house.gov/about-me/full-biography|website=U.S. Congressman Jeff Duncan|accessdate=8 December 2014}}</ref><ref name=Baptist>{{cite news|last=Staff|first=|title=Ten Southern Baptists sworn in as new reps.|url=http://www.bpnews.net/bpnews.asp?id=34378|accessdate=25 December 2014|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20141226010629/http://www.bpnews.net/34378|archivedate=25 December 2014|newspaper=Baptist Press|date=5 January 2011}}</ref>

==Paris Terror Attacks==

Immediately following the November 13, 2015 terrorist attacks in Paris, France, the deadliest in that country's recent history, Rep. Duncan tweeted "How's that Syrian refugee resettlement look now? How about that mass migration into Europe? Terrorism is alive & well in the world. #No"<ref>{{cite web|url=https://twitter.com/RepJeffDuncan/status/665326737924976641|title=Rep. Jeff Duncan on Twitter|work=Twitter}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.vox.com/2015/11/13/9733192/paris-attacks-congressman-syria-refugee|title=This congressman just showed how not to respond to the Paris attacks|author=German Lopez|date=14 November 2015|work=Vox}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://radio.foxnews.com/2015/11/14/republican-congressman-blames-fleeing-syrian-refugees-for-paris-attack/|title=Republican Congressman Blames Fleeing Syrian Refugees For Paris Attack|work=FOX News Radio}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.thewrap.com/ben-carson-newt-gingrich-waste-no-time-politicizing-paris-attacks/|title=Ben Carson, Newt Gingrich Waste No Time Politicizing Paris Attacks|author=Itay Hod|date=14 November 2015|work=TheWrap}}</ref> Rep. Duncan stated in a recent press release that "Greater offensive action needs to be taken to weaken ISIS’s appeal, exploit its vulnerabilities, and weaken its influence over people."<ref>{{cite web|url=http://jeffduncan.house.gov/press-release/rep-jeff-duncan-issues-statement-obama-administrations-syrian-rebel-training-program|title=Rep. Jeff Duncan Issues Statement on the Obama Administration's Syrian Rebel Training Program|publisher=}}</ref> Duncan further voiced his concerns at an October 21, 2015 hearing saying, "I will tell you folks in South Carolina are very concerned about our inability to vet properly the refugees that are coming." <ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.jeffduncan.com/snews.php?n=192|title=Jeff Duncan - Republican for Congress|publisher=}}</ref>
