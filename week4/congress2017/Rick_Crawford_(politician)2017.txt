'''Eric Alan "Rick" Crawford''' (born January 22, 1966)<ref>[http://crawford.house.gov/biography/ crawford.house.gov]</ref> is an [[United States|American]] politician who has been the [[United States House of Representatives|U.S. Representative]] for {{ushr|AR|1}} since 2011. He is a member of the [[Republican Party (United States)|Republican Party]]. Before he was elected to Congress, Crawford was a radio announcer, businessman and a soldier in the [[U.S. Army]].

==Early life and education==

Crawford was born in the [[Homestead Air Force Base]] in [[Florida]], the son of Ruth Anne and Donny J. "Don" Crawford.<ref>[http://freepages.genealogy.rootsweb.ancestry.com/%7Ebattle/reps/crawford.htm freepages.genealogy.rootsweb.ancestry.com]</ref> Crawford grew up in a military family; his father served in the [[United States Air Force]]. He graduated from [[Alvirne High School]] in [[Hudson, New Hampshire]]. He enlisted in the Army, where he served as an [[Explosive Ordnance Disposal|explosive ordnance disposal technician]] for four years,<ref>{{cite news | first = Andrew | last = Stiles | title = National Review: Turning Arkansas Red | date = 2010-10-27 | url = http://www.npr.org/templates/story/story.php?storyId=130855895 | work = [[National Public Radio]] | accessdate = 2010-10-31 }}</ref> and advanced to the rank of [[Sergeant]]. After his service, he attended [[Arkansas State University]] and graduated in 1996 with a [[Bachelor of Arts|B.S.]] in Agriculture Business and Economics.

== Radio career ==
He has been a [[news anchor]] and agri-reporter on [[KAIT-TV]] in [[Jonesboro, Arkansas|Jonesboro]] and Farm Director on [[KFIN|KFIN-FM]]. He owned and operated the AgWatch Network, a farm news network heard on 39 radio stations in [[Arkansas]], [[Missouri]], [[Tennessee]], [[Mississippi]] and [[Kentucky]].<ref>[http://www.meetrickcrawford.com/about/ meetrickcrawford.com About]</ref>

== U.S. House of Representatives ==

===Elections===
;2010
{{Main article|United States House of Representatives elections in Arkansas, 2010#District 1*}}
Crawford chose to run for [[Arkansas' 1st congressional district]] after Democratic incumbent U.S. Congressman [[Robert Marion Berry|Marion Berry]] decided to retire. Crawford received the endorsements of Minnesota Governor [[Tim Pawlenty]], former Arkansas Governor [[Mike Huckabee]], former Congressman [[Asa Hutchinson]], and former Congressman [[Ed Bethune]].<ref>[http://www.meetrickcrawford.com/endorsements/ meetrickcrawford.com endorsements]</ref> He won the Republican [[Primary election|primary]], defeating [[Princella Smith]] 72%–28%.<ref>[http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=572929 www.ourcampaigns.com AR District 01 – R Primary, 2010]</ref> He won the [[general election]], defeating Berry's chief of staff [[Chad Causey]] 52%–43%.<ref>[http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=487762 ourcampaigns.com, AR – District 01, 2011]</ref>

;2012
{{Main article|United States House of Representatives elections in Arkansas, 2012#District 1*}}
Crawford won re-election to a second term, defeating Democratic prosecutor, Scott Ellington, 56%–39%.<ref>[http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=705041 ourcampaigns.com, AR – District 01, 2012]</ref>

;2014
{{Main article|United States House of Representatives elections in Arkansas, 2014#District 1*}}
Crawford won re=election to a third term, defeating [[Heber Springs, Arkansas|Heber Springs]] Mayor Jackie McPherson 63%-33%.<ref>http://results.enr.clarityelections.com/AR/53237/149792/Web01/en/summary.html</ref>

===Tenure===
Crawford was sworn into office on January 5, 2011, as a member of the [[112th United States Congress|112th Congress]]. He is the first Republican to represent his district in Washington since [[Reconstruction era of the United States|Reconstruction]]. The last Republican to represent the district was [[Asa Hodges]] who departed the seat on March 3, 1875.<ref>[http://bioguide.congress.gov/scripts/biodisplay.pl?index=H000671 bioguide.congress.gov Asa Hodges]</ref> Crawford is a member of the [[Republican Study Committee]].<ref>[http://www.mcallennow.com/voter-resources/official/283914/Rick/Crawford mcallennow.com voter resources, Rick Crawford]</ref>

Crawford voted to repeal the [[Patient Protection and Affordable Care Act]] and to return federal non-security spending to fiscal year 2008 levels.  He also voted to terminate taxpayer financing of presidential election campaigns and party conventions.<ref>[http://crawford.house.gov/voterecord/ crawford.house.gov vote record]</ref>

In 2010, Crawford signed a pledge sponsored by [[Americans for Prosperity]] promising to vote against any [[Politics of global warming|Global Warming legislation]] that would raise taxes.<ref>[http://americansforprosperity.org/noclimatetax//wp-content/uploads/2010/04/crawford-rick.pdf /americansforprosperity.org Americans for Prosperity Applauds  U.S. House  Candidate Rick Crawford]</ref>

===Legislation===
On January 18, 2013, Crawford introduced the [[Farmers Undertake Environmental Land Stewardship Act (H.R. 311; 113th Congress)]] into the House.<ref name=311sum>{{cite web|title=H.R. 311 – Summary|url=http://beta.congress.gov/bill/113th-congress/house-bill/311|publisher=United States Congress|accessdate=10 March 2014}}</ref> The bill would require the [[Environmental Protection Agency]] (EPA) to modify the Spill Prevention, Control, and Countermeasure (SPCC) rule, which regulates oil discharges into navigable waters and adjoining shorelines.<ref name=cbo311>{{cite web|title=H.R. 311 – CBO|url=http://www.cbo.gov/publication/44719|publisher=Congressional Budget Office|accessdate=10 March 2014}}</ref> The rule requires certain farmers to develop an [[oil spill]] prevention plan that is certified by a professional [[engineer]] and may require them to make [[infrastructure]] changes.<ref name="cbo311"/> According to supporters, this bill would "ease the burden placed on farmers and ranchers" by making it easier for smaller farms to self-certify and raising the level of storage capacity under which farms are exempted.<ref name=HighPlainsJ1>{{cite news|title=Committee passes legislation to ease burden of SPCC program|url=http://www.hpj.com/archives/2013/dec13/dec23/1104FUELSActsr.cfm|accessdate=11 March 2014|newspaper=High Plains Journal|date=23 December 2013}}</ref>

Crawford and his wife, Stacy, live in [[Jonesboro, Arkansas|Jonesboro]] with their children. He attends Nettleton Baptist Church, a [[Southern Baptist]] church in Jonesboro.<ref>{{cite news |author=Staff |title=Ten Southern Baptists sworn in as new reps. |url=http://www.bpnews.net/bpnews.asp?id=34378 |accessdate=25 December 2014 |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20141226010629/http://www.bpnews.net/34378 |archivedate=25 December 2014 |newspaper=Baptist Press |date=5 January 2011}}</ref>
