'''Jeffrey John''' "'''Jeff'''" '''Denham''' (born July 29, 1967) is an American businessman, [[United States Air Force]] veteran, and politician. A member of the [[Republican Party (United States)|Republican Party]], he is the [[United States House of Representatives|U.S. Representative]] for {{ushr|CA|10}}. Denham first won election to the U.S. House in 2010, representing {{ushr|CA|19}} for one term before redistricting led him to run in {{ushr|CA|10}} in 2012.

Prior to seeking political office, Denham served on active and reserve status in the [[United States Air Force]] for 16 years, and served in both [[Gulf War|Operation Desert Storm]] in Iraq and [[Unified Task Force|Operation Restore Hope]] in Somalia. He has worked as a farmer and started a plastics container company to ship produce.<ref name=wsj/>

From 2002-10, Denham served in the [[California State Senate]], representing the [[California's 12th State Senate district|12th District]], which includes [[Madera County, California|Madera]], [[Merced County, California|Merced]], [[Monterey County, California|Monterey]], [[San Benito County, California|San Benito]] and [[Stanislaus County, California|Stanislaus]] counties.

==Early life, education, and military service==
Enlisting at the age of 17, Denham served on active and reserve status for 16 years in the [[United States Air Force]] and is a recipient of the [[Meritorious Service Medal (United States)|Meritorious Service Medal]] for his service during [[Operation Desert Storm]] ([[Iraq]]) and [[Operation Restore Hope]] ([[Somalia]]).<ref name=wsj>{{cite web|title=Jeff Denham (R)|url=http://projects.wsj.com/campaign2012/candidates/view/jeff-denham--CA-H|website=Election 2012|publisher=Wall Street Journal|accessdate=14 November 2016}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|last1=Doyle|first1=Michael|title=Rep. Jeff Denham sues over ad in hotly contested California race|url=http://www.mcclatchydc.com/news/politics-government/election/article24739333.html|accessdate=14 November 2016|publisher=McClatchy DC|date=October 26, 2012}}</ref> In 2006, he was honored by the [[California National Guard]] with its highest military award, the "Order of California" service award, for distinguished service.

Denham received an associate's degree from [[Victor Valley College]] in 1989 and a B.A. in political science from [[California Polytechnic State University, San Luis Obispo]] in 1992.<ref>{{cite web|title=DENHAM, Jeff, (1967 - )|url=http://bioguide.congress.gov/scripts/biodisplay.pl?index=D000612|publisher=Biographical Directory of the United States Congress|accessdate=14 November 2016}}</ref> After graduating, Denham went into the [[Agriculture in the United States|agriculture industry]].

==2000 State Assembly campaign==
{{main article|California State Assembly election, 2000#District 28}}
In 2000, Denham ran for the [[California State Assembly]] against City Councilman [[Simon Salinas]], a Democrat, who defeated Denham with 52% of the vote. A total of $2.5 million was spent in the race.<ref>{{cite news|last1=Goldmacher|first1=Shane|title=Ex-assemblyman will challenge Sen. Denham in recall campaign|url=http://www.modbee.com/news/politics-government/election/article3104490.html|accessdate=2 March 2016|publisher=Sacramento Bee|date=April 4, 2008}}</ref>

==California State Senate==

===Elections===
;2002
Denham ran for the State Senate in 2002 against former Democratic State Assemblyman [[Rusty Areias]]. The election occurred on November 5, 2002, but the close vote count caused the lead in the race to go back and forth for several days.<ref>{{cite news|last1=Miller|first1=Jim|title=Denham says victory in 12th is guaranteed|url=http://www.modbee.com/2002/election/november/state/story/5192590p-6201114c.html|accessdate=March 2, 2016|publisher=Modesto Bee|date=November 14, 2002|deadurl=yes|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20030427002523/http://www.modbee.com/2002/election/november/state/story/5192590p-6201114c.html|archivedate=April 27, 2003}}</ref>

The race featured a number of negative political advertisements and it was one of the most expensive legislative races in state history with over $6.3 million spent.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.modbee.com/2002/election/november/state/story/5148192p-6157185c.html|title=Negative ads apparently paid off, as shown by Denham-Areias tilt|accessdate=July 21, 2007|deadurl=yes|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20051106170338/http://www.modbee.com/2002/election/november/state/story/5148192p-6157185c.html|archivedate=November 6, 2005}}</ref> His campaign highlighted Areias's financial difficulties, including unpaid taxes.<ref>{{cite news|last1=Caldwell|first1=Michelle|title=Rusty Areias takes another shot at the State Senate|url=http://www.montereycountyweekly.com/news/local_news/rusty-areias-takes-another-shot-at-the-state-senate/article_2b87fb72-0f12-53e1-ab0f-b346ebac1fd5.html|accessdate=2 March 2016|publisher=Monterrey County Now|date=October 17, 2002}}</ref>

Denham won the race 48%–47%, or by 1,843 votes.<ref>{{cite web|title=CA State Senate 12|url=http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=5747|publisher=Our Campaigns|accessdate=March 2, 2016}}</ref>

;2006
Denham won re-election to a second term with 58% of the vote.<ref>{{cite web|title=CA State Senate 12|url=http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=214061|publisher=Our Campaigns|accessdate=March 2, 2016}}</ref>

;2008 recall attempt
In 2008, a [[Recall election|recall]] effort was instigated against Denham by Democrat [[Don Perata]] after Denham declined to cast a deadlock-breaking vote on the state budget. Supporters of the recall turned in over 60,000 signatures, and a recall election was scheduled for June 3, 2008. Denham easily survived the recall election, with 77% of voters casting ballots to retain him.<ref>{{cite news|last1=Richman|first1=Josh|title=The Jeff Denham recall is a flop|url=http://www.ibabuzz.com/politics/2008/06/03/the-jeff-denham-recall-is-a-flop/|accessdate=2 March 2016|work=Political Blotter|publisher=Bay Area News Group|date=June 3, 2008}}</ref>

;2008 Lieutenant Governor and State Assembly campaigns
Denham announced in December 2008 that he would be a candidate for [[Lieutenant Governor of California]] in the [[California lieutenant gubernatorial election, 2010|2010 election]],<ref>{{cite web|url=http://blogs.sacbee.com/capitolalertlatest/2008/12/jeff-denham-ann.html|title=Jeff Denham announces run for lieutenant governor|publisher=The Sacramento Bee|date=December 11, 2008|accessdate=February 5, 2014}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.thecalifornian.com/article/20090512/NEWS01/90512022/Sen-John-McCain-endorses-Jeff-Denham-lt-governor?nclick_check=1 |title=Sen. John McCain endorses Jeff Denham for lt. governor|publisher=The Californian|date=May 12, 2009|accessdate=February 5, 2014|deadurl=yes|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20160119222001/http://www.thecalifornian.com/article/20090512/NEWS01/90512022/Sen-John-McCain-endorses-Jeff-Denham-lt-governor?nclick_check=1|archivedate=January 19, 2016}}</ref> ending his campaign when Governor [[Arnold Schwarzenegger]] appointed Republican State Senator [[Abel Maldonado]] to fill the vacancy left by former Lieutenant Governor [[John Garamendi]]'s election to Congress.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://articles.latimes.com/2009/nov/24/local/la-me-lt-gov24-2009nov24|title=Schwarzenegger taps Abel Maldonado for lieutenant governor|publisher=Los Angeles Times|date=November 24, 2009|accessdate=February 5, 2014|first=Shane|last=Goldmacher}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ibabuzz.com/politics/2010/04/26/breaking-down-the-maldonado-confirmation-vote|title=Breaking down the Maldonado confirmation vote|publisher=Political Blotter|date=April 26, 2010|accessdate=February 5, 2014}}</ref> Denham subsequently campaigned for the [[California State Assembly|State Assembly]] before being asked by retiring Republican Congressman [[George Radanovich]] to run for his open seat in {{ushr|California|19}} in December 2009.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.modbee.com/2009/12/10/967946/denham-quits-lt-gov-race-will.html|title=Denham quits Lt. ===Tenure===
During his time in the state legislature, Denham focused his efforts on education, agriculture and job creation. He called for a reduction in government waste in Sacramento, better use of state surplus assets and a crackdown on sexual predators.<ref name="Path to Power">{{cite news|title=Jeff Denham Path to Power|url=http://www.washingtonpost.com/politics/jeff-denham-r-calif/gIQA6BMZKP_topic.html#path-to-power|work=WhoRunsGov|publisher=The Washington Post|date=August 21, 2012}}</ref>

Denham's awards include Legislator of the Year by the California State Sheriffs' Association (2004), Outstanding Legislator by the California State Sheriff's Association (2005), Legislator of the Year by the California Small Business Association (2005), Most Progressive Law Enforcement Leader of the Year by A Woman's Place of Merced (2005), and [[California Teachers Association]] Gold Award (2005).<ref>{{cite web|title=Jeff Denham Biography|url=http://www.vvc.edu/Jeff%20Denham%20BIOGRAPHY.pdf|publisher=Victory Valley College|accessdate=26 May 2014}}</ref>

==U.S. House of Representatives==

===Elections===
;2010
{{Main article|United States House of Representatives elections in California, 2010}}
In December 2009, seven-term U.S. Congressman [[George Radanovich]] announced he would retire due to health reasons. He endorsed Denham as his successor.<ref>{{cite news|last=Blake|first=Aaron|title=Radanovich to retire|url=http://thehill.com/blogs/blog-briefing-room/news/73839-radanovich-to-retire|accessdate=May 26, 2014|newspaper=The Hill|date=December 29, 2009}}</ref>

In the Republican primary he defeated [[Fresno, California|Fresno]] mayor [[Jim Patterson (mayor)|Jim Patterson]], former U.S. Congressman [[Richard W. Pombo|Richard Pombo]], and Fresno City Councilman Larry Westerlund 36%–31%–21%–12%.<ref>{{cite web|title=CA District 19 - R Primary|url=http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=611938|publisher=Our Campaigns|accessdate=March 2, 2016}}</ref>

In the 2010 general election, Denham defeated Democrat Loraine Goodwin with 65% of the vote.<ref>{{cite web|title=CA - District 19|url=http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=488352|publisher=Our Campaigns|accessdate=March 2, 2016}}</ref>

;2012
{{Main article|United States House of Representatives elections in California, 2012}}
After redistricting, Denham decided to run in the newly redrawn [[California's 10th congressional district]]. Former [[NASA]] astronaut [[José Hernández (astronaut)|José Hernández]] ran against him as the Democratic candidate.<ref>{{cite web|title=CA - District 10 - Open Primary|url=http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=740815|publisher=Our Campaigns|accessdate=2 March 2016}}</ref> The 10th is a swing district with a 25% Hispanic voting age population.<ref>{{cite news|last1=Trygstad|first1=Kyle|title=Astronaut Challenging Freshman Jeff Denham|url=http://www.rollcall.com/issues/57_41/astronaut_challenging_freshman_jeff_denham-209437-1.html|accessdate=March 2, 2016|publisher=Roll Call|date=October 13, 2011}}</ref> Denham won with 53% of the vote to Democratic Hernández's 47%.

;2014
{{Main article|United States House of Representatives elections in California, 2014#District 10}}
In 2014, Denham ran unopposed in the Republican primary. Denham was expected to receive media endorsements over his Democratic opponent, Michael Eggman, a beekeeper,<ref>{{cite news|last1=Caine|first1=Eric|title=Election Post-Mortem|url=http://thevalleycitizen.com/election-post-mortem/|accessdate=March 2, 2016|publisher=The Valley Citizen|date=June 6, 2014}}</ref> although the seat was regarded by some as "vulnerable" earlier in the year.<ref>{{cite news|last1=Barragan Ruiz|first1=Olivia|title=Eggman lands a spot on ballot Latino Democrat will face Jeff Denham in November|url=http://www.vidaenelvalle.com/2014/06/09/2027988/politicspolitica-eggman-lands.html|accessdate=March 2, 2016 |publisher=Fresno Bee|date=June 10, 2014|deadurl=yes|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20140614001343/http://www.vidaenelvalle.com/2014/06/09/2027988/politicspolitica-eggman-lands.html|archivedate=June 14, 2014}}</ref>

The ''[[Rothenberg Political Report]]'' ultimately listed the race as "Republican Favored".<ref>{{cite news|title=House Ratings|url=https://rothenbergpoliticalreport.com/ratings/house|accessdate=October 20, 2014|publisher=Rothenberg Political Report|date=17 October 2014}}</ref> Denham defeated Eggman with 56% of the vote.<ref>{{cite web|title=Statement of the Vote November 4, 2014|url=http://elections.cdn.sos.ca.gov/sov/2014-general/pdf/2014-complete-sov.pdf|publisher=California Secretary of State|accessdate=March 2, 2016}}</ref>

;2016
{{Main article|United States House of Representatives elections in California, 2016#District 10}}
Denham ran for re-election in 2016.<ref>{{cite news|last1=Holland|first1=John|title=Challengers for Denham’s House seat range across spectrum|url=http://www.modbee.com/news/politics-government/election/article79094722.html|accessdate=May 24, 2016|publisher=Modesto Bee|date=May 21, 2016}}</ref> Denham finished first in the top-two primary on June 7, 2016. He faced second-place finisher, Democrat Michael Eggman, in the general election on November 8, 2016.<ref>{{cite news|title=California Primary Results|url=http://www.nytimes.com/elections/results/california|publisher=New York Times|accessdate=June 8, 2016}}</ref> Denham won re-election with 51.7% of the vote.<ref>{{cite news|last1=Holland|first1=John|title=Denham defeats Eggman for Modesto-area House seat|url=http://www.modbee.com/news/politics-government/election/article113530788.html|accessdate=November 11, 2016|publisher=Modesto Bee|date=November 8, 2016}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|last=Cockerham |first=Sean |url=http://www.kansas.com/news/politics-government/election/article113621003.html |title=Darrell Issa, Jeff Denham and Steve Knight win reelection &#124; The Wichita Eagle |website=Kansas.com |date= |accessdate=2016-11-12}}</ref>

===Tenure===
In the 114th Congress, Denham voted with his party 92.6% of the time.<ref>{{cite web|title=Voting With Party|url=https://www.opencongress.org/people/votes_with_party/house/republican|website=Open Congress|publisher=Sunlight Foundation|accessdate=2 March 2016}}</ref>

In July 2012, he criticized an event put on by the [[General Services Administration]], which cost $268,732. He said that he believes the controversy goes even further than the GSA.<ref name="GSA on event">{{cite news|last=Parni|first=Tarini|title=Lawmakers: GSA spent $270K on event|url=http://www.politico.com/news/stories/0712/78732.html|accessdate=17 August 2012|newspaper=[[Politico]]|date=19 July 2012}}</ref>

In October 2013, Denham co-sponsored the comprehensive immigration reform bill introduced by House Democrats. In announcing his support for the bill, he stated: "We can't afford any more delays. I support an earned path to citizenship to allow those who want to become citizens to demonstrate a commitment to our country, learn English, pay fines and back taxes and pass background checks."<ref>{{cite news|title=Immigration: California Republican Rep. Denham backs overhaul bill|author=Richard Simon|url=http://www.latimes.com/nation/politics/politicsnow/la-pn-immigration-denham-20131027,0,4393251.story#axzz2lClubayZ|publisher=LA Times|date=October 27, 2013|accessdate=November 20, 2013}}</ref>

Denham has argued that the national deficit is the biggest issue, which he called a threat to the nation's freedom. He has stressed his desire to use the economic downturn to reassess and find different ways to drive the United States forward.<ref name="Denham The Issues">{{cite news|title=Jeff Denham The Issues|url=http://www.washingtonpost.com/politics/jeff-denham-r-calif/gIQA6BMZKP_topic.html#the-issues|work=WhoRunsGov|publisher=The Washington Post|accessdate=August 17, 2012}}</ref>

In August 2014, Denham broke ranks with the Republican Party and voted against a bill that would have dismantled the [[Deferred Action for Childhood Arrivals]].<ref>{{cite news|last1=Foley|first1=Elise|title=House Votes To Strip Deportation Relief From Dreamers|url=http://www.huffingtonpost.com/2014/08/01/house-bill-daca_n_5643287.html|accessdate=4 September 2014|publisher=Huffington Post|date=August 1, 2014}}</ref>

==Personal life==
He owns and operates Denham Plastics, a supplier of reusable containers in the agriculture industry.<ref>{{cite news|last=Parsons|first=Larry|title=Salinas fire won't disrupt Denham Plastics operations, congressman says|url=http://www.montereyherald.com/ci_21179725/salinas-fire-wont-disrupt-denham-plastics-operations-congressman|accessdate=26 May 2014|newspaper=Monterey Herald|date=2012-07-27}}</ref> He and his family also farm almonds at their ranch in Merced County.<ref>{{cite news|last=Skelton|first=George|title=Recall is polluting spring air|url=http://articles.latimes.com/2008/mar/31/local/me-cap31|accessdate=26 May 2014|newspaper=Los Angeles Times|date=2008-03-31}}</ref>

Denham and his wife Sonia have two children.<ref name="About Denham">{{cite web|title=Meet Rep. Jeff Denham|url=http://denham.house.gov/about-me/full-biography|publisher=House of Representatives|accessdate=August 17, 2012}}</ref>
