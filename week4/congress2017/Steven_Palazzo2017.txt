'''Steven McCarty Palazzo''' (born February 21, 1970) is an [[United States|American]] politician who has been the [[United States House of Representatives|U.S. Representative]] for {{ushr|MS|4}} since 2011. The district takes in most of [[Mississippi]]'s share of the Gulf Coast and includes [[Biloxi]], [[Gulfport, Mississippi|Gulfport]], [[Pascagoula]], [[Laurel, Mississippi|Laurel]] and [[Hattiesburg, Mississippi|Hattiesburg]]. He is a member of the [[Republican Party (United States)|Republican Party]]. Palazzo defeated 10-term [[Democratic Party (US)|Democratic]] [[incumbent]] [[Gene Taylor (Mississippi)|Gene Taylor]] with 52% to 47% of the vote in the 2010 mid-term election. Previously, he served in the [[Mississippi House of Representatives]], having represented District 116 from 2006 to 2011. Palazzo again defeated Taylor in the [[United States House of Representatives elections in Mississippi, 2014|June 2014 Republican primary election]]. Taylor switched parties in a bid to return to his former House seat.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://thehill.com/blogs/ballot-box/senate-races/199634-ex-rep-gene-taylor-becomes-republican-to-run-again|title=Cameron Joseph, Ex-Rep. switches parties to run again|publisher=Thehill.com|accessdate=March 1, 2014}}</ref>

==Early life, education, and military service==
Palazzo was born February 21, 1970 in Gulfport.<ref name="state1">{{cite web|url=http://billstatus.ls.state.ms.us/members/house/palazzo.xml |accessdate=June 3, 2010 |deadurl=yes |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20100511074353/http://billstatus.ls.state.ms.us/members/house/palazzo.xml |archivedate=May 11, 2010 }}</ref> He enlisted in the [[Marine Corps Reserve]] in 1988, and served with the [[3rd Force Reconnaissance Company]] in the [[Gulf War|Persian Gulf War]]. He currently serves in the [[Mississippi Army National Guard]]. He received a [[Bachelor's]] and [[Master of Accountancy|MPA]] from the [[University of Southern Mississippi]], and is a member of the [[Sigma Chi]] fraternity. He graduated from [[Gulfport High School]] in 1988. He is a [[Certified Public Accountant]].

==Mississippi House of Representatives==

===Elections===
In April 2006, incumbent Republican State Representative Leonard Bentz of Mississippi's 116th House District resigned because he was appointed to the [[Mississippi Public Service Commission]]. Palazzo defeated Democratic candidate Maryann Graczyk, an education lobbyist, and Republican George Emile, a funeral home director, 51%–26%–24%.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=396624 |title=MS State House 116 – Special Election Race – Nov 07, 2006 |publisher=Ourcampaigns.com |date= |accessdate=2015-03-14}}</ref> In 2007, running unopposed, he was elected to a full term.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=396601 |title=MS State House 116 Race – Nov 06, 2007 |publisher=Ourcampaigns.com |date= |accessdate=2015-03-14}}</ref>

===Tenure===
Palazzo was awarded the 2009 Elected Official of the Year from the MS Wildlife Federation. The Mississippi Business Journal and the Sun Herald (2005) named Palazzo as a Top Business Leader under 40.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://palazzoforcongress.com/about-steven-palazzo |title=Steven Palazzo – U.S. Congress |publisher=Palazzoforcongress.com |date= |accessdate=2015-03-14}}</ref>

===Committee assignments===
He served on the Banking and Financial Services, Juvenile Justice, Labor, Select Committee on the Gulf Coast Disaster, and the Wildlife, Fisheries and Parks Committees.<ref name="state1"/>

==U.S. House of Representatives==

===Elections===

====2010====
{{See also|United States House of Representatives elections in Mississippi, 2010#District 4}}
Palazzo entered the Republican primary for [[Mississippi's 4th congressional district]] and won the nomination with 57% of the vote. He faced 10-term Democratic incumbent [[Gene Taylor (Mississippi)|Gene Taylor]] in the general election. Although the 4th has turned almost solidly Republican at the federal level, Taylor had held the seat without serious difficulty. His voting record had been very conservative even by [[Mississippi Democratic Party|Mississippi Democratic]] standards, and he'd often broken with his party. Palazzo established himself as Taylor's strongest opponent since 1996. In particular, he attacked Taylor for supporting [[Nancy Pelosi]] for House Speaker in 2006 and 2008.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.politico.com/news/stories/1010/44119.html|title=Taylor: I voted for McCain – Jessica Taylor|publisher=Politico.Com|accessdate=2010-11-03}}</ref> He was endorsed by [[Sarah Palin]] and in the November 2010 general election, Palazzo defeated Taylor 52%–47%.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=496790 |title=MS – District 04 Race – Nov 02, 2010 |publisher=Ourcampaigns.com |date= |accessdate=2015-03-14}}</ref>

====2012====
{{See also|United States House of Representatives elections in Mississippi, 2012#District 4}}
In the Republican primary, he defeated two challengers with 74% of the vote.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=751580 |title=MS District 04 – R Primary Race – Mar 13, 2012 |publisher=Ourcampaigns.com |date= |accessdate=2015-03-14}}</ref> In the November election, he defeated Democratic candidate Matthew Moore 64%–29%.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=724373 |title=MS – District 04 Race – Nov 06, 2012 |publisher=Ourcampaigns.com |date= |accessdate=2015-03-14}}</ref>

====2014====
{{See also|United States House of Representatives elections in Mississippi, 2014#District 4}}
In the Republican primary, Palazzo faced four challengers, including his predecessor, Taylor. Palazzo won 50.5% of the vote while Taylor won 43% of the vote. Three minor candidates made up the remaining percentage. Taylor actively campaigned and drew large numbers of Democrats into the Republican Primary.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://msbusiness.com/blog/2014/06/05/palazzo-defeats-taylor-4th-congressional-district-primary/ |title=Palazzo defeats Taylor in 4th Congressional District primary|publisher=Msbusiness.com |date=2014-06-05 |accessdate=2015-03-14}}</ref> Palazzo was targeted by the [[Club for Growth]].<ref>{{cite web|last=Jaffe |first=Alexandra |url=http://thehill.com/blogs/ballot-box/house-races/285193-club-for-growth-targeting-rino-republicans |title=Club for Growth targeting 9 'RINO' Republicans for primary challenges – The Hill's Ballot Box |publisher=Thehill.com |date=February 27, 2013 |accessdate=February 19, 2015}}</ref>

On Nov. 4th 2014, Palazzo easily defeated Democrat Challenger Matt Moore 69.9% to 24.3% four minor party or independent candidates accounted for 3.8% of the vote.  He had previously defeated Moore in 2012 64%–29%.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.sos.ms.gov/Elections-Voting/Pages/2014-General-Elections.aspx |title=2014 General Elections |publisher=Sos.ms.gov |date= |accessdate=2015-03-14}}</ref>

====2016====
In the Republican primary, Palazzo ran unopposed for the first time in his political career.  On November 8, Palazzo garnered 65.2% of the vote defeating Democrat Mark Gladney and two minor candidates.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.politico.com/2016-election/results/map/house/mississippi/ |title=2016 Mississippi House Election Results|date=2016-11-11 |accessdate=2016-11-12}}</ref>

===Tenure===
Palazzo voted with his Republican colleagues to pass a Balanced Budget Amendment, and force to repeal the [[Patient Protection and Affordable Care Act]].

===Legislation===
Palazzo was one of the initial co-sponsors of the [[Social Media Working Group Act of 2014 (H.R. 4263; 113th Congress)]], a bill that would direct the [[United States Secretary of Homeland Security]] to establish within the [[United States Department of Homeland Security]] (DHS) a [[social media]] working group (the Group) to provide guidance and best practices to the [[Emergency management|emergency preparedness and response]] community on the use of social media technologies before, during, and after a [[terrorist attack]].<ref name=4263sum>{{cite web|title=H.R. 4263 – Summary|url=https://beta.congress.gov/bill/113th-congress/house-bill/4263|publisher=United States Congress|accessdate=7 July 2014}}</ref> Palazzo stated that "social media has played a crucial role in emergency preparedness and response in Mississippi, including during disasters like Hurricane Isaac and the tornadoes that hit the Hattiesburg area a little over a year ago."<ref name=WXXVreport>{{cite news|last1=Thomas|first1=Rachael|title=Palazzo Introduces Social Media Emergency Response Bill|url=http://www.wxxv25.com/news/local/story/Palazzo-Introduces-Social-Media-Emergency/oXdyZRilcE282zWtHC9_qg.cspx|accessdate=9 July 2014|publisher=Fox News 25 – WXXV|date=17 March 2014}}</ref> He said that their goal with the bill was to "build upon existing public-private partnerships and use social media in a more strategic way in order to help save lives and property."<ref name="WXXVreport"/>

On April 7, 2014, Palazzo introduced the [[National Aeronautics and Space Administration Authorization Act of 2014 (H.R. 4412; 113th Congress)]], a bill that would [[authorization bill|authorize]] the [[appropriations bill (United States)|appropriation]] of $17.6 billion in fiscal year 2014 to the [[National Aeronautics and Space Administration]] (NASA).<ref name=4412sum>{{cite web|title=H.R. 4412 – Summary|url=http://beta.congress.gov/bill/113th-congress/house-bill/4412|publisher=United States Congress|accessdate=9 June 2014}}</ref><ref name=cbo4412>{{cite web|title=CBO – H.R. 4412|url=http://www.cbo.gov/publication/45376|publisher=Congressional Budget Office|accessdate=9 June 2014}}</ref> NASA would use the funding for human exploration of space, the [[Space Launch System]], the [[Orion (spacecraft)|Orion multipurpose crew vehicle]], the commercial crew program, the [[International Space Station]] (ISS), and various technological and educational projects.<ref name="4412sum"/><ref name=HillNASAreauth>{{cite news|last1=Marcos|first1=Cristina|title=House passes NASA reauthorization|url=http://thehill.com/blogs/floor-action/house/208739-house-passes-nasa-reauthorization|accessdate=10 June 2014|publisher=The Hill|date=9 June 2014}}</ref> Palazzo said that "American leadership in space depends on our ability to put people and sound policy ahead of politics."<ref name="HillNASAreauth"/>

==Personal life==
Palazzo is married to the former Lisa M. Belvin. He is a member of the Rotary, VFW,  NRA, MS Society of CPAs, USM Alumni Association; Boys & Girls Club Board Member; Marine Corps Association and AICPA.
