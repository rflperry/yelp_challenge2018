'''John Luigi Mica''' (born January 27, 1943) is an American businessman, consultant and [[United States Republican Party|Republican]] politician who has represented {{ushr|Florida|7|}} in the [[United States House of Representatives|U.S. House of Representatives]] since 1993. He was defeated by Democrat [[Stephanie Murphy]] in the November 8, 2016 general election while serving his 12th term in office.

==Early life, education, and business career==
Mica was born in [[Binghamton, New York]] and grew up in Florida. He was educated at [[Miami Edison High School]], [[Miami-Dade Community College]] and the [[University of Florida]], where he received a degree in education and was a member of [[Delta Chi]] Fraternity and [[Florida Blue Key]]. He has been a businessman serving in the real estate, telecommunications, government affairs and consulting fields.

== Early political career ==
Mica was a member of the [[Florida House of Representatives]] from 1976–80 and served on several committees, including the Appropriations Committee. He was a staff member for Senator [[Paula Hawkins|Paula F. Hawkins]] from 1981–85 and became her chief of staff.

==U.S. House of Representatives==

===Elections===
;1992
In 1992, Mica ran for Congress in the 7th District, previously the 4th District represented by two-term Republican [[Craig T. James]].

[[File:John L. Mica.jpg|150px|left|thumb|John Mica while a member of the Florida state legislature]]
Mica won the Republican primary with 53% of the vote, defeating State Representative [[Richard Graham (politician)|Richard Graham]] (34%) and Vaughn Forrest (13%).<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=368334 |title=FL District 07 – R Primary Race – Sep 01, 1992 |publisher=Our Campaigns |date= |accessdate=2012-10-31}}</ref> In the general election, he defeated Democrat Dan Webster 56%–44%.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=27629 |title=FL District 07 Race – Nov 03, 1992 |publisher=Our Campaigns |date= |accessdate=2012-10-31}}</ref>

;1994–2004
During this time period, he won re-election every two years with at least 60% of the vote.<ref name="ourcampaigns1">{{cite web|url=http://www.ourcampaigns.com/CandidateDetail.html?CandidateID=1026 |title=Candidate – John L. Mica |publisher=Our Campaigns |date= |accessdate=2012-10-31}}</ref>

;2006

Mica defeated Jack Chagnon 63%–37%.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=206805 |title=FL – District 07 Race – Nov 07, 2006 |publisher=Our Campaigns |date= |accessdate=2012-10-31}}</ref>

;2008
{{See also|United States House of Representatives elections in Florida, 2008#District 7}}

;2010
{{See also|United States House of Representatives elections in Florida, 2010#District 7}}

That district had previously been the 24th District, represented by freshman Republican [[Sandy Adams]].  Mica defeated Adams in the Republican primary with 61 percent of the vote.  In the general election, Mica defeated Jason Kendall 59%–41%.<ref>http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=736117</ref>

;2014

==Policy positions==

===Abortion===
Mica opposes abortion. Regarding taxpayer funding of abortion through [[Planned Parenthood]], he has said “I think the majority of Americans would oppose public, federal dollars going into abortion."<ref>{{cite news|last1=Martinez|first1=Jack|title=Facing Congress, Planned Parenthood CEO Defends Abortion as a Health Service|url=http://www.newsweek.com/planned-parenthood-ceo-testifies-hearing-abortion-378112|accessdate=26 September 2016|publisher=Newsweek|date=September 29, 2015}}</ref>

===Economic issues===
Mica voted against the [[American Recovery and Reinvestment Act of 2009|2009 stimulus]].<ref>{{cite news |last1=Finley |first1=Ben |title=Florida Primary Flier Frames Republican|url=http://www.factcheck.org/2012/07/florida-primary-flier-frames-republican/|accessdate=26 September 2016|publisher=FactCheck.org|date=July 20, 2012}}</ref> He has brought federal money for Florida highways, [[SunRail]], the Veterans Administration Medical Center, and the [[University of Central Florida]].<ref>{{cite news|last1=Powers|first1=Scott|title=John Mica gets tune-up election out of the way, set for Democratic challenge in CD 7|url=http://floridapolitics.com/archives/220744-john-mica-gets-tune-election-way-set-democratic-challenge-cd-7|accessdate=26 September 2016|publisher=Florida Politics|date=August 30, 2016}}</ref>

===Transportation===
Mica supports [[Amtrak]] [[privatization]].<ref>{{cite news | first = Larry | last = Hannan | title = John Mica, Corrine Brown split over privatizing Amtrak in Northeast | date = June 16, 2011 | url = http://jacksonville.com/news/florida/2011-06-16/story/john-mica-corrine-brown-split-over-privatizing-amtrak-northeast#ixzz29JHED1zX | work = [[The Florida Times-Union]] | accessdate = October 14, 2012}}</ref> In 2009, he earmarked $13 million for the [[Central Florida Commuter Rail]], which was supported by a client of Mica's daughter.<ref>{{cite news | first = Kenric | last = Ward | title = Report: Two Florida Congressmen Kept Earmarks in the Family | date = March 28, 2012 | url = http://www.sunshinestatenews.com/blog/report-two-florida-congressmen-kept-earmarks-family | work = Sunshine State News | accessdate = November 8, 2012}}</ref>

Mica has been opposed to the federal government contributing any additional funds towards repairing the Washington DC [[WMATA|Metrorail system]].<ref>{{cite news | first = Benjamin | last = Freed | title = Metro Chairman Tells Congress to Chip in $300 Million Per Year. Congress Yells at Metro. | date = April 13, 2016 | url = http://www.washingtonian.com/2016/04/13/metro-chairman-tells-congress-chip-300-million-per-year | work = Washingtonian Magazine | accessdate = April 14, 2016}}</ref>

===Marijuana===
As chairman of the House Oversight Committee's subcommittee on government operations, Mica convened a hearing on marijuana legalization in 1999. It was the first such hearing since 1988. Mica opposes the legalization of recreational marijuana.<ref>{{cite web|last1=Itkowitz|first1=Colby|title=Rep. Mica holds (another) hearing on being stoned|url=http://www.washingtonpost.com/blogs/in-the-loop/wp/2014/07/28/oversight-panel-holds-another-hearing-on-being-stoned/|website=www.washingtonpost.com|publisher=The Washington Post|accessdate=29 July 2014}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|last1=Fang|first1=Marina|title=Congressional Republicans Rail Against Legalization Of Marijuana|url=http://www.huffingtonpost.com/2014/07/31/colorado-marijuana-legalization_n_5638758.html|accessdate=26 September 2016|publisher=Huffington Post|date=July 31, 2014}}</ref>

==Personal life==
Mica is married to Patricia, a schoolteacher, and has two grown children. He lives in Winter Park, Florida. He is the brother of former Representative [[Daniel A. Mica]], while his other brother, David, is a former ranking staffer of Senator [[Lawton Chiles]]. He is a member of the Kennedy Center Board of Trustees and the Coast Guard Academy Board of Visitors.<ref>{{cite web|title=Company Overview of The John F. Kennedy Center for the Performing Arts|url=http://www.bloomberg.com/Research/stocks/private/person.asp?personId=231716548&privcapId=4279962&previousCapId=145449191&previousTitle=Infofree.com|publisher=Bloomberg|accessdate=26 September 2016}}</ref><ref>{{cite book|title=Congressional Record|date=July 2007|publisher=Government Printing Office|edition=V. 153, Pt. 15|accessdate=26 September 2016}}</ref>

Mica and his wife own six properties on [[Capitol Hill]]. From 2006–13, they paid $1,071.81 in 17 penalty payments for paying their property taxes late.<ref>{{cite news |first=Will |last=Sommer |title=District-Bashing Congressman Doesn't Pay His Own D.C. Taxes On Time |date=April 18, 2016 |url=http://www.washingtoncitypaper.com/blogs/looselips/2016/04/18/district-bashing-congressman-doesnt-pay-his-own-d-c-taxes-on-time/#more-29348 |work=Washington City Paper |accessdate=April 18, 2016}}</ref>
