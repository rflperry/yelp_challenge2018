'''Joseph John “Joe” Heck''' (born October 30, 1961) is an [[United States|American]] politician, physician, and [[U.S. Army]] [[Brigadier general (United States)|Brigadier General]] who has been the [[United States House of Representatives|U.S. Representative]] for [[Nevada's 3rd congressional district]] since 2011. Heck, a member of the [[Republican Party (United States)|Republican Party]], is a board-certified [[physician]] and served as a [[Nevada Senate|Nevada State Senator]] from 2004-08. He ran unsuccessfully against Democrat [[Catherine Cortez Masto]] in the general election for the open Nevada [[United States Senate]] seat in [[United States Senate election in Nevada, 2016|2016]]. In the same year, Heck made headlines by joining a [[List of Republicans who opposed Donald Trump presidential campaign, 2016|long list of Republicans who opposed the GOP nominee]] for President, [[Donald Trump]].<ref name=PoliticoHeckHardy>{{cite web |url=http://www.politico.com/story/2016/10/nevada-heck-hardy-trump-229354 |title=Heck, Hardy withdraw Trump support in Nevada |last=Schneider |first=Elena |date=October 8, 2016 |work=[[Politico]] |accessdate=October 8, 2016}}</ref>

Heck's congressional district includes the cities of [[Henderson, Nevada|Henderson]], [[Boulder City]], parts of [[Las Vegas metropolitan area|Las Vegas]], unincorporated [[Clark County, Nevada|Clark County]], and several smaller towns and cities in the Southern Nevada region. He resides in [[Henderson, Nevada]].

==Early life, education, and military service==
Heck was born in [[Jamaica, New York]], and was raised in Pennsylvania, where he graduated from [[Wallenpaupack Area School District|Wallenpaupack Area High School]] in 1979. He graduated from the [[Pennsylvania State University]] in 1984 with a degree in [[Health Education]]. He is a member of the [[Beta Theta Pi]] fraternity. He received his [[Doctor of Osteopathic Medicine]] in 1988 from the [[Philadelphia College of Osteopathic Medicine]] and obtained a residency in [[Emergency Medicine]] in 1992 at the [[Albert Einstein Medical Center]]. In 1992, he moved to Clark County, Nevada. He earned a [[Strategic Studies|Masters of Strategic Studies]] degree from the [[U.S. Army War College]] in 2006.<ref>{{cite news|last1=Corasaniti|first1=Nick|last2=Martin|first2=Jonathan|title=Army War College Starts Plagiarism Inquiry of Senator John Walsh’s Thesis|url=http://www.nytimes.com/2014/07/25/us/politics/plagiarism-raises-ethical-alarm-at-military-school.html|accessdate=5 February 2016|publisher=New York Times|date=July 24, 2014}}</ref>

Heck served in the [[United States Army Reserve]] since 1991 and was promoted to [[Brigadier general (United States)|brigadier general]] in 2014.<ref>{{cite news|title=Senate OKs Rep. Joe Heck's promotion to 1-star general|url=http://lasvegassun.com/news/2014/sep/18/senate-oks-rep-joe-hecks-promotion-1-star-general|accessdate=January 28, 2016|agency=Associated Press|publisher=Las Vegas Sun|date=September 18, 2014}}</ref> He has commanded a Medical Readiness Support Group overseeing more than 2,000 soldiers in 6 western states, and continues to serve in this capacity while in Congress. He has served in [[Operation Joint Endeavor]], [[Operation Noble Eagle]], and [[Operation Iraqi Freedom]]. His last deployment was in January 2008 when he commanded an emergency room in a combat hospital outside Baghdad.<ref>{{cite news|last1=O'Toole|first1=Molly|title=Meet Joe Heck, the GOP One-Star General Who Could Take Reid’s Senate Seat|url=http://www.defenseone.com/politics/2015/05/meet-joe-heck-gop-one-star-general-who-could-take-reids-senate-seat/114076|accessdate=February 5, 2016|publisher=Defense One|date=May 31, 2015}}</ref><ref name=LVRJ>{{cite news|last1=Tetreault|first1=Steve|title=Rep. Joe Heck says he's running for US Senate|url=http://www.reviewjournal.com/politics/elections/rep-joe-heck-says-he-s-running-us-senate|accessdate=April 26, 2016|publisher=Las Vegas Review-Journal|date=July 6, 2015}}</ref>

==Medical career==
Heck was the president, owner, and medical director of Specialized Medical Operations until 2011. The company provided medical training, consulting, and operational support to law enforcement agencies, EMS, and military special operations. Heck has lectured and is published on special operations medical support, the medical response to acts of terrorism, and emergency preparedness and response.<ref>{{cite news|title=Joe Heck (R)|url=http://projects.wsj.com/campaign2012/candidates/view/joe-heck--NV-H|accessdate=February 5, 2016|publisher=Wall Street Journal}}</ref>

From 1998 to 2003, Heck served as the medical director of the Casualty Care Research Center of the [[Uniformed Services University of the Health Sciences]] in [[Bethesda, Maryland]], where he provided medical support for several federal law enforcement agencies and oversight for the medical response to acts of terrorism. Heck started his medical career as a [[volunteer firefighter]] and ambulance attendant in rural Pennsylvania. He volunteered as a Medical Team Manager with the Nevada Urban Search & Rescue Team&nbsp;– Task Force 1 and as a member of the [[Las Vegas Metropolitan Police Department]] Search & Rescue team. He served as a tactical physician with the LVMPD SWAT team.<ref name=LVRJ/>

Heck served as a member of the Nevada State Homeland Security Commission Sub-committee on Health, the [[American Osteopathic Association|American Osteopathic Association's]] Task Force on Bioterrorism, and as the Medical Director for the Nevada Hospital Association’s Hospital Preparedness program. He also served as the Medical Director for the Southern Nevada Health District's Office of Public Health Preparedness.<ref name="leg.state.nv.us">{{cite web|url=http://www.leg.state.nv.us/73rd/Legislators/Senators/Heck.cfm|title=Senator Joseph (Joe) J. Heck|work=state.nv.us}}</ref>

==Nevada Senate==
Heck served one four-year term in the Nevada Senate, representing Clark County’s 5th district.<ref name="leg.state.nv.us"/>

===Elections===
Heck was first elected to the Nevada Senate to represent Clark County's 5th district in 2004, after defeating Senator Ann O’Connell in the Republican primary. Heck narrowly lost re-election in 2008 to [[Democratic Party (United States)|Democrat]] [[Shirley Breeden]] by a margin of 47% to 46% and a plurality of 765 votes. Libertarian T. Rex Hagan received 4,754 votes (8%).<ref>{{cite web|title=Races for the November 4, 2008 general election|url=http://lasvegassun.com/elections/2008/nov/04/races|publisher=Las Vegas Sun|accessdate=February 5, 2016}}</ref>

===Committee assignments===
He served on the Natural Resources, Human Resources and Education, the Commerce and Labor Committees. He was also the vice-chair of the Transportation and Homeland Security Committee.<ref name="Committee Assignments">{{cite web|title=Joe Heck|url=https://www.gop.gov/member/joe-heck|publisher=House Republicans|accessdate=October 19, 2016}}</ref>

==U.S. House of Representatives==

===Elections===

====2010====
{{See also|United States House of Representatives elections in Nevada, 2010#District 3|Nevada gubernatorial election, 2010}}
Although Heck had earlier announced he would challenge incumbent Republican [[Jim Gibbons (U. ====2012====

On November 6, he defeated Speaker of the [[Nevada Assembly]] [[John Oceguera]] 50%–43%.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=705010|title=Our Campaigns - NV - District 03 Race|date=November 6, 2012|work=ourcampaigns.com}}</ref>

====2014====

====Energy and environmental policy====
In 2011, as a Representative, Heck voted to prevent the [[EPA]] from regulating [[greenhouse gas]]es.<ref>{{cite web|title=Final Vote Results for Roll Call 249|url=http://clerk.house.gov/evs/2011/roll249.xml|website=clerk.house.gov|accessdate=11 November 2016}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=House Vote 249 – Prevents E.P.A. From Regulating Greenhouse Gases|url=http://politics.nytimes.com/congress/votes/112/house/1/249?ref=politics|publisher=New York Times|accessdate=April 22, 2014}}</ref>

In 2012, when asked about climate change and regulating carbon dioxide, he stated: “When you start looking at trying to regulate something like carbon dioxide, which is a natural, biological process, you start running into areas of confusion” and “I think certainly over the millennia, we've seen changes in our climate both ways, and I think throughout the future millennia we will continue to see climate change that goes both ways. But the issue for this election is not what's going to be happening in the next 200 years, it's going to be what's happening in the next 12 months.”<ref>Jason Margolis, [http://www.pri.org/stories/2012-10-17/politicians-talking-green-energy-not-climate-change “Politicians Talking Green Energy, But Not Climate Change”], PRI.org, October 17, 2012.</ref>

In 2010, he signed the [[Americans for Prosperity]]'s No Climate Tax pledge.<ref>{{cite news|last1=Kerpen|first1=Phil|title=Americans for Prosperity Applauds U.S. House Candidate Joe Heck|url=http://site.americansforprosperity.org/noclimatetax//wp-content/uploads/2010/03/heck.pdf|accessdate=February 5, 2016|publisher=Americans for Prosperity|date=March 9, 2010}}</ref> He supports an "all of the above" energy policy which includes natural gas, domestic oil production, and alternative energy sources such as wind, solar, hydropower, geothermal and nuclear.<ref>{{cite web|title=Issue Position: Energy|url=http://www.votesmart.org/public-statement/626589/issue-position-energy|accessdate=November 21, 2011}}</ref>

He is in favor of the [[Keystone XL Pipeline]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://votesmart.org/bill/15085/39638/44082/extension-of-surface-transportation-funding-and-approval-of-the-keystone-xl-pipeline#.WAVLJ_krKHs|title=The Voter's Self Defense System|publisher=votesmart.org}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.reviewjournal.com/news/nation-and-world/keystone-xl-pipeline-shelved-senate-vote|title=Keystone XL pipeline shelved in Senate vote|author=Steve Tetreault|date=November 23, 2014|publisher=reviewjournal.com}}</ref>

Heck voted to ease the exploration and extraction of minerals and energy resources from [[Native Americans in the United States|Native American]] lands, and restrict the ability of non-resident tribal members to vote on these issues.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://votesmart.org/bill/20469/53997/44082/native-american-energy-act#.WAVFZPkrKHs|title=The Voter's Self Defense System|publisher=votesmart.org}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://votesmart.org/bill/17410/46341/44082/authorizes-the-secretary-of-the-interior-to-require-certain-findings-for-categorical-exclusions#.WAVG0fkrKHs|title=The Voter's Self Defense System|publisher=votesmart.org}}</ref>

====Economic policy====
Heck supports an audit of the Federal Reserve and a [[Balanced budget amendment|balanced budget amendment to the Constitution]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Joe Heck (R)|url=http://projects.washingtonpost.com/congress/members/H001055/|website=U.S. Congress Vote Database|publisher=Washington Post|accessdate=5 February 2016}}</ref> Heck voted against increasing the debt limit in 2011, stating "Raising the debt ceiling without significant spending cuts will only prolong the uncertainty preventing an economic recovery".<ref>{{cite web|title=Heck Votes Against Debt Limit Increase|url=http://www.votesmart.org/public-statement/612747/heck-votes-against-debt-limit-increase|accessdate=November 21, 2011}}</ref>

In 2011, Heck called [[Social Security (United States)|Social Security]] a "pyramid scheme".<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Y6MGfETizuQ|title=REP. JOE HECK: Social Security is a "Pyramid Scheme"|first=|last=Americans United For Change|date=June 2, 2011|publisher=|via=YouTube}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.lasvegasnow.com/news/joe-heck-says-social-security-is-inverted-pyramid|title=Joe Heck Says Social Security is Inverted Pyramid|date=June 10, 2011|publisher=}}</ref> The remark aroused a political controversy in Nevada, and Heck clarified that he meant to refer to it as an "inverted pyramid".<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.lasvegasnow.com/news/joe-heck-vows-to-preserve-social-security|title=Joe Heck Vows to Preserve Social Security|date=June 9, 2011|publisher=lasvegasnow.com}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.kolotv.com/home/headlines/Heck-Campaigns-in-Reno-Answers-Social-Security-Charge-321501971.html|title=Heck Campaigns in Reno, Answers Social Security Charge|first=Ed|last=Pearce|publisher=kolotv.com}}</ref> He has suggested that today's young people may need to retire later to keep the program fiscally viable.<ref name="auto1">{{cite web|url=http://www.ktvn.com/story/29764242/rep-joe-heck-campaigns-in-reno|title=Rep. Joe Heck Campaigns in Reno|first=Paul|last=Nelson|publisher=ktvn.com|accessdate=November 9, 2016}}</ref>

In 2015, Heck voted to eliminate the estate tax.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://votesmart.org/bill/20603/54353/44082/death-tax-repeal-act-of-2015#.WAVIM_krKHs|title=The Voter's Self Defense System|publisher=}}</ref> In 2010, he signed the Taxpayer Protection Pledge by [[Americans for Tax Reform]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.atr.org/current-list-taxpayer-protection-pledge-signers-a5597|title=Current List of Taxpayer Protection Pledge Signers for the 112th Congress|publisher=atr.org}}</ref> He has been critical of Governor [[Brian Sandoval]]'s Commerce Tax.<ref name="auto1"/> He is opposed to raising the [[federal minimum wage]] in favor of leaving the decision to local governments.<ref name="auto"/><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.senate.gov/legislative/LIS/roll_call_lists/roll_call_vote_cfm.cfm?congress=113&session=2&vote=00117|title=U.S. Senate: Roll Call Vote|publisher=Senate.gov|accessdate=2016-11-03}}</ref>

====Foreign policy and veterans affairs====
In 2011, Heck voted for the [[National Defense Authorization Act for Fiscal Year 2012]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ibtimes.com/ndaa-bill-how-did-your-congress-member-vote-384362|title=NDAA Bill: How Did Your Congress Member Vote?|author=Connor Adams Sheets|date=16 December 2011|work=International Business Times}}</ref>  In 2011, Heck voted not to withdraw American troops from the [[War in Afghanistan (2001–2014)|war in Afghanistan]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ontheissues.org/House/Joe_Heck_War_+_Peace.htm|title=Joe Heck on War & Peace|publisher=Ontheissues.org|accessdate=November 3, 2016}}</ref>

Heck opposed United States involvement in Libya, saying, “We are already engaged in military operations on two fronts, and Libya opened a third. We cannot afford the troops or taxpayer dollars—especially without a national security objective.”<ref>{{cite web|title=Heck Votes Against Libya Measures|url=http://www.votesmart.org/public-statement/622143/heck-votes-against-libya-measures|accessdate=November 21, 2011|publisher=votesmart.org}}</ref>

Heck opposes the [[Iran nuclear deal framework]], calling it unenforceable,<ref>{{cite news|last1=Botkin|first1=Ben|title=Cortez Masto supports Iran deal while Heck calls it unenforceable|url=http://www.reviewjournal.com/politics/national/cortez-masto-supports-iran-deal-while-heck-calls-it-unenforceable|accessdate=February 19, 2016|publisher=Las Vegas Review Journal|date=January 26, 2016}}</ref> and has voted to censure and block President Obama's nuclear treaty with Iran.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://votesmart.org/candidate/key-votes/44082/joe-heck-jr/32/foreign-affairs#.WAVEgfkrKHs|title=The Voter's Self Defense System|publisher=votesmart.org}}</ref>

Heck has voted for the [[Patriot Act]] and has endorsed the indefinite military incarceration of alleged terrorists.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://votesmart.org/candidate/key-votes/44082/joe-heck-jr/13/civil-liberties-and-civil-rights#.WAU5f_krKHs|title=The Voter's Self Defense System|publisher=votesmart.org}}</ref> He voted to end the bulk collection of metadata from phone calls by the NSA.<ref name=RGJ/>

In 2015, Heck cosponsored a bill with Democrat [[Tulsi Gabbard]] that would award a [[Congressional Gold Medal]] to [[Military History of the Philippines during World War II|Filipinos who fought in World War II]], who now live in the [[Philippines]] and the United States.<ref>{{cite news|last=Rueda|first=Nimfa|title=US solons introduce bill recognizing Filipino, Fil-Am war vets|url=http://globalnation.inquirer.net/124574/us-solons-introduce-bill-recognizing-filipino-fil-am-war-vets|newspaper=Philippine Daily Inquirer|accessdate=June 12, 2015}}</ref>

Heck authored the Vulnerable Veterans Housing Reform Act which prevents disabled veterans who receive in-home care from the VA from having their housing benefits reduced. The bill became law in 2016.<ref name=RGJ>{{cite news|last=Theobald|first=William|title=Who's Joe Heck?: A reliable GOP vote who sometimes bucked the party line|url=http://www.rgj.com/story/news/politics/2016/10/20/whos-joe-heck-reliable-gop-vote-who-sometimes-bucked-party-line/90855946|newspaper=Reno-Gazette Journal|date=October 20, 2016|accessdate=November 7, 2016}}</ref>

====Gun policy====
Heck opposes most laws which restrict the sale of firearms. He is in favor of expanded and perhaps universal [[background check]]s.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.lasvegasnow.com/news/gun-control-universal-background-checks-at-forefront-nationally-statewide|title=Gun control: Universal background checks at forefront nationally, statewide|first=Patrick|last=Walker|date=June 17, 2016|publisher=}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.reviewjournal.com/news/politics/heck-favors-universal-background-checks-gun-buyers|title=Heck favors universal background checks for gun buyers|first=Laura Myers|last=REVIEW-JOURNAL|date=February 19, 2013|publisher=reviewjournal.com}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.nbcnews.com/id/50875706/t/gop-rep-implies-gabby-giffords-prop-gun-debate/#.WAYd1_krKHs|title=GOP rep implies Gabby Giffords is a ‘prop’ in gun debate|date=February 20, 2013|publisher=nbcnews.com}}</ref>

Following the [[2014 Isla Vista killings|Isla Vista massacre]], Heck co-sponsored an amendment to increase funding for criminal background checks.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.msnbc.com/msnbc/house-approves-background-check-funding-ammendment|title=House approves gun control bill to boost background check funding|publisher=MSNBC}}</ref> He voted against restricting gun sales to those on terror watch lists.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.nytimes.com/2016/09/05/us/politics/gun-control-groups-divide-their-loyalties-in-senate-races.html|title=Gun Control Groups Divide Their Loyalties in Senate Races|date=September 5, 2016|work=The New York Times}}</ref> He voted to prevent [[Washington D.C.]] from implementing many provisions of [[gun control]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://votesmart.org/bill/18726/49464/44082/prohibits-district-of-columbia-from-implementing-certain-firearm-laws#.WAVAQ_krKHs|title=The Voter's Self Defense System|publisher=votesmart.org}}</ref>

====Health policy====

====Social policy====
In 2011, he voted to prohibit federal funding of [[National Public Radio]], and to support the continuing use of federal funds for [[NASCAR]] sponsorships.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://votesmart.org/candidate/key-votes/44082/joe-heck-jr/7/arts-entertainment-and-history#.WAU8b_krKHs|title=The Voter's Self Defense System|publisher=votesmart.org}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=Final Vote Results for Roll Call 192|url=http://clerk.house.gov/evs/2011/roll192.xml|accessdate=11 November 2016}}</ref>

In 2012, he voted to reauthorize the expiring [[Violence Against Women Act]].<ref>{{cite news|author=Jennifer Bendery|url=http://www.huffingtonpost.com/2012/12/11/violence-against-women-act-john-boehner-eric-cantor_n_2278549.html|title=Violence Against Women Act: John Boehner, Eric Cantor Pressured By Republicans To Act|date=December 11, 2012|publisher=Huffington Post}}</ref>

During his time in the House, Heck has voted in favor of prohibiting federal funding of abortion, and prohibiting the use of federal funds for health services at [[Planned Parenthood]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://votesmart.org/candidate/key-votes/44082/joe-heck-jr/2/abortion#.WAU7WfkrKHs|title=The Voter's Self Defense System|publisher=votesmart.org}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ktnv.com/news/political/politifact-new-ads-mostly-accurate-in-highlighting-joe-hecks-record-on-abortion|title=PolitiFact: New ads mostly accurate in highlighting Joe Heck's record on abortion|first=Riley|last=Snyder|date=September 29, 2016|publisher=ktnv.com}}</ref>

====Campaign finance policy====
Heck is opposed to the [[DISCLOSE Act]], which would require funders of political ads to put their names on advertisements. He is a supporter of the ''[[Citizens United v. FEC]]'' U.S. Supreme Court decision.<ref name="auto"/>

===Sponsored legislation===
The following is a partial list of legislation that was directly sponsored by Heck.
* [[Three Kids Mine Remediation and Reclamation Act (H.R. 697; 113th Congress)]] – a bill that would authorize the sale of approximately 950 acres of federal land to the city of [[Henderson, Nevada]].<ref name=697sum>{{cite web|title=H.R. 697 – Summary|url=http://beta.congress.gov/bill/113th-congress/house-bill/697|publisher=United States Congress|accessdate=22 July 2013}}</ref>  The land used to be a mine and now needs significant environmental remediation and reclamation.<ref name=cbo697>{{cite web|title=CBO&nbsp;– H.R. 697|url=http://www.cbo.gov/publication/44357|publisher=Congressional Budget Office|accessdate=22 July 2013}}</ref>
* [[Vulnerable Veterans Housing Reform Act of 2013 (H.R. 1742; 113th Congress)]] – a bill that would change the way the [[United States Department of Housing and Urban Development]] (HUD) calculates a veterans' income to exclude some aid that some veterans receive from the [[United States Department of Veterans Affairs]] (VA).<ref name=nlihcMay3>{{cite web|title=Bills to Address Housing Needs of Veterans Introduced|url=http://nlihc.org/article/bills-address-housing-needs-veterans-introduced|publisher=National Low Income Housing Coalition|accessdate=October 28, 2013|date=May 3, 2013}}</ref> The bill would also change utility allowances.<ref name=1742sum>{{cite web|title=H.R. 1742|url=http://beta.congress.gov/bill/113th-congress/house-bill/1742|publisher=United States Congress|accessdate=October 28, 2013}}</ref>
* [[Good Samaritan Search and Recovery Act of 2013 (H.R. 2166; 113th Congress)]] – a bill that would require the federal government to issue the appropriate permits within 48 hours to volunteer search and rescue groups to allow them to search [[federal lands]] for [[missing person]]s.<ref name=2166sum>{{cite web|title=H.R. 2166 – Summary|url=http://beta.congress.gov/bill/113th/house-bill/2166|publisher=United States Congress|accessdate=January 27, 2014}}</ref><ref name=houselifthurdles>{{cite news|last=Kasperowicz|first=Pete|title=House to lift government hurdles to missing person searches|url=http://thehill.com/blogs/floor-action/house/196357-house-to-lift-government-hurdles-to-missing-person-searches|accessdate=January 27, 2014|newspaper=The Hill|date=January 24, 2014}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|last1=Tetreault|first1=Steve|last2=Brean|first2=Henry|title=Heck seeks to cut red tape on desert, lake searches|url=http://www.reviewjournal.com/news/fire-rescue/heck-seeks-cut-red-tape-desert-lake-searches|accessdate=5 February 2016|publisher=Las Vegas Review Journal|date=May 7, 2013}}</ref>

==2016 U.S. Senate campaign==
{{main article|United States Senate election in Nevada, 2016}}
In July 2015, Heck announced that he would run for the [[United States Senate election in Nevada, 2016|U.S. Senate seat]] left open due to [[Harry Reid]]'s retirement.<ref>{{cite news|last1=Tetreault|first1=Steve|last2=Botkin|first2=Ben|title=Rep. Joe Heck says he’s running for US Senate|url=http://www.reviewjournal.com/politics/elections/rep-joe-heck-says-he-s-running-us-senate|accessdate=January 28, 2016|publisher=Las Vegas Review-Journal|date=July 6, 2015}}</ref> The Republican and Democratic primaries, which were both contested, took place on June 14, 2016.<ref>{{cite news|last1=Messerly|first1=Megan|title=Heck, Cortez Masto each top $1M for quarter in bid to succeed Reid in Senate|url=http://lasvegassun.com/news/2016/feb/02/tight-fundraising-race-in-bid-to-succeed-reid-in-s/|accessdate=5 February 2016|publisher=Las Vegas Sun|date=February 2, 2016}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.nytimes.com/elections/results/nevada|title= Nevada Primary Results 2016|author=Staff|publisher= New York Times|accessdate=2015-07-17}}</ref>

Heck easily defeated primary opponent [[Sharron Angle]], who had narrowly lost to [[Harry Reid]] in 2010. Heck's campaign received millions of dollars of indirect support from the [[Koch brothers]], according to the ''New York Times''. The Kochs paid for ads on his behalf and for millions of dollars of ads against his rival,<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.nytimes.com/2016/08/31/us/politics/harry-reid-koch-brothers-nevada-senate-race.html|title=Harry Reid and Koch Brothers Wage Proxy War for a Nevada Senate Seat|date=August 31, 2016|work=The New York Times}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.opensecrets.org/news/2016/08/harry-reids-retiring-enter-piles-of-dark-money|title=Harry Reid's retiring? Enter piles of dark money|date=August 19, 2016|publisher=opensecrets/org}}</ref><ref name="lasvegassun.com">{{cite web|url=http://lasvegassun.com/news/2016/may/06/new-ad-takes-aim-at-joe-hecks-record-in-congress|title=New ad takes aim at Joe Heck's record in state Senate|date=May 6, 2016|publisher=}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://lasvegassun.com/news/2016/mar/01/koch-brothers-backed-ad-trumpets-joe-heck-for-sena|title=Koch brothers-backed ad trumpets Joe Heck for Senate|date=March 1, 2016|publisher=Las Vegas Sun}}</ref> and whose organizations have 30 paid staff members working in Nevada.<ref name="wnem.com">{{cite web|url=http://www.wnem.com/story/33360832/heavy-hitters-cash-flow-to-nevada-in-fight-to-replace-reid|title=Heavy-hitters, cash flow to Nevada in fight to replace Reid|publisher=wnem.com}} {{dead link|date=November 2016}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.rgj.com/story/news/politics/2016/10/11/heck-and-cortez-masto-outspent-outside-groups/91747818|title=Outside groups are the biggest influencers in this year's Nevada Senate race|publisher=rgj.com|date=October 11, 2016}}</ref> Heck was, in the 3rd quarter of 2016, the House member receiving the largest amount of political donations.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://lasvegassun.com/news/2013/jul/16/joe-heck-leads-pack-campaign-contributions|title=Joe Heck leads the pack in campaign contributions|date=July 16, 2013|publisher=Las Vegas Sun}}</ref> His opponents, including organized labor and environmental groups, spent significant sums on advertising against him.<ref name="lasvegassun.com"/><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.rgj.com/story/news/politics/2016/09/06/environmental-group-runs-ad-attacking-gop-senate-candidate/89923642|title=Environmental group runs ad attacking GOP Senate candidate|publisher=rgj.com|date=September 6, 2016}}</ref>

Heck supported [[Donald Trump]]'s candidacy for President until the [[Donald Trump and Billy Bush recording controversy]] of October 2016. Heck then withdrew his support.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://drjoeheck.com/news-releases/dr-heck-remarks-donald-trump/|title=Dr. Heck remarks on Donald Trump - Joe Heck for U.S. Senate|date=October 8, 2016|publisher=drjoeheck.com}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.reviewjournal.com/politics/election-2016/republicans-heck-hardy-say-they-no-longer-support-trump|title=Republican Heck, Hardy say they no longer support Trump|author=Kimber Laux &#124; Las Vegas Review-Journal|date=October 8, 2016|publisher=reviewjournal.com |deadurl=no |accessdate=November 10, 2016}}</ref>

In a recording of Heck at a private event in October 2016, he said he believed Trump may hurt other Republicans' electoral bids.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.rgj.com/story/news/politics/2016/10/17/recording-shows-heck-fears-trump-will-hurt-senate-race/92315410|title=Recording shows Heck fears Trump will hurt Senate race|publisher=rgj.com|date=October 17, 2016}}</ref> Prior to the election, he did not say whether or not he would vote for Trump.<ref>{{cite web|author=Associated Press|url=https://lasvegassun.com/news/2016/nov/02/joe-heck-struggles-to-answer-on-vote-for-trump|title=Joe Heck struggles to answer on vote for Donald Trump|publisher=Las Vegas Sun News|accessdate=2016-11-03}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://elkodaily.com/news/state-and-regional/critics-say-heck-making-misstep-on-trump-stance/article_74f1fb26-c8f4-56da-a6ef-4a1bf6124a0b.html|title=Critics say Heck making misstep on Trump stance|publisher=elkodaily.com|date=1970-01-01|accessdate=2016-11-03}}</ref>

Heck lost to [[Catherine Cortez Masto]] in the 2016 general election, held on November 8, 2016.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.politico.com/story/2016/11/cortez-masto-heck-nevada-senate-race-2016-election-results-231040|title=Cortez Masto defeats Heck in Nevada|publisher=}}</ref>

==Personal life==
Joe and his wife, Lisa Heck (née Mattiello), have three children: Monica, Chelsea and Joseph III. Lisa is a registered nurse. Heck is a [[Roman Catholic]]. He believes that employers have the right to deny health coverage for contraception to women employees if they have moral objections to it.<ref name="auto"/> He is active with the [[American Legion]] Paradise Post 149, the [[Knights of Columbus]] Council 13456, and Catholic War Veterans Post 1947.<ref>{{cite web|title=Full Biography|url=https://heck.house.gov/about-joe/full-biography|publisher=United States Congressman Joe Heck|accessdate=February 5, 2016}}</ref> Heck cited his wife's abuse in a prior relationship when distancing himself from Donald Trump.{{citation needed|date=November 2016}}
