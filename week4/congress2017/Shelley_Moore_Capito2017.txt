'''Shelley Wellons Moore Capito''' (born November 26, 1953) is the [[Seniority in the United States Senate|junior]] [[United States Senate|United States Senator]] from [[West Virginia]]. She was a member of the [[United States House of Representatives]] for {{ushr|West Virginia|2}} from 2001 until her election to the [[United States Senate]] in 2014.

A graduate of [[Duke University]] and the [[University of Virginia]], she is the daughter of the late [[Governor of West Virginia]] [[Arch A. Moore, Jr.|Arch Moore]].  Capito was the only [[Republican Party (United States)|Republican]] in the [[West Virginia]] congressional delegation until 2011 and the first Republican woman elected to [[United States Congress|Congress]] from West Virginia.  Capito was elected to the Senate in [[United States Senate election in West Virginia, 2014|2014]], becoming the first [[Women in the United States Senate|woman elected to the U.S. Senate]] in the history of West Virginia<ref>{{cite web|url=http://m.huffpost.com/us/entry/5830642|title=West Virginia Senate Election Results: Shelley Moore Capito Is State's First Female Senator|work=The Huffington Post|accessdate=13 January 2015}}</ref> and the first Republican to win a full term in the Senate from West Virginia since 1942.  She won mostly on large majorities in the counties along the [[Ohio]], [[Pennsylvania]], and [[Virginia]] borders.

In Congress, Senator Capito has sponsored over 113 pieces of [[legislation]], making her the most prolific federal [[legislator]] of the freshmen Senators elected in 2014.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://ballotpedia.org/Shelley_Moore_Capito |title=Shelley Moore Captio|publisher=Ballotpedia|date=4 May 2016}}</ref>  Senator Capito is Chairman of the [[United States Senate Environment and Public Works Subcommittee on Clean Air and Nuclear Safety|Subcommittee on Clean Air and Nuclear Safety]] and [[United States Senate Appropriations Subcommittee on the Legislative Branch|Subcommittee on the Legislative Branch]]; she is the only freshman Senator to chair a subcommittee on the powerful [[United States Senate Committee on Appropriations|Appropriations Committee]].  She is a member of the [[Republican Main Street Partnership|Main Street Partnership]], with three fellow Republican senators, focused on [[Centrism|centrist]] goals in Congress.<ref name="Three New">{{cite web|url=http://www.republicanmainstreet.org/history/|title=Three New Congressional Members Join Main Street|website=Republicanmainstreet.org|accessdate=2014-08-11}}</ref>  The group is the rough equivalent of the [[Blue Dog Coalition|Blue Dog Democrats]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://rockefeller.dartmouth.edu/assets/pdf/gte_lucasdeutchman.pdf|title=Looking for the Productive Center in the 2006 Elections: Running for Congress as a Blue Dog or Main Streeter|last=Lucas|first=DeWayne|author2=Iva Deutchman|website=Rockefeller.dartmouth.edu|date=June 19, 2008|accessdate=2009-05-11}}</ref>

==Personal life and career==
Capito was born '''Shelley Wellons Moore''' in [[Glen Dale, West Virginia]], the daughter of [[Shelley Riley Moore|Shelley]] (née Riley) and [[Arch A. Moore, Jr.|Arch Alfred Moore, Jr.]], who served three terms as the state's [[List of Governors of West Virginia|Governor]].  A resident of Charleston, Capito was educated at the [[Holton-Arms School]]; [[Duke University]], where she earned her bachelor's degree in [[zoology]]; and the [[Curry School of Education|University of Virginia Curry School of Education]], where she earned her master's degree.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.c-span.org/video/?189201-1/qa-shelley-moore-capito |title=Transcript of interview with Rep. Shelley Moore Capito |publisher=Q & A |date=October 30, 2005 |accessdate=2014-11-29}}</ref>  She is a member of [[Kappa Kappa Gamma]] sorority<ref>{{cite web|last=Huston |first=Andy |url=http://www.nicindy.org/blog/23-of-house-41-of-senate-is-greek/ |title=23% of House & 41% of Senate is Greek |publisher=North-American Interfraternity Conference |date= |accessdate=2013-04-14}}</ref> and represented the state of West Virginia as the 1972 Cherry Blossom Princess.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://thehill.com/capital-living/24023-queens-of-the-cherry-blossoms |title=Queens of the cherry blossoms |publisher=TheHill.com |date= |accessdate=2015-05-06}}</ref>  Capito was elected to the 31st district of the [[West Virginia House of Delegates]] in 1996 and served two terms from  December 1, 1996 to December 1, 2000.  The district included a portion of the [[Charleston, West Virginia|Charleston]] area.  Capito was named Minority Chairman of the Health and Human Resources Committee and a member of the Judiciary and Banking and Insurance Committees.

She is married to Charles L. Capito and they have three children:  sons Charles and Moore and daughter Shelley.<ref>[http://www.senate.gov/senators/114thCongress/Capito_ShelleyMoore.htm U.S. Senate – Shelly Moore Capito] Retrieved 11 January 2015.</ref>  In September 2015, ''[[Runner’s World]]'' featured Senator Capito in its “I’m a Runner” [[vlog]], where she states she has been a [[distance running|distance runner]] for over 30 years.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.runnersworld.com/im-a-runner/im-a-runner-shelley-moore-capito|title=I’m a Runner:  Shelley Moore Capito|date=September 8, 2015|work=Runner’s World|accessdate=June 18, 2016}}</ref>

===United States House elections:  2000–12===
;2000, 2002, & 2004
When 2nd district U.S. Congressman [[Bob Wise]] ran for governor in 2000, Capito decided to run and she won the Republican nomination for West Virginia's 2nd district, which was anchored in Charleston and stretched from the [[Ohio River]] in the west to the Eastern Panhandle, which borders with [[Virginia]] and [[Maryland]].  She narrowly defeated millionaire asbestos lawyer [[Jim Humphreys (West Virginia politician)|Jim Humphreys]] 48%–46%.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=701|title=Our Campaigns – WV District 2 Race – Nov 07, 2000|publisher=Ourcampaigns.com|accessdate=13 January 2015}}</ref>  She was the first Republican to represent West Virginia in Congress since 1983, as well as the first woman elected to Congress from West Virginia in her own right.  She won re-election to a second term, defeating Humphreys in rematch 60%–40%.  She won every county in the district except [[Braxton County, West Virginia|Braxton]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=1249|title=Our Campaigns – WV District 2 Race – Nov 05, 2002|publisher=Ourcampaigns.com|accessdate=13 January 2015}}</ref>  She became the first West Virginia Republican to win reelection to Congress since her father, who represented the 1st district in the state's northern region from 1957 to 1969.  She won re-election to a third term, defeating former newscaster [[Erik Wells]] 57%–42%.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=4409|title=Our Campaigns – WV – District 02 Race – Nov 02, 2004|publisher=Ourcampaigns.com|accessdate=13 January 2015}}</ref>

;2010 & 2012

During the 2010 election cycle, she was mentioned as a Republican candidate to challenge [[Joe Manchin]] for the vacated [[United State Senate]] seat of the late Robert C. Byrd.  Capito ultimately decided against a Senate bid, pointing out that, even though the [[West Virginia Legislature]] passed a law allowing her to run for both her House seat and the Senate, "running for two offices simultaneously is not who I am as a person. More importantly, this is not about me, but what is right for the people of West Virginia."<ref>{{cite web|last=Rivard |first=Ry |url=http://www.dailymail.com/News/statenews/201007210263 |title=Capito will not run against Manchin for Byrd's seat |publisher=Charleston Daily Mail |date=July 21, 2010 |accessdate=2013-04-14}}</ref>  Capito won re-election to a sixth term, defeating Lynch Graf,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.newsandsentinel.com/page/content.detail/id/540826/Capito-wins-big--Rahall-bests-former-justice.html?nav=5061 |title=Capito wins big, Rahall bests former justice |publisher=Parkersburg News and Sentinel |date=November 2, 2010 |accessdate=2013-04-14}}</ref> 68%–30%.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=515069|title=Our Campaigns – WV – District 02 Race – Nov 02, 2010|publisher=Ourcampaigns.com|accessdate=13 January 2015}}</ref>  For the first time in her career, she won all 18 counties of the district.  After redistricting, Capito was challenged in the Republican primary for the first time in her career. Capito said she planned on fighting to "dismantle the federal health care overhaul and challenge the U.S. Environmental Protection Agency."<ref name="7th term">{{cite news|title=WVa US Rep Shelley Moore Capito overcomes rare GOP primary challenge in bid for 7th term|url=http://www.therepublic.com/view/story/3009a07fd299494da45ce8ec0fabb7d3/WV-WVa-Primary-2nd-District-GOP/|accessdate=14 May 2012|newspaper=Associated Press|date=8 May 2012}}</ref>  She defeated Delegate [[Jonathan Miller (West Virginia politician)|Jonathan Miller]] and Michael Davis 83%–11%–6%.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=740141|title=Our Campaigns – WV District 2 – R Primary Race – May 08, 2012|publisher=Ourcampaigns.com|accessdate=13 January 2015}}</ref>  She won re-election to a seventh term, defeating former gubernatorial aide Howard Swint, 70%–30%.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=705840|title=Our Campaigns – WV – District 02 Race – Nov 06, 2012|publisher=Ourcampaigns.com|accessdate=13 January 2015}}</ref><ref name="Campaigns Panhandle">{{cite news|last=BELISLE|first=RICHARD|title=Congressional candidate Swint campaigns in the Panhandle|url=http://articles.herald-mail.com/2012-06-11/news/32179582_1_congressional-race-eastern-panhandle-shelley-moore-capito|accessdate=27 August 2012|newspaper=Herald-Mail|date=11 June 2012}}</ref>

===2014 election===
{{Main|United States Senate election in West Virginia, 2014}}
On November 26, 2012, Capito announced her intention to seek the United States Senate seat in play for the 2014 election, intending to challenge longtime incumbent [[Jay Rockefeller]],<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.politico.com/news/stories/1112/84211.html?hp=f3 |title=Shelley Moore Capito makes Senate bid vs. Jay Rockefeller official |first=David |last=Catanese |publisher=Politico |date=November 25, 2012 |accessdate=2013-04-14}}</ref> but he subsequently announced his retirement. Capito’s “Shared Values” commercial featured her saying “We want our country back; we don’t want government coming in and telling us how to pick our doctor, how to educate our children.”<ref>{{cite web|url=https://ballotpedia.org/Shelley_Moore_Capito |title=Shelley Moore Captio|publisher=Ballotpedia|date=4 May 2016}}</ref>  Despite initial protests from [[Tea Party movement|Tea Party]] groups and anti-establishment conservatives that Moore Capito's House voting record was "too liberal,"<ref>Catanese, David. "[http://www.politico.com/news/stories/1112/84240.html GOP split resurfaces after Shelley Moore Capito announcement]." [[Politico.com]]. 2012-11-26. Retrieved 2014-11-08.</ref> she ultimately won 87% of the Republican primary vote.

Rockefeller dropped out of the race on January 11, 2013, making Capito the overwhelming favorite in the general election.  She went on to defeat Democratic Secretary of State [[Natalie Tennant]] in the general election, 62% to 35%—the largest victory margin for a Republican running in a statewide race in West Virginia history.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://riponadvance.com/stories/510649001-capito-resolution-would-void-epa-s-existing-power-plant-emission-regulations|title=Capito resolution would void EPA’s existing power plant emission regulations|publisher=[[Ripon Advance]]|date=2015-11-19}}</ref>  She also carried every county in the state.

==Political positions==
Senator Capito is a member of the [[Republican Main Street Partnership|Republican Main street Partnership]] which seeks centrist solutions and she is described as being more moderate. She "is a relative moderate who supports abortion rights and has crossed the aisle on some votes."<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.washingtonpost.com/news/post-politics/wp/2012/11/26/conservative-backlash-to-shelley-moore-capito-grows/|title=Conservative backlash to Shelley Moore Capito grows|website=Washington Post|access-date=2016-12-16}}</ref> CrowdPac, which provides a conservative or liberal score based on donations they receive and give, has given Senator Capito a score of 3.8C with 10C being the most conservative and 10L being the most liberal.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.crowdpac.com/candidates/866/shelley-capito|title=Shelley Capito {{!}} US Senate, primary (2020) in West Virginia (WV) {{!}} Crowdpac|website=www.crowdpac.com|access-date=2016-12-16}}</ref> The [[American Conservative Union]] has given Capito a lifetime rating of 67.2% conservative.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://acuratings.conservative.org/acu-federal-legislative-ratings/?year1=2015&chamber=11&state1=0&sortable=1|title=ACU Ratings|website=ACU Ratings|access-date=2016-12-20}}</ref> The [[Americans for Democratic Action]] has given her a rating of 15% liberal.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://projects.wsj.com/campaign2012/candidates/view/shelley-moore-capito--WV-H|title=Shelley Moore Capito - Candidate for U.S. President, Republican Nomination - Election 2012|website=WSJ.com|access-date=2016-12-20}}</ref>

===Social policy===
Senator Capito is sponsoring the Gender Advancement in Pay (GAP) Act, saying:  {{quote| “In 2016, it should be common sense that women and men get [[equal pay for equal work]].  Your salary should be based on your experience and qualifications – not your gender.  But unfortunately, gender-based wage differences still exist, and for many women, discrimination in the workplace is an ever-present concern.”<ref>{{cite news|last=Moore Capito|first=Shelley|title=Op-Ed by Shelley Moore Capito on Equal Pay|url=https://www.capito.senate.gov/news/in-the-news/op-ed-by-sens-shelley-moore-capito-and-kelly-ayotte-fight-pay-discrimination|date=2016|work=Press release|accessdate=4 May 2016}}</ref>}}  Senator Capito has sponsored 8 bills on health.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://ballotpedia.org/Shelley_Moore_Capito |title=Shelley Moore Captio|publisher=Ballotpedia|date=4 May 2016}}</ref>  Senator Capito is sponsoring the Rural Access to Hospice Act to improve the quality, access, and retention of [[hospice|hospice facilities]] in [[rural]] parts of the nation.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://hospiceactionnetwork.org/rural-access-to-hospice-act-introduced/|title=Rural Access to Hospice Act introduced|website=hospiceactionnetwork.org|accessdate=10 May 2016}}</ref>  In response to the Supreme Court’s decision in [[Obergefell v. Hodges]] to recognize [[same-sex marriage]], Senator Capito released a statement after noting that her state’s greatest asset are its caring people:  {{quote| “Regardless of our differences, we care for our neighbors, friends and communities in need. Acknowledging that we have differing views, the Supreme Court has made its decision.  While I would have preferred that the Supreme Court leave this decision to the states, it is my hope that all West Virginians will move forward and continue to care for and respect one another.”<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.statejournal.com/story/29416288/us-supreme-court-extends-same-sex-marriage-nationwide|title=WV REACTS:  US Supreme Court extends same-sex marriage nationwide|publisher=The State Journal|accessdate=4 May 2016}}</ref>}}  [[File:Shelley Moore Capito CPAC 2013-1.jpg|thumb|250px|right|Capito addresses [[Conservative Political Action Conference|CPAC]] in 2013.]]  The [[Human Rights Campaign]], in its Congressional Scorecard rating support for [[LGBT]] issues during the [[114th United States Congress|114th Congress]], gave Capito a score of 64%.<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|url=http://hrc-assets.s3-website-us-east-1.amazonaws.com//files/assets/resources/114thCongressionalScorecard.pdf|title=Congressional Scorecard: Measuring Support for Equality in the 114th Congress|last=|first=|date=2016|website=hrc.org|publisher=Human Rights Campaign|access-date=January 1, 2017}}</ref> During the [[113th United States Congress|113th Congress]], Capito received a score of 30%.<ref name=":0" />

Capito has a mixed record on the issue of [[abortion]]. She has been rated 30% by [[NARAL Pro-Choice America|NARAL]] indicating a [[pro-life]] voting record, but she was rated 55% by the [[National Right to Life Committee|National Right to Life]], indicating a mixed record. Her campaign manager stated that "she does not want to overturn Roe v. Wade."<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.newsweek.com/2013/09/13/rep-shelley-moore-capito-moderate-or-loyalist-237942.html|title=Moderate or Loyalist?|last=AM|first=Eleanor Clift On 9/14/13 at 4:45|date=2013-09-14|website=Newsweek|access-date=2016-12-05}}</ref> She has voted against providing Federal funding for abortion and in favor of the [[Partial-Birth Abortion Ban Act]] in 2003. However, she voted against banning family-planning funding in US aid abroad.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.ontheissues.org/House/Shelley_Moore_Capito_Abortion.htm|title=Shelley Moore Capito on Abortion|website=www.ontheissues.org|access-date=2016-12-05}}</ref> She supports [[embryonic stem cell research]] and in 2010 described herself as "[[pro-choice]]." While she selected the label "pro-choice" in 2010, she was supported by pro-life groups as well, making the overall question of whether she is "pro-life" or "pro-choice" difficult to determine.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://votesmart.org/candidate/political-courage-test/11701/shelley-capito/|title=The Voter's Self Defense System|website=Project Vote Smart|language=en-US|access-date=2016-12-05}}</ref> She was described as "pro-choice" again in 2012.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.msnbc.com/rachel-maddow-show/why-the-rights-reaction-shelley-moor|title=Why the right's reaction to Shelley Moore Capito matters|newspaper=MSNBC|access-date=2016-12-05}}</ref> She was also supported by the [[Republican Majority for Choice]] a pro-choice Republican PAC.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.gopchoice.org/about/rmc-in-the-news/|title=Republican Majority for Choice {{!}}   PRESS|website=www.gopchoice.org|access-date=2016-12-05}}</ref> Capito typically avoids discussing the matter.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.ontheissues.org/House/Shelley_Moore_Capito_Abortion.htm |title=Shelley Moore Capito on Abortion |website=Ontheissues.org |access-date=November 17, 2016}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.wvgazettemail.com/article/20141122/GZ01/141129789 |last=Gutman |first=David |title=Capito, Manchin Confound Labels on Abortion |publisher=''Charleston Gazette-Mail'' |access-date=November 19, 2016}}</ref>

She is partnering with Democrat [[Kirsten Gillibrand]] to sponsor the [[Campus Accountability and Safety Act]] (CASA) to help stop sexual assaults on college campuses.

As a representative, Capito voted against the [[Patient Protection and Affordable Care Act]] (commonly called ObamaCare or the Affordable Care Act) in March 2010.  In January 2009, Capito voted to expand the [[Children's Health Insurance Program]] (CHIP) as part of its re-authorization. The expanded coverage would include about four million more children in the program.<ref>{{cite web| url=http://www.govtrack.us/congress/votes/111-2009/h16 |title=H.R. 2 (111th) |work = GovTrack.us | accessdate = September 17, 2013}}</ref>  In May 2008, Capito voted for the [[Post-9/11 Veterans Educational Assistance Act of 2008]] (commonly called the new G.I. Bill), which expanded the educational benefits for military veterans who have served since September 11, 2001.<ref>{{cite web| url=http://clerk.house.gov/evs/2008/roll330.xml | title=Final Vote Results for Roll Call 330 |work = House.gov | accessdate = September 17, 2013}}</ref>

===Foreign policy===
Senator Capito has sponsored approximately 40 bills about [[international trade]] and [[international finance]], the most of any other legislative topic during her career.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://ballotpedia.org/Shelley_Moore_Capito |title=Shelley Moore Captio|publisher=Ballotpedia|date=4 May 2016}}</ref> Senator Capito has criticized the vulnerabilities in current national security policy in the wake of the [[2015 San Bernardino attack|San Bernardino terrorist attack]]<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=1c697CZqUGg|title=Shelley Moore Capito on Trump and education|publisher=West Virginia MetroNews|accessdate=4 May 2016}}</ref> and has sponsored 8 bills on the military and national security.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://ballotpedia.org/Shelley_Moore_Capito |title=Shelley Moore Captio|publisher=Ballotpedia|date=4 May 2016}}</ref> Senator Capito was one of 47 Republican senators to sign Senator [[Tom Cotton]]'s [[Tom Cotton#Letter to Iran's Leaders|open letter to the Iranian government]] in 2015.<ref>{{cite web|author=Jose A. DelReal |url=http://www.washingtonpost.com/blogs/post-politics/wp/2015/03/10/heres-a-list-of-the-gop-senators-who-signed-the-iran-letter/ |title=Here’s a list of the GOP senators who signed the Iran letter |publisher=Washingtonpost.com |date=2012-12-14 |accessdate=2015-05-06}}</ref>  The letter, which sought to dissuade Iran from reaching an agreement with President [[Barack Obama]] regarding [[nuclear peace]], was described by the [[White House]] as "undercutting foreign policy".<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.nytimes.com/2015/03/10/world/asia/white-house-faults-gop-senators-letter-to-irans-leaders.html|title=G.O.P. Senators’ Letter to Iran About Nuclear Deal Angers White House|newspaper=[[The New York Times]]|accessdate=2015-05-06}}</ref>

Capito has a nuanced record on issues concerning [[fair trade]], as follows.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ontheissues.org/House/Shelley_Moore_Capito_Free_Trade.htm|title=Shelley Moore Capito on Free Trade|publisher=On the Issues}}</ref>  Capito voted No in 2005 on [[CAFTA]], the major trade agreement negotiated under President [[George W. Bush]].  She voted Yes in 2003, 2004, and 2007 to approve [[free trade agreement]]s with [[Chile]], [[Singapore]], [[Australia]], and [[Peru]].  She is rated 22% by the [[Cato Institute]], indicating her pro-fair trade voting record.  She supports tariffs against countries that [[currency manipulation|manipulate currencies]], and she sponsored a bill that would create an import fee on countries with an undervalued currency.

===Interior policy===
Senator Capito has sponsored 9 bills on [[environmentalism|environmental integrity]],<ref>{{cite web|url=https://ballotpedia.org/Shelley_Moore_Capito |title=Shelley Moore Captio|publisher=Ballotpedia|date=4 May 2016}}</ref> and has a clear voting record on the issue, including increasing prohibitions against animal fighting.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ontheissues.org/House/Shelley_Moore_Capito_Environment.htm|title=Shelley Moore Capito on the Environment|publisher=On the Issues}}</ref>  Senator Capito supports the Republican Main Street Partnership’s motion to elevate the [[United States Environmental Protection Agency|E.P.A.]] to be a Cabinet-level department, which would bring more oversight to the entity.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ontheissues.org/Notebook/Note_02-RMSP3.htm|title=Republican Main Street Partnership:  Department of Environmental Protection Act|publisher=On the Issues}}</ref>  Capito opposes legislation aimed at capping greenhouse gas emissions.<ref name="Coal dirty">{{cite web|title=POLITICO: Note to EPA: 'Coal' isn't a dirty word|url=http://capito.house.gov/op-eds/politico-note-to-epa-coal-isnt-a-dirty-word/|work=Press Release|publisher=US House of Representatives|accessdate=27 July 2012}}</ref>  In January 2010 she reportedly asked the president if he would reconsider "job-killing" policies like limiting greenhouse gases.<ref name="Capito Political Profile">{{cite news|title=Political Profile for Shelley Moore Capito|url=http://www.washingtonpost.com/politics/shelley-moore-capito-r-wva/gIQA3s9PAP_topic.html#the-issues|work=On the Issues|publisher=Washington Post|accessdate=27 July 2012|first=Al|last=Kamen|date=24 July 2012}}</ref>

===Fiscal policy===
In the House, Capito voted for the Constitutional Balanced Budget Amendment of 2011.<ref>{{cite web| url=http://projects.washingtonpost.com/congress/112/house/1/votes/858/ |title=Balanced Budget Amendment |work = The U.S. Congress Votes Database | accessdate = September 16, 2013}}</ref>  In December 2010, Capito voted to extend the tax cuts enacted during the administration of President George W. Bush.<ref>{{cite web| url=http://projects.washingtonpost.com/congress/111/house/2/votes/647/ |title=To extend Bush tax cuts |work = The U.S. Congress Votes Database | accessdate = September 16, 2013}}</ref>  Capito has sponsored 13 bills on the domestic financial sector, including protections for small, family-operated banks.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://ballotpedia.org/Shelley_Moore_Capito |title=Shelley Moore Captio|publisher=Ballotpedia|date=4 May 2016}}</ref>  Capito supports a federal prohibition on online poker.  In 2006, she cosponsored H.R. 4777, the Internet Gambling Prohibition and Enforcement Act,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://thomas.loc.gov/cgi-bin/bdquery/z?d109:h.r.04777: |title=HR 4777: Internet Gambling Prohibition Act |publisher=Thomas (Library of Congress) |year=2006 |accessdate=2013-04-14}}</ref> and supported H.R. 4411, the [[Goodlatte]]-[[Jim Leach|Leach]] Internet Gambling Prohibition Act.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://thomas.loc.gov/cgi-bin/bdquery/z?d109:h.r.04411: |title=HR 4411: Internet Gambling Prohibition and Enforcement Act |publisher=Thomas (Library of Congress) |date= |accessdate=2013-04-14}}</ref>  In June 2003, Capito introduced the Family Fairness in Taxing Act of 2003.  The bill would accelerate the increase to the child tax credit, increase the qualification age for children, and revise refundability criteria for the credit.<ref>{{cite web| url=http://www.govtrack.us/congress/bills/108/hr2324 |title=H.R. 2324 (108th) |work = GovTrack.us | accessdate = September 16, 2013}}</ref>
